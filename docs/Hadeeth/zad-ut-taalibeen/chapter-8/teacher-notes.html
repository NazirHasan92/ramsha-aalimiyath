★ Master Overview — نَقْشَةُ الْبَابِ

Ten aḥādīth, three movements — the foundations, faith lived in the world, weigh what you take in. One discipline beneath them all: baṣīra — weigh the reality, not the label.

Teaching arc: the chapter trains the believer in three movements, all under one discipline. First, the foundations there's no substitute for — faith is proven by trustworthiness and keeping covenants (#71); true nobility is planning, good character, and restraint, not lineage (#72); and clemency and wisdom are forged by stumbling and experience (#73). Then, a faith lived in the world — within limits — there is no withdrawal or celibacy in Islam (#74); obedience to any creation stops where disobedience to the Creator begins (#75); and wealth is no harm in a God-fearing hand (#76). Finally, weigh what you take in — and why you give — eloquent speech can bewitch toward truth or falsehood (#77); some poetry is genuine wisdom (#78); some "knowledge" is really ignorance (#79); and the slightest ostentation hollows a good deed into shirk (#80).

Chapter ka safar: buniyaadein (jin ka koi badal nahin — 71, 72, 73) → dunya mein zindagi, hudood ke andar (74, 75, 76) → jo lo use taulo, aur jo do uski niyyat dekho (77, 78, 79, 80). In sab ke neeche ek dhaaga: asal ko taulo, zaahir ko nahin — baṣīra.

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the student will be able to:

  1. Recite all 10 aḥādīth in Arabic (every matn is given this chapter), with correct meaning in English + Roman Urdu.
  2. Name the source of each ḥadīth (Bayhaqī, Tirmidhī, Mustadrak, Abū Dāwūd, Baghawī, Ibn Māja, Aḥmad, Bukhārī) at the book's exact level.
  3. Recognise the book's two grammatical groups — the "Lā" sayings (71–76) and the "Inna" sayings (77–80) — and why these aḥādīth are grouped so.
  4. State #71's qualification — it negates the completeness (kamāl) of faith, not faith itself (it is not takfīr).
  5. Give #72's three resets (intelligence = planning, nobility = character not lineage, piety = restraint) and #73's consolation (clemency & wisdom are forged by stumbling and experience).
  6. Explain #74 as Islam's rejection of withdrawal-as-piety, not a ruling that all single people sin.
  7. Teach #75 carefully as a ceiling on obedience (obey creation only within obedience to Allah) — not a licence to rebel or disrespect.
  8. Hold #76 (wealth is neutral; taqwā is the variable) together with "love of the world is the root of sin" — no contradiction.
  9. Pair #77 (eloquence cuts both ways) with #78 (poetry can be wisdom) — judge by content, not container — and read #79 (some "knowledge" is ignorance) as non-beneficial / unacted-upon knowledge.
  10. Land #80 as lesser shirk about intention (riyāʾ hollows a deed), without breeding waswasa — the chapter's deep thread being baṣīra: weigh the reality, not the label.

Ḥadīth 71 · لَا إِيْمَانَ لِمَنْ لَّا أَمَانَةَ لَهُ، وَلَا دِينَ لِمَنْ لَّا عَهْدَ لَهُ · Faith is proven by trust

Theme 1 — The foundations. "Lā" saying. Faith shows in trustworthiness and keeping your word.

لَا إِيْمَانَ لِمَنْ لَّا أَمَانَةَ لَهُ، وَلَا دِينَ لِمَنْ لَّا عَهْدَ لَهُ

lā īmāna li-man lā amānata lah, wa lā dīna li-man lā ʿahda lah

"There is no faith for the one who has no trustworthiness, and no religion for the one who keeps no covenant."

"Jis mein amaanat nahin uska īmān nahin, aur jo apna ahd (waada) na nibhaaye uska deen nahin."

Bayhaqī (Shuʿab al-īmān)
Where this fits: Theme 1, node 1 — opens the chapter by tying the invisible (faith) to the testable (amāna, ʿahd). The chapter's "weigh the reality, not the label" begins here.

أَمَانَة / عَهْد · amāna / ʿahd — "trustworthiness / covenant"

Meaning
Amāna is honouring what is entrusted to you — a deposit, a secret, a duty, a relationship. ʿAhd is keeping your promises and agreements. The ḥadīth ties the invisible (faith, religion) to the visible and testable: you can't inspect a heart, but you can watch whether a person returns trusts and keeps his word. That is the proof.
Roman Urdu
Amāna = jo cheez tumhe sonpi gayi (deposit, raaz, zimmedaari, rishta) use sahi rakhna; ʿahd = waade poore karna. Hadith ghaib (īmān) ko qaabil-e-test cheez se baandhti hai: dil nahin dekha jaa sakta, magar amaanat lautaana aur waada nibhaana dekha jaa sakta hai.
⚠️ The qualification (from the book)
The negation is most likely of the completeness (kamāl) of faith and the certitude (yaqīn) of religion — not of faith and religion themselves. A person who betrays a trust is doing something unbecoming of a believer; he does not thereby become an unbeliever. (Second reading: making a habit of such treachery could, over time, lead to losing faith entirely.)
The takeaway
Yeh ek sakht tambeeh hai — takfīr ka fatwa nahin. Amaanat aur waade ko bohot sanjeedgi se lo, kyunki sacha īmān inhi se zaahir hota hai.

⚠️ Landmine 1 — "so a promise-breaker is a kāfir"

The wording sounds like takfīr; it is not. "Yeh hadith yeh nahin keh rahi ke amaanat-khor kaafir ho gaya. Kitaab saaf kehti hai — yeh kamāl (poore) īmān ki nafi hai, asal īmān ki nahin. Aisa shakhs ek momin ke laayaq nahin kaam kar raha hai, magar deen se nikla nahin. Yeh sakht tambeeh hai: amaanat aur waade ko sanjeedgi se lo."

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 23:8

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ لِأَمَانَاتِهِمْ وَعَهْدِهِمْ رَاعُونَ

"And they who are attentive to their trusts and their covenants."

Why this fits: Among the very signs of the successful believers (al-Muʾminūn), Allah names guarding trusts (amānāt) and covenants (ʿahd) — the exact two things #71 makes the proof of faith.

Urdu mein: Kaamyaab momineen ki nishaaniyon mein Allah amaanaton aur ahd ki hifaazat ginvaata hai — wahi do cheezein jinhein #71 īmān ki daleel banaati hai.

Ḥadīth 72 · لَا عَقْلَ كَالتَّدْبِيرِ، وَلَا حَسَبَ كَحُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ، وَلَا وَرَعَ كَالْكَفِّ · Three counterfeits exposed

Theme 1 — The foundations. Intelligence, nobility, piety — each reset to its real measure.

لَا عَقْلَ كَالتَّدْبِيرِ، وَلَا حَسَبَ كَحُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ، وَلَا وَرَعَ كَالْكَفِّ

lā ʿaqla ka-t-tadbīr, wa lā ḥasaba ka-ḥusni l-khuluq, wa lā waraʿa ka-l-kaff

"There is no intelligence like planning, no nobility like good character, and no piety like restraint."

"Koi ʿaql tadbeer (planning) jaisi nahin, koi sharaafat husn-e-akhlaaq jaisi nahin, aur koi taqwa (rukne) jaisa nahin."

Bayhaqī (Shuʿab al-īmān)
Where this fits: Theme 1 — takes three things people think they understand and resets each to its substance: plan well, be of good character, restrain yourself.

تَدْبِير / حَسَب / كَفّ · tadbīr / ḥasab / kaff

Meaning
(1) No intelligence (ʿaql) like tadbīr — real intelligence is thinking through the outcomes of your actions and what benefits you in the Hereafter. (2) No nobility (ḥasab) like good character — ḥasab normally means ancestral honour; the real nobility is cordial dealing with people (mudārāt) within Allah's limits. (3) No piety (waraʿ) like restraint (kaff) — holding yourself back from all that is prohibited and doubtful.
Roman Urdu
(1) Asli ʿaql = anjaam soch kar Aakhirat ke liye sahi raasta chunna, tez zabaan nahin. (2) Asli sharaafat = khoon/khaandaan nahin, balke logon se achha bartaao Allah ki hudood mein. (3) Asli waraʿ = har haraam aur mashkook cheez se ruk jaana.
The shared point
Three labels — intelligence, nobility, piety — each redefined by its substance, not its surface. This is the chapter's "weigh the reality, not the label" in a single breath.
The takeaway
Teen labels — har ek apne asal se naya taula gaya. Yahi poore chapter ki basheerat ek saans mein.

Ḥadīth 73 · لَا حَلِيمَ إِلَّا ذُو عَثْرَةٍ، وَلَا حَكِيمَ إِلَّا ذُو تَجْرِبَةٍ · Clemency & wisdom are forged

Theme 1 — The foundations. Your past stumbles become the raw material of clemency.

لَا حَلِيمَ إِلَّا ذُو عَثْرَةٍ، وَلَا حَكِيمَ إِلَّا ذُو تَجْرِبَةٍ

lā ḥalīma illā dhū ʿathra, wa lā ḥakīma illā dhū tajriba

"There is no clement person except one who has stumbled, and no wise person except one of experience."

"Koi sacha bardaasht-waala nahin siwaaye us ke jo khud thokar kha chuka, aur koi sacha hakeem nahin siwaaye tajrubekaar ke."

Tirmidhī, Mustadrak
Where this fits: Theme 1 closes — clemency and wisdom are not gifts dropped on the lucky; they are forged in real life. A consoling note: past stumbles become raw material.

حِلْم / حِكْمَة · ḥilm / ḥikma — "forbearance / wisdom"

Meaning
Ḥilm is forbearance — the calm self-control to forgive and not lash out. Ḥikma is wisdom, sound judgement. The book's logic: the one who has himself slipped (ʿathra) knows what forgiveness is worth, so he is gentler when others slip; and a wise person is precisely one whose decisions are built on experience (tajriba), not theory.
Roman Urdu
Ḥilm = bardaasht, woh thandak jo maaf karne deti hai. Ḥikma = sahi faisla. Kitaab: jo khud phisla woh maafi ki qadar jaanta hai — isliye doosron par narm; aur ḥakīm wahi jiska faisla tajrube par bana ho.
The consolation
There is real comfort here. Your past stumbles are not only shame — handled rightly, they become the raw material of clemency. The one who never failed is often the harshest judge; the one who fell and was forgiven becomes soft toward the fallen.
The takeaway
Tumhari pichli thokarein sirf sharm nahin — sahi sambhaali jaayein to ḥilm ka khaam maal ban jaati hain. Hikmat-o-bardaasht zindagi ki aag mein dhalti hain.

Ḥadīth 74 · لَا صَرُورَةَ فِي الْإِسْلَامِ · There is no celibacy in Islam

Theme 2 — Faith in the world. The path runs through the world, not away from it.

Where this fits: Theme 2 opens — turning outward from who the believer is (Theme 1) to how he engages the world. The "label vs reality" of piety itself.

صَرُورَة · ṣarūra — "celibacy / renouncing the obligatory"

Meaning
Ṣarūra is celibacy — renouncing marriage as a supposed act of devotion (the monk's path). Islam does not consider withdrawal from married life a virtue; the Prophetic way is to engage: marry, build a household, be in the world. The book adds a second meaning: ṣarūra also refers to abandoning the pilgrimage (ḥajj) after it has become obligatory.
Roman Urdu
Ṣarūra = shaadi se jaan-boojh kar "ibaadat" samajh kar kinaara-kashi (rahbaaniyat). Islam ise fazeelat nahin samajhta — Nabawi raasta shaamil hona hai: nikah, ghar, dunya mein rehna. Kitaab ka doosra matlab: farz ḥajj ko chhod dena bhi ṣarūra hai.
⚠️ NOT marriage-policing
On either meaning the book's verdict is: such a person is not a complete Muslim. But this targets withdrawal-as-piety — it is not a ruling that everyone single is sinning, nor a stick to beat someone who can't marry. Piety is restraint within a fully lived life (link #72's waraʿ), not retreat from life.
The takeaway
Asli paakeezgi dunya se bhaagne mein nahin — dunya ke beech se guzarti hai. Yeh har ghair-shaadi-shuda par taana nahin — rahbaaniyat ka radd hai.

⚠️ Landmine 2 — "so marriage is compulsory and staying single is sinful"

"Yeh us shakhs ke baare mein hai jo shaadi ko 'ibaadat' samajh kar chhod de — rahbaaniyat. Yeh har ghair-shaadi-shuda ko gunaahgaar nahin keh rahi, na us par taana hai jise rishta nahin mila ya jo majboor hai. Sabaq: Islam mein paakeezgi dunya se bhaagne mein nahin — dunya mein shaamil hona hai."

Ḥadīth 75 · لَا طَاعَةَ لِمَخْلُوقٍ فِي مَعْصِيَةِ الْخَالِقِ · The one ceiling on every authority

Theme 2 — Faith in the world. The weightiest ḥadīth of the chapter — teach with care.

لَا طَاعَةَ لِمَخْلُوقٍ فِي مَعْصِيَةِ الْخَالِقِ

lā ṭāʿata li-makhlūqin fī maʿṣiyati l-khāliq

"There is no obedience to any creation in [something involving] disobedience to the Creator."

"Kisi makhlooq ki itaa'at us cheez mein nahin jisme Khaaliq ki naafarmaani ho."

Baghawī (Sharḥ al-Sunna)
Where this fits: Theme 2 — Islam commands obedience in many directions, but every one has a ceiling. Handle Landmine 3 carefully: this is a limit, not a licence.

طَاعَة / مَخْلُوق / خَالِق · ṭāʿa / makhlūq / khāliq — "obedience / creation / Creator"

Meaning
Islam commands obedience to parents, rulers, teachers, those over you — and takes it seriously. But every one has a ceiling: the moment a created authority commands what Allah has forbidden, that command loses its claim on you. The book's example: one is strongly commanded to respect and obey parents — but when they command something conflicting with Allah or His Messenger ﷺ, their command is overruled.
Roman Urdu
Islam maa-baap, hukmraan, ustaad ki itaa'at ka hukm deta hai. Magar har itaa'at ki ek chhat hai: jis lamhe koi makhlooq Allah ke mana karda kaam ka hukm de, us hukm ka tum par koi haq nahin rehta. Kitaab ki misaal: maa-baap ki izzat ka hukm hai — magar Allah/Rasool ﷺ ke khilaaf hukm radd ho jaata hai.
⚠️ Two truths together
(1) This does not cancel the underlying duty of respect, kindness, and service — you still honour your parents, ruler, elders. (2) Obedience to people is never absolute — it is nested inside obedience to Allah. So it is a limit, not a charter for rebellion: decline the specific sinful command, gently, while keeping the relationship and general obedience intact.
The takeaway
Itaa'at-e-makhlooq hamesha Allah ki itaa'at ke andar hai. Yeh hadd hai — baghaawat ya bad-tameezi ka parwaana nahin. Sirf us khaas gunaah waale hukm se narmi-adab se inkaar.

⚠️ Landmine 3 — "so I can disobey/disrespect my parents or rulers whenever I disagree"

The heaviest landmine in the chapter. "Khabardaar — yeh har ikhtilaaf par naafarmaani ka parwaana nahin. Yeh sirf tab laagu hota hai jab koi Allah ki naafarmaani ka hukm de. Aur tab bhi: tum us khaas gunaah waale hukm se — narmi aur adab se — inkaar karte ho, jabke izzat, mehrbaani, aur baaqi har jaayaz baat mein itaa'at barqaraar rakhte ho. Itaa'at-e-makhlooq hamesha Allah ki itaa'at ke andar hai — yeh hadd hai, baghaawat nahin."

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 31:15

وَإِن جَاهَدَاكَ عَلَىٰ أَن تُشْرِكَ بِي مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِ عِلْمٌ فَلَا تُطِعْهُمَا

"But if they strive to make you associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, do not obey them."

Why this fits: The verse names the exact limit — even parents, whom Allah commands us to honour, are not to be obeyed in disobedience to Allah. And note: the very next clause still says accompany them in this world with kindness — the relationship stays intact. That is #75 precisely.

Urdu mein: Aayat wahi hadd batati hai — maa-baap bhi, jinki izzat ka Allah hukm deta hai, Allah ki naafarmaani mein itaa'at ke laayaq nahin. Aur aage hi hukm hai: dunya mein unke saath achha bartaao karo — rishta qaayam rehta hai. Yahi #75.

Ḥadīth 76 · لَا بَأْسَ بِالْغِنَى لِمَنِ اتَّقَى اللهَ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ · Wealth is no harm — to the God-fearing

Theme 2 — Faith in the world. The variable is not the money; it is the taqwā that holds it.

لَا بَأْسَ بِالْغِنَى لِمَنِ اتَّقَى اللهَ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ

lā baʾsa bi-l-ghinā li-mani-ttaqā Llāha ʿazza wa jall

"There is no harm in wealth for one who fears Allah, the Mighty and Majestic."

"Maal mein koi harj nahin us ke liye jo Allah ʿazza wa jalla se darta hai."

Ibn Māja, Aḥmad, Mustadrak
Where this fits: Theme 2 closes — just as Islam doesn't ask you to flee marriage (#74) or rebel against all authority (#75), it doesn't ask you to flee wealth. Hold it with taqwā.

wealth is NEUTRAL"]:::root W --> A["a God-fearing owner →

not POSSESSING it with taqwā"]:::ex classDef root fill:#14532d,stroke:#14532d,color:#fff,stroke-width:2px,font-weight:bold; classDef main fill:#cffafe,stroke:#0e7490,color:#0c4a6e,stroke-width:2px,font-weight:bold; classDef ex fill:#ffe4e6,stroke:#be123c,color:#881337;

غِنَى · ghinā — "wealth, affluence"

Meaning
The variable is not the money but the taqwā of the one who holds it. The book: a pious, God-fearing wealthy person spends in Allah's path and promotes good — so his wealth is a blessing and a tool. An unrighteous person squanders it — a liability in the Hereafter. The book quotes: "How excellent is pure wealth in the possession of a righteous person" (Al-Adab al-mufrad).
Roman Urdu
Variable paisa nahin, balke us ka taqwa jo paisa rakhta hai. Muttaqī maaldaar use Allah ke raaste aur bhalaai mein kharch karta hai — maal ne'mat-o-auzaar. Naafarmaan use zaaya karta hai — Aakhirat mein bojh.
⚠️ No contradiction with "love of the world"
Hold this with the wider teaching (Chapter 5 #49, "love of the world is the root of all sin"). The danger is love of wealth that displaces Allah; the safety is possessing wealth while fearing Allah and spending it rightly. Don't let #76 become "money is always fine" — it is fine for the muttaqī.
The takeaway
Maal neutral hai — jis haath mein ho usi ka rang le leta hai. Khatra maal ki mohabbat hai; salaamati maal taqwa ke saath rakhna hai.

Ḥadīth 77 · إِنَّ مِنَ الْبَيَانِ لَسِحْرًا · Eloquence is a power — both ways

Theme 3 — Weigh what you take in. The "Inna" sayings begin. Judge by content, not charm.

إِنَّ مِنَ الْبَيَانِ لَسِحْرًا

inna mina l-bayāni la-siḥran

"Indeed, some eloquent speech is [like] magic."

"Beshak baʿz fasaahat (khoobsoorat baat) mein jaadu hota hai."

Bukhārī
Where this fits: Theme 3 opens — the grammar shifts from "Lā" to "Inna". Eloquence is a neutral power; the discernment runs two ways (listener & speaker).

eloquence can BEWITCH"]:::root B --> A["as a LISTENER:
charm ≠ proof — weigh content"]:::main B --> C["as a SPEAKER:
aim the gift at truth"]:::main A --> E["a moving speech can carry
truth OR falsehood"]:::ex C --> N["'whether for good or otherwise'
(the book's neutral note)"]:::ex classDef root fill:#14532d,stroke:#14532d,color:#fff,stroke-width:2px,font-weight:bold; classDef main fill:#cffafe,stroke:#0e7490,color:#0c4a6e,stroke-width:2px,font-weight:bold; classDef ex fill:#ffe4e6,stroke:#be123c,color:#881337;

بَيَان / سِحْر · bayān / siḥr — "eloquence / magic"

Meaning
Bayān is eloquence — clear, beautiful, persuasive speech. The book: such speech is "magical" in that it is spellbinding — it captivates hearts, sweeps people along, bends their will — whether for the sake of good or otherwise. Eloquence is a power, neutral in itself; it takes the direction the speaker points it.
Roman Urdu
Bayān = fasaahat, asar-daar baat. Kitaab: aisi baat "jaadu" is maʿne mein ke woh dilon ko moh leti hai — chahe bhalaai ke liye ho ya kisi aur cheez ke liye. Fasaahat taaqat hai, khud-ba-khud achhi ya buri nahin.
Discernment runs two ways
Listener: don't let a moving speech be its own proof — the fact that words bewitch says nothing about whether they're true; weigh the content, not the charm. Speaker: if Allah gave you eloquence, aim it at truth and good, never at deceiving people.
The takeaway
Sunne waale: charm nahin, content taulo — moh lena sach hone ki daleel nahin. Bolne waale: apni fasaahat haq ki taraf chalaao, dhoke mein kabhi nahin.

Ḥadīth 78 · إِنَّ مِنَ الشِّعْرِ حِكْمَةً · Some poetry is wisdom

Theme 3 — Weigh what you take in. Don't dismiss a form — weigh its content (the mirror of #77).

إِنَّ مِنَ الشِّعْرِ حِكْمَةً

inna mina sh-shiʿri ḥikmatan

"Indeed, some poetry is wisdom."

"Beshak baʿz shaayri mein hikmat hoti hai."

Bukhārī
Where this fits: Theme 3 — the exact mirror of #77. In #77 beautiful speech isn't automatically good; in #78 dismissible poetry isn't automatically bad. Both attack judging by the container.

SOME poetry = wisdom"]:::root S --> A["the FORM doesn't decide worth —

شِعْر / حِكْمَة · shiʿr / ḥikma — "poetry / wisdom"

Meaning
People over-glorify poetry or wave it away as frivolous. The ḥadīth cuts through both: some poetry is wisdom — not all. The form doesn't decide worth; the content does. Poetry carrying truth, sound counsel, and moral insight is ḥikma and is valued; poetry carrying falsehood or indecency is not redeemed by being well-crafted.
Roman Urdu
Log shaayri ko ya barhaate hain ya faaltu kehte hain. Hadith dono ko kaat-ti hai: baʿz shaayri hikmat hai — saari nahin. Qaalib nahin, content qadar tay karta hai. Sach aur naseehat waali shaayri = ḥikma; jhoot/behayaai waali khoobsoorti se paak nahin hoti.
The pair with #77
The same lazy shortcut attacked twice: judging by the container, not the contents. Don't accept a thing because it's eloquent (#77), nor reject it because it's "just poetry" (#78) — open it and weigh what's inside.
The takeaway
Qaalib (form) qadar tay nahin karta — content karta hai. Sach aur naseehat waali shaayri hikmat hai; jhoot waali khoobsoorti se paak nahin hoti.

Ḥadīth 79 · إِنَّ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ جَهْلًا، وَإِنَّ مِنَ الْقَوْلِ عِيَالًا · Not everything called "knowledge" benefits

Theme 3 — Weigh what you take in. The deepest "Inna" saying — handle as discernment, not anti-knowledge.

إِنَّ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ جَهْلًا، وَإِنَّ مِنَ الْقَوْلِ عِيَالًا

inna mina l-ʿilmi jahlan, wa inna mina l-qawli ʿiyālan

"Indeed, some 'knowledge' is ignorance, and some speech is a burden."

"Beshak baʿz 'ilm' mein jahaalat hai, aur baʿz baat (sunne waale par) bojh hai."

Abū Dāwūd
Where this fits: Theme 3 — a thing wearing the label "knowledge" or "speech" may, in reality, be ignorance or a burden. Weigh whether what you learn benefits and whether what you say lands.

some 'knowledge' = ignorance"]:::root I --> A["non-beneficial 'knowledge'

speech can be a BURDEN"]:::main A --> E1["⚠️ NOT anti-knowledge — sacred,
beneficial ʿilm is praised"]:::ex C --> E2["burden = said to one who
already knows, or can't grasp it"]:::ex classDef root fill:#14532d,stroke:#14532d,color:#fff,stroke-width:2px,font-weight:bold; classDef main fill:#cffafe,stroke:#0e7490,color:#0c4a6e,stroke-width:2px,font-weight:bold; classDef ex fill:#ffe4e6,stroke:#be123c,color:#881337;

عِلْم / جَهْل / عِيَال · ʿilm / jahl / ʿiyāl — "knowledge / ignorance / burden"

Meaning
Sacred knowledge lights the path and turns one from sin — praiseworthy. But some "knowledge" the Sharīʿa doesn't count beneficial, and chasing it derails a person from what does benefit — so it becomes, for him, a form of ignorance. The book's image (from Azharī): one who acquires knowledge but never acts on it is like a donkey carrying huge tomes — bearing books, understanding nothing (Qurʾān 62:5). And the second half: some speech is a burden when said to one who already knows it or cannot grasp it.
Roman Urdu
Deeni ʿilm hidaayat ka raasta — qaabil-e-tareef. Magar baʿz "ilm" Shari'a ke nazdeek faayda-mand nahin aur asal faayde se hata deta hai — to woh ek qism ki jahaalat. Kitaab ki tasveer: jo ilm seekhe magar amal na kare woh gadhe jaisa jo kitaabein laade — samajh kuch nahin (62:5). Doosra hissa: baat bojh jab us ko ki jaaye jo pehle se jaanta ho ya samajh hi na sake.
⚠️ Not anti-knowledge
The cure is beneficial knowledge, acted upon — not less knowledge. The ḥadīth targets non-beneficial or unacted-upon knowledge, and Qatāda's verdict closes it: whoever disobeys Allah is ignorant, however much he "knows."
The takeaway
Taulo: jo seekhte ho woh faayda deta hai (aur uspar amal hota hai)? Aur jo kehte ho woh sunne waale tak pohanchta hai — ya us par bojh ban-ta hai?

⚠️ Landmine 4 — "so the dīn is against knowledge / learning"

"Yeh ilm ke khilaaf nahin — deeni aur faayda-mand ilm ki to deen bhar mein tareef hai. Hadith do cheezein nishaana banaati hai: (1) woh 'ilm' jo faayda-mand nahin aur asal faayde se hata de, aur (2) woh ilm jo seekha magar uspar amal nahin — woh gadha jo kitaabein laade phirta hai magar samajhta kuch nahin (62:5). Ilaaj kam ilm nahin — faayda-mand ilm, jis par amal ho."

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 62:5

كَمَثَلِ الْحِمَارِ يَحْمِلُ أَسْفَارًا

"...like the likeness of a donkey carrying volumes [of books]."

Why this fits: This is the verse the book itself cites for #79 — those entrusted with knowledge who do not uphold it are like a donkey loaded with books it cannot understand. Knowledge unacted-upon is no knowledge at all.

Urdu mein: Yahi woh aayat hai jo kitaab khud #79 ke liye naqal karti hai — jinhein ilm diya gaya magar amal nahin kiya, woh us gadhe jaise jo kitaabein laade. Be-amal ilm goya ilm hi nahin.

Ḥadīth 80 · إِنَّ يَسِيرَ الرِّيَاءِ شِرْكٌ · The final scale — weigh why you act

Theme 3 — Weigh what you take in. The chapter's close — turn the x-ray inward.

إِنَّ يَسِيرَ الرِّيَاءِ شِرْكٌ

inna yasīra r-riyāʾi shirk

"Indeed, even the slightest ostentation is shirk."

"Beshak thoda sa riyāʾ (dikhaawa) bhi shirk hai."

Ibn Māja
Where this fits: Theme 3 closes — and crowns the chapter. After weighing others' speech, poetry, and "knowledge," the last ḥadīth turns the scale onto the hidden motive of your own deeds.

رِيَاء · riyāʾ — "ostentation, showing off in worship"

Meaning
Even a small amount of riyāʾ — performing worship to be seen — is shirk. The book supports it with the Prophet's ﷺ words that the thing he feared most for his community was "the lesser association" (al-shirk al-aṣghar), and when asked, he answered: riyāʾ. On the Day of Judgement, Allah will tell the ostentatious: "Go to those for whom you performed your deeds — do you find any reward with them?"
Roman Urdu
Thoda sa riyāʾ — ʿibaadat logon ko dikhaane ke liye — bhi shirk hai. Kitaab Nabi ﷺ ke farmaan se taaeed karti hai: jis se woh sab se zyada darte the woh "chhota shirk" (al-shirk al-aṣghar) — yaʿni riyāʾ. Qayaamat ke din Allah dikhaawe waalon se kahega: "jaao un ke paas jinke liye amal karte the — koi ajr paate ho?"
⚠️ Lesser shirk — handle gently
This is shirk aṣghar — lesser shirk. It does not mean the person leaves Islam (that is major shirk); it means a deed done to be seen is hollowed out — its reward stripped — because the intention was split between Allah and an audience. The cure is #69 territory: do the deed for Allah alone.
Why it is the perfect close
Poore chapter ne cheezon ke label ke peechhe dekhna sikhaaya; aakhri baat woh x-ray tum par mod deti hai: nek kaam nek nahin agar uski asli wajah dikhaawa ho. Nabi ﷺ ka sab se bara khauf uunchi awaaz waale gunaah nahin — yeh khaamosh cheez jo hamaare behtareen aamaal mein chhupti hai.

⚠️ Landmine 5 — "so any good deed someone notices is ruined / I'm always sinning"

This breeds waswasa. "Ise dil par bojh mat banao. Yeh chhota shirk (shirk aṣghar) hai — Islam se nikaalne wala bara shirk nahin. Aur yeh us niyyat ke baare mein hai jab amal logon ko dikhaane ke liye ho. Agar koi nek kaam dekh le aur qudrati khushi ho — ya tum doosron ko targheeb dene ke liye khule-aam neki karo — woh riyāʾ nahin. Zeher yeh hai ke amal Allah ke liye nahin, logon ki nazron ke liye ho. Ilaaj: niyyat sirf Allah ke liye (#69)."

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 18:110

فَمَن كَانَ يَرْجُو لِقَاءَ رَبِّهِ فَلْيَعْمَلْ عَمَلًا صَالِحًا وَلَا يُشْرِكْ بِعِبَادَةِ رَبِّهِ أَحَدًا

"So whoever hopes for the meeting with his Lord — let him do righteous deeds and associate none in the worship of his Lord."

Why this fits: The verse names the exact remedy for riyāʾ — a righteous deed must be done for Allah alone, with no one associated in its worship. Showing off "associates" an audience into worship — the very thing this verse forbids.

Urdu mein: Aayat riyāʾ ka theek ilaaj batati hai — nek amal sirf Allah ke liye ho, uski ʿibaadat mein kisi ko shareek na karo. Dikhaawa naazireen ko ʿibaadat mein "shareek" karta hai — wahi jo aayat mana karti hai.

⚠️ Common student confusions

#71 — "a promise-breaker is a kāfir"

The negation is of the completeness (kamāl) of faith, not faith itself. Betraying a trust is gravely unbecoming of a believer — but it is not, in itself, kufr.

Yeh kamāl (poore) īmān ki nafi hai, asal īmān ki nahin. Amaanat-khori momin ke laayaq nahin — magar kufr nahin.

#74 — "marriage is compulsory / single = sinful"

It condemns withdrawal-as-piety (the monk's path), not every unmarried person. Islam's path runs through the world, not away from it.

Yeh rahbaaniyat ka radd hai, har ghair-shaadi-shuda par taana nahin. Paakeezgi dunya mein shaamil hone mein hai.

#75 — "I can disobey/disrespect my parents or rulers whenever I disagree"

A ceiling on obedience, not a licence. It applies only when a created authority commands an actual sin — and even then you decline the specific command gently, keeping respect and the relationship intact.

Yeh itaa'at ki hadd hai, baghaawat nahin. Sirf gunaah waale hukm par — narmi se inkaar; izzat aur rishta barqaraar.

#76 — "money is always fine"

Wealth is neutral; the variable is taqwā. It is no harm for the God-fearing. Hold it with "love of the world is the root of sin" — the danger is love of wealth, not possessing it rightly.

Maal neutral hai — variable taqwa. Khatra maal ki mohabbat hai; muttaqī ke haath mein maal achha hai.

#79 — "the dīn is against knowledge"

The opposite — beneficial knowledge is praised. The ḥadīth targets non-beneficial knowledge and knowledge never acted on (the donkey, 62:5). The cure is more beneficial knowledge, acted upon.

Iska ulta — faayda-mand ilm ki tareef. Nishaana be-faayda aur be-amal ilm hai (gadha, 62:5). Ilaaj: faayda-mand ilm + amal.

#80 — "any good deed someone notices is ruined / I'm always sinning"

Lesser shirk, about intention — performing the act to be seen. Natural gladness that good was noticed, or doing good openly to encourage others, is not riyāʾ. Don't spiral into waswasa.

Yeh chhota shirk hai, niyyat ka — dikhaane ke liye karna. Qudrati khushi ya targheeb ke liye khuli neki riyāʾ nahin. Waswasa mat banao.
