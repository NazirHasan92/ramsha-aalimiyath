MOVEMENT 1 · THE FOUNDATIONS

No substitute for these

Aḥādīth 71, 72, 73 — what the believer is truly built on.

Amaanat · asli sharaafat · tajrube se hikmat.

Ḥadīth 71

لَا إِيْمَانَ لِمَنْ لَّا أَمَانَةَ لَهُ، وَلَا دِينَ لِمَنْ لَّا عَهْدَ لَهُ

lā īmāna li-man lā amānata lah, wa lā dīna li-man lā ʿahda lah

"There is no faith for one with no trustworthiness, and no religion for one who keeps no covenant."

"Jis mein amaanat nahin uska īmān nahin; jo waada na nibhaaye uska deen nahin."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-īmān

Faith shows in what you do

You can't inspect a heart — but you can see if a person keeps trusts and promises.

Not takfīr. The negation is of the completeness of faith — a promise-breaker is unbecoming of a believer, but does not leave Islam.

Yeh poore īmān ki nafi hai — kufr ka fatwa nahin. Sakht tambeeh.

📿 Qurʾān · 23:8

وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ لِأَمَانَاتِهِمْ وَعَهْدِهِمْ رَاعُونَ

"And they who are attentive to their trusts and covenants."

Among the signs of the successful believers: guarding amāna & ʿahd — the very proof #71 names.

Kaamyaab momineen ki nishaani — amaanat aur ahd ki hifaazat.

Ḥadīth 72

لَا عَقْلَ كَالتَّدْبِيرِ، وَلَا حَسَبَ كَحُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ، وَلَا وَرَعَ كَالْكَفِّ

lā ʿaqla ka-t-tadbīr, wa lā ḥasaba ka-ḥusni l-khuluq, wa lā waraʿa ka-l-kaff

"No intelligence like planning, no nobility like good character, no piety like restraint."

"Koi ʿaql tadbeer jaisi nahin, koi sharaafat akhlaaq jaisi nahin, koi taqwa rukne jaisa nahin."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-īmān

Three labels, reset to reality

LabelReal measure
Intelligence (ʿaql)planning toward the Hereafter
Nobility (ḥasab)good character, not lineage
Piety (waraʿ)restraint — what you don't do

The chapter's "weigh the reality, not the label" — in a single breath.

Teen labels — har ek apne asal se taula gaya.

Ḥadīth 73

لَا حَلِيمَ إِلَّا ذُو عَثْرَةٍ، وَلَا حَكِيمَ إِلَّا ذُو تَجْرِبَةٍ

lā ḥalīma illā dhū ʿathra, wa lā ḥakīma illā dhū tajriba

"No clement person except one who has stumbled, no wise person except one of experience."

"Koi bardaasht-waala nahin siwaaye jo thokar kha chuka; koi hakeem nahin siwaaye tajrubekaar ke."

Tirmidhī · Mustadrak

Forged, not granted

The one who has slipped knows the worth of forgiveness — so he is gentler when others slip.

Your past stumbles, handled rightly, become the raw material of clemency. The one who never failed is often the harshest judge.

Pichli thokarein ḥilm ka khaam maal ban jaati hain.

MOVEMENT 2 · FAITH IN THE WORLD

Within limits

Aḥādīth 74, 75, 76 — engage with life, on Allah's terms.

Rahbaaniyat nahin · itaa'at ki hadd · taqwa ke saath maal.

Ḥadīth 74

لَا صَرُورَةَ فِي الْإِسْلَامِ

lā ṣarūrata fi l-islām

"There is no celibacy in Islam."

"Islam mein koi (rahbaaniyat jaisi) be-nikaahi nahin."

Abū Dāwūd · Mustadrak

The path runs through the world

Islam rejects withdrawal-as-piety. The Prophetic way: marry, build a household, engage.

Not marriage-policing. It condemns the monk's renunciation — not every unmarried person, nor one who can't marry.

Rahbaaniyat ka radd — har ghair-shaadi-shuda par taana nahin.

Ḥadīth 75

لَا طَاعَةَ لِمَخْلُوقٍ فِي مَعْصِيَةِ الْخَالِقِ

lā ṭāʿata li-makhlūqin fī maʿṣiyati l-khāliq

"There is no obedience to any creation in disobedience of the Creator."

"Kisi makhlooq ki itaa'at us cheez mein nahin jisme Khaaliq ki naafarmaani ho."

Baghawī · Sharḥ al-Sunna

The one ceiling on obedience

Obey parents, rulers, elders — seriously. But every obedience has a ceiling: a command of sin loses its claim on you. (Even parents — yet keep honouring them.)

A limit, not a licence. Not permission to rebel or be rude. Decline only the sinful command, gently — keep the relationship and general obedience intact.

Itaa'at ki hadd — baghaawat nahin. Sirf gunaah waale hukm par narmi se inkaar.

📿 Qurʾān · 31:15

وَإِن جَاهَدَاكَ عَلَىٰ أَن تُشْرِكَ بِي ... فَلَا تُطِعْهُمَا

"If they strive to make you associate with Me… do not obey them."

Even parents — yet the verse still commands kindness to them. The relationship stays; only the sinful command is refused.

Maa-baap bhi — magar aage hukm: dunya mein unse achha bartaao. Rishta qaayam.

Ḥadīth 76

لَا بَأْسَ بِالْغِنَى لِمَنِ اتَّقَى اللهَ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ

lā baʾsa bi-l-ghinā li-mani-ttaqā Llāha ʿazza wa jall

"There is no harm in wealth for one who fears Allah, the Mighty and Majestic."

"Maal mein koi harj nahin us ke liye jo Allah se darta hai."

Ibn Māja · Aḥmad · Mustadrak

Wealth is neutral

The variable is not the money — it's the taqwā of the hand that holds it.

"How excellent is pure wealth in the possession of a righteous person." (Al-Adab al-mufrad)

No contradiction with "love of the world is the root of sin" — the danger is love of wealth, not possessing it with taqwā.

Khatra maal ki mohabbat hai — muttaqī ke haath mein maal achha.

MOVEMENT 3 · WEIGH WHAT YOU TAKE IN

& why you give

Aḥādīth 77, 78, 79, 80 — sift speech, poetry, knowledge — and your own intention.

Jo lo use taulo, aur jo do uski niyyat dekho.

Ḥadīth 77

إِنَّ مِنَ الْبَيَانِ لَسِحْرًا

inna mina l-bayāni la-siḥran

"Indeed, some eloquent speech is [like] magic."

"Beshak baʿz fasaahat mein jaadu hota hai."

Bukhārī

Eloquence cuts both ways

Beautiful speech can bewitch — "whether for good or otherwise."

As a listenercharm ≠ proof — weigh the content
As a speakeraim the gift at truth, never deceit

Charm nahin — content taulo. Apni fasaahat haq ki taraf chalaao.

Ḥadīth 78

إِنَّ مِنَ الشِّعْرِ حِكْمَةً

inna mina sh-shiʿri ḥikmatan

"Indeed, some poetry is wisdom."

"Beshak baʿz shaayri mein hikmat hoti hai."

Bukhārī

Judge the content, not the container

Don't accept a thing for being eloquent (#77). Don't reject a thing for being "just poetry" (#78).

Some poetry is wisdom — not all. The form doesn't decide worth; the content does. Open it and weigh what's inside.

Dabbe se nahin — andar ke maal se faisla.

Ḥadīth 79

إِنَّ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ جَهْلًا، وَإِنَّ مِنَ الْقَوْلِ عِيَالًا

inna mina l-ʿilmi jahlan, wa inna mina l-qawli ʿiyālan

"Indeed, some 'knowledge' is ignorance, and some speech is a burden."

"Beshak baʿz 'ilm' mein jahaalat hai, aur baʿz baat bojh hai."

Abū Dāwūd

Not everything called "knowledge" benefits

Non-beneficial "knowledge" derails you from what benefits; knowledge never acted on is a donkey carrying books (62:5).

Not anti-knowledge. Beneficial ʿilm is praised. The cure is more beneficial knowledge, acted upon.

Be-faayda aur be-amal ilm nishaana hai — ilaaj: faayda-mand ilm + amal.

📿 Qurʾān · 62:5

كَمَثَلِ الْحِمَارِ يَحْمِلُ أَسْفَارًا

"…like a donkey carrying volumes of books."

The verse the book itself cites: knowledge not upheld is no knowledge at all.

Be-amal ilm goya ilm hi nahin.

Ḥadīth 80

إِنَّ يَسِيرَ الرِّيَاءِ شِرْكٌ

inna yasīra r-riyāʾi shirk

"Indeed, even the slightest ostentation is shirk."

"Beshak thoda sa riyāʾ (dikhaawa) bhi shirk hai."

Ibn Māja

Now weigh why you act

A deed done to be seen earns its "reward" only from those eyes — nothing from Allah.

The Prophet ﷺ feared al-shirk al-aṣghar (lesser shirk) most for us — and named it: riyāʾ.

Lesser shirk — not waswasa. It does not exit Islam, and natural gladness that good was seen is not riyāʾ. Cure: do it for Allah alone.

Chhota shirk, niyyat ka — waswasa nahin. Ilaaj: niyyat sirf Allah ke liye.

📿 Qurʾān · 18:110

فَلْيَعْمَلْ عَمَلًا صَالِحًا وَلَا يُشْرِكْ بِعِبَادَةِ رَبِّهِ أَحَدًا

"…let him do righteous deeds and associate none in the worship of his Lord."

The exact remedy: a deed for Allah alone, no audience "associated" into it.

Nek amal sirf Allah ke liye — ʿibaadat mein kisi ko shareek na karo.
