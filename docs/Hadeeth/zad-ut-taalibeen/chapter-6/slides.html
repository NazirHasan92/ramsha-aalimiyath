MOVEMENT 1 · AIM YOUR HUNGER

Knowledge & striving for Allah

Aḥādīth 51, 52, 53, 54 — point your drive at what lasts.

ʿIlm ki bhook · haq ki himmat · Allah ka raasta · samajh ka hathiyaar.

Ḥadīth 51

مَنْهُومَانِ لَا يَشْبَعَانِ، مَنْهُومٌ فِي الْعِلْمِ لَا يَشْبَعُ مِنْهُ، وَمَنْهُومٌ فِي الدُّنْيَا لَا يَشْبَعُ مِنْهَا

manhūmāni lā yashbaʿān, manhūmun fī l-ʿilmi lā yashbaʿu minhu, wa manhūmun fī l-dunyā lā yashbaʿu minhā

"Two greedy ones are never satisfied: one greedy for knowledge, and one greedy for the world."

"Do log kabhi nahin bharte: ʿilm ka talabgaar, aur dunya ka talabgaar."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

One appetite, two destinations

Hunger for KNOWLEDGE

never fills — and it ennobles you

Hunger for the WORLD

never fills — and it destroys you (= Ch 5 #49)

Don't kill the appetite — re-aim it toward knowledge of Allah.

Bhook ko maaro mat — uska rukh badlo.

⚠️ The catch: even the hunger for knowledge must be for Allah.

Knowledge for pride, debate, or position is just the world in a scholar's cloak.

Connect to Qurʾān · 20:114 — "My Lord, increase me in knowledge."

ʿIlm bhi Allah ke liye — ghuroor ya maqaam ke liye nahin.

Ḥadīth 52

أَفْضَلُ الْجِهَادِ مَنْ قَالَ كَلِمَةَ حَقٍّ عِنْدَ سُلْطَانٍ جَائِرٍ

afḍalu l-jihādi man qāla kalimata ḥaqqin ʿinda sulṭānin jāʾir

"The most virtuous jihād is when one speaks a word of truth before an unjust ruler."

"Sab se afzal jihād zaalim hukmraan ke saamne haq baat kehna."

Abū Dāwūd · Tirmidhī · Ibn Mājah

Why the highest jihād?

1. The speaker risks himself — like a warrior in battle.

2. If the word stops oppression, the benefit reaches a far larger group than a battlefield victory.

One brave sentence can shelter a whole people.

Ek bahaadur jumla poori riʿaaya ko zulm se bacha sakta hai.

⚠️ Courage, not chaos.

Not a license to stir trouble, humiliate, or rebel. The virtue is truth said for Allah and for the oppressed — accepting the risk.

For Allah — not to be a hero.

Akhlaqi himmat — fasaad ya baghaawat nahin.

Ḥadīth 53

لَغَدْوَةٌ فِي سَبِيلِ اللهِ أَوْ رَوْحَةٌ خَيْرٌ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا وَمَا فِيهَا

la-ghadwatun fī sabīli Llāhi aw rawḥatun khayrun mina l-dunyā wa mā fīhā

"A morning or an evening in the path of Allah is better than the world and all it contains."

"Allah ke raaste mein ek subah ya shaam — dunya aur jo kuch usme hai, us se behtar."

Bukhārī · Muslim

Two ways to read it

1. It lasts. Its reward is eternal; the world is transient.

2. It out-weighs charity. Even giving the whole world away would not match it.

Time given to Allah > wealth given for Allah.

Connect to Qurʾān · 4:77 — "the enjoyment of this world is little."

Allah ko diya waqt, maal se bhi qeemti.

Ḥadīth 54

فَقِيهٌ وَاحِدٌ أَشَدُّ عَلَى الشَّيْطَانِ مِنْ أَلْفِ عَابِدٍ

faqīhun wāḥidun ashaddu ʿalā l-shayṭāni min alfi ʿābid

"A single jurist is harder on Satan than a thousand worshippers."

"Ek faqīh Shaytaan par hazaar ʿaabidon se zyada bhaari hai."

Tirmidhī · Ibn Mājah

Understanding is eyesight

The battleground is against Satan. The faqīh recognises his ploys, warns others, and enjoins good.

The ordinary worshipper — sincere, but without understanding — is more easily trapped, because he can't see it coming.

ʿIlm rooh ki jang mein aankh hai.

⚠️ This does NOT belittle worship.

Narrow comparison — against Satan, understanding is the sharper weapon. ʿIlm and ʿibādah belong together. A "faqīh" is one who understands, acts, and warns — not a title.

Satan simply fears the one who can see him.

ʿIbādat bekaar nahin — bas Shaytaan samajh wale se zyada darta hai.

MOVEMENT 2 · HONOUR YOUR TIES

Parents & elders

Aḥādīth 56, 57 — don't trample the people closest to you.

Baap ki khushi mein Allah ki khushi · baray bhai ka haq baap jaisa.

Ḥadīth 56

رِضَا الرَّبِّ فِي رِضَا الْوَالِدِ وَسَخَطُ الرَّبِّ فِي سَخَطِ الْوَالِدِ

riḍā l-rabbi fī riḍā l-wālid, wa sakhaṭu l-rabbi fī sakhaṭi l-wālid

"The pleasure of the Lord (Allah) is in the pleasure of the father, and His displeasure in the father's displeasure."

"Rabb ki khushi baap ki khushi mein, naaraazi baap ki naaraazi mein."

Tirmidhī

Your standing with Allah runs through your parent

Not merely "be kind" — Allah's own pleasure is bound up with the father's.

Pleasing him → a route to pleasing Allah. Angering him → a route to His anger.

Connect to Qurʾān · 17:23 — kindness to parents, right after worship of Allah.

Maamla akhlaaq se uth kar Allah ke taʿalluq ka ban jaata hai.

⚠️ Two limits — hold them firmly

No obedience in SIN

A ḥarām command is not obeyed — but kindness & respect remain.

The MOTHER is not lowered

Her right is even greater: "your mother" ×3, then "your father."

Naming the father does not rank him over the mother.

Gunaah mein itaaʿat nahin; aur maa ka haq aur bhi zyada.

Ḥadīth 57

"The right of an elder brother over the younger ones is like the right of the father over his children."

"Baray bhai ka chhoton par haq waisa jaisa baap ka aulaad par."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

(Arabic read directly from the book.)

Seniority carries weight

The eldest often helps raise, protect, and provide for the younger ones — so he carries a father-like right of respect.

"Like a father's right" = respect & care, not identical in every ruling. And it's two-way — the elder owes the younger a father's care.

Ehtiraam upar, shafqat neeche — do-tarfa rishta.

MOVEMENT 3 · GUARD YOUR HEART

Sincerity, repentance & restraint

Aḥādīth 55, 58, 59, 60 — keep the heart sincere and returning.

Istighfār · sab gunaah karte hain, palto · ikhlāṣ · faaltu chhodo.

Ḥadīth 55

طُوبَى لِمَنْ وَجَدَ فِي صَحِيفَتِهِ اسْتِغْفَارًا كَثِيرًا

ṭūbā li-man wajada fī ṣaḥīfatihi istighfāran kathīrā

"Glad tidings for the one who finds in his record abundant seeking of forgiveness."

"Khushkhabri us ke liye jo apne aʿmaal-naame mein kasrat se istighfār paaye."

Ibn Mājah

The page that delights you on the worst day

On the Day of Judgement, the records open — and blessed is the one who finds page after page of sincere istighfār that Allah accepted.

The cheapest investment with the richest return. Make it a daily habit — after every prayer, when you slip, before sleep.

Sab se sasta sauda, sab se bara nafa.

Ḥadīth 58

كُلُّ بَنِي آدَمَ خَطَّاءٌ وَخَيْرُ الْخَطَّائِينَ التَّوَّابُونَ

kullu banī ādama khaṭṭāʾun wa khayru l-khaṭṭāʾīna l-tawwābūn

"All the children of Adam are sinners, and the best of sinners are those who repent."

"Tamaam bani Aadam khataa-kaar; in mein behtareen woh jo tawba karte hain."

Tirmidhī

A diagnosis and a door

Humbling

All of us sin — only the prophets are infallible. Sinning makes you a child of Adam, not a monster.

Hopeful

The best of sinners return. The door of tawba is open to everyone.

Giro to maayoos mat ho — darwaza tawba hai.

⚠️ Not a permission slip.

"Everyone sins" ≠ "so the harm is small." The praise is on returning, never on the sinning. (And #20: one who truly repents is like one who never sinned.)

Connect to Qurʾān · 2:222 — "Allah loves those who turn to Him in repentance."

Taʿreef tawba par — gunaah se dosti mat karo.

Ḥadīth 59

كَمْ مِنْ صَائِمٍ لَيْسَ لَهُ مِنْ صِيَامِهِ إِلَّا الظَّمَأُ

kam min ṣāʾimin laysa lahu min ṣiyāmihi illā l-ẓamaʾ …

"How many fast but gain nothing but hunger, and how many stand in night prayer but gain nothing but sleeplessness."

"Kitne roza-daar jinhe sirf bhook milti hai, aur kitne raat ko khaday jinhe sirf bedaari."

Dārimī

Full form, empty heart

The body does the work; the heart gets no reward — when the sincerity is missing.

(1) Fasting without seeking Allah's reward / without leaving sin. (2) Night prayer done to show off (riyāʾ).

Ikhlāṣ is the soul of every act of worship.

ʿIbādat ki shakl poori, rooh ghaayab.

⚠️ A mirror — not a telescope

Don't despair

The minimal obligation is fulfilled — this is a push to raise sincerity, not a verdict of "void."

Don't judge others

Only Allah sees intentions. Turn it on yourself.

Ask: "Am I doing this for Allah, or for hunger, or for eyes?"

Connect to Qurʾān · 98:5 — "…sincere to Him in religion."

Aaina, door-been nahin.

Ḥadīth 60 — the chapter's close

مِنْ حُسْنِ إِسْلَامِ الْمَرْءِ تَرْكُهُ مَا لَا يَعْنِيهِ

min ḥusni islāmi l-marʾi tarkuhu mā lā yaʿnīh

"Part of the excellence of a person's Islam is his leaving what does not concern him."

"Aadmi ke Islam ki khoobi: woh chhod de jo us se taʿalluq nahin."

Tirmidhī · Aḥmad

A clean, focused life

The beauty of your Islam shows in one habit: leave the futile — gossip, prying, meddling — and stay occupied with what Allah loves.

Reward: endless bliss and salvation in the Hereafter. (One of the famous Forty traditions.)

Faaltu chhodo, asal par lago.

⚠️ Focus, not withdrawal.

"What does not concern you" = the futile — not your real duties to family or the dīn. Prune the wasteful; don't become cold or cut off.

#60 is the habit that protects all the rest.

Tawajjuh — kinaara-kashi nahin.
