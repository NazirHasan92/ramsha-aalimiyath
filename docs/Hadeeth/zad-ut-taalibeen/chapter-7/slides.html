MOVEMENT 1 · ANSWER FOR YOUR WATCH

Responsibility, place & death

Aḥādīth 61, 62, 64 — you will be questioned for it all.

Har ek charwaaha · masjid ka maahaul · maut ka tohfa.

Ḥadīth 61

أَلَا كُلُّكُمْ رَاعٍ وَكُلُّكُمْ مَسْؤُولٌ عَنْ رَعِيَّتِهِ

kullukum rāʿin wa kullukum masʾūlun ʿan raʿiyyatih

"Each of you is a shepherd, and each will be questioned about his flock."

"Tum mein se har ek charwaaha hai, aur har ek se uske galle ka hisaab hoga."

Bukhārī · Muslim

Everyone has a flock

Kullukum — all of you. Not just rulers.

a ruler→his subjects
a man→his household
a woman→her husband's home

Authority is a trust (amānah), not a trophy. The bigger the flock, the heavier the questioning.

Ikhtiyaar inʿaam nahin — amānah hai.

📿 Qurʾān · 37:24

وَقِفُوهُمْ إِنَّهُم مَّسْئُولُونَ

"And stop them; indeed, they are to be questioned."

The verse names the scene; #61 names the subject: your flock.

Aayat manzar dikhaati hai; #61 batati hai kis baare mein — tumhara galla.

Ḥadīth 62

أَحَبُّ الْبِلَادِ إِلَى اللهِ مَسَاجِدُهَا وَأَبْغَضُ الْبِلَادِ إِلَى اللهِ أَسْوَاقُهَا

aḥabbu l-bilādi ilā Llāhi masājiduhā, wa abghaḍu l-bilādi ilā Llāhi aswāquhā

"The most beloved places to Allah are the masjids; the most disliked are the markets."

"Allah ko sab se mehboob masjidein, sab se na-pasand bazaar."

Muslim

It's about atmosphere

MASJID — most loved

where one draws near Allah

MARKET — most disliked

where greed, deceit & heedlessness gather
NOT against trade. The dislike is for the heedlessness, not commerce. Carry the masjid into the market.

Tijaarat jaayaz — na-pasand ghaflat hai. Bazaar mein bhi Allah ko yaad rakho.

Ḥadīth 64

تُحْفَةُ الْمُؤْمِنِ الْمَوْتُ

tuḥfatu l-muʾmini l-mawt

"Death is a gift for a believer."

"Maut momin ke liye ek tohfa hai."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

The thing we fear, reframed

The gateway of death → the door to eternal contentment.

The world was the waiting room; death opens onto meeting Allah.

Do NOT wish for death. This is how the believer views death (hope, not dread) — the Prophet ﷺ forbade longing for it. Reassurance, not a summons.

Maut ko umeed se dekho — magar maango mat.

MOVEMENT 2 · STAND TOGETHER, GUARD YOUR TONGUE

Community & speech

Aḥādīth 65, 63, 66 — whom to stand with, and how to speak.

Jamāʿat ke saath · achhi sohbat aur achhi baat · zubaan ka hisaab.

Ḥadīth 65

يَدُ اللهِ عَلَى الْجَمَاعَةِ

yadu Llāhi ʿalā l-jamāʿa

"Allah's hand is with the group." (…and whoever splits away is set apart in the Fire.)

"Allah ka haath jamāʿa ke saath hai."

Book prints عَلَى (ʿalā); English renders it "with."

Tirmidhī

Stay with the body

"Allah's hand" — affirmed as the salaf did, its reality left to Allah — alludes to His help, guidance, mercy & protection, and it is with the jamāʿa: the believers united on truth.

Two cautions: (1) "jamāʿa" = the body on truth, not any majority. (2) The warning is against deviation, not respectful difference of opinion.

Jamāʿa = haq par muttahid guroh. Tambeeh gumraahi ke khilaaf, ikhtilaaf-e-raaye ke nahin.

📿 Qurʾān · 3:103

وَاعْتَصِمُوا بِحَبْلِ اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا وَلَا تَفَرَّقُوا

"Hold firmly to the rope of Allah all together, and do not become divided."

Sab mil kar Allah ki rassi thaamo, tafarqa mat daalo — yahi #65 ki wahdat.

Ḥadīth 63

"Solitude is better than bad company; good company is better than solitude. Good speech is better than silence; silence is better than bad speech."

"Tanhaai buri sohbat se behtar; achhi sohbat tanhaai se behtar. Achhi baat khaamoshi se behtar; khaamoshi buri baat se behtar."

Mustadrak · Bayhaqī

(Arabic read from the book.)

Two ladders

CompanySpeech
good company (best)saying good (best)
solitude (safe)silence (safe)
bad company (worst)saying bad (worst)

The middle rung is a safe fallback — not the ideal. The top of each ladder is active good.

Tanhaai aur khaamoshi panaah hain — manzil achhi sohbat aur achhi baat.

Ḥadīth 66

كُلُّ كَلَامِ ابْنِ آدَمَ عَلَيْهِ لَا لَهُ، إِلَّا أَمْرٌ بِمَعْرُوفٍ أَوْ نَهْيٌ عَنْ مُنْكَرٍ أَوْ ذِكْرُ اللهِ

kullu kalāmi bni ādama ʿalayhi lā lah, illā amrun bi-maʿrūfin aw nahyun ʿan munkarin aw dhikru Llāh

"Every word counts against a person, not for him — except enjoining good, forbidding wrong, and the remembrance of Allah."

"Har baat khilaaf jaati hai — siwaaye neki ka hukm, burai se rokne, aur Allah ki yaad ke."

Tirmidhī · Ibn Māja

Make your words count

Speech defaults to liability. Three categories flip it to asset:

Enjoin good

amr bi-l-maʿrūf

Forbid wrong

nahy ʿani-l-munkar

Remembrance

dhikr, duʿāʾ, Qurʾān, ṣalawāt

Not a vow of silence — recall #63: good speech beats silence. Make your words count.

Zubaan band mat karo — qeemti banao.

📿 Qurʾān · 50:18

مَّا يَلْفِظُ مِن قَوْلٍ إِلَّا لَدَيْهِ رَقِيبٌ عَتِيدٌ

"He utters not a word except that with him is a ready observer."

Har lafz ek nigraan farishta likh leta hai — isi liye har baat ka hisaab.

MOVEMENT 3 · KEEP YOUR HEART ALIVE

Dhikr & living knowledge

Aḥādīth 67, 68, 69, 70 — remembrance is the heart's life.

Dil ko Allah ki yaad se zinda rakho.

Ḥadīth 67

مَثَلُ الَّذِي يَذْكُرُ رَبَّهُ وَالَّذِي لَا يَذْكُرُ رَبَّهُ مَثَلُ الْحَيِّ وَالْمَيِّتِ

mathalu lladhī yadhkuru rabbahu wa-lladhī lā yadhkuru rabbahu mathalu l-ḥayyi wa-l-mayyit

"The one who remembers his Lord and the one who does not are like the living and the dead."

"Jo Rabb ko yaad kare aur jo nahin — zinda aur murde jaise."

Bukhārī · Muslim

Dhikr is the pulse of the heart

Remembrance is nourishment for the heart. Without it, the heart is — in this likeness — dead.

The body may walk, eat, talk, and trade while the heart inside has gone cold.

Yaad dil ki ghiza hai — iske baghair dil murda.

Ḥadīth 68

"Knowledge from which no benefit is derived is like a treasure from which nothing is spent in the path of Allah."

"Jis ʿilm se faayda nahin liya jaata, woh dafan khazaane jaisa hai jisme se kuch kharch nahin hota."

Aḥmad · Dārimī

(Arabic read from the book.)

Knowledge must be activated

A treasure sealed in the ground feeds no one — and becomes a burden on the Day of Judgement.

#67 — dhikr activates the heart.
#68 — action activates knowledge.

Dil dhikr se zinda; ʿilm ʿamal se zinda.

Ḥadīth 69

أَفْضَلُ الذِّكْرِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَفْضَلُ الدُّعَاءِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ

afḍalu dh-dhikri lā ilāha illa Llāh, wa afḍalu d-duʿāʾi l-ḥamdu li-Llāh

"The best remembrance is Lā ilāha illa 'Llāh, and the best supplication is Al-ḥamdu li 'Llāh."

"Behtareen dhikr Lā ilāha illa 'Llāh, behtareen duʿā Al-ḥamdu li 'Llāh."

Tirmidhī · Ibn Māja

Two crowns

Best dhikr

Lā ilāha = renounce all false gods;
illa 'Llāh = affirm Allah alone. Purifies the heart.

Best duʿāʾ

Praise = gratitude = asking for more.
"If you are grateful, I will increase you" (14:7).

Al-ḥamdu li-Llāh is dhikr, duʿāʾ, and shukr — three acts in three words.

Ḥadīth 70

"The first to be called to Paradise will be those who praised Allah in prosperity and in adversity."

"Sab se pehle Jannat woh bulaaye jaayenge jo khushhaali aur tangi — dono — mein Allah ki taʿreef karte rahe."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

(Arabic read from the book.)

Praise in both weathers

Praise in ease is easy. Praise in hardship is the mark of a heart anchored in Allah.

The constant praiser (#69's al-ḥamd, in every condition) is called first — the happiest answer to #61's "you will be questioned".

Mushkil mein bhi taʿreef — yahi pehle Jannat ki daʿwat.

🏠 Homework

  1. Memorise the Arabic of 4 aḥādīth (the short ones: 61, 64, 65, 67).
  2. Know all 10 in translation + their sources.
  3. Complete Exercises 1–4 from the handout.
  4. Reflection journal (Ex. 5) — private, for yourself.
  5. Daily dhikr — fix a habit of Lā ilāha illa 'Llāh & Al-ḥamdu li-Llāh (living #67 & #69).
  6. The "for or against me?" check: before you speak, pause — "Will this count for me or against me?" (#66).
