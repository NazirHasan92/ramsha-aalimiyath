MOVEMENT 1 · PROVISION — KEEP IT CLEAN

Your rizq is decreed

Aḥādīth 91, 92, 93 — sin costs barakah, yet your provision arrives in full, so give from it.

Gunaah ki chhupi qeemat · rizq poora aata hai · sadaqa rehmat kheenchta hai.

Ḥadīth 91

إِنَّ الرَّجُلَ لَيُحْرَمُ الرِّزْقَ بِالذَّنْبِ يُصِيْبُهُ

inna r-rajula la-yuḥramu r-rizqa bi-dh-dhanbi yuṣībuh

"A person is deprived of livelihood by the sins he commits."

"Insaan ek gunaah ki wajah se rizq se mehroom ho jaata hai."

Ibn Māja · Mustadrak

Sin has a hidden cost

"Provision" = the reward of the next world, or a worldly barakah withdrawn (which can be a mercy that wipes the sin).

Not a wealth-meter. Not "the poor are sinful." #92 (next) guarantees your decreed rizq in full.

Gunaah muft nahin — magar maal/gareebi se faisla mat karo.

Ḥadīth 92

إِنَّ نَفْسًا لَّنْ تَمُوْتَ حَتَّى تَسْتَكْمِلَ رِزْقَهَا

inna nafsan lan tamūta ḥattā tastakmila rizqahā

"A soul will never die until it fully receives all of its livelihood."

"Koi jaan tab tak nahin marti jab tak apna poora rizq paa na le."

Ibn Māja

Your provision arrives in full

You won't die until you've received every morsel decreed for you. (Book: "See ḥadīth 87 and 91.")

So #91's "deprivation" is a barakah/reward — never your guaranteed floor.

Freed twice: na rizq ke peechhe gunaah, na maal se faisla. Sukoon se halaal koshish.

📿 Qurʾān · 51:22

وَفِي السَّمَاءِ رِزْقُكُمْ وَمَا تُوعَدُونَ

"And in the heaven is your provision and whatever you are promised."

Provision is decreed "in the heaven" — guaranteed, not down to your scramble.

Rizq "aasman mein" muqarrar hai. Isi liye koi jaan use poora hue baghair nahin marti.

Ḥadīth 93

إِنَّ الصَّدَقَةَ لَتُطْفِئُ غَضَبَ الرَّبِّ وَتَدْفَعُ مِيْتَةَ السُّوْءِ

inna ṣ-ṣadaqata la-tuṭfiʾu ghaḍaba r-rabbi wa-tadfaʿu mītata s-sūʾ

"Voluntary charity extinguishes the anger of the Lord and prevents an unpleasant death."

"Sadaqa Rabb ke ghusse ko bujha deta hai aur buri maut ko taal deta hai."

Tirmidhī · Ibn Ḥibbān

The open hand draws mercy back

Giving from the rizq Allah guaranteed (92) does the opposite of sin: it puts out His anger and wards off an evil death.

Sincere, not a transaction. Not "drop a coin, dodge a bad death." The heart behind the hand is what Allah weighs (#95).

Dil se do — sauda nahin, sakhaawat ka roohani rawayya.

MOVEMENT 2 · ALLAH WEIGHS THE HEART

The real scale

Aḥādīth 94, 95 — the chapter's pivot: only taqwā raises you, and Allah looks at hearts & deeds.

Rang nahin, taqwa · soorat-o-maal nahin, dil-o-amal.

Ḥadīth 94

إِنَّكَ لَسْتَ بِخَيْرٍ مِّنْ أَحْمَرَ وَلَا أَسْوَدَ إِلَّا أَنْ تَفْضُلَهُ بِتَقْوَى

innaka lasta bi-khayrin min aḥmara wa-lā aswada illā an tafḍulahu bi-taqwā

"You are not superior to a white- [lit., red], or black-skinned person, unless you excel him in piety."

"Tum kisi gore ya kaale shakhs se behtar nahin — siwaaye taqwa ke."

Aḥmad

The only superiority is taqwā

Race, colour, lineage, nation — all wiped off the scale. The one axis left: taqwā.

…and watch the reverse. Taqwā you parade is gone the moment you look down on the "less religious." Real taqwā humbles.

Nasl-parasti giraana — aur roohani ghuroor se bachna.

📿 Qurʾān · 49:13 (cited by the book)

إِنَّ أَكْرَمَكُمْ عِندَ اللَّهِ أَتْقَاكُمْ

"The most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous of you."

The Qurʾānic root of the ḥadīth — nations and tribes only to know one another.

Izzat ka واحد paimana taqwa — rang ya qabeela nahin.

Ḥadīth 95

إِنَّ اللهَ لَا يَنْظُرُ إِلَى صُوَرِكُمْ وَأَمْوَالِكُمْ، وَلٰكِنْ يَنْظُرُ إِلَى قُلُوْبِكُمْ وَأَعْمَالِكُمْ

inna Llāha lā yanẓuru ilā ṣuwarikum wa-amwālikum, wa-lākin yanẓuru ilā qulūbikum wa-aʿmālikum

"Allāh does not look at your appearance or wealth but looks at your heart and actions."

"Allah sooraton aur maal ko nahin, dilon aur aʿmaal ko dekhta hai."

Muslim

He photographs the heart

NOT your forms & wealth — but your hearts & deeds (sincerity, and whether it shows in action).

Heart and deed, paired. A sound heart must show up in sound action.

Allah ki nazar chehre/maal par nahin — dil aur amal par.

📿 Qurʾān · 26:88–89

يَوْمَ لَا يَنفَعُ مَالٌ وَلَا بَنُونَ ۝ إِلَّا مَنْ أَتَى اللَّهَ بِقَلْبٍ سَلِيمٍ

"The Day when neither wealth nor children will avail — except him who comes to Allah with a sound heart."

On the Day that decides everything, the one currency is a sound heart.

Us din ek hi sikka: saleem dil. Yahi #95 ka taraazu.

MOVEMENT 3 · CARRY IT TO PEOPLE

An open face, the first salām

Aḥādīth 96, 97 — the heart's soundness shows: a cheerful face and greeting first.

Khula chehra neki hai · pehla salaam Allah ke qareeb karta hai.

Ḥadīth 96

إِنَّ مِنَ الْمَعْرُوْفِ أَنْ تَلْقَى أَخَاكَ بِوَجْهٍ طَلْقٍ

inna mina l-maʿrūfi an talqā akhāka bi-wajhin ṭalq

"It is a good deed that you meet your [Muslim] brother with a smiling face."

"Neki mein se yeh bhi hai ke apne bhai se khile chehre se milo."

Muslim · Tirmidhī

The cheapest good deed, and a real one

A warm face is counted maʿrūf — costs nothing, needs no wealth, yet a real ṣadaqa of the face.

And it can't be faked — a sound heart (#95) naturally produces a kind face.

Muskurahat sirf aadaab nahin — dil ki saaf-dili ka zaahir hona.

Ḥadīth 97

إِنَّ أَوْلَى النَّاسِ بِاللهِ مَنْ بَدَأَهُمْ بِالسَّلَام

inna awlā n-nāsi bi-Llāhi man badaʾahum bi-s-salām

"The closest to Allāh of people is the one who begins the salām."

"Logon mein Allah ke sab se qareeb woh hai jo salaam mein pehl kare."

Abū Dāwūd

Beat them to the peace

Greeting first takes a small death of the ego — you give honour and peace first, to high and low alike.

The theme complete: heart (#95) → face (#96) → tongue quick to greet (#97).

Pehle salaam karo — yeh tawazuʿ Allah ke qareeb kar deta hai.

MOVEMENT 4 · WHAT RUINS, WHAT SAVES

The closing fork

Aḥādīth 98, 99, 100 — interest ruins, anger rots, and the capstone: truth → Paradise.

Sood tabaahi · ghussa imaan ko · aur sach Jannat ki taraf.

Ḥadīth 98

إِنَّ الرِّبَا وَإِنْ كَثُرَ فَإِنَّ عَاقِبَتَهُ تَصِيْرُ إِلَى قُلٍّ

inna r-ribā wa-in kathura fa-inna ʿāqibatahu taṣīru ilā qull

"Interest, however much it might accrue, leads in the end to penury."

"Sood, chahe kitna bhi barhe, uska anjaam qillat par aata hai."

Ibn Māja · Aḥmad · Mustadrak

What looks like increase ends in ruin

The number grows — but read the ʿāqiba (end-state): it comes to ruin.

Spiritual, not naive. Not "all interest-earners go broke" — the ruin is barakah-stripped, faith-endangering. #91 at full volume.

Upar-upar barhta number mat parho — rooh ka anjaam parho.

📿 Qurʾān · 2:276

يَمْحَقُ اللَّهُ الرِّبَا وَيُرْبِي الصَّدَقَاتِ

"Allah destroys interest and gives increase for charities."

The exact inversion — and it ties #98 to #93. The real measure runs opposite to the surface.

Jo barhta dikhe (sood) Allah mitaata hai; jo nuqsaan lage (sadaqa) barhaata hai.

Ḥadīth 99

إِنَّ الْغَضَبَ لَيُفْسِدُ الْإِيْمَانَ كَمَا يُفْسِدُ الصَّبِرُ الْعَسَلَ

inna l-ghaḍaba la-yufsidu l-īmāna kamā yufsidu ṣ-ṣabiru l-ʿasal

"Anger ruins faith just as the aloe plant ruins honey."

"Ghussa imaan ko aise saada deta hai jaise kadwa aloe shahad ko."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

The corrosive that eats faith's sweetness

"Ruins" = ruins faith's completeness, light & beauty — bitter dropped in honey.

Not every flash. Not "feeling angry = losing īmān"; righteous anger for Allah exists. The target is uncontrolled, ego-driven rage.

(الصَّبِر = kadwa aloe, "sabar" nahin.) Be-qaaboo ghusse se imaan ki mithaas bachaao.

📿 Qurʾān · 3:134

وَالْكَاظِمِينَ الْغَيْظَ وَالْعَافِينَ عَنِ النَّاسِ

"…those who restrain anger and who pardon people."

The cure for the corrosive: swallow the rage, and pardon.

Ghussa peena aur maaf karna — #99 ke zang ka ilaaj.

Ḥadīth 100 · the capstone of the hundred

إِنَّ الصِّدْقَ بِرٌّ وَإِنَّ الْبِرَّ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ، وَإِنَّ الْكَذِبَ فُجُوْرٌ وَإِنَّ الْفُجُوْرَ يَهْدِي إِلَى النَّارِ

inna ṣ-ṣidqa birr, wa-inna l-birra yahdī ilā l-jannah, wa-inna l-kadhiba fujūr, wa-inna l-fujūra yahdī ilā n-nār

"Truth is a piety, and piety leads to Paradise; falsehood is an iniquity, and iniquity leads to Hellfire."

"Sach neki hai, aur neki Jannat ki taraf; jhoot badi hai, aur badi Aag ki taraf."

Muslim · Abū Dāwūd

Two roads, one choice

صِدْق → بِرّ → → Paradise

كَذِب → فُجُور → → the Fire

A habituated direction — framed as hope. Not "one lie damns you." Truth is the road back too: return to ṣidq by being truthful now.

Har sach zindagi ko Jannat ki taraf modta hai → ṣiddīq. Sach wapsi ka raasta bhi hai.

📿 Qurʾān · 9:119 — the hundred in one verse

اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَكُونُوا مَعَ الصَّادِقِينَ

"Fear Allah, and be with the truthful."

Taqwā (the #94 axis) paired with ṣidq — and the command to keep the company of the truthful.

Taqwa aur sach ek saath — sacchon ke saath raho.
