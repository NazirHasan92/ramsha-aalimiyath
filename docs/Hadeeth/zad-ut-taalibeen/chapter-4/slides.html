THEME 1

Who IS a believer?

Aḥādīth 31, 32, 33, 34 — four nested definitions.

Muslim · Muʾmin · Mujāhid · Muhājir — char level, ek doosre par bante hain.

Ḥadīth 31

الْمُسْلِمُ مَنْ سَلِمَ الْمُسْلِمُونَ مِنْ لِسَانِهِ وَيَدِهِ

al-muslimu man salima l-muslimūna min lisānihi wa yadih

"The Muslim is the one from whose tongue and hand other believers are safe."

"Musalman woh hai jiski zaban aur haath se baqi musalman mehfooz hain."

Bukhārī · Muslim

The test is outward

The Prophet ﷺ did NOT say "the Muslim is one who prays, fasts, gives zakāh".

He defined a Muslim by what other people experience of him.

The yardstick is relational — not private.

Other people are the judges of your "Muslim" claim, not your own self-image.

Test outward hai — namaz tum aur Allah ke beech, magar tumhari zaban-haath ka asar sab dekh sakte hain.

Why tongue and hand?

LISĀN (tongue)

Speech-harm:
gheebat, slander, insult, lies, mocking, broken promises

YAD (hand)

Action-harm:
striking, stealing, withholding rights, harmful posts
(today's hand = smartphone too)

These two are the main exits of harm — guard them and most relationships become safe.

Zaban aur haath — yahi do darwaze hain. Yeh do mehfooz, to bohot kuch mehfooz.

⚠️ The trap: "I haven't hit anyone — I'm fine."

Most check the hand and forget the tongue. But the tongue wounds daily — taunts, sarcasm, gheebat, hurtful jokes.

Apne zaban ka roz hisaab lo.

Har shaam pucho — kya aaj koi mom mein mehfooz nahin raha mere zaban se?

Ḥadīth 32

وَالْمُؤْمِنُ مَنْ أَمِنَهُ النَّاسُ عَلَى دِمَائِهِمْ وَأَمْوَالِهِمْ

wa-l-muʾminu man aminahu l-nāsu ʿalā dimāʾihim wa amwālihim

"And the believer is the one whom people entrust with their lives and their wealth."

"Aur muʾmin woh hai jis pe log apni jaan aur maal soup dein."

Tirmidhī

The upgrade from #31 to #32

#31 — Muslim#32 — Muʾmin
Audience The Muslims ALL people (al-nās)
What's at stake Tongue, hand Life, wealth
Posture Don't harm Be trusted

Two upgrades at once. Bigger audience, higher stake, different posture.

Pehla bar: threat na ho. Doosra bar: refuge ho.

The Makkan model

Even before prophethood, the polytheists of Quraysh called the Prophet ﷺ al-Amīn — "the Trustworthy."

They did not share his beliefs.

But they deposited their valuables with him.

That is the standard muʾmin means.

"Al-Amīn" laqab — nubuwwat ke pehle — kuffar-e-Quraish ne diya tha. Yahi yardstick hai.

Ḥadīth 33

وَالْمُجَاهِدُ مَنْ جَاهَدَ نَفْسَهُ فِي طَاعَةِ الله

wa-l-mujāhidu man jāhada nafsahu fī ṭāʿati Llāh

"The mujāhid is one who strives against his own self in obedience to Allah."

"Mujāhid woh hai jo apne nafs se Allah ki itāʿat mein mehnat karta hai."

Aḥmad · Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

Re-grounding jihād

Root j-h-d = exertion against resistance.

Battlefield jihād = one application of the root. Not the only meaning.

The real mujāhid strives against his own nafs — daily, constantly, internally.

"Jihād" ka asal matlab — mehnat against resistance. Battlefield ek application, asal nahin. Asal jihād nafs ke khilaaf.

What nafs-jihād looks like

  • Fajr when nafs wants sleep → jihād
  • Holding tongue when nafs wants to gossip → jihād
  • Lowering gaze when nafs wants to look → jihād
  • Paying zakāh when nafs clings to money → jihād
  • Forgiving when nafs wants revenge → jihād

Each is exertion against resistance, in obedience to Allah.

Yeh roz ki cheezein hain. Har ek struggle = mujāhid ka kaam.

⚠️ Pre-empt: Don't conclude battlefield jihād is unimportant.

The Prophet ﷺ is expanding the definition — not abolishing the battlefield meaning. Both are valid. The nafs-jihad is just the most constant.

Yeh hadith maidan ka jihād khatam nahin kar rahi — sirf usay shamil kar rahi hai aur ek bara framework de rahi hai. Donon haqiqi hain.

Ḥadīth 34

وَالْمُهَاجِرُ مَنْ هَجَرَ الْخَطَايَا وَالذُّنُوبَ

wa-l-muhājiru man hajara l-khaṭāyā wa-l-dhunūb

"The muhājir is one who abandons mistakes and sins."

"Muhājir woh hai jo khataaon aur gunaahon ko chhor de."

Aḥmad · Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

Re-grounding hijrah

After the Conquest of Makkah, the Prophet ﷺ said:

"There is no hijrah after the Conquest."

The physical hijrah (Makkah → Madīnah) ended. But the spirit of hijrah is permanent: leaving behind for Allah.

Today's hijrah = abandoning sins. Open until Qiyāmah, for every Muslim.

Geography wali hijrah khatam, magar hijrah ki rooḥ qayamat tak khuli hai. Aaj ki hijrah = gunaahon ko chhorna.

Why moral hijrah is harder

  1. Physical hijrah ends. You arrive, you settle. Moral hijrah is constant.
  2. Physical hijrah was one act. Moral hijrah is every act, every day.
  3. Physical hijrah had visible borders. Moral hijrah has no clear exit point.

Geography wali aasaan thi — pohnchi, kaam khatam. Gunah wali har waqt chalti hai.

The four-step staircase (#31 → #34)

  1. Min Muslim · others safe from his tongue/hand
  2. Up Muʾmin · others trust him with life/wealth
  3. Inner Mujāhid · strives against his nafs
  4. Daily Muhājir · abandons his sins

Four levels — each nests the previous. Where are you?

Char level, ek doosre par bante hain. Apne aap se poochho — abhi kis level pe hoon?

THEME 2

Believers among believers

Aḥādīth 35, 36, 37 — justice, mirroring, one body.

Momin doosre momin ke saath kaisa hai — yeh teen hadith.

Ḥadīth 35

الْبَيِّنَةُ عَلَى الْمُدَّعِيْ وَالْيَمِينُ عَلَى الْمُدَّعَى عَلَيْهِ

al-bayyinatu ʿalā l-muddaʿī wa-l-yamīnu ʿalā l-muddaʿā ʿalayh

"The burden of proof is on the accuser, and the oath is on the one accused."

"Saboot ilzaam lagaane wale ke zimme, qasam jis par ilzaam hai (mulzim) ke zimme."

Bayhaqī

The principle

MUDDAʿĪ
(the accuser)

Must bring BAYYINAH
(evidence / witnesses)

AL-MUDDAʿĀ ʿALAYH
(the one accused)

Swears YAMĪN
(oath by Allah)

No evidence + oath of denial = case stands in favour of the accused.

Ilzaam wala saboot laaye, mulzim qasam khaaye. Saboot nahin, qasam laazim.

Why on the accuser?

Default state of every person = innocence.

The one claiming a departure from the default carries the burden.

Islam established this 14 centuries ago — modern "innocent until proven guilty" is its echo.

Default = bay-gunah. Saboot uska kaam jo "normal" se hatkar dawa kar raha hai. Yahi aqlmandi aur insaaniyat ka usool hai.

Apply it everywhere — not just court

  • Family — don't accuse spouse/sibling/child without evidence.
  • Workplace — don't repeat claims about colleagues without proof.
  • WhatsApp — don't forward claims without verification.
  • Your own heart — when suspicion rises, ask: "What's my evidence?"

No evidence = claim/suspicion DISMISSED. This is the foundation of ḥusn al-ẓann (good opinion).

Yeh hadith roz marra mein laagu hai — har rishtay mein. Bina saboot ilzaam mat lagao. Husn-e-zann default rakho.

Ḥadīth 36

الْمُؤْمِنُ مِرْآةُ الْمُؤْمِنِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُ أَخُو الْمُؤْمِنِ، يَكُفُّ عَلَيْهِ ضَيْعَتَهُ وَيَحُوْطُهُ مِنْ وَرَائِهِ

al-muʾminu mirʾātu l-muʾmini wa-l-muʾminu akhū l-muʾmin...

"A believer is a mirror for a believer, and a brother to a believer — protecting him in his absence."

"Momin doosre momin ka aaina hai aur uska bhai bhi — uski peeth peechay uska defence karta hai."

Abū Dāwūd · Tirmidhī · al-Bukhārī's al-Adab al-Mufrad

The mirror — three properties

1. PRIVATELY

Mirror shows you only to yourself

2. TRUTHFULLY

Mirror doesn't flatter or lie

3. GENTLY

Mirror doesn't mock

All three must come together. Just being "honest" is not enough.

Teen shartein — saamne, sach, gentle. Teenon ki zaroorat hai. Sirf "sach kaha" kafi nahin.

The "brother" — protection behind the back

Mullā ʿAlī al-Qārī: "preserving his respect, honour, and rights in front of others in his absence."

When your brother/sister is not in the room and someone speaks badly of them — you defend.

When their reputation is damaged — you intervene.

When their interests are at stake in a meeting they can't attend — you represent faithfully.

"Min warāʾihi" = uski peeth peechay. Uski izzat aur haq tumhari zimmedaari hain — chahe woh saath na ho.

The reversal — worldly vs believer

Worldly pattern

Flattery in front
Gossip behind

Believer pattern

Truth in front
Defence behind

This single reversal transforms a community.

Dunyawi tarz: saamne taareef, peeth peechay buraai. Momin ka tarz: saamne sach, peeth peechay defence.

Ḥadīth 37

الْمُؤْمِنُونَ كَرَجُلٍ وَّاحِدٍ إِنِ اشْتَكَى عَيْنُهُ اشْتَكَى كُلُّهُ وَإِنِ اشْتَكَى رَأْسُهُ اشْتَكَى كُلُّهُ

al-muʾminūna ka-rajulin wāḥidin, ini shtakā ʿaynuhu shtakā kulluh...

"Believers are like a single person; if his eye is in pain his whole body is in pain, and if his head is in pain his whole body is in pain."

"Momin ek hi shakhs (jism) ki tarah hain — agar aankh ya sar mein dard ho, poora jism dard mein hota hai."

Muslim

The escalation #36 → #37

#36

Two believers

= mirror & brother
(distinct individuals in close relationship)

→

↓

#37

One body

= limbs of one organism
(separation dissolved)

Not even two individuals — one body.

Hadith 36 = do alag log. Hadith 37 = ek hi badan. Upgrade hai. Tumhare bhai ki takleef = tumhari takleef.

The medical accuracy

Pain in one limb is signalled through the nervous system to the whole body.

Headache → poor appetite, disturbed sleep, low energy. The whole body knows.

The community of believers is supposed to have a spiritual nervous system with the same connectivity.

Body wali misal scientific hai — sar dard mein poora badan affected. Ummah ka "spiritual nerve system" bhi waisa hi hona chahiye.

Diagnostic — your own heart

  • When refugees suffer (Gaza, Sudan, Myanmar) — do you feel it, or scroll past?
  • When a neighbour struggles with rent — do you know?
  • When a sister hasn't been in masjid for three weeks — do you notice?

If you don't feel the pain — the spiritual nerve has weakened. Re-connect.

Apne dil ko check karo. Agar pain mehsoos nahin — to "jasad ka nerve" kahaan disconnect hua, dekho.

Connect to Qurʾān · 9:71

وَالْمُؤْمِنُوْنَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتُ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَاءُ بَعْضٍ

"The believing men and women — they are awliyāʾ (protectors, allies) of one another..."

Qurʾān establishes the legal walāyah; this ḥadīth gives the organic metaphor — they are one body.

Quran ne legal walāyah qaayam ki; hadith ne body wali tasveer di — ek hi haqeeqat, do pehlu.

THEME 3

The believer on the road

Aḥādīth 38, 39, 40 — travel, return, leadership.

Safar mein discipline, lautne ka ajr, aur sardari = khidmat.

Ḥadīth 38

السَّفَرُ قِطْعَةٌ مِّنَ الْعَذَابِ يَمْنَعُ أَحَدَكُمْ نَوْمَهُ وَطَعَامَهُ وَشَرَابَهُ

al-safaru qiṭʿatun mina l-ʿadhābi yamnaʿu aḥadakum nawmahu wa ṭaʿāmahu wa sharābah...

"Travelling involves a degree of punishment; it deprives one of his sleep, food, and drink. Therefore, when one has accomplished his purpose, he should hurry back to his family."

"Safar azaab ka tukda hai — yeh neend, khaana, paani cheen leta hai. Toh kaam khatam ho, ghar wapas lautho jaldi."

Bukhārī · Muslim

The opening surprise

We think of travel as vacation.

The Prophet ﷺ frames it as qiṭʿah min al-ʿadhāb — a piece of punishment.

Not moral punishment.

Physical and emotional deprivation: sleep, food, drink, family, prayer routines, work rhythms.

Aaj ki dunya safar ko "vacation" kehti hai — magar Rasool ﷺ ne usay azaab ka tukda kaha. Neend, khaana, ghar walay — sab disturb. Yeh haqeeqat hai jo hum bhul jaate hain.

The operational rule — hasten home

When the purpose is complete: fal-yuʿajjil ilā ahlihi.

  1. Your family has rights — they're deprived too.
  2. Extended travel = missed Jumʿah and congregation (per Imam Khaṭṭābī).
  3. Extended travel erodes your spiritual rhythms.

Kaam khatam = ghar wapas. Teen wajhain: ghar walay, jamaʿat, apni roḥaani routine.

⚠️ This is NOT "travel is haraam".

The Prophet ﷺ himself travelled — for Hajj, trade, battles. The point: don't over-romanticise travel, and return when business is done.

The deeper teaching: this dunyā is also a journey. Kaam khatam, ghar wapas — both for trips and for life itself.

Aalim kehte hain: dunya ka safar bhi qiṭʿah min al-ʿadhāb hai. Asal ghar Akhirah hai.

Ḥadīth 39

قَفْلَةٌ كَغَزْوَةٍ

qaflatun ka-ghazwah

"The return from battle is like the battle itself [in reward]."

"Jang se wapsi (qaflah) bhi ek ghazwah (jang) jaisi hai (ajr mein)."

Abū Dāwūd

The startling generosity

We normally think: the active fighter is rewarded, the returning one is just heading home.

The Prophet ﷺ flips this. The return is not free time.

It is still part of the worship.

Hum sochte hain "lautna toh rest hai" — magar Rasool ﷺ ne lautne ko bhi worship ka hissa qaraar diya hai.

Why? Two anchors (Mullā ʿAlī al-Qārī)

1. INTENTION

Intention to return to battle if needed. Heart hasn't exited.

2. FAMILY RIGHTS

Going home is itself an obligation (per Ḥadīth 38).

The "intention chain" — worship continuous across pauses.

Niyyat saath hai? Toh gap mein bhi ajr hai. Family rights bhi ibadat hain.

Apply broadly

  • The ḥāfizah on break to care for parents — if intending to return to recitation, the gap is rewarded.
  • The student stepping away for a family crisis — if intending to return, time not lost.
  • The daʿwah worker slowing down for a season — preserved if intention preserved.

Allah counts the intention chain — not just the active moments.

Yeh hadith bohot raahat wali hai. Gaps mein bhi ajr mil sakta hai — agar niyyat ki zanjeer toot na rahi ho.

Ḥadīth 40 — the closing of Part 1

سَيِّدُ الْقَوْمِ فِي السَّفَرِ خَادِمُهُمْ

sayyidu l-qawmi fī l-safari khādimuhum

"The leader of a group on a journey is their servant."

"Safar mein qaum ka sardar woh hai jo unki khidmat karta hai."

Ibn Māja · Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

Two readings (both true)

1. DUTY

If you lead → you must serve. Leadership = responsibility, not privilege.

2. MERIT

If you serve → you qualify to lead. Service = the path to leadership.

Khidmat = sardari ka haq.

Do matlab — agar tum sardar ho, khidmat karo. Aur agar tum khidmat karte ho, sardari tumhara haq hai.

The Prophet's ﷺ example

Khandaq — he carried earth alongside the Companions.

Tabūk — when a camel slipped, he helped push it.

Distribution of food — he took the last portion.

He was the leader because he was the servant.

Aap ki sardari isi liye thi — woh sab se bara khadim thay.

Apply it — to YOUR life

  • Household — your "headship" is the work you do, not the comfort you extract.
  • Class / halqa — leading means carrying chairs, setting up tea. The honour is the work.
  • Project / team — authority comes from the burden carried, not the title.

The service IS the honour. No separate "izzat" exists.

Ghar, class, halqa, project — jahaan bhi tum "lead" kar rahi ho, teri izzat wahi hai jo tu khidmat karti hai. Khidmat hi izzat hai.

The chapter's closing arc

#31 (open)

"A Muslim is one from whom others are safe."

Don't harm. (Minimum.)

#40 (close)

"A leader is one who serves those under him."

Actively serve. (Maximum.)

Minimum protection → maximum offering.

That is the whole chapter — and the whole of Part 1 — in one line.

Chapter ka safar: "doosron ko nuksaan na do" se shuru, "doosron ki khidmat karo" pe khatam. Yeh hai Part 1 ka asal payaam.
