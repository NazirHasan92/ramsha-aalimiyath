THEME 1 · THE MIND

Sacred knowledge

Aḥādīth 41, 42, 43 — know your dīn.

Jaano · sachay khwaab · seekho aur sikhao.

Ḥadīth 41

طَلَبُ الْعِلْمِ فَرِيْضَةٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ

ṭalabu l-ʿilmi farīḍatun ʿalā kulli muslim

"Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim."

"ʿIlm haasil karna har musalman par farz hai."

Ibn Mājah

Which knowledge?

FARḌ ʿAYN

On every Muslim:
who Allah is, tawḥīd, prophethood, how to pray & fast

FARḌ KIFĀYAH

On the community:
fatāwā, deep tafsīr, ḥadīth sciences — enough that scholars exist

Learn enough to do your own dīn correctly.

Har ek ko ʿālim banna zaroori nahin — itna seekho jitne se apni dīn sahi ho.

⚠️ The trap: "I can never be a scholar — this is too much."

The obligation is the farḍ ʿayn — your own dīn's basics. Scholarship is a communal duty, not yours alone.

Connect to Qurʾān · 58:11 — "Allah raises those given knowledge by degrees."

Dabaav mat lo. Bunyaadi ʿilm har ek par; gehra ʿilm community par.

Ḥadīth 42

أَصْدَقُ الرُّؤْيَا بِالْأَسْحَارِ

aṣdaqu l-ruʾyā bi-l-asḥār

"The most truthful dreams are those [seen] just before dawn."

"Sab se sachay khwaab seher ke waqt (fajr se thori pehle) aate hain."

Tirmidhī

Why the pre-dawn hour?

1. After a night of rest, the mind is calm — a clearer receiver.

2. It is the hour of the angels' descent, who witness the morning prayer.

⚠️ Keep it small. NOT a basis for action, law, or dream-interpretation. One narrow observation — nothing more.

Khwaab pe amal ya taʿbīr ki baat nahin. Choti baat — choti rakho. ʿIlm laazim, khwaab nahin.

Ḥadīth 43

خَيْرُكُمْ مَنْ تَعَلَّمَ الْقُرْآنَ وَعَلَّمَهُ

khayrukum man taʿallama l-qurʾāna wa ʿallamah

"The best among you is the one who learns the Qurʾān and teaches it."

"Tum mein behtareen woh hai jo Qurʾān seekhe aur sikhaaye."

Bukhārī · ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān

Two parts — joined

تَعَلَّمَ

Learned the Qurʾān

 +

عَلَّمَهُ

Taught it

Not one or the other — both. The 14-century chain: learn → teach → they learn → they teach.

"Behtareen" woh jo dono kare — seekhe aur sikhaaye.

This is what we are doing

Every one of us is inside this ḥadīth: we learn, then we teach.

You need not be a scholar. Teaching a child Sūrat al-Fātiḥah already counts.

Pass on what you learn this week — even one ḥadīth.

Hum sab is hadith ke andar hain. Jo seekho, kisi ko sikhao.

THEME 2 · THE HEART

Steady deeds & where love leads

Aḥādīth 44, 46, 49 — constancy, and the danger of misplaced love.

Pabandi · mohabbat andha karti hai · dunya ki mohabbat har gunaah ki jaḍ.

Ḥadīth 44

أَحَبُّ الْأَعْمَالِ إِلَى اللهِ أَدْوَمُهَا وَإِنْ قَلَّ

aḥabbu l-aʿmāli ilā Llāhi adwamuhā wa in qall

"The most beloved deeds to Allah are the most constant, even if few."

"Allah ko sab se pyaare aʿmāl woh hain jo hamesha kiye jaayein, chahe thoray hon."

Bukhārī · Muslim · ʿĀʾishah

Consistency beats intensity

A small wird done every day beats a huge burst that burns out in a week.

The "deeds" = voluntary acts (nafl prayer, daily recitation, dhikr) you take on regularly.

Why? It proves a settled relationship with Allah — not a mood — and it is sustainable.

Roz ka chhota wird bare josh se behtar. Ramadan ka josh phir gyarah maheene khaali — yeh kamzor model.

⚠️ Not a laziness license.

"Even if few" praises constancy — not smallness. The lesson is steadiness, not minimum effort.

Pick one small thing and never drop it.

Connect to Qurʾān · 11:112 — "So remain steadfast as you are commanded."

Ek chhoti cheez pakdo aur kabhi mat chhodo. Yeh istiqāmat hai.

Ḥadīth 46

حُبُّكَ الشَّيْءَ يُعْمِيْ وَيُصِمُّ

ḥubbuka l-shayʾa yuʿmī wa yuṣimm

"Your love for a thing blinds you and deafens you."

"Kisi cheez se teri mohabbat tujhe andha aur behra kar deti hai."

Abū Dāwūd · Aḥmad · Abū al-Dardāʾ

Love as a distorting lens

يُعْمِيْ — BLINDS

you to the thing's defects

يُصِمُّ — DEAFENS

you to criticism of it

A parent blind to a child's faults; the infatuated deaf to all warning. Love becomes a filter on reality.

Mohabbat ʿaib chhupa deti hai, tanqeed sunne nahin deti. Khud parda ban jaati hai.

The hinge — and the flip side

If love blinds & deafens — what is the most dangerous thing to love?

The next ḥadīth answers: the dunyā (#49).

Love is not the enemy — misplaced love is. Love of Allah & His Messenger ﷺ re-tunes you toward the truth.

Dushman mohabbat nahin — ghalat jagah ki mohabbat. Sahi rukh do, to wahi taaqat rehnumā.

Ḥadīth 49

حُبُّ الدُّنْيَا رَأْسُ كُلِّ خَطِيْئَةٍ

ḥubbu l-dunyā raʾsu kulli khaṭīʾah

"Love of this world is the head [origin] of every sin."

"Dunya ki mohabbat har gunaah ki jaḍ hai."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

The root of every sin

  • Lying → to protect worldly standing
  • Stealing → love of wealth
  • Backbiting → love of status
  • Abandoning prayer → love of comfort

Strip any sin down — at the bottom sits attachment to this world.

Koi bhi gunaah khol ke dekho — neeche dunya se lagaav milega.

⚠️ The love — NOT the world

Dunyā in your HAND

Fine — it's your field, gift, tool. The Prophet ﷺ married, traded, ate.

Dunyā in your HEART

The root of sin — the goal you'd disobey Allah to keep.

Like water: under the boat it carries you; inside the boat it sinks you.

Maal kashti ke neeche theek, kashti ke andar doobne ka sabab.

The chapter is also the cure

#41 knowledge — re-orders priorities

#44 steady worship — keeps the heart turned to Allah

#47 contentment · #48 lawful earning · #50 giving it away

If #49 is the disease, the chapter prescribes the medicine.

Connect to Qurʾān · 3:14 — the love of desires made attractive, then Allah as the better return.

THEME 3 · THE HANDS

Wealth, debt & provision

Aḥādīth 45, 47, 48, 50 — pay debts, be content, earn lawfully, feed the hungry.

Qarz ada karo · qanāʿat · halāl kamaai · bhooke ko khilao.

Ḥadīth 45

مَطْلُ الْغَنِيِّ ظُلْمٌ

maṭlu l-ghaniyyi ẓulm

"The delay [in repayment] by a wealthy [debtor] is oppression."

"Maaldaar (jo de sakta hai) ka qarz taalna zulm hai."

Bukhārī · Muslim · Abū Hurayrah

A weighty word — ẓulm

Not "rude". Not "inconvenient". Oppression — the word for grave injustice.

Maṭl = deliberately stalling — "next month," "after Eid" — when you can pay now.

The creditor helped you with his own wealth and trusted you. Making him chase it = injustice.

De sakte ho aur taal rahe ho? Sirf bad-akhlaqi nahin — zulm hai.

⚠️ The genuinely broke are NOT the target.

The ḥadīth targets al-ghaniyy — the one who can pay and stalls. The truly poor debtor is owed patience.

If you can pay — pay on time. It's part of your dīn, not just manners.

Muflis ho to zaalim nahin — magar taaqat aate hi foran ada karo.

Ḥadīth 47

مَا قَلَّ وَكَفَى خَيْرٌ مِمَّا كَثُرَ وَأَلْهَى

mā qalla wa kafā khayrun mimmā kathura wa alhā

"What is little but sufficient is better than what is abundant but distracts."

"Jo thora magar kaafi ho, woh us se behtar jo zyada magar ghaafil karne wala ho."

Ibn Ḥibbān

The scale — kafā vs alhā

قَلَّ وَكَفَى

little + sufficient
(meets your need)

كَثُرَ وَأَلْهَى

much + distracting
(pulls you from Allah)

Not praise of little, nor blame of much — but of sufficiency vs distraction. The test is the heart, not the amount.

Sawaal miqdaar ka nahin — dil par asar ka. Yeh qanāʿat hai.

The same principle as #44

#44: little-but-constant worship > burst-and-burnout.

#47: little-but-sufficient provision > much-but-heedless wealth.

Sustainable sufficiency over distracting excess — in worship and provision.

Yeh #49 (dunya ki mohabbat) ka ʿamali toḍ hai.

Ḥadīth 48

طَلَبُ الْحَلَالِ فَرِيْضَةٌ بَعْدَ الْفَرِيْضَةِ

ṭalabu l-ḥalāli farīḍatun baʿda l-farīḍah

"Seeking the lawful [earning] is an obligation after the [primary] obligation."

"Halāl kamaai talab karna bunyaadi farz ke baad (agla) farz hai."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

Lawful earning IS worship

Echo of #41 — both ʿilm and ḥalāl rizq are called farīḍah.

After the primary obligations, earning a lawful living is the next obligation.

The one who earns ḥalāl for his dependants is doing an act of dīn — not just "working".

Halāl kamaana ʿibādat hai — sirf "naukri" nahin.

⚠️ Not "everyone must earn".

The obligation falls on those responsible for their own & dependants' support. One already provided for (e.g. a woman supported by her father or husband) is not obligated.

The duty tracks responsibility, not gender or people as such.

Yeh farz zimmedaari ke saath juda hai. Connect: Qurʾān 2:168 — halāl ṭayyib khaao.

Ḥadīth 50 — the chapter's close

أَفْضَلُ الصَّدَقَةِ أَنْ تُشْبِعَ كَبِدًا جَائِعَةً

afḍalu l-ṣadaqati an tushbiʿa kabidan jāʾiʿah

"The most virtuous charity is that you satisfy a hungry stomach."

"Sab se afzal sadaqah yeh ke kisi bhooke ka pet bhar do."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

The most concrete charity

Kabid = "liver", the organ of hunger (= "stomach"). To fill a hungry kabid = feed someone hungry, right now.

The book widens it: a hungry Muslim, a hungry non-Muslim, or even a hungry animal. The merit is in relieving real hunger, wherever found.

Musalman ho ya ghair-musalman ya jaanwar — asli bhook mitaane mein fazīlat hai.

Why the perfect ending

Charity is love of the world reversed — taking what the world-lover hoards and giving it away.

And the chapter chooses the humblest charity — a fed stomach — so its highest reward is within reach of even the poorest believer.

You may never build a masjid; you can always feed someone hungry.

Sab se bara ajr sab se chhote kaam mein. Connect: Qurʾān 76:8 — bhooke ko khilana.
