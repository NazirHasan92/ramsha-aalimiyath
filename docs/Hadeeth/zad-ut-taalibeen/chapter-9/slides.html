MOVEMENT 1 · TRUST & TRIALS

Given to you, sent to you

Aḥādīth 81, 82, 83 — how the believer stands in trusts and trials.

Mashware ki amaanat · fitnon se bachna · mohabbat mein chhupa darr.

Ḥadīth 81

إِنَّ الْمُسْتَشَارَ مُؤْتَمَنٌ

inna l-mustashāra muʾtaman

"The consultant is placed in a position of trust."

"Jis se mashwara liya jaaye woh amaanat-daar hai."

Tirmidhī · Ibn Māja

Advice is a trust

When someone asks "what should I do?", you owe them the counsel you'd give yourself.

No fobbing off, no half-answer, never steered to your own benefit. To betray that is to betray an amāna.

Mashwara aam baat nahin — amaanat hai. Sacha aur khaalis mashwara do.

Ḥadīth 82

إِنَّ السَّعِيدَ لَمَنْ جُنِّبَ الْفِتَنَ

inna s-saʿīda la-man junniba l-fitan

"The fortunate one is he who is protected from trials."

"Khush-naseeb woh hai jise fitnon se bacha liya jaaye."

Abū Dāwūd

Spared the storm — or patient in it

We rarely notice the trials we were protected from.

The Prophet ﷺ added (×3): "the one who is tested but perseveres — glad tidings are for him."

A trial is not displeasure. Spared or patient — the believer is safe either way.

Bachaaya gaya — ya sabr kiya — dono khush-naseeb.

Ḥadīth 83

إِنَّ الْوَلَدَ مَبْخَلَةٌ مَجْبَنَةٌ

inna l-walada mabkhalatun majbanah

"Children are a source of miserliness and cowardice."

"Aulaad bukhl aur buzdili ka sabab (ban sakti) hai."

Ibn Māja · Aḥmad

The fear, not the child

Two effects of unguarded love: miserliness (fear of poverty) and cowardice (fear of leaving orphans).

Not a condemnation of children. They are a blessing. The target is the fear love can breed. Cure: the child's rizq is decreed too (#87).

Nishaana darr hai — bachcha nahin. Mohabbat ko Allah par bharose ki jagah mat lene do.

📿 Qurʾān · 8:28

وَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّمَا أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَأَوْلَادُكُمْ فِتْنَةٌ

"…your wealth and your children are but a trial."

The things we love most can become the arena of our test.

Jin se sab se zyada mohabbat, wahi aazmaaish ban sakti hain.

MOVEMENT 2 · KEEP THE HEART

Steady & true

Aḥādīth 84, 85, 86 — the inner life: beauty, steadiness, truth.

Husn = shukr · josh ke baad susti · dil ka sach-jhoot sensor.

Ḥadīth 84

إِنَّ اللهَ جَمِيلٌ يُحِبُّ الْجَمَالَ

inna Llāha jamīlun yuḥibbu l-jamāl

"Allāh is beautiful and loves beauty."

"Allah khoobsoorat hai aur khoobsoorti ko pasand karta hai."

Muslim

Beauty = gratitude made visible

"Allah loves to see the effects of His blessings upon His servant."

Clean clothes, a cared-for look, beautiful recitation — as a quiet thank-you. Beauty aimed up (to the Giver) is worship.

Not vanity. Beauty aimed across — to impress people — slides into riyāʾ (#80).

Husn = shukr jo nazar aaye. Logon ko impress karne wala husn riyāʾ.

Ḥadīth 85

إِنَّ لِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ شِرَّةً وَلِكُلِّ شِرَّةٍ فَتْرَةً

inna li-kulli shayʾin shirratan, wa li-kulli shirratin fatratan

"For everything there is eagerness, and for every eagerness there is a weakening."

"Har josh ke baad ek susti aati hai."

Tirmidhī

Plan for the slump

The big start isn't the achievement — outlasting the slump is.

Don't take on more than you can sustain. The Sharīʿa praises moderation — a steady load, kept up "unabated."

Mutawassit, mustaqil amal. (Yaad rakho #44: thoda magar paaband.)

Ḥadīth 86

إِنَّ الصِّدْقَ طُمَأْنِينَةٌ وَإِنَّ الْكَذِبَ رِيبَةٌ

inna ṣ-ṣidqa ṭumaʾnīnatun, wa inna l-kadhiba rībah

"Truthfulness is contentment, and falsehood is doubt."

"Sachaai dili sukoon, jhoot bechaini."

Tirmidhī

The heart is a truth-detector

Truth → a settled calm. Falsehood → a nagging unease you can't silence.

For a purified heart. A sin-caked heart loses the signal — so keep the heart clean. (Not a blanket "if it feels fine, it's halal.")

Paak dil ka sensor. Dil saaf rakho to woh tez hota hai.

MOVEMENT 3 · THE UNSEEN AT WORK

Forces you can't see

Aḥādīth 87, 88, 89, 90 — provision, the enemy, the test, and the weapon.

Rizq tumhe dhoondta hai · dushman andar · maal imtihaan · poshida duʿāʾ.

Ḥadīth 87

إِنَّ الرِّزْقَ لَيَطْلُبُ الْعَبْدَ كَمَا يَطْلُبُهُ أَجَلُهُ

inna r-rizqa la-yaṭlubu l-ʿabda kamā yaṭlubuhu ajaluh

"Livelihood seeks a person out in the same way that his death does."

"Rizq bande ko aise dhoondta hai jaise uski maut."

Abū Nuʿaym · Ḥilya

Your provision is chasing you

You won't die until you've received every morsel decreed for you.

Not idleness. Islam commands lawful effort (#48). It removes the panic — strive calmly & lawfully.

Sukoon se halaal koshish. (Yeh #83 ke gareebi-darr ka jawab hai.)

📿 Qurʾān · 11:6

وَمَا مِن دَابَّةٍ فِي الْأَرْضِ إِلَّا عَلَى اللَّهِ رِزْقُهَا

"…no creature on earth but that its provision is upon Allah."

Provision is decreed and secured — so strive without fear of the unlawful.

Har jaandaar ka rizq Allah ke zimme. Darr ke maare haraam ki taraf mat jaao.

Ḥadīth 88

إِنَّ الشَّيْطَانَ يَجْرِي مِنَ الْإِنْسَانِ مَجْرَى الدَّمِ

inna sh-shayṭāna yajrī mina l-insāni majrā d-dam

"Satan runs through a human like blood runs [through the body]."

"Shaitaan insaan mein khoon ki tarah daudta hai."

Bukhārī

The enemy inside the walls

Swift, close, unfelt — the whisper arrives in your own "voice," through your lower desires (nafs ammāra).

Not fatalism. "Satan made me do it" is no excuse. The enemy inside means stay alert — dhikr, taʿawwudh, starve the nafs. Responsibility stays yours.

Pehra barhao — bahaana nahin. Dhikr aur taʿawwudh darwaza band karte hain.

📿 Qurʾān · 35:6

إِنَّ الشَّيْطَانَ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّ فَاتَّخِذُوهُ عَدُوًّا

"Indeed, Satan is an enemy to you, so take him as an enemy."

The response to the enemy in your bloodstream: vigilance, not surrender.

Use dushman samjho — chaukanna raho, haar nahin.

Ḥadīth 89

إِنَّ لِكُلِّ أُمَّةٍ فِتْنَةً وَفِتْنَةُ أُمَّتِي الْمَالُ

inna li-kulli ummatin fitnatan, wa fitnatu ummatī l-māl

"For every Umma there is a temptation, and the temptation of my Umma is wealth."

"Har ummat ka fitnah hai; meri ummat ka fitnah maal hai."

Tirmidhī

Know your community's test

Naming the test is half of passing it. Wealth bends the heart slowly — one small compromise at a time.

Wealth is not evil. #76 settled it — wealth is neutral, no harm for the God-fearing. #89 is a diagnosis.

Maal halke haath se pakdo, us mein Allah se daro. Gareebi-parasti nahin.

📿 Qurʾān · 64:15

إِنَّمَا أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَأَوْلَادُكُمْ فِتْنَةٌ

"Your wealth and children are only a trial."

The same word — fitnah. #89 names which one defines this Umma.

Wahi lafz — fitnah. #89 batati hai is Umma ka khaas imtihaan kaun sa.

Ḥadīth 90

إِنَّ أَسْرَعَ الدُّعَاءِ إِجَابَةً دَعْوَةُ غَائِبٍ لِغَائِبٍ

inna asraʿa d-duʿāʾi ijābatan daʿwatu ghāʾibin li-ghāʾib

"The fastest supplication to be accepted is one made by somebody for an absent person."

"Sab se jaldi qubool duʿāʾ woh hai jo ghaair-haazir ke liye ki jaaye."

Abū Dāwūd · Tirmidhī

The most powerful prayer is the secret one

No audience, no thanks, nothing in it for you → pure ikhlāṣ → rises fastest.

It is the exact opposite of riyāʾ (#80) — worship with the audience deliberately removed.

Apne ghaair-haazir bhaiyon ke liye chupke se duʿāʾ karo — riyāʾ ka theek ulta.

📿 Qurʾān · 40:60

ادْعُونِي أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ

"Call upon Me; I will respond to you."

Allah promises to answer — and #90 names the call that rises fastest: the sincere, hidden one.

Niyyat jitni saaf, jawab utna tez.
