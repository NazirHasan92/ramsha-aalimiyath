Chapter 2 — Student Handout

Aḥādīth 11–20 from Part 1 of Zad ut-Taalibeen · The believer's daily life

👋 Welcome back — what we'll learn today

Last week we drew the portrait of a believer — what faith is, what character looks like, how the believer relates to the world. This week, we move from portrait to practice: how does this believer actually live, day to day?

"Pichlay hafte hum ne momin ki tasveer banaayi thi. Aaj hum dekhenge momin rozmarra mein kaise jeeta hai."

This chapter covers 10 aḥādīth across three themes:

  1. Outward acts of worship (#12, #15, #16, #18) — purity, salām, the tooth-stick, the Qurʾān.
  2. Inner posture toward Allah's bounty (#11, #14, #20) — giving > taking, gratitude with food, sincere return.
  3. Vigilance against Satan's quiet traps (#13, #17, #19) — bells, backbiting, gendered fitnah.

The chapter closes on mercy — Ḥadīth 20 says the sincere repenter is "like one who has no sin." After heavy warnings, the door of return is always open.

"Chapter ka aakhri sandesh — tawbah ka dawaza band nahin hua. Allah ki rahmat se mayoos mat hona."

Ḥadīth 11 · The upper hand is better than the lower hand

الْيَدُ الْعُلْيَا خَيْرٌ مِنَ الْيَدِ السُّفْلَى

al-yadu l-ʿulyā khayrun mina l-yadi s-suflā

"The upper [giving] hand is better than the lower [taking] hand."

"Upar wala hath (denay wala) neeche wale hath (lenay wale) se behtar hai."

Bukhārī, Muslim

The picture: Imagine two hands. The giving hand reaches down into another's open palm. The receiving hand reaches up to take. The giving hand is in the higher position — literally and spiritually.

What it means: Both giving and receiving have benefit. The receiver, in genuine need, is not shamed by this ḥadīth — the Prophet ﷺ himself accepted gifts. But the station of giving is higher because:

  • The giver's heart is trained in trust — Allah will give again.
  • The giver becomes a channel of Allah's provision to others.
  • The giver's nafs is trained to release.

"Denay wala hath dil ko bharosa sikhata hai — Allah aur dega. Lenay wala hath dil ko aajazi sikhata hai. Dono mein faayda — magar manzil yeh hai ke hath neeche jaye, upar nahin."

💡 Key insight

If today your hand has to reach up — that's okay. Tomorrow, aspire to reach down. The hand changes direction when Allah lifts the situation. Don't despise the lower hand — yours may once have been there, and may be again.

Ḥadīth 12 · Purity is half of faith

الطُّهُوْرُ شَطْرُ الإِيْمَانِ

al-ṭuhūru shaṭru l-īmān

"Purity (ṭuhūr) is half of faith."

"Taharah iman ka aadha hissa hai."

Muslim · Abū Mālik al-Ashʿarī raḍiyallāhu ʿanhu

Two readings (both correct):

  1. "Īmān" here can mean ṣalāh. Wuḍūʾ is the most important prerequisite of ṣalāh — so purity is "half" of the prayer (without it, the prayer doesn't begin).
  2. Just as entering faith wipes out major and minor sins, wuḍūʾ wipes out minor sins. The two are parallel cleaning processes — hence "half".

"Donon meaning saath rakho — yeh aapas mein takraate nahin, ek doosre ko sambhaalte hain."

Critical distinction — ṭahāra vs naẓāfa:

WordMeaningWho has it?
ṬahāraReligious purity — free of najāsa (urine, blood) AND of ritual impurity (ḥadath, janāba, removed by wuḍūʾ / ghusl)Only a Muslim
NaẓāfaOutward cleanliness — washed clothes, brushed teeth, tidy homeAnyone — even non-Muslims

⚠️ Common confusion

Urdu speakers translate both with "saaf-suthra-pan". They are not the same. The ḥadīth is about ṭahāra, not naẓāfa. Both are good — the Prophet ﷺ was both — but only the first is what this ḥadīth promises is "half of faith."

"Saaf-suthra dikhna alag cheez hai, sharʿi taur par paak hona alag cheez hai. Hadith pehlay wali keh rahi hai."

Ḥadīth 13 · Bells are the flutes of Satan

الْجَرَسُ مَزَامِيْرُ الشَّيْطَانِ

al-jarasu mazāmīru sh-shayṭān

"Bells are the (musical) flutes of Satan."

"Ghantiyaan Shaitan ki bansariyaan hain."

Muslim

The reason: A bell's incessant repeating ring resembles a flute's continuous notes — and the Prophet ﷺ calls this Shayṭān's instrument. Why? Because Shayṭān's primary work is distraction from remembrance of Allah. Anything whose function is to repeatedly tug your attention away from dhikr is in his service.

"Hadith ka asal logic: jo cheez tumhe Allah ki yaad se hata kar baar baar apni taraf khinche, woh "Shaitan ki bansari" hai. Ghanti misaal hai — usool wider hai."

The principle extends: The fiqh ruling on bells specifically is detailed (and not all uses are haram). But the principle applies to:

  • Notification dings that yank you out of khushūʿ in ṣalāh
  • Background music filling every quiet moment
  • Doom-scrolling feeds engineered to pull continuous attention

💡 The personal question

Ask yourself: What in my life keeps pulling my mind away from Allah, again and again? Identify it. Not to declare it haram — but to see it. Once you see it, you can choose to limit it.

"Apne aap se poocho — meri zindagi mein kya cheez baar baar Allah ki yaad ko hata rahi hai? Jab nazar mein aa jaaye — phir tum decide kar sakti ho."

Ḥadīth 14 · The thankful eater is like the patient faster

الطَّاعِمُ الشَّاكِرُ كَالصَّائِمِ الصَّابِرِ

al-ṭāʿimu sh-shākiru ka-ṣ-ṣāʾimi ṣ-ṣābir

"The one who eats and is thankful is like the one who fasts and is patient."

"Khaane wala jo shukr karta hai, roza-daar jo sabr karta hai — donon ek hi class mein hain."

Tirmidhī, Dārimī

Beautiful parallel: The eater adds something (dhikr — Bismillāh and Al-ḥamdulillāh). The faster subtracts something (food and harmful speech). Both are conscious presence with Allah while the body does what it does naturally.

What "thankful eating" requires (from the book):

  • Begin with Bismi'Llāh — recognise the food has come by Allah's name.
  • End with Al-ḥamdu li'Llāh — recognise He, not your work, was the Giver.

Without these two bookends, eating is just consumption. With them, it becomes ʿibādah.

"In do lafzon ke saath khaana ek ibadat ban jaata hai. In ke baghair sirf pet bharna hai."

⚠️ One important note

The ḥadīth says the two are "alike" — but the book is careful: the fasting person's reward is still greater. The ḥadīth places eating-with-shukr in the same category as fasting — not at the same weight. Don't use this ḥadīth as an excuse to skip Ramaḍān fasts.

"Hadith yeh nahin keh rahi ke khaana = roza ka barabar ka ajr. Roza-daar ka ajr zaroor zyada hai. Magar khushkhabri yeh hai ke khaana bhi ibadat ban sakti hai — agar Bismillāh aur Al-ḥamdulillāh ho."

Ḥadīth 15 · The one who initiates the salām is free of pride

الْبَادِئُ بِالسَّلَامِ بَرِيْءٌ مِنَ الْكِبْرِ

al-bādiʾ bi-s-salām barīʾun mina l-kibr

"The one who initiates the salām is free of pride (kibr)."

"Jo pehlay salām kare, woh kibr (takabbur) se baalkul saaf hai."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

The Arabic word barīʾ is a legal term — it means "acquitted, cleared of charges." So this ḥadīth says: initiating the salām is a legal-spiritual acquittal from the charge of kibr. Powerful language for such a small act.

"Barīʾ matlab adaalat ki zubaan mein — bilkul saaf, ilzaam se aazaad. Yeh strong language hai ek choti si baat ke liye."

Why initiating exposes kibr: Kibr's core is the inner stance "I am bigger than them — they should approach me first." Initiating the salām directly contradicts that stance. With your tongue you say what your nafs would refuse: "I do not wait for you to acknowledge me. I acknowledge you first."

Real-life examples from the Sunnah:

  • The Prophet ﷺ greeted children first when he met them.
  • ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (raḍiyallāhu ʿanhumā) went to the bazaar specifically to scatter salām — not to buy or sell.
  • The Prophet ﷺ commanded: afshū l-salām — "spread the salām" abundantly.

⚠️ The cultural trap

In Urdu culture there is a "younger should salām first" rule. That Sunnah exists too. But the spirit of this ḥadīth is universal: whoever, of two people, chooses to be first — that one wins the inner battle. Don't rationalise with "I'm shy" or "they should say first" — that's the exact kibr the ḥadīth exposes.

"Tum chhoti ho ya badi — yeh tafseel hai. Asal sawaal: kya tumhe lag raha hai 'main pehlay kyun kahoon?' Agar haan — woh barīʾ min al-kibr nahin hai jo hadith maang rahi hai."

Ḥadīth 16 · The tooth-stick purifies the mouth and pleases the Lord

السِّوَاكُ مَطْهَرَةٌ لِلْفَمِ مَرْضَاةٌ لِلرَّبِّ

al-siwāku maṭharatun li-l-fam, marḍātun li-r-rabb

"The tooth-stick (siwāk) is a purifier for the mouth and a cause of pleasure for the Lord."

"Miswak munh ki paaki bhi hai aur Rabb ki khushnudi bhi."

Bukhārī (taʿlīqan), Nasāʾī, Dārimī, Aḥmad

Two effects in one tiny act:

  1. Physical — maṭharatun li-l-fam (purifier for the mouth)
  2. Spiritual — marḍātun li-l-rabb (cause of pleasure for the Lord)

The deeper teaching: Islam doesn't split body and soul into separate domains. A tiny physical act — cleaning the teeth — done with the right intention, is an act that elevates the soul. The Sunnah is full of these integrated acts: eating with shukr, walking with humility, even resting with Allah's name on the tongue.

"Jism aur rooh alag domain nahin — jism ki choti cheez bhi rooh ko Allah ke qareeb le ja sakti hai. Miswak ki misaal poori zindagi ka paradigm hai."

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Were it not that it would be a hardship on my Ummah, I would have ordered them to use the siwāk at every prayer" (Bukhārī, Muslim). So siwāk is near-wājib in spirit — only held back as Sunnah out of His ﷺ mercy.

"Rasool ﷺ ne farmaaya — agar Ummah par bojh na hota, har namaz se pehlay miswak farḍ kar deta. Yeh mehbooba Sunnah hai."

💡 Connect to Ḥadīth 12

Ḥadīth 12 was about big purity (wuḍūʾ, ghusl). This one is about small purity (the mouth). The believer's purity is layered — from the body down to the smallest entry point. We are paak at every level.

Ḥadīth 17 · Backbiting is more severe than unlawful intercourse

الْغِيْبَةُ أَشَدُّ مِنَ الزِّنَا

al-ghībatu ashaddu mina z-zinā

"Backbiting is more severe than unlawful intercourse."

"Gheebat zinā se zyada bhaari (sangeen) hai."

Bayhaqī · Shuʿab al-Īmān

This is a shocking ḥadīth — the Companions themselves were shocked. They asked the Prophet ﷺ "How is backbiting more severe than zinā?" The Prophet ﷺ gave two reasons, both decisive:

Reason 1 — the forgiveness mechanism differs:

The Prophet ﷺ said: "The person who fornicates is forgiven by Allah if he sincerely repents. But the one who backbites is not forgiven until the person he transgressed against forgives him."

  • Zinā = a violation of Allah's right (ḥaqq Allāh). Sincere tawbah, in principle, settles it. Allah is al-Ghafūr.
  • Backbiting = a violation of a human's right (ḥaqq al-ʿabd). Allah does not forgive what is owed to another human until that human forgives. The backbiter is trapped until the victim forgives in this life — or until the Day of Judgement (where good deeds are surrendered to victims).

"Zinā Allah ka haq hai — tawbah se cleared. Gheebat banday ka haq hai — banda maaf na kare to Allah bhi maaf nahin karega. Gheebat-karne wala phans gaya — apne baghair, apne haath se."

Reason 2 — the sinner's self-recognition differs:

The book continues: "Unlawful intercourse is normally recognised by the fornicator as a serious sin; hence, there is a greater chance of him turning to Allah and repenting. On the other hand, a person who backbites does not normally realise the seriousness of his sin and takes it lightly; hence, he does not stop and repent."

"Zānī ko apni galti maloom hai. Gheebat-karne wala ko yaqeen hi nahin hota ke yeh gunaah hai. Aur jo gunaah tumhe gunaah nahin lagta — usse tum tawbah karogi bhi kab?"

⚠️ What this ḥadīth is NOT saying

This ḥadīth does NOT mean:

  1. That zinā is "small". No. Zinā remains a major sin with severe consequences.
  2. That one can do zinā freely. No. The comparison is about post-sin clearing, not pre-sin permission.
  3. That all speech about others is haram. There are narrow, defined fiqh exceptions to gheebah.

The ḥadīth is raising gheebah, not lowering zinā. Both are heavy. Gheebah is heavier because its clearing path is longer.

"Hadith zinā ko chhota NAHIN keh rahi — gheebat ko bara keh rahi hai. Dono major sins hain."

A working definition of gheebah (Muslim, from Abū Hurayrah ﷺ):

"Gheebah is to mention your brother / sister with what they would dislike — even if it is true. If it is untrue, it is buhtān (slander), which is even worse."

"Sach bolne ki "azaadi" wala bahaana yahaan nahin chalta. Sach bhi gheebat hai. Jhoot hota to buhtān — aur zyada bhaari."

Ḥadīth 18 · The Qurʾān — evidence for you, or against you

الْقُرْآنُ حُجَّةٌ لَكَ أَوْ عَلَيْكَ

al-qurʾānu ḥujjatun laka aw ʿalayk

"The Qurʾān will be a proof for you, or against you."

"Qurʾān Qiyamat ke din tumhare haq mein gawaahi dega — ya tumhare khilaaf."

Muslim · Abū Mālik al-Ashʿarī raḍiyallāhu ʿanhu

The Arabic word ḥujjah is a legal / courtroom term — "evidence, proof, testimony." The Prophet ﷺ is describing the Day of Judgement as a court, with the Qurʾān itself as the evidence. Which side it stands on is decided now.

"Ḥujjah matlab adaalat ka 'proof'. Qiyamat ki adaalat hai, Qurʾān gawaah hai — par kis taraf? Yeh tum aaj decide kar rahi ho, kal nahin."

The Qurʾān stands FOR you if you act on it — three layers:

  1. Recitation — you read it; you do not abandon it.
  2. Understanding — you study what it says.
  3. Practice — your life bends to what it commands.

Without all three, even your recitation can become evidence against you.

"Sirf parhna kaafi nahin. Sirf samajhna kaafi nahin. Amal ke baghair tilaawat tumhare khilaaf gawaahi ban jaati hai."

The Prophet ﷺ's own complaint, recorded in the Qurʾān (25:30):

وَقَالَ الرَّسُوْلُ يَا رَبِّ إِنَّ قَوْمِيْ اتَّخَذُوْا هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ مَهْجُوْرًا

"And the Messenger will say: 'O my Lord, indeed my people have taken this Qurʾān as abandoned (mahjūr).'"

The complaint is not about disbelievers — it is about the Prophet ﷺ's own people. A Qurʾān on a shelf, kissed once a year, opened for two minutes on Friday — that is mahjūr.

💡 Personal diagnostic

If you died today, what would the Qurʾān say about you in court? Did you read it? Did you try to understand it? Did one verse change one action in your life this year?

"Agar aaj tumhari maut ho, to Qurʾān adaalat mein khada ho ke kya kahega — ḥujjatun laka, ya ḥujjatun ʿalayk?"

Ḥadīth 19 · Women are the snares of Satan

النِّسَاءُ حَبَائِلُ الشَّيْطَانِ

al-nisāʾu ḥabāʾilu sh-shayṭān

"Women are the snares (lures) of Satan."

"Auratein Shaitan ke jaal hain (jin se woh mardon ko phansa ta hai)."

Razīn

This ḥadīth can sound harsh on first reading — and the book's author, Shaykh Bulandshehrī, takes time to disarm the wrong reading. Read carefully.

What the ḥadīth is NOT saying (in Bulandshehrī's own words):

  • NOT that women are inherently evil.
  • NOT that women are the sole cause of men's sins.
  • NOT that men are excused from their own choices.

What it IS saying (Bulandshehrī):

"The essence of the narration is that Satan often employs women as a lure for men, time and time again, leading to the committal of sinful acts. The narration alludes to the natural attraction men possess for women, by which Satan lures them into sin."

So the ḥadīth is about Satan's strategy, not women's nature. Women are not the "device" — the device is the natural male attraction, which Satan exploits.

"Yeh hadith aurat ki nature ke baare mein NAHIN — yeh Shaitan ki strategy ke baare mein hai. Hadith MARDON ko warning de rahi hai."

Both-sex safety measures: The Qurʾān commands men first (24:30), then women (24:31). Both have responsibility:

  • Men → lower the gaze ("Tell the believing men to lower their gaze..." 24:30)
  • Women → modest dress ("...and tell the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their modesty..." 24:31)

"Sharīʿah ne dono ko bola hai. Quran 24:30 mein pehlay mard ko hukm aaya hai. Phir 24:31 mein aurat ko."

⚠️ Bulandshehrī's modern reframing

The author makes a striking point:

"People who clamour about women's abuse and strive to eradicate it ought to turn their attention to this degradation of women — [the use of women in advertising, modelling, pornography, prostitution]... Men who use women for commercial gain are as accursed, perhaps even more than the women whom they exploit."

So the ḥadīth was a warning — the modern world has weaponised exactly what it warned about. The ḥadīth doesn't degrade women — modern industries do.

"Hadith warning thi — duniya ne usay industry bana di. Aurat ki tazleel hadith mein nahin — modern markets mein hai."

Final framing — fitnah, not "evil": A supporting ḥadīth in Bukhārī/Muslim uses the word fitnah (trial, test) for the same dynamic. The Qurʾān uses the same word for wealth and children (64:15). Are wealth and children evil? No — they are tests. Same category here.

"Fitnah matlab test, na ke ilzaam. Maal, bachay, aurat — sab is hi category mein. Azmaish, na ke gunaah ki cheez."

Ḥadīth 20 · The sincere repenter is like one who has no sin

التَّائِبُ مِنَ الذَّنْبِ كَمَنْ لَا ذَنْبَ لَهُ

al-tāʾibu mina dh-dhanbi ka-man lā dhanba lah

"The one who repents from a sin is like the one who has no sin."

"Sachhi tawbah karne wala us insaan ke barabar hai jisne kabhi gunaah hi nahin kiya."

Ibn Mājah, Ṭabarānī

After two heavy ḥadīths (17 and 19), the chapter closes on mercy. The sincere repenter is equivalent — in accountability — to a person who never sinned.

"Chapter ka aakhri sandesh — sab landmines ke baad — rahmat ki khabar. Tawbah ka dawaza band nahin hua."

Two qualifications — read carefully:

1. "Sincere" tawbah only. The book opens with this word. Sincere tawbah (tawbah naṣūḥ) has four conditions:

  1. Stop the sin immediately.
  2. Regret it in the heart.
  3. Resolve never to return to it.
  4. Restore any human right (return property, ask forgiveness from the wronged).

Without all four, the tawbah is incomplete — and the promise of this ḥadīth doesn't apply.

"Char sharaait — gunaah turant chodo, dil mein nadamat, phir kabhi na karne ka pakka iraadah, aur agar kisi ka haq hai to wapas karo. Char ke baghair tawbah sachhi nahin."

2. "Equivalent in accountability." The book is precise: the equivalence is in the accountability ledger. The repenter's file on those specific sins is closed. They will not be asked about them on the Day. (In station, the one who never sinned remains higher — closer to the angels' state — but in accountability, they are equal.)

"Hisab ke baab mein barabar — file band ho jaata hai. Yeh promise hai."

⚠️ Don't make this a license

The mercy of this ḥadīth is for responsive tawbah — after a slip. It is NOT a license for planned sin. If you sin intending to repent later — that is not tawbah, that is istidrāj (Allah's slow grip, where He lets a person sink without their noticing). The door is open for those who have fallen, not for those who plan to fall.

"Rahmat ki dawaza khula hai gir-ne ke baad aane walon ke liye, plan kar ke aane walon ke liye nahin."

The chapter's closing message: Whatever happened in this chapter — gheebah, kibr, neglected Qurʾān, lowered gaze that didn't lower, ungrateful eating — every door is open. Sincere return, and the file closes.

"Chahe tum gheebat kar baithi ho, ya nazar uthaai ho, ya kibr mein salām se mukar gayi ho, ya Qurʾān ko taaq par rakha ho — raasta khula hai."

  7. Name one sin you have been delaying tawbah for. After Ḥadīth 20 — what stops you from making sincere tawbah today?
