Teaching arc: we open by defining what faith actually is (Theme 1), move to embodying it as character (Theme 2), and close by positioning the believer against the world's temptations (Theme 3). This is the journey of a ṭālib (seeker) — which is why the book is called Zād al-Ṭālibīn.

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the student will be able to:

  1. Recite each of the 10 aḥādīth in Arabic, with correct meaning in English + Roman Urdu.
  2. Name the source of each ḥadīth (Bukhārī, Muslim, Tirmidhī, Abū Dāwūd, Bayhaqī, Razīn).
  3. Explain that naṣīḥa is not "naseehat / advice" but a pure inner disposition toward another's good — directed at five objects.
  4. Articulate why duʿāʾ is called the marrow of worship — two reasons.
  5. State both sides of the "person with whom they love" ḥadīth — aspiration and warning.
  6. Apply the correct scope of "calmness from Allah" — daily affairs vs acts of worship.
  7. Identify the three life-protecting exceptions to gathering-confidentiality.
  8. Distinguish natural shyness from īmānī ḥayāʾ, and name what ḥayāʾ protects (head / stomach / heart).
  9. Apply the principle of khamr = jummāʿ al-ithm beyond alcohol — to anything that overcomes ʿaql.
  10. Explain why being ghirr (uncynical) is praised in the believer.
  11. Name the three directions of ẓulm (self / Allah / others) and which is the gravest.
  12. Read "world as prison" comparatively, not absolutely — and use it as a diagnostic for heart-attachment.

Ḥadīth 1 · الدِّيْنُ النَّصِيْحَةُ · Religion is naṣīḥa

Theme 1 — What faith IS. The Prophet ﷺ said this three times; the Companions then asked, "Toward whom?"

الدِّيْنُ النَّصِيْحَةُ

al-dīnu al-naṣīḥa

"The Religion is naṣīḥa — sincere, pure-hearted goodwill."

"Deen naṣīḥa hai — saaf-dili se doosron ki bhalaai chahna."

Muslim · Narrated by Tamīm al-Dārī (raḍiyallāhu ʿanhu)
Where this fits: Theme 1, node 1 — the foundational statement of what religion is, not just what religion contains.

النَّصِيْحَةُ · al-naṣīḥa

Meaning
Pure, unmixed wishing-well for another. Expresses as counsel, action, support — whatever the situation needs. The root (n-ṣ-ḥ) literally means "to be free of contamination" (used for pure honey, ʿasal nāṣiḥ).
Roman Urdu
Dil ki saaf-dili — kisi ke fayde ki sachhi tamanna, bina kisi milaawat ke. Sirf "naseehat" (advice) nahin — yeh poori disposition hai.
Important
The Prophet ﷺ said it three times, then specified five objects. Imam Muslim placed this ḥadīth at a foundational position; scholars say it represents nearly a quarter of religion.
Example
Correcting a brother gently before others can hear is naṣīḥa toward him. Loudly humiliating him is not — that's tashhīr. Same outward act (correction) — different inner disposition.

⚠️ The big landmine (Landmine 1)

Classmates will hear naṣīḥa and translate it as Urdu "naseehat" (advice). Stop this immediately. Say: "Naṣīḥa ≠ naseehat. Naṣīḥa = saaf-dil ki bhalaai chahna. Aur yeh sirf insaano ke liye nahin — Allah ke liye bhi, Qur'an ke liye bhi, Rasool ﷺ ke liye bhi."

💡 Teaching tip

Write the five objects on the board: Allah · His Book · His Messenger ﷺ · the leaders · the common people. Ask the class: "What does naṣīḥa toward Allah look like?" Let them struggle. Then unpack: faith in His attributes, obedience, gratitude. Same for the Qurʾān: honour it, recite correctly, practise it. The five-direction structure is the spine of the ḥadīth — don't skip it.

Ḥadīth 2 · الدُّعَاءُ مُخُّ الْعِبَادَةِ · Duʿāʾ is the marrow of worship

Theme 1 — What faith IS. The act that contains the essence of every other act of worship.

الدُّعَاءُ مُخُّ الْعِبَادَةِ

al-duʿāʾu mukhkhu l-ʿibādah

"Supplication (duʿāʾ) is the marrow (essence) of worship."

"Dua, ibadat ki gudda (asal jauhar) hai."

Bukhārī
Where this fits: Theme 1, node 2 — the inner reality of every formal act of worship.

مُخّ · mukhkh — the marrow

Meaning
Literally the marrow of a bone — the innermost, most concentrated, most nourishing part. Sometimes translated as "brain" or "essence". Not just a component — the core substance.
Roman Urdu
"Gudda" — haddi ke andar ka. Asal jauhar. Yani dua sirf ibadat ka ek hissa nahin — har ibadat ke andar ka asal jism dua hi hai.
Rule
In every formal worship — ṣalāh, ṣawm, ḥajj, dhikr, recitation — the inner act is turning to Allah in need. That posture IS duʿāʾ. Take it away and the worship is hollow.
Example
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِيْنُ
iyyāka naʿbudu wa-iyyāka nastaʿīn — "You alone we worship, You alone we ask for help" — recited in every rakʿah. That nastaʿīn (we ask) is duʿāʾ — sitting inside ṣalāh.

⚠️ Common confusion (Landmine 2)

Classmates separate "ibadat" (namaz/roza) from "dua" (asking after namaz). This ḥadīth collapses that separation. The Fātiḥah is a long duʿāʾ. Sujūd is the bodily posture of duʿāʾ. Iyyāka nastaʿīn is duʿāʾ. Make this collapse explicit — most have never thought of it this way.

💡 Teaching tip

Ask: "When you stand in ṣalāh and recite Sūrat al-Fātiḥah — is that worship, or is that duʿāʾ?" Let them debate. Then read the verse Ihdinā al-ṣirāṭa al-mustaqīm — "Guide us to the straight path" — and ask: "Is this a request? Then it's duʿāʾ. And it's in ṣalāh. So which is the duʿāʾ — and which is the worship?" The answer lands on its own.

Ḥadīth 3 · الْمَرْءُ مَعَ مَنْ أَحَبَّ · A person is with whom they love

Theme 2 — Character. Aspiration and warning — both edges of the same blade.

الْمَرْءُ مَعَ مَنْ أَحَبَّ

al-marʾu maʿa man aḥabb

"A person will be with whom they love."

"Insan unhi ke saath hoga jin se woh mohabbat karta hai."

Bukhārī, Muslim
Where this fits: Theme 2, node 1 — the first principle of character formation: who you love is who you become.

The story (sabab al-wurūd)

Context
A Companion came to the Prophet ﷺ and asked: "What about someone who loves a group of people but is unable to join them?" — meaning, I love the righteous but I'm not at their level. The Prophet ﷺ replied with this ḥadīth.
Roman Urdu
Ek sahabi ne pucha: "Main neki walon se mohabbat to karta hoon, magar unke darjay tak nahin pohncha." Rasool ﷺ ne yeh hadith bata di — dilaasa diya, magar saath warning bhi.
Key word
أَحَبَّ — aḥabb — deep, life-orienting love. Not casual "I like that person on Instagram". This is the love that shapes your choices. Surface likes don't fall under this; deep affinity does.

⚠️ The double-edged delivery (Landmine 3)

Don't deliver only the comforting half. Classmates need both: "Tum jin ko sachhe dil se chahti ho, woh tumhari Akhirah ki company hai. Naik logon se mohabbat = unke saath uthayi jaaogi. Magar yeh ulta bhi sach hai — agar tum kisi gunahgaar ko dil se admire karti ho, uske social media follow karti ho, uske kaam pasand karti ho, beshak khud woh kaam na karo — tum unke saath uthaayi jaaogi."

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 4:69

وَمَنْ يُّطِعِ اللَّهَ وَالرَّسُولَ فَأُولَٰئِكَ مَعَ الَّذِيْنَ أَنْعَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِم مِّنَ النَّبِيِّيْنَ وَالصِّدِّيْقِيْنَ وَالشُّهَدَاءِ وَالصَّالِحِيْنَ

"Whoever obeys Allah and the Messenger — they will be with those upon whom Allah has bestowed favour: of the Prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous."

Same word مَعَ (maʿa = "with") as in our ḥadīth. The Qurʾān and the Sunnah are saying the same thing.

Ḥadīth 4 · الْأَنَاةُ مِنَ اللهِ وَالْعَجَلَةُ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ · Calmness vs haste

Theme 2 — Character. Scope critical: daily affairs only. The opposite rule applies to acts of worship.

الْأَنَاةُ مِنَ اللهِ وَالْعَجَلَةُ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ

al-anāh min Allāh, wa-l-ʿajalatu min al-shayṭān

"Calmness and patient deliberation is from Allah; haste is from Satan."

"Tahmal aur ṣabr Allah ki taraf se hai; jaldbazi Shaitan ki taraf se hai."

Tirmidhī
Where this fits: Theme 2, node 2 — the believer's tempo in daily life. Different tempo applies to worship.

The three speeds rule

Daily affairs
Slow. Calmness, deliberation, patience. Acting in haste spoils the action.
Toward worship
Fast. Make wuḍūʾ at home and set out early; hasten to good deeds; race for forgiveness.
Inside worship
Calm. Once you're in the prayer / charity / dhikr, slow down — concentration, ṭamaʾnīnah, devotion.
Roman Urdu
Teen alag speeds: rozmarra mein ṣabr · ibadat ki taraf jaldi · ibadat ke andar ṭamaʾnīnah. Class ko ek line mein clarify karo.

Why ʿajala (haste) is Satan's tool

Logic
Haste skips the ʿaql (intellect). Without ʿaql engaged, only the nafs drives — and the nafs is what Satan exploits. Slow down → ʿaql wakes up → Satan's leverage drops.
Roman Urdu
Jaldi mein insan ʿaql istimal nahin karta — sirf impulse (nafs) chalata hai. Shaitan ko bas yahi chahiye. Ṣabr ʿaql ko jagaata hai, ʿajala usay sulaata hai.

⚠️ Misuse alert (Landmine 4)

Don't let this ḥadīth become a license for laziness. Classmates may quote it to excuse missing prayer time, procrastinating, dawdling. Pre-empt: "Yeh hadith ibadat mein nahin laagu — rozmarra mein hai. Namaz par jaldi, sadaqah par jaldi, neki par jaldi — yeh Quran ka hukm hai."

Ḥadīth 5 · الْمَجَالِسُ بِالأَمَانَةِ · Gatherings are with trust

"Mehfilon ki baat amanat hoti hai — usay chhupaaya jaaye."

Abū Dāwūd

أَمَانَة · amānah — a trust

Meaning
Something deposited in your care, which you are obligated to safeguard and return as it was. Words spoken in a gathering are deposited with you. By default, you safeguard them.
Roman Urdu
"Amānah" = jo cheez tumhare paas rakhi gayi hai use safe rakhna. Mehfil ki baat by default tumhare paas amānah hai — hifaazat karo.
Exception rule
If confidentiality would protect a crime against another's maqāṣid al-sharīʿah (life / honour / wealth) — the trust yields. Three exceptions only. This is from the rest of the narration.
Roman Urdu
Magar agar mehfil mein kisi ne *jurm* ki baat ki — kisi ki jaan, izzat, ya maal ke khilaaf — to confidentiality khatam. Yeh teen exceptions hain.

⚠️ The cover-up trap (Landmine 5)

Some classmates may unconsciously bring a "family privacy" frame and over-extend the rule to cover abuse, fraud, etc. The ḥadīth protects gossip, not crime. Make this explicit. Domestic violence, financial fraud, predatory behaviour — none of these fall under amānah of a gathering. They are within the three exceptions.

Ḥadīth 6 · الْحَيَاءُ شُعْبَةٌ مِّنَ الإِيْمَانِ · Modesty is a branch of faith

Theme 1 — What faith IS. Out of 70+ branches, the Prophet ﷺ singled out this one. Why?

الْحَيَاءُ شُعْبَةٌ مِّنَ الإِيْمَانِ

al-ḥayāʾu shuʿbatun min al-īmān

"Ḥayāʾ (modesty / God-conscious restraint) is a branch of faith."

"Ḥayāʾ iman ki ek shaakh hai."

Bukhārī, Muslim
Where this fits: Theme 1, node 3 — the one branch of faith that restrains, where all others propel.

Why ḥayāʾ uniquely

Logic
Of 70+ branches of faith, every other branch propels you toward something (prayer, charity, justice). Ḥayāʾ alone restrains you from sin. It's the brake. Without a brake, the accelerator destroys you.
Roman Urdu
Iman ke 70+ branches mein se sirf "ḥayāʾ" gunaah se rokne wala break hai. Baqi sab accelerator hain — naik kaam ki taraf le jaate hain. Break ke baghair gaadi crash ho jaati hai.

The operational definition (from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd's ḥadīth)

Prophet ﷺ
"Be shy of Allah as much as is His due." The Companions said, "We are." He replied: "That is not the point. Whoever is shy of Allah as His due should protect his head and what it comprises (mind, mouth, ears), his stomach and what is adjoining it (lawful earnings, lawful private parts), and remember death and what comes after."
Source
Aḥmad. Quoted in Zad ut-Taalibeen as the fuller definition.
Roman Urdu
Ḥayāʾ sirf feeling nahin — yeh teen jagah ki discipline hai: sar (kya dekha, suna, kaha), pet (kya khaaya, kahaan se kamaaya), qalb (Akhirah ki yaad, dunya se hatka taʿalluq).

💡 Teaching tip

Draw three columns on the board labelled سَر · پَیٹ · قَلْب (head, stomach, heart). Ask each classmate: "Right now, what's one thing in each column you could tighten this week?" Make it personal. Ḥayāʾ becomes concrete only when applied.

جُمَّاع · jummāʿ — the gathering

Meaning
"The gathering, the convening, the bringing-together". Not "one sin among many" — the place where many sins meet and feed each other. The gateway.
Roman Urdu
"Jummāʿ" = majma (gathering). Sharab akela gunaah nahin — woh dawaza hai jahaan se baqi sab gunaah ghar mein daakhil hote hain.
Supporting narration
From Sunan al-Bayhaqī (ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar raḍiyallāhu ʿanhumā): "Alcohol is the mother of all obscenities and the gravest of sins. Whoever drinks alcohol abandons prayer..." — the graphic specifics name what happens when ʿaql is offline.
The principle
Anything that overcomes the rational faculty (ʿaql) opens the gate. Recreational drugs are categorised as khamr in fiqh. The principle extends to anger that takes you over, lust that takes you over, anything that suspends judgement.

💡 Teaching tip

Tell the story from the book: a man refused to prostrate to an idol; refused to murder; refused to commit adultery. Then he accepted alcohol — and committed all three. "Ek 'haan' ke baad sab 'haan' ho gaya, kyunki ʿaql so gayi." Then bridge: "Aaj kal sharab nahin to kya — magar koi aur cheez tumhari ʿaql ko sulaati hai? Phone? Ghussa? Lust? Same principle."

Ḥadīth 8 · الْمُؤْمِنُ غِرٌّ كَرِيْمٌ وَالْفَاجِرُ خَبٌّ لَئِيْمٌ · The believer is uncynical & noble

Theme 2 — Character. Two paired contrasts. The hardest ḥadīth of the chapter to teach.

الْمُؤْمِنُ غِرٌّ كَرِيْمٌ وَالْفَاجِرُ خَبٌّ لَئِيْمٌ

al-muʾminu ghirrun karīm, wa-l-fājiru khabbun laʾīm

"The believer is uncynical and noble; the transgressor is deceitful and ignoble."

Abū Dāwūd, Tirmidhī

Why ghirr (deceivable) is praised

Logic
The believer's mind is optimised for Akhirah, not for dunyā. They don't plan deception, so they don't anticipate it. Losing 200 rupees to a dishonest shopkeeper is a non-event in the Akhirah ledger — so why pre-empt with cynicism?
Roman Urdu
Momin ka mind Akhirah ka hisaab rakhta hai, dunya ka nahin. Isi liye woh saaf-dil hai — dunya ke kaamon mein chālāki ko apni identity nahin banaata. Yeh kamzori nahin — yeh azmat ki nishaani hai.
The pairing
Notice: ghirr + karīm come together. Khibb + laʾīm come together. You cannot be cunning AND noble simultaneously — being calculating makes you mean-spirited. Two choices: noble-uncynical, or cunning-mean. No middle.

⚠️ The hardest question (Landmine 6)

Smart classmates will push: "But isn't being smart and discerning good?" Don't dismiss. Answer: "Yeh hadith yeh nahin keh rahi ke bewakoof bano. Yeh keh rahi hai ke chālāki ko apni identity mat banao. Momin ki ʿaql zinda hai, magar uska target Akhirah hai. Sharaafat aur saaf-dili ek package mein aati hain — chālāki aur kameena pan ek package mein aate hain. Tum kaunsa package choose karti ho?"

Ḥadīth 9 · الظُّلْمُ ظُلُمَاتٌ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ · Ẓulm becomes literal darkness

Theme 2 — Character. Arabic wordplay at its sharpest — the act becomes the consequence in its own root.

The wordplay

Root
Both ẓulm (الظُّلْم) and ẓulumāt (ظُلُمَات) share the root ẓ-l-m — literally to place a thing where it doesn't belong. Physical = light displaced by darkness. Moral = rights displaced by wrong.
Roman Urdu
"Ẓulm" aur "ẓulumāt" — dono ek hi jad se. Ẓulm = haq apni jagah se hatana. Ẓulumāt = roshni apni jagah se hatna. Hadith mein dono ek saath aaye hain — kyunki ek dusre ka phal hai.
Why plural
Ẓulumāt is plural — stacked, layered darknesses. Each act of ẓulm adds another layer. On the Day, the oppressor walks in a compound darkness — not one.

The three directions (from Jāmiʿ al-ʿUlūm wa-l-Ḥikam)

1. Self
Every sin = ẓulm against yourself. Shirk is the greatest (Qurʾān 31:13). Then major sins, then minor sins. Most overlooked direction.
2. Allah
Refusing His rights, denying His attributes, withholding worship.
3. Others
Honour, life, property of fellow humans (and animals, and the natural order). The only direction most people think of.

⚠️ Don't limit ẓulm to "harming others" (Landmine in the room)

Classmates will hear "ẓulm" and think only of #3 ("I haven't oppressed anyone"). Push them: "Har gunaah apne aap par ẓulm hai. Yeh sabse zyada bhul jaate hain. Tum apne nafs ko Akhirah ka nuksaan pohncha rahi ho — yeh bhi ẓulm hai. Aur shirk — woh sabse bara ẓulm hai, Quran kehti hai."

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 31:13

إِنَّ الشِّرْكَ لَظُلْمٌ عَظِيْمٌ

"Indeed, shirk is a grave ẓulm." — Luqmān's advice to his son. The Qurʾān itself defines shirk as the greatest ẓulm. This is what makes ẓulm-against-self the gravest of all three directions. (Verse linked by us; the book names shirk as the worst ẓulm but does not cite this āyah here.)

📿 Connect to Qurʾān · 57:12 — the believers' opposite

يَوْمَ تَرَى الْمُؤْمِنِيْنَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ يَسْعَىٰ نُوْرُهُم بَيْنَ أَيْدِيْهِمْ وَبِأَيْمَانِهِمْ

"On the Day you will see the believing men and women — their light proceeding before them and on their right..."

The believer's deeds become light; the oppressor's ẓulm becomes darknesses. Exact opposites on the same Day. Sharp contrast worth drawing for the class. (Verse linked by us, not cited in the book on these pages.)

Three misreadings to disarm

Misread 1
"So a believer must be miserable." Wrong. The Prophet ﷺ enjoyed lawful pleasures — good food, perfume, his family. Halāl joy is encouraged.
Misread 2
"So we should never enjoy anything." Wrong. Halāl enjoyments thank the Giver. Asceticism for its own sake is not Islamic.
Misread 3
"So all disbelievers are happy." Wrong. Many are deeply miserable in this life. The "paradise" claim is not psychological — it's relative to their Akhirah.
Roman Urdu
Hadith comparison hai, absolute statement nahin. Tum khush bhi raho, halāl mein lazzat bhi lo. Bas dil ka attachment dunya pe nahin, Akhirah pe rakho. Yeh hadith ka asal maqsood hai.

The Salaf-saying (Fuḍayl ibn ʿIyāḍ, d. 187 AH)

Quote
"Whoever forgoes the pleasures and desires of the world is in a prison. As for the one who does not abandon them — what kind of prison is he in?!"
Meaning
The "prison" sensation only arises if the believer is actually constraining themselves for the sake of Allah. If they indulge everything, they don't feel the prison — but they also lose the reward. The "prison" is the discipline.
Roman Urdu
Fuḍayl bin ʿIyāḍ kehte hain: "Qaid khana sirf usi ko mahsoos hota hai jo apne aap ko sharʿi haddon mein rakhta hai. Agar har cheez allow kar do, to qaid bhi nahin, magar reward bhi nahin." Yeh diagnostic hai.

⚠️ Don't deliver as "be sad" (Landmine 7)

Many classmates will read this as a Quranic call to misery. Disarm clearly: "Allah ke Rasool ﷺ khud halāl khushiyon mein khush hote thay. 'Qaid khana' matlab Akhirah ki badi niyamat ke saamne yeh dunya chhoti lagti hai — bus. Dil dunya pe na chipke — yeh hadith ka asal maqsood hai."

💡 Closing diagnostic question

End the chapter with this question to the class: "Apne dil ko check karo — kya tum dunya ko chhorne ke liye tayar ho? Ya darti ho? Agar darti ho — to yeh hadith tumhe diagnostic de rahi hai: tumhara dil kahaan attached hai?" Don't expect an answer aloud — let it sit. This is the closing impact of the chapter.

⚠️ Common student confusions — watch for these

1. Naṣīḥa heard as Urdu naseehat

Classmates will reduce it to "advice / lecture". Push for the deeper meaning: pure inner goodwill, directed at five objects (Allah, His Book, His Messenger ﷺ, leaders, common people).

2. Separating duʿāʾ from ʿibādah

Most classmates have never thought of ṣalāh itself as containing duʿāʾ. Make the collapse explicit. The Fātiḥah is a duʿāʾ. Sujūd is the posture of duʿāʾ.

3. Reading "you are with whom you love" as only comforting

The warning half is harder but more important. Whatever you secretly admire — that's where you'll be raised.

4. Misapplying "calmness from Allah" to worship

Three different speeds: daily affairs = slow, toward worship = hasten, inside worship = calm. Drill this distinction.

5. Using "gatherings are with trust" to cover crimes

Three exceptions: plans against life, against honour, against wealth. The ḥadīth protects gossip, not crime.

6. "Isn't being clever good?" — the believer's uncynicism

Take the question seriously. The ḥadīth doesn't praise stupidity — it praises not optimising for worldly cunning. Two packages: noble-uncynical vs cunning-mean. No middle.

7. Reducing ẓulm to "harming others"

Three directions: self / Allah / others. Self is most overlooked. Shirk is the greatest. Every sin is self-ẓulm.

8. "World as prison" → unnecessary misery

Halāl pleasures are encouraged. The "prison" is comparison to Akhirah, not a call to asceticism.

📿 Connect to the Qurʾān

Verses that mirror the ḥadīths of this chapter. Use 2–3 in class — don't overload. Source note: 40:60 (Ḥadīth 2) and 4:69 (Ḥadīth 3) are cited by the book itself; 31:13 and 57:12 are linked by us to illuminate Ḥadīth 9 — the book does not print them on these pages.

Surah Ghāfir · 40:60 — duʿāʾ as worship

وَقَالَ رَبُّكُمُ ادْعُونِيْ أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ الَّذِيْنَ يَسْتَكْبِرُوْنَ عَنْ عِبَادَتِيْ سَيَدْخُلُوْنَ جَهَنَّمَ دَاخِرِيْنَ

"And your Lord says: 'Call upon Me, I will answer you. Indeed those who are too proud to worship Me — they will enter Hellfire humiliated.'"

Why this fits: Allah equates calling on Him (duʿāʾ) with worshipping Him in the same verse — "too proud to worship Me" follows "call upon Me". Mirror image of Ḥadīth 2.

Surah al-Nisāʾ · 4:69 — companions in the Hereafter

وَمَنْ يُّطِعِ اللَّهَ وَالرَّسُولَ فَأُولَٰئِكَ مَعَ الَّذِيْنَ أَنْعَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِم مِّنَ النَّبِيِّيْنَ وَالصِّدِّيْقِيْنَ وَالشُّهَدَاءِ وَالصَّالِحِيْنَ ۚ وَحَسُنَ أُولَٰئِكَ رَفِيْقًا

"Whoever obeys Allah and the Messenger — they will be with those upon whom Allah has bestowed favour: of the Prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous. The best of company are they."

Why this fits: Same word maʿa (with) as in Ḥadīth 3. The Qurʾān describes the company; the ḥadīth gives the mechanism — love.

Surah Luqmān · 31:13 — shirk as the greatest ẓulm

يَا بُنَيَّ لَا تُشْرِكْ بِاللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ الشِّرْكَ لَظُلْمٌ عَظِيْمٌ

"O my son, do not associate partners with Allah. Indeed shirk is a grave wrong (ẓulm)."

Why this fits: Locates the most severe form of ẓulm — against Allah, the worst kind of self-ẓulm. Directly supports Ḥadīth 9's three-directions framework.

Surah al-Ḥadīd · 57:12 — light of the believers vs ẓulumāt

يَوْمَ تَرَى الْمُؤْمِنِيْنَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ يَسْعَىٰ نُوْرُهُم بَيْنَ أَيْدِيْهِمْ وَبِأَيْمَانِهِم بُشْرَاكُمُ الْيَوْمَ جَنَّاتٌ تَجْرِيْ مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الأَنْهَارُ

"On the Day you will see the believing men and women — their light proceeding before them and on their right: 'Glad tidings for you today: Gardens beneath which rivers flow.'"

Why this fits: The believer's deeds become nūr (light); the oppressor's ẓulm becomes ẓulumāt (darknesses). Exact opposites on the same Day.
