Rule 1 · "That" — far away

ذَلِكَ

dhālika

= "that" (a far thing, masculine)

= "woh" (jo door hai)

This (near) ↔ That (far)

Near · paas

هَذَا

hādhā

"this" · yeh

Far · door

ذَلِكَ

dhālika

"that" · woh

Point near → hādhā · Point far → dhālika

Say it correctly ⚠️

ذ = soft "th" (like English that) — not "z".

Hidden long "ā" (no alif written): dhā-li-ka.

Ends in "-ka".

✅ dhā-li-ka   ❌ zālika   ❌ dhalika (short)

`ذ` woh naram "th" hai, "z" nahin — bilkul `هَذَا` jaisa.

From the lesson — read aloud

ذَلِكَ نَجْمٌThat is a starWoh sitaara hai
هَذَا مَسْجِدٌ وَذَلِكَ بَيْتٌThis is a mosque and that is a houseYeh masjid, woh ghar
هَذَا حِصَانٌ وَذَلِكَ حِمَارٌThis is a horse and that is a donkeyYeh ghoda, woh gadha

Rule 2 · Asking "what?" & "who?"

Pick the question word by thing vs person.

Cheez ya insaan — uske hisaab se sawaali lafz chuno.

Two question words

مَا ذَلِكَ ؟

mā dhālika?

"What is that?"

things & animals
cheez ya jaanwar (kya?)

ذَلِكَ حَجَرٌ

مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟

man dhālika?

"Who is that?"

people only
sirf insaan (kaun?)

ذَلِكَ إِمَامٌ

⚠️ Animals are "what", not "who"

Horse, donkey, dog, cat → مَا (what)

Only people → مَنْ (who)

Insaan → مَنْ (kaun). Cheez ya jaanwar → مَا (kya).

Who is this, who is that?

مَنْ هَذَا وَمَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟

هَذَا مُدَرِّسٌ وَذَلِكَ إِمَامٌ

"Who is this and who is that? — This is a teacher and that is an imam."

"Yeh kaun aur woh kaun? — Yeh ustaad hai aur woh imam hai."

Rule 3 · Yes & No

Put أَ on the front = a yes/no question.

نَعَمْ = yes (haan) · لَا = no (nahin)

Ask → answer "no" → correct it

أَذَلِكَ كَلْبٌ ؟

لَا، ذَلِكَ قِطٌّ

"Is that a dog? — No, that is a cat."

"Kya woh kutta hai? — Nahin, woh billi hai."

Always answer in a full sentence

❌ just "لَا"

✅ "لَا، ذَلِكَ قِطٌّ" — no, and what it really is

Sirf "nahin" mat kaho — "nahin, woh billi hai" — poora jumla.

"That is the Book; there is no doubt in it."
