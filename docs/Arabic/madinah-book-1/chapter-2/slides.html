📋 Quick recap — Chapter 1

  • هَذَا = "this is..." (for near, masculine)
  • No word for "is" — silence does the work
  • الـ = "the" — drops tanween doubling
  • Sun letters: L silent · Moon letters: L heard
  • أَ + sentence = yes/no question

All of this comes back today — Chapter 2 builds on it.

Rule 1 · The word for "that" (far)

ذَلِكَ

dhaalika

= "that is..." (pointing to something far)

= "woh hai..." (door wali cheez ki taraf ishaara)

⚠️ Pronunciation — the biggest mistake

Say dhaalika — NOT zaalika.

The Arabic ذ is the English "th" in "this", "that", "the".

Urdu mein "ذ" ko "z" bolte ho — Arabic mein nahin. Zubaan daaton ke beech mein laao — jaisa English mein "this" bolte ho.

Try it now: say English "this" — feel your tongue. Same position for dhaalika.

Two more things

  1. The middle alif is silent.
    Say "dhaa-li-ka" — not "dhaa-AA-li-ka".
    Beech wali alif silent — "aa" beech mein hai
  2. Use it for masculine, far things only.
    (Feminine "that" comes later.)
    Sirf mardaana, door wali cheez ke liye

Example

ذَلِكَ بَيْتٌ

dhaalika baytun

"That is a house"

"Woh ek ghar hai"

Near vs Far — same noun, different word

🤚 Near · qareeb

هَذَا قَلَمٌ

haadhaa qalamun

"This is a pen"

"Yeh ek qalam hai"

👉 Far · door

ذَلِكَ قَلَمٌ

dhaalika qalamun

"That is a pen"

"Woh ek qalam hai"

Same word for "pen" — different demonstrative based on distance.

Rule 2 · Asking "What is this?"

مَا

maa

= "what?" — for things, animals, non-humans

= "kya?" — sirf cheezon, jaanwaron ke liye

The pattern

مَا هَذَا ؟

"What is this?" · "Yeh kya hai?"

⬇️
هَذَا كِتَابٌ

"This is a book" · "Yeh ek kitaab hai"

The answer = a Chapter 1 sentence!

More examples

مَا هَذَا ؟ هَذَا حَجَرٌ What is this? — A stone
مَا ذَلِكَ ؟ ذَلِكَ نَهْرٌ What is that? — A river
مَا هَذَا ؟ هَذَا لَبَنٌ What is this? — Milk

Notice the Arabic question mark ؟ — curves the other way (RTL).

Rule 3 · Asking "Who is this?"

مَنْ

man

= "who?" — for people only

= "kaun?" — sirf insaanon ke liye

The strict rule

📦 Things, animals

مَا

maa

"what?"

cheez ke liye

👤 People

مَنْ

man

"who?"

insaan ke liye

In Arabic, this split is strict.

Urdu mein "kya" har cheez chal jaata hai — Arabic mein nahin. Insaan ke liye sirf "man".

The pattern

مَنْ هَذَا ؟

"Who is this?" · "Yeh kaun hai?"

⬇️
هَذَا وَلَدٌ

"This is a boy" · "Yeh ek ladka hai"

More examples

مَنْ هَذَا ؟ هَذَا صَدِيقٌ Who is this? — A friend
مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟ ذَلِكَ إِمَامٌ Who is that? — An imam
مَنْ هَذَا ؟ هَذَا ابْنٌ Who is this? — A son

Rule 4 · "Or" in a question

أَمْ

am

= "or" — to offer a choice in a question

= "ya" — sawal ke andar do options dene ke liye

The structure

أَ question + option 1 + أَمْ or + option 2 + ؟

أَهَذَا قَلَمٌ أَمْ مِفْتَاحٌ ؟

a-haadhaa qalamun am miftaaḥun?

"Is this a pen or a key?"

"Kya yeh qalam hai ya chaabi?"

⚠️ The answer must be a FULL sentence

NOT naʿam (yes). NOT just the noun. Full sentence.

  • ❌ naʿam — wrong (this isn't yes/no!)
  • ❌ qalamun — wrong (incomplete!)
  • ✅ haadhaa qalamun — correct (full sentence!)

Choice wala sawal hai — "haan/nahin" se kaam nahin chalega. Poora jumla chahiye, jaisa Ch 1 mein seekha.

Q&A example

أَذَلِكَ جَبَلٌ أَمْ حَجَرٌ ؟

"Is that a mountain or a stone?" · "Woh pahaad hai ya patthar?"

ذَلِكَ جَبَلٌ

"That is a mountain." · "Woh pahaad hai."

More examples

أَهَذَا سُكَّرٌ أَمْ لَبَنٌ ؟ هَذَا سُكَّرٌ
"Sugar or milk?" — "This is sugar."
أَهَذَا أَنْفٌ أَمْ فَمٌ ؟ هَذَا أَنْفٌ
"Nose or mouth?" — "This is a nose."
أَهَذَا ابْنٌ أَمْ خَالٌ ؟ هَذَا ابْنٌ
"Son or maternal uncle?" — "This is a son."
