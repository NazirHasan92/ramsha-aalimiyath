الدَّرْسُ الثَّانِي

Chapter 2 — Student Handout

"That is a ___" · "What/Who is this?" · "Is it X or Y?"

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Point to something far away and say "That is a ___" in Arabic.
  2. Ask "What is this?" (for things) and "Who is this?" (for people).
  3. Ask a choice question — "Is this X or Y?" — and answer it with a full sentence.
  4. Pronounce ذ correctly — like "th" in "this", not "z".
  5. Know about 16 new Arabic words.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · ذَلِكَ means "that is..." (far)

Last week you learned هَذَا (haadhaa) — "this is..." for something nearby.

This week you learn its partner — ذَلِكَ (pronounced dhaalika) — "that is..." for something far away.

ذَلِكَ بَيْتٌ

dhaalika baytun · "That is a house" · Woh ek ghar hai

💡 Three important things

  1. The ذ sounds like the English "th" in "this" or "that" — NOT like "z". Put your tongue between your teeth and say "dhaa-li-ka".
  2. The alif in the middle is silent — just like هَذَا last week. Say "dhaa-li-ka", not "dhaa-AA-li-ka".
  3. Use ذَلِكَ only for masculine, far-away things. (Feminine "that" comes later.)

Rule 2 · مَا هَذَا ؟ — "What is this?"

Use مَا (pronounced maa) to ask "what?" — but only when asking about things, animals, or non-humans (a pen, a stone, a river, etc.).

مَا هَذَا ؟

maa haadhaa? · "What is this?" · Yeh kya hai?

The answer is a full Chapter 1 sentence:

مَا هَذَا ؟ — هَذَا كِتَابٌ

maa haadhaa? — haadhaa kitaabun.
"What is this? — This is a book." · "Yeh kya hai? — Yeh ek kitaab hai."

💡 Notice the Arabic question mark

Arabic uses ؟ (mirrored) — the hook curves the other way because Arabic reads right-to-left.

More examples:

مَا هَذَا ؟هَذَا حَجَرٌWhat is this? — This is a stone.Yeh kya hai? — Yeh ek patthar hai.
مَا ذَلِكَ ؟ذَلِكَ نَهْرٌWhat is that? — That is a river.Woh kya hai? — Woh ek nahar hai.
مَا هَذَا ؟هَذَا لَبَنٌWhat is this? — This is milk.Yeh kya hai? — Yeh doodh hai.

Rule 3 · مَنْ هَذَا ؟ — "Who is this?"

Use مَنْ (pronounced man) to ask "who?" — but only when asking about a person (a boy, an imam, a friend, etc.).

مَنْ هَذَا ؟

man haadhaa? · "Who is this?" · Yeh kaun hai?

⚠️ Important — strict rule

Arabic separates "what?" and "who?" strictly:

  • مَا (maa) — only for things, animals, non-humans.
  • مَنْ (man) — only for people (humans).

In Urdu we say "yeh kya hai?" about a person sometimes — in Arabic, NEVER. Always use مَنْ for people.

More examples:

مَنْ هَذَا ؟هَذَا وَلَدٌWho is this? — This is a boy.Yeh kaun hai? — Yeh ek ladka hai.
مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟ذَلِكَ إِمَامٌWho is that? — That is an imam.Woh kaun hai? — Woh ek imam hai.
مَنْ هَذَا ؟هَذَا صَدِيقٌWho is this? — This is a friend.Yeh kaun hai? — Yeh ek dost hai.

Rule 4 · أَمْ — "or" (in a question)

To offer a choice inside a question — "Is this X or Y?" — use أَمْ (pronounced am) between the two options.

أَهَذَا قَلَمٌ أَمْ مِفْتَاحٌ ؟

a-haadhaa qalamun am miftaaḥun? · "Is this a pen or a key?" · Kya yeh qalam hai ya chaabi?

The structure:

أَ (question) + option 1 + أَمْ (or) + option 2 + ؟

⚠️ Answer with a FULL sentence — not yes/no!

An am question is not yes/no — it's "pick which one". So you must answer with a full Chapter 1 nominal sentence:

  • ❌ naʿam (yes) — wrong! This isn't a yes/no question.
  • ❌ Just qalamun (a pen) — wrong! Incomplete.
  • ✅ haadhaa qalamun ("this is a pen") — correct! Full sentence.

More examples:

أَذَلِكَ جَبَلٌ أَمْ حَجَرٌ ؟ ذَلِكَ جَبَلٌ Is that a mountain or a stone? — That is a mountain.
أَهَذَا سُكَّرٌ أَمْ لَبَنٌ ؟ هَذَا سُكَّرٌ Is this sugar or milk? — This is sugar.
أَهَذَا ابْنٌ أَمْ خَالٌ ؟ هَذَا ابْنٌ Is this a son or a maternal uncle? — This is a son.
