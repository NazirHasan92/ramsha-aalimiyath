الدَّرْسُ الثَّانِي

Chapter 2 — Student Handout

"That" (ذَلِكَ) · asking what & who · saying yes & no

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Point to something far away and say "that" — ذَلِكَ.
  2. Tell the difference between "this" (هَذَا, near) and "that" (ذَلِكَ, far).
  3. Ask "What is that?" (مَا ذَلِكَ ؟) and "Who is that?" (مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟).
  4. Answer a question with "yes" (نَعَمْ) or "no" (لَا).

Good news: almost everything here you already met in Lesson 1 — the only brand-new word is ذَلِكَ ("that"). This is an easy, confidence-building lesson.

Rule 1 · ذَلِكَ means "that" (far)

In Lesson 1 you learned هَذَا = "this" (something near). Today you learn its partner for something far:

ذَلِكَ

dhālika · "that" · woh

Near — "this" Far — "that"
هَذَاذَلِكَ
hādhā · yehdhālika · woh

💡 Say it correctly

Three things about ذَلِكَ:

• The ذ is a soft "th" (like English that) — not "z". So dhālika, never zālika.
• There is a hidden long "ā" even though no alif is written — dhā-li-ka (just like هَذَا).
• The end is "-ka" — dhāli-ka.

Examples straight from the lesson:

مَا ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ نَجْمٌmā dhālika? — dhālika najmunWhat is that? — That is a starWoh kya hai? — Woh sitaara hai
هَذَا مَسْجِدٌ وَذَلِكَ بَيْتٌhādhā masjidun wa-dhālika baytunThis is a mosque and that is a houseYeh masjid hai aur woh ghar hai
هَذَا حِصَانٌ وَذَلِكَ حِمَارٌhādhā ḥiṣānun wa-dhālika ḥimārunThis is a horse and that is a donkeyYeh ghoda hai aur woh gadha hai
مَا ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ سَرِيرٌmā dhālika? — dhālika sarīrunWhat is that? — That is a bedWoh kya hai? — Woh palang hai

Rule 2 · Asking "What?" and "Who?"

Use the right question word depending on whether you mean a thing or a person:

مَا ذَلِكَ ؟ — "What is that?"

For things and animals.

mā = kya

ذَلِكَ حَجَرٌ

dhālika ḥajarun — "That is a stone"

مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟ — "Who is that?"

For people only.

man = kaun

ذَلِكَ إِمَامٌ

dhālika imāmun — "That is an imam"

⚠️ Animals use مَا, not مَنْ

A horse, donkey, dog, or cat is asked about with مَا ("what") — only people get مَنْ ("who").
Insaan → مَنْ (kaun). Cheez ya jaanwar → مَا (kya).

Rule 3 · Saying "yes" and "no"

Put أَ on the front of a sentence to turn it into a yes/no question. Then answer:

أَذَلِكَ كَلْبٌ ؟ — لَا، ذَلِكَ قِطٌّ

a-dhālika kalbun? — lā, dhālika qiṭṭun
"Is that a dog? — No, that is a cat" · Kya woh kutta hai? — Nahin, woh billi hai

نَعَمْnaʿamyeshaan
لَاlānonahin

💡 Always answer in a full sentence

Don't just say "lā". Say "no" and then what it really is: لَا، ذَلِكَ قِطٌّ — "No, that is a cat." It's more polite and gives you more practice.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · "this" or "that"? Fill in هَذَا or ذَلِكَ

Near you → هَذَا (yeh). Far away → ذَلِكَ (woh).

  1. (far) نَجْمٌ — that is a star
  2. (near) كِتَابٌ — this is a book
  3. (far) حَجَرٌ — that is a stone
  4. (near) قَلَمٌ — this is a pen
  5. (far) سَرِيرٌ — that is a bed

Exercise 2 · مَا or مَنْ? Choose the right question word

Person → مَنْ (who). Thing or animal → مَا (what).

  1. ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ إِمَامٌ
  2. ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ حِمَارٌ
  3. ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ سُكَّرٌ
  4. هَذَا ؟ — هَذَا مُدَرِّسٌ
  5. ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ قِطٌّ

Exercise 3 · Answer with لَا + the correct word

Each guess is wrong. Answer "No, that is …" using the word in brackets. Example: أَذَلِكَ كَلْبٌ ؟ (قِطّ) → لَا، ذَلِكَ قِطٌّ.

  1. أَذَلِكَ بَيْتٌ ؟ (مَسْجِد) —
  2. أَذَلِكَ سُكَّرٌ ؟ (لَبَن) —
  3. أَذَلِكَ حِصَانٌ ؟ (حِمَار) —
  4. أَذَلِكَ نَجْمٌ ؟ (حَجَر) —

Exercise 4 · Translate into Arabic

  1. That is a stone. —
  2. This is sugar and that is milk. —
  3. Who is that? That is an imam. —
  4. Is that a dog? No, that is a cat. —

Exercise 5 · Look around — point and say it!

Find 2 things near you and 2 things far away. Name each: near → hādhā …, far → dhālika ….

  2. (near)
  3. (far)

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the 4 new words — إِمَام، حَجَر، سُكَّر، لَبَن — with their meanings.
  2. Read out loud every example sentence in this handout — at least 3 times each. Watch the ذ sound (dh, not z).
  3. Do all 5 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
  4. Practice the near/far split at home: point at 5 near things (hādhā) and 5 far things (dhālika).
