Rule 1 · The word for "this"

هَذَا

haadhaa

= "this is..." (for a nearby thing)

= "yeh hai..." (kisi qareeb cheez ki taraf ishaara)

Two things to remember

  1. The ا is silent.
    Say "haadhaa" — not "ha-aa-dhaa".
    ا silent hai — "aa" ki awaaz beech mein, aakhir mein nahin
  2. Use it for masculine things only.
    (Feminine "this" comes later.)
    Sirf mardaana cheezon ke liye — feminine baad mein

Example

هَذَا بَيْتٌ

haadhaa baytun

"This is a house"

"Yeh ek ghar hai"

Rule 2 · Arabic has NO word for "is"

In English: "This is a house" (3 words)

In Urdu: "Yeh ek ghar hai" (4 words)

In Arabic: هَذَا بَيْتٌ (only 2 words!)

The silence between the words IS the "is".

Khaali jagah hi "hai" ka kaam karti hai — koi extra lafz nahin.

The two parts of the sentence

Mubtada' مُبْتَدَأٌ — subject + Khabar خَبَرٌ — predicate

Mubtada' = jis se shuruaat hoti hai · Khabar = us ke baare mein khabar

هَذَا + بَيْتٌ

= "This is a house" · "Yeh ek ghar hai"

This kind of sentence is called Jumlah Ismiyyah — جُمْلَةٌ اِسْمِيَّةٌ — naam wala jumla.

Rule 3 · Adding "the"

الـ

al-

= "the"

= "the" / khaas wala

Just stick it to the front

a ___
(aam · any)		 the ___
(khaas · specific)
بَيْتٌالْبَيْتُ
قَلَمٌالْقَلَمُ
كِتَابٌالْكِتَابُ

Notice the ending

بَيْتٌ ends in ـٌ (un — doubled)

الْبَيْتُ ends in ـُ (u — single)

When you add al-, the doubling drops.

baytun → al-baytu (not al-baytun)

"al-" lagaane se tanween (doubling) hat jaati hai.

Rule 4 · Sun & Moon Letters

When you say "al-", sometimes the L is heard, sometimes silent.

It depends on the next letter.

"al-" ke baad ka harf decide karta hai — L sunaayi degi ya gaayab ho jaayegi.

Two examples — same "al-", different sound

🌙 Moon · chand

الْقَمَرُ

al-qamar

"the moon" · chand

The L is pronounced.
L saaf sunaayi deti hai

☀️ Sun · sooraj

الشَّمْسُ

ash-shams

"the sun" · sooraj

The L is silent, ش doubles.
L gaayab, ش double ho gayi

How to remember

☀️ The Sun is loud —
it eats the L.

The Moon 🌙 lets the L through.

Sooraj zor se bolta hai — L kha jaata hai. Chand naram hai — L guzar jaane deta hai.

The 14 Moon letters (L is heard)

ا · ب · ج · ح · خ · ع · غ · ف · ق · ك · م · هـ · و · ي

The 14 Sun letters (L silent, next doubles)

ت · ث · د · ذ · ر · ز · س · ش · ص · ض · ط · ظ · ل · ن

Moon letters — moonh ke andar peechhe wale. Sun letters — moonh ke saamne wale (zubaan dataon ke paas).

Rule 5 · Asking a question

Just add أَ in front!

(pronounced "a-")

Bas "a-" jumle ke shuru mein lagao — sawal ban gaya.

Statement → Question

هَذَا بَيْتٌ

"This is a house" · "Yeh ek ghar hai"

⬇️
أَهَذَا بَيْتٌ ؟

"Is this a house?" · "Kya yeh ek ghar hai?"

Answer with...

نَعَمْ

naʿam

YES

haan

لَا

laa

NO

nahin
