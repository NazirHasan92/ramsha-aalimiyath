Chapter 1 — Student Handout

My first Arabic chapter · "This is a ___"

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Point to anything in the room and say "This is a ___" in Arabic.
  2. Ask "What is this?" about a thing and "Who is this?" about a person.
  3. Ask "Is this a ___?" and answer "yes" / "no".
  4. Know 24 new Arabic words.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · هَذَا means "this is..."

When you point to something nearby (a book, a pen, a man), say هَذَا (pronounced haadhaa).

هَذَا بَيْتٌ

haadhaa baytun · "This is a house" · Yeh ek ghar hai

💡 Two important things

  1. The ا in هَذَا is silent. Say "haadhaa", not "ha-aa-dhaa".
  2. Use هَذَا only for masculine, singular things (a book, a man, a chair). We'll learn the feminine form later.

More examples — say each one aloud:

هَذَا قَلَمٌhaadhaa qalamunThis is a penYeh ek qalam hai
هَذَا كِتَابٌhaadhaa kitaabunThis is a bookYeh ek kitaab hai
هَذَا مَسْجِدٌhaadhaa masjidunThis is a mosqueYeh ek masjid hai
هَذَا وَلَدٌhaadhaa waladunThis is a boyYeh ek ladka hai

Rule 2 · Arabic has NO word for "is"

In English we say "this is a house". In Urdu we say "yeh ek ghar hai". In Arabic, the word "is/hai" is just silent — the two words sit next to each other.

⚠️ Do NOT add any extra word

Just say: هَذَا بَيْتٌ
Don't try to insert "is" or "huwa" or anything else. The silence is the "is".

Two words make the whole sentence — no "is" in between:

Word 1Word 2= Sentence
هَذَا
haadhaa · "this"		 بَيْتٌ
baytun · "a house"		 "This is a house"
(no "is" needed)

💡 Notice the ending

Each "a ___" word ends in a little doubled mark ـٌ read as -un: baytun, qalamun, kitaabun. Just copy the sound — that's the natural ending of the word.

Rule 3 · Ask مَا هَذَا؟ / مَنْ هَذَا؟

Two question words let you ask about anything you don't recognise — one for things, one for people:

QuestionUsed forExample answer
مَا هَذَا؟
maa haadhaa? · "What is this?"		 things & animals
cheezein / jaanwar		 هَذَا نَجْمٌ
"This is a star" · yeh ek sitara hai
مَنْ هَذَا؟
man haadhaa? · "Who is this?"		 people
log		 هَذَا طَبِيبٌ
"This is a doctor" · yeh ek doctor hai

⚠️ مَا vs مَنْ — don't mix them

مَا (maa, ends in alif) = "what", for things & animals.
مَنْ (man, ends in nūn) = "who", for people.

Rule 4 · Ask a yes/no question with أَ

To turn any statement into a yes/no question, just put أَ ("a-") in front:

StatementQuestion
هَذَا بَيْتٌ
haadhaa baytun · This is a house		 أَهَذَا بَيْتٌ ؟
a-haadhaa baytun? · Is this a house?

Answer with:

نَعَمْ (naʿam) = YES  ·  لَا (laa) = NO

Examples:

أَهَذَا بَيْتٌ ؟Is this a house?نَعَمْ ، هَذَا بَيْتٌYes, this is a house.
أَهَذَا سَرِيرٌ ؟Is this a bed?لَا ، هَذَا كُرْسِيٌّNo, this is a chair.
أَهَذَا كَلْبٌ ؟Is this a dog?لَا ، هَذَا قِطٌّNo, this is a cat.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · مَا or مَنْ? Choose the right question word

Write مَا (for a thing) or مَنْ (for a person) before each هَذَا؟

  1. هَذَا؟ — هَذَا بَيْتٌ
  2. هَذَا؟ — هَذَا طَبِيبٌ
  3. هَذَا؟ — هَذَا جَمَلٌ
  4. هَذَا؟ — هَذَا مُدَرِّسٌ
  5. هَذَا؟ — هَذَا قَلَمٌ
  6. هَذَا؟ — هَذَا رَجُلٌ

Exercise 2 · Answer the yes/no question

Answer each with a full sentence — نَعَمْ ، هَذَا ___ or لَا ، هَذَا ___.

  1. أَهَذَا بَيْتٌ ؟ (yes) —
  2. أَهَذَا حِصَانٌ ؟ (no — it's a donkey) —
  3. أَهَذَا كَلْبٌ ؟ (no — it's a cat) —
  4. أَهَذَا قَمِيصٌ ؟ (no — it's a handkerchief) —
  5. أَهَذَا دِيكٌ ؟ (yes) —

Exercise 3 · Translate into Arabic

  1. This is a house. —
  2. This is a man. —
  3. What is this? —
  4. Who is this? This is a teacher. —
  5. Is this a key? —
  6. Yes, this is a key. —
  7. No, this is a pen. —
  8. Is this a horse? — No, this is a donkey. —

Exercise 4 · Translate into English

  1. هَذَا قَمِيصٌ —
  2. مَا هَذَا؟ هَذَا مَسْجِدٌ —
  3. أَهَذَا طَبِيبٌ ؟ —
  4. نَعَمْ ، هَذَا طَبِيبٌ —
  5. أَهَذَا جَمَلٌ ؟ لَا ، هَذَا حِصَانٌ —

Exercise 5 · Look around — make your own!

Find 5 objects near you. For each one, write a sentence in Arabic using هَذَا. If you don't know the word, try anyway — write what you remember.

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise all 24 vocab words with their meanings.
  2. Read out loud every example sentence in this handout — at least 3 times each.
  3. Do all 5 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
  4. Spot 5 things around your home and write a sentence in Arabic for each, using هَذَا.
