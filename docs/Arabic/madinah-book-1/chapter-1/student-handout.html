الدَّرْسُ الأَوَّلُ

Chapter 1 — Student Handout

My first Arabic chapter · "This is a ___"

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Point to anything in the room and say "This is a ___" in Arabic.
  2. Add the word "the" to any noun.
  3. Ask "Is this a ___?" and answer "yes" / "no".
  4. Know about 22 new Arabic words.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · هَذَا means "this is..."

When you point to something nearby (a book, a pen, a man), say هَذَا (pronounced haadhaa).

هَذَا بَيْتٌ

haadhaa baytun · "This is a house" · Yeh ek ghar hai

💡 Two important things

  1. The ا in هَذَا is silent. Say "haadhaa", not "ha-aa-dhaa".
  2. Use هَذَا only for masculine, singular things (a book, a man, a chair). We'll learn the feminine form later.

More examples — say each one aloud:

هَذَا قَلَمٌhaadhaa qalamunThis is a penYeh ek qalam hai
هَذَا كِتَابٌhaadhaa kitaabunThis is a bookYeh ek kitaab hai
هَذَا مَسْجِدٌhaadhaa masjidunThis is a mosqueYeh ek masjid hai
هَذَا وَلَدٌhaadhaa waladunThis is a boyYeh ek ladka hai

Rule 2 · Arabic has NO word for "is"

In English we say "this is a house". In Urdu we say "yeh ek ghar hai". In Arabic, the word "is/hai" is just silent — the two words sit next to each other.

⚠️ Do NOT add any extra word

Just say: هَذَا بَيْتٌ
Don't try to insert "is" or "huwa" or anything else. The silence is the "is".

This kind of sentence (two nouns side by side, no verb) is called a Jumlah Ismiyyah (جُمْلَةٌ اِسْمِيَّةٌ) — a "nominal sentence". The first noun is the Mubtada' (مُبْتَدَأٌ — what we're talking about) and the second is the Khabar (خَبَرٌ — the news about it).

Mubtada'
هَذَا
haadhaa + Khabar
بَيْتٌ
baytun = "This is a house"

Rule 3 · الـ means "the"

Attach الـ (pronounced "al-") to the front of any noun to make it specific:

Indefinite ("a ___") Definite ("the ___") Meaning
بَيْتٌالْبَيْتُa house → the house
قَلَمٌالْقَلَمُa pen → the pen
كِتَابٌالْكِتَابُa book → the book
مَسْجِدٌالْمَسْجِدُa mosque → the mosque

💡 Notice the ending

The doubled ending ـٌ (-un) drops to a single ـُ (-u) when you add الـ.

baytun → al-baytu (not al-baytun)

Rule 4 · Sun letters & Moon letters (pronunciation)

When you attach الـ, sometimes the ل is pronounced and sometimes it goes silent. It depends on the next letter.

☀️ Sun letters (the L is silent)

The L disappears. The next letter doubles.

الشَّمْسُ

ash-shams
("the sun" — NOT "al-shams")

The 14 sun letters:

ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل ن

🌙 Moon letters (the L is pronounced)

The L is heard clearly.

الْقَمَرُ

al-qamar
("the moon")

The 14 moon letters:

ا ب ج ح خ ع غ ف ق ك م هـ و ي

💡 Easy way to remember

The Sun is loud — it eats the L. The Moon is gentle — it lets the L through.

Rule 5 · Ask a question with أَ

To turn any statement into a yes/no question, just put أَ ("a-") in front:

StatementQuestion
هَذَا بَيْتٌ
haadhaa baytun · This is a house		 أَهَذَا بَيْتٌ ؟
a-haadhaa baytun? · Is this a house?

Answer with:

نَعَمْ (naʿam) = YES  ·  لَا (laa) = NO

Examples:

أَهَذَا قَلَمٌ ؟Is this a pen?نَعَمْ ، هَذَا قَلَمٌYes, this is a pen.
أَهَذَا بَابٌ ؟Is this a door?لَا ، هَذَا كِتَابٌNo, this is a book.
