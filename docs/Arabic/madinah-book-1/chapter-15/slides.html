Men carry …م · women carry the doubled …نّ (like هُنَّ).

Rule 1 · "You all" (women) = أَنْتُنَّ, "your" = ـكُنَّ

مَنْ أَنْتُنَّ ؟

who are you (all, women)?

أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُنَّ ؟

where is your house?

أَنْتُمْ → أَنْتُنَّ; tail ـكُمْ → ـكُنَّ.

Men …م, women …نّ

to mento women
بَيْتُكُمْبَيْتُكُنَّ
أَنْتُمْأَنْتُنَّ

The doubled نّ is the same sound as هُنَّ ("they-women") from last lesson.

Mard = "…m"; aurat = "…nn". (أَخ par: أَخُوكُنَّ — Ch 10 wala shape.)

In the dialogue

أَذَهَبْتُنَّ ؟ — نَعَمْ ، ذَهَبْنَا وَرَجَعْنَا

"Did you (women) go? — Yes, we went and came back."

Chhota ت = "tum"; نّ = aurton ka group; ـنَا = "hum".

Rule 3 · Time words — قَبْلَ / بَعْدَ / مَتَى

قَبْلَ أُسْبُوعٍ

a week ago

بَعْدَ شَهْرٍ

after a month

قَبْلَ/بَعْدَ work like تَحْتَ — the next word takes kasra.

Wahi purana kasra-qaida: قَبْلَ أُسْبُوعٍ (na ke أُسْبُوعٌ). مَتَى = "kab?".

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

The feminine plural — ـكُنَّ, ـتُنَّ, هُنَّ — in the Qur'an.

Aaj ke alfaaz — Qur'an mein.

Yūsuf · 12:51 — ـكُنَّ + the verb ـتُنَّ

مَا خَطْبُكُنَّ إِذْ رَاوَدتُّنَّ يُوسُفَ

"What was your affair when you sought to seduce Yūsuf?"

خَطْبُكُنَّ = "your affair" (ـكُنَّ); رَاوَدتُّنَّ = "you (women) did" (ـتُنَّ).

Al-Aḥzāb · 33:33 — ـكُنَّ ("your houses")

وَقَرْنَ فِي بُيُوتِكُنَّ

"And stay in your houses." (addressed to the wives of the Prophet ﷺ)

بُيُوتِكُنَّ = "your houses" — the tail ـكُنَّ, same as بَيْتُكُنَّ.

Al-Baqarah · 2:187 — the whole plural set

هُنَّ لِبَاسٌ لَّكُمْ وَأَنْتُمْ لِبَاسٌ لَّهُنَّ

"They are a garment for you, and you are a garment for them."

Four plural pronouns at once: هُنَّ · ـكُمْ · أَنْتُمْ · ـهُنَّ — the chart clicks shut!
