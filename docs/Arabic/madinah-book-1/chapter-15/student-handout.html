Chapter 15 — Student Handout

The women's "you all" · أَنْتُنَّ · the tail ـكُنَّ ("your") · the verb ذَهَبْتُنَّ · time words قَبْلَ/بَعْدَ · and the complete pronoun table

👋 Welcome — the lesson where the chart is finished

Last lesson we learned "you all" (أَنْتُمْ) and "we" (نَحْنُ), and only one box was left empty — the women's "you all". Today we fill it, and then we draw the complete table of "I / you / he / she / we / they". By the end you will be able to:

  1. Say "you all" to a group of women — أَنْتُنَّ — and "your" — the tail ـكُنَّ (بَيْتُكُنَّ = your house).
  2. Say "you (women) went" — ذَهَبْتُنَّ — and tell it apart from "they went" / "we went".
  3. Use the time words قَبْلَ (before), بَعْدَ (after), مَتَى (when?).
  4. Read the whole pronoun table — and greet with the full السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ.

Time needed: one class (≈60 min) + about 30 minutes of practice at home.

🧭 The one big idea — the women's "you all" (the نّ twin)

Everything today is the feminine mirror of last lesson. Men end in …م; women end in the doubled …نّ (the same نّ as هُنَّ, "they-women"):

For a group of…Pronoun "you all"Tail "your"Verb "…went"Roman Urdu
men (Ch 14)أَنْتُمْـكُمْذَهَبْتُمْmardon ko · …م
women (today)أَنْتُنَّـكُنَّذَهَبْتُنَّaurton ko · …نّ

Rule 1 · "You all" (women) = أَنْتُنَّ, and "your" = ـكُنَّ

When you speak to a group of women, use أَنْتُنَّ ("you all"), and "your" is the tail ـكُنَّ stuck on the end of a word:

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
مَنْ أَنْتُنَّ ؟who are you (all, women)?tum sab kaun ho? (auratein)
كَيْفَ حَالُكُنَّ ؟how are you (all)?tum sab kaise ho?
أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُنَّ ؟where is your house?tumhara ghar kahan hai?
أُمُّكُنَّ أُسْتَاذَتِيyour mother is my teachertumhari ammi meri ustaani hain

💡 Men …م, women …نّ

For a group of women it is أَنْتُنَّ / ـكُنَّ, not أَنْتُمْ / ـكُمْ. Mard = "…m"; aurat = "…nn". Yeh wahi "nn" hai jo هُنَّ ("woh auratein") mein hai. On "father / brother" it becomes أَبُوكُنَّ / أَخُوكُنَّ (the long و from Chapter 10).

Rule 2 · "You (women) went" = ذَهَبْتُنَّ

The past verb adds ـتُنَّ for "you all (women)". Be careful — three forms look alike:

WhoEndingExampleRoman Urdu
they (women) · L13ـْنَذَهَبْنَwoh auratein gayin
you all (women) · todayـتُنَّذَهَبْتُنَّtum auratein gayin
we · Ch 14ـنَاذَهَبْنَاhum gaye

⚠️ The little ت and the doubled نّ

ذَهَبْتُنَّ has a ت ("you") and a doubled نّ ("women"). ذَهَبْنَ ("they") has neither. ذَهَبْنَا ("we") ends in ـنَا. Farq: chhota "t" = "tum", "nn" = aurton ka group, "naa" = "hum". From the dialogue: أَذَهَبْتُنَّ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ ؟ — نَعَمْ ، ذَهَبْنَا وَرَجَعْنَا .

Rule 3 · Time words — قَبْلَ / بَعْدَ / مَتَى

قَبْلَ (before) and بَعْدَ (after) work like تَحْتَ/أَمَامَ — the next word takes kasra:

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
قَبْلَ أُسْبُوعٍa week agoek hafta pehle
بَعْدَ شَهْرٍafter a monthek maheene baad
قَبْلَ الصَّلَاةِbefore the prayernamaz se pehle
مَتَى اخْتِبَارُكُنَّ ؟when is your exam?tumhara imtihaan kab hai?

💡 Same old kasra rule

You already do this after فِي/عَلَى/تَحْتَ. Two new members join the family: قَبْلَ/بَعْدَ. Agla noun kasra leta hai: قَبْلَ أُسْبُوعٍ (na ke أُسْبُوعٌ).

🎉 The complete pronoun table — the chart clicks shut

This is the prize of the whole book so far — every "I / you / he / she / we / they", singular and plural, in one frame. Read it down each column:

personone (singular)a group (plural)Roman Urdu
he / they (m)هُوَ طَالِبٌهُمْ طُلَّابٌwoh / woh log (mard)
she / they (f)هِيَ طَالِبَةٌهُنَّ طَالِبَاتٌwoh / woh log (aurat)
you (m)أَنْتَ طَالِبٌأَنْتُمْ طُلَّابٌtu / tum sab (mard)
you (f)أَنْتِ طَالِبَةٌأَنْتُنَّ طَالِبَاتٌtu / tum sab (aurat)
I / we (m)أَنَا طَالِبٌنَحْنُ طُلَّابٌmain / hum (mard)
I / we (f)أَنَا طَالِبَةٌنَحْنُ طَالِبَاتٌmain / hum (aurat)

Patterns: the هـ words are "he/she/they" (other people); the أنت words are "you…"; أَنَا/نَحْنُ are "I/we". أَنَا and نَحْنُ are the same for men and women — that's why each shows twice.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Answer about yourselves (lesson Exercise 1) — women's "we / our"

Answer each question as a group of women (use نَحْنُ / ـنَا):

  1. مَنْ أَنْتُنَّ ؟ —
  2. أَيْنَ أُمُّكُنَّ ؟ —
  3. أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُنَّ ؟ —
  4. أَذَهَبْتُنَّ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْيَوْمَ ؟ —

Exercise 2 · Make the subject feminine (lesson Exercise 2)

Rewrite each sentence with أَنْتُنَّ ("you all", women). Example: أَأَنْتُمْ طُلَّابٌ ؟ → أَأَنْتُنَّ طَالِبَاتٌ ؟

  1. أَأَنْتُمْ مُدَرِّسُونَ ؟ →
  2. أَأَنْتُمْ مُسْلِمُونَ ؟ →
  3. أَأَنْتُمْ أَبْنَاءُ الْمُدِيرِ ؟ →

Exercise 3 · Change "men" to "women" (lesson Exercise 3) — ـكُمْ → ـكُنَّ

Rewrite, addressing women. Example: أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُمْ يَا إِخْوَانُ ؟ → أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُنَّ يَا أَخَوَاتُ ؟

  1. أَيْنَ أَخُوكُمْ يَا إِخْوَانُ ؟ →
  2. أَيْنَ مَدْرَسَتُكُمْ يَا إِخْوَانُ ؟ →
  3. مَتَى اخْتِبَارُكُمْ يَا إِخْوَانُ ؟ →

Exercise 4 · Choose the right "you" — detached pronoun (lesson Exercise 4)

Fill in with أَنْتَ / أَنْتِ / أَنْتُمْ / أَنْتُنَّ to fit the meaning. Tip: one man = أَنْتَ, one woman = أَنْتِ, men = أَنْتُمْ, women = أَنْتُنَّ.

  1. أَ مُسْلِمٌ ؟  (one man)
  2. أَ مَرِيضَةٌ ؟  (one woman)
  3. أَ طَبِيبَاتٌ ؟  (women)
  4. أَ طُلَّابٌ ؟  (men)

Exercise 5 · Choose the right tail — attached pronoun (lesson Exercise 5)

Add the right tail: ـكَ / ـكِ / ـكُمْ / ـكُنَّ, matching who is addressed.

  1. أَيْنَ بَيْتُ يَا إِخْوَانُ ؟  (men)
  2. أَهَذَا كِتَابُ يَا حَامِدُ ؟  (one man)
  3. مَا اسْمُ يَا أُخْتِي ؟  (one woman)
  4. مَنْ أَبُو يَا أَخَوَاتُ ؟  (women)

Exercise 6 · Read and write (lesson Exercise 7) — the time words

Copy each sentence, then translate it:

  1. ذَهَبَ أَبِي إِلَى الْقَاهِرَةِ قَبْلَ أُسْبُوعٍ . —
  2. ذَهَبْتُ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ قَبْلَ الْأَذَانِ . —
  3. أَقَبْلَ الصَّلَاةِ ذَهَبْتَ إِلَى الْمَطْعَمِ ؟ — لَا ، ذَهَبْتُ بَعْدَ الصَّلَاةِ . —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the complete pronoun table — be able to say all twelve forms (six singular, six plural) from memory.
  2. Write 4 things with ـكُمْ (to men) and the same 4 with ـكُنَّ (to women) — e.g. بَيْتُكُمْ / بَيْتُكُنَّ.
  3. Write the three look-alikes ذَهَبْنَ / ذَهَبْتُنَّ / ذَهَبْنَا and say which is "they / you / we".
  4. Write 3 sentences using قَبْلَ or بَعْدَ — remember the kasra on the next word.
  5. Practise the full greeting السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ with a partner.
  6. Complete all 6 exercises above.
