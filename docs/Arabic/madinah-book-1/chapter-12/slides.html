By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Greet a woman: السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكِ · ask كَيْفَ حَالُكِ ؟
  2. Say "your" to a woman — ـكِ (اسْمُكِ).
  3. Call someone — يَا مُحَمَّدُ, يَا أُخْتِي.
  4. Say "she went" — ذَهَبَتْ (add ـتْ).
  5. Describe with a clause — الَّذِي / الَّتِي.

📋 One idea ties it together: gender-matching

  • You already match gender: هُوَ/هِيَ (Ch 5), هَذَا/هَذِهِ (Ch 6/7)
  • Today the same reflex appears in 3 new places:
  • ـكَ/ـكِ (you) · خَرَجَ/خَرَجَتْ (verb) · الَّذِي/الَّتِي (who)
  • …plus one tiny new word: يَا ("O…")

4 "new" things = 1 old instinct + يَا.

Rule 1 · "Your" to a woman = ـكِ

ـكَ — to a man

اسْمُكَ

fatḥa (zabar)

ـكِ — to a woman

اسْمُكِ

kasra (zer)

Same "your" — only the vowel on ك changes.

The greeting — now real speech

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكِ وَرَحْمَةُ اللهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ

"Peace be upon you, and the mercy of Allah, and His blessings." (to a woman)

كَيْفَ حَالُكِ ؟ — أَنَا بِخَيْرٍ ، وَالْحَمْدُ لِلهِ

"Tum kaisi ho? — Main theek hoon, alhamdulillah."

To a man: عَلَيْكَ / حَالُكَ. (Plural عَلَيْكُمْ — later.)

Rule 2 · يَا — calling someone

يَا مُحَمَّدُ

"O Muhammad" — put يَا before a name to call someone.

Pukaarne ka lafz — jaise "ai / o".

يَا + "my" (family)

يَا أَبِييَا أُمِّي
يَا أُخْتِييَا خَالَتِي

A called name takes one ـُ: ✅ يَا مُحَمَّدُ · ❌ يَا مُحَمَّدٌ

Rule 3 · "She did…" — add ـتْ

male doer

خَرَجَ مُحَمَّدٌ

bare verb

female doer

خَرَجَتْ آمِنَةُ

add ـتْ

The ـتْ follows the doer's gender.

In the dialogue + the الـ smoothing

ذَهَبَتْ إِلَى الْمُسْتَشْفَى

"She went to the hospital."

جَلَسَتْ → before الـ → جَلَسَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ

ـتْ + الـ → zer. Wahi مِنَ الـ wali smoothing.

Rule 4 · الَّذِي / الَّتِي — "who / which"

one wordالْفَتَاةُ الْجَمِيلَةُthe beautiful girl
a clauseالْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي مَعَكِthe girl who is with you

Describe with a whole sentence, not just one word.

Choose by gender

masc noun → الَّذِي

الطِّفْلُ الَّذِي مَعَكِ

fem noun → الَّتِي

السَّيَّارَةُ الَّتِي خَرَجَتْ

Only after a الـ noun: ✅ الْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي…. Urdu "jo" works anywhere — Arabic needs the الـ first.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

الَّذِي/الَّتِي, يَا, and ـكِ are everywhere.

Aaj ke lafz — Qur'an mein.

Surah Al-ʿAlaq · 96:1 — الَّذِي

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ

"Read in the name of your Lord who created."

الَّذِي = "who" — today's word — and رَبِّكَ with the "your" tail ـكَ. The first revelation.

Surah Al-Baqarah · 2:24 — الَّتِي

فَاتَّقُوا النَّارَ الَّتِي وَقُودُهَا النَّاسُ وَالْحِجَارَةُ

"So fear the Fire whose fuel is men and stones."

الَّتِي describing النَّار (feminine) — a whole clause on one noun.

Surah Al-Fajr · 89:27–28 — يَا + ـكِ

يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ

"O reassured soul, return to your Lord."

The calling-word يَا and the feminine "your" ـكِ (رَبِّكِ) — both today's. (ارْجِعِي = a feminine command; just recognise it.)

📋 Recap — what we covered

  1. ـكِ = "your" to a woman (ـكَ to a man) + greetings
  2. يَا = "O…" — call someone (يَا أُخْتِي)
  3. ـتْ = female doer (ذَهَبَتْ); before الـ → ـتِ
  4. الَّذِي (m) / الَّتِي (f) — "who/which", only after a الـ noun
