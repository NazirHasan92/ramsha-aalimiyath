الدَّرْسُ الثَّانِيَ عَشَرَ

Chapter 12 — Student Handout

Greetings & the feminine ـكِ · the calling-word يَا · feminine verb ذَهَبَتْ · الَّذِي / الَّتِي ("who / which")

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

Today Arabic starts to feel like a conversation. We read a dialogue between two girls and learn four new tools. By the end you will be able to:

  1. Greet a female with السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكِ and ask كَيْفَ حَالُكِ ؟ ("How are you?").
  2. Say "your" to a woman: ـكِ (اسْمُكِ = "your name") — and to a man: ـكَ (اسْمُكَ).
  3. Call someone with يَا: يَا مُحَمَّدُ, يَا أُخْتِي.
  4. Say "she went / went out / sat": add ـتْ for a female doer (ذَهَبَتْ, خَرَجَتْ, جَلَسَتْ).
  5. Describe with a whole sentence using الَّذِي (m) / الَّتِي (f) — "the one who / which".

Time needed: a full lesson — about 60 minutes of class (your teacher may split it into two) + 25 minutes of practice at home.

📖 The dialogue — two girls meet

Read it aloud. Notice every "your" is ـكِ (because Suʿād is talking to a girl).

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكِ وَرَحْمَةُ اللهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ . — وَعَلَيْكِ السَّلَامُ وَرَحْمَةُ اللهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ .
كَيْفَ حَالُكِ يَا بِنْتُ ؟ — أَنَا بِخَيْرٍ ، وَالْحَمْدُ لِلهِ .
مِنْ أَيْنَ أَنْتِ ؟ — أَنَا مِنَ الرِّيَاضِ . مَا اسْمُكِ ؟ — اسْمِي آمِنَةُ .
أَيْنَ أَبُوكِ ؟ — أَبِي هُنَا فِي الْمَدِينَةِ الْمُنَوَّرَةِ .
وَمَنْ هَذِهِ الْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي مَعَكِ ؟ أَهِيَ أُخْتُكِ ؟ — لَا . هِيَ بِنْتُ عَمِّي .
وَمَنْ هَذَا الطِّفْلُ الَّذِي مَعَكِ ؟ — هُوَ ابْنُ أَخِي . اسْمُهُ سَعْدٌ .
أَأُمُّكِ فِي الْبَيْتِ الْآنَ ؟ — لَا . ذَهَبَتْ إِلَى الْمُسْتَشْفَى .

"Peace be upon you… — And upon you peace… How are you, O girl? — I am well, praise be to Allah. Where are you from? — I am from Riyadh. What is your name? — My name is Āminah. Where is your father? — My father is here in Madinah. And who is this girl who is with you? Is she your sister? — No, she is my (paternal) uncle's daughter. And who is this child who is with you? — He is my brother's son; his name is Saʿd. Is your mother at home now? — No, she went to the hospital."

Rule 1 · "Your" to a woman = ـكِ

You already know ـكَ ("your", to a man). To a woman, the same tail has a kasra (zer): ـكِ.

To a man (ـكَ)To a woman (ـكِ)Means
اسْمُكَاسْمُكِyour name
حَالُكَحَالُكِyour condition (how you are)
أَبُوكَأَبُوكِyour father
عَلَيْكَعَلَيْكِupon you

💡 The greeting

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكِ (to a woman) / عَلَيْكَ (to a man). كَيْفَ حَالُكِ ؟ = "How are you?" — answer أَنَا بِخَيْرٍ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلهِ ("I am well, praise be to Allah"). "Tum kaisi ho? — Main theek hoon, alhamdulillah."

Rule 2 · يَا — the word for calling someone

To call or address someone, put يَا ("O…") before the name:

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
يَا مُحَمَّدُ · يَا فَاطِمَةُO Muhammad · O Fatimaai Muhammad · ai Fatima
يَا أَبِي · يَا أُمِّيO my father · O my motherai mere abbu · ai meri ammi
يَا أُخْتِي · يَا خَالَتِي · يَا عَمَّتِيO my sister · O my (maternal) aunt · O my (paternal) auntai meri behan · ai meri khala · ai meri phuphi

⚠️ One ḍamma on the name — no tanween

A called name takes a single ـُ: يَا مُحَمَّدُ ✓, not يَا مُحَمَّدٌ ✗. For family, attach "my": يَا أَخِي, يَا أُخْتِي.

Rule 3 · "She did…" — add ـتْ for a female doer

When the doer is a woman, a past verb adds a quiet ـتْ at the end:

Male doerFemale doer (ـتْ)Means (English · Urdu)
خَرَجَ مُحَمَّدٌخَرَجَتْ آمِنَةُ… went out · bahar gaya/gayi
ذَهَبَ أَبِيذَهَبَتْ أُمِّي… went · gaya/gayi
جَلَسَ الطَّالِبُجَلَسَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ… sat · baitha/baithi

💡 ـتْ becomes ـتِ before الـ

On its own: جَلَسَتْ. But before a word with الـ, the ending reads with a kasra: جَلَسَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ ("the girl sat"). Just read it smoothly — like مِنَ الـ.

Rule 4 · الَّذِي / الَّتِي — "the one who / which"

These let you describe a noun with a whole sentence, not just one word:

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
الْفَتَاةُ الْجَمِيلَةُthe beautiful girl (one word)khoobsurat larki
الْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي مَعَكِthe girl who is with you (a clause)woh larki jo tere saath hai

Choose by the noun's gender — just like هَذَا/هَذِهِ (mard ism → الَّذِي; aurat ism → الَّتِي):

After a…useExample (· Urdu)
masculine nounالَّذِيالطِّفْلُ الَّذِي مَعَكِ · woh bacha jo tere saath hai
feminine nounالَّتِيالسَّيَّارَةُ الَّتِي خَرَجَتْ · woh gaari jo nikli

⚠️ Only after a "the" (الـ) noun

Arabic uses الَّذِي/الَّتِي only after a definite noun: الْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي… ✓. (Urdu "jo" works anywhere, but Arabic needs the الـ first.)

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Read & complete the greeting (lesson Exercise 1)

Fill the gap with the right "you"/calling form.

  1. كَيْفَ حَالُ يَا أَبِي ؟ (to your father)
  2. كَيْفَ حَالُ يَا أُمِّي ؟ (to your mother)
  3. أَيْنَ ابْنُ يَا زَيْنَبُ ؟ ذَهَبَ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ .
  4. أَيْنَ بِنْتُ يَا آمِنَةُ ؟ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ . (she went)

Exercise 2 · Re-address it to a female (lesson Exercise 2)

Change the person being called from male to female — and flip every ـكَ to ـكِ. Example: أَيْنَ قَلَمُكَ يَا أَبِي ؟ → أَيْنَ قَلَمُكِ يَا أُمِّي ؟

  1. أَعِنْدَكَ قَلَمٌ يَا مُحَمَّدُ ؟ → يَا آمِنَةُ ؟
  2. مِنْ أَيْنَ أَنْتَ يَا أَخِي ؟ → يَا أُخْتِي ؟
  3. أَيْنَ أَبُوكَ يَا خَالِدُ ؟ → يَا خَدِيجَةُ ؟
  4. أَلَكَ أَخٌ يَا حَامِدُ ؟ → يَا سُعَادُ ؟

Exercise 3 · Make the doer female (lesson Exercise 3 · أَنِّثْ الْفَاعِل)

Rewrite with a female doer, adding ـتْ to the verb. Example: خَرَجَ مُحَمَّدٌ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ → خَرَجَتْ آمِنَةُ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ.

  1. ذَهَبَ الْمُدَرِّسُ إِلَى الْفَصْلِ . →
  2. جَلَسَ الطَّالِبُ فِي الْفَصْلِ . →
  3. خَرَجَ أَخِي مِنَ الْبَيْتِ . →

Exercise 4 · Read aloud — الَّذِي / الَّتِي (lesson Exercise 5)

Read each sentence, then underline the relative pronoun and say if its noun is masculine or feminine.

  1. مَنِ الْفَتَى الَّذِي خَرَجَ مِنْ بَيْتِكَ الْآنَ ؟ — هُوَ ابْنُ عَمِّي .
  2. مَنِ الْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي خَرَجَتْ مِنْ بَيْتِكَ يَا مُحَمَّدُ ؟ — هِيَ بِنْتُ خَالَتِي .
  3. لِمَنِ الْمِفْتَاحُ الَّذِي عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ ؟ — هُوَ لِلْمُدَرِّسِ .
  4. لِمَنِ السَّاعَةُ الَّتِي عَلَى السَّرِيرِ ؟ — هِيَ لِزَوْجِ أُخْتِي .

Exercise 5 · Fill in الَّذِي or الَّتِي (lesson Exercise 6)

Put the right relative pronoun in the gap — match the noun's gender.

  1. الْكِتَابُ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ لِلْمُدَرِّسِ .
  2. السَّيَّارَةُ خَرَجَتْ مِنَ الْمُسْتَشْفَى لِلطَّبِيبِ .
  3. الْفَتَى خَرَجَ مِنَ الْمَسْجِدِ ابْنُ الْمُؤَذِّنِ .
  4. الْحَقِيبَةُ تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ لِخَالِدٍ .

Exercise 6 · Translate both ways

(a) Into Arabic:

  1. How are you, O my sister? — I am well, praise be to Allah. —
  2. Āminah went out of the school. —

(b) Into English:

  1. مَنْ هَذِهِ الْفَتَاةُ الَّتِي مَعَكِ ؟ —
  2. أَيْنَ أُمُّكِ ؟ — ذَهَبَتْ إِلَى مُسْتَشْفَى الْوِلَادَةِ . —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Read the dialogue aloud three times — until it flows. Notice every ـكِ.
  2. Write the greeting both ways — to a male (عَلَيْكَ / حَالُكَ) and to a female (عَلَيْكِ / حَالُكِ).
  3. Write 4 "calling" sentences with يَا (e.g. كَيْفَ حَالُكِ يَا خَالَتِي ؟).
  4. Write 3 sentences with a female doer using خَرَجَتْ / ذَهَبَتْ / جَلَسَتْ.
  5. Write 2 sentences with الَّذِي and 2 with الَّتِي (e.g. هَذَا الطِّفْلُ الَّذِي مَعِي).
  6. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
