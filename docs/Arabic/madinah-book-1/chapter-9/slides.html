📋 Quick recap

  • Name things: هَذَا بَيْتٌ (Ch 1)
  • Add الـ = a phrase that needs finishing: هَذَا الْبَيْتُ … (Ch 8)
  • Feminine agreement: هِيَ طَبِيبَةٌ (Ch 6)
  • Pronouns: هُوَ · هِيَ (Ch 5)

Today: we learn to describe — and the الـ idea from Ch 8 returns.

Rule 1 · النَّعْت — adjective after the noun

بَيْتٌ جَدِيدٌ

literally "a house — new" = "a new house"

English/Urdu: "naya ghar". Arabic ULTA: pehle ism, phir sifat.

The adjective copies the noun — 3 ways

فَاكِهَةٌ لَذِيذَةٌgender (both fem — ة)
الْمَدِينَةُ الْكَبِيرَةُdefiniteness (both الـ)
فِي الْغُرْفَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِending (both kasra)

The book's diagram: بَيْتٌ = مَنْعُوت · جَدِيدٌ = نَعْت (p. 53)

⚠️ After the noun, and matching gender

✅ بَيْتٌ جَدِيدٌ · ❌ جَدِيدٌ بَيْتٌ

✅ لُغَةٌ سَهْلَةٌ · ❌ لُغَةٌ سَهْلٌ

Sifat ism ke peeche, aur uske kapde pehnti hai.

Rule 2 · The big one — الـ changes everything

only noun has الـ

الْمُدَرِّسُ جَدِيدٌ

"The teacher IS new." — SENTENCE

both have الـ

الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ

"the new teacher" — PHRASE ⏳

Matched الـ = description · mismatched الـ = sentence.

Finish the phrase (like Chapter 8)

الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ فِي الْفَصْلِ

"The new teacher is in the classroom." ✓

Same "open switch" as هَذَا الْبَيْتُ: الـ on both → a phrase, still open → add a predicate.

Dono par الـ = phrase; poora karne ke liye khabar lagao.

Rule 3 · أَنَا (I) & أَنْتَ (you)

أَنَاI (m/f) · main
أَنْتَyou, m · tum / aap

After هُوَ/هِيَ (Ch 5) — now the people talking.

In a nominal sentence

مَنْ أَنْتَ ؟ — أَنَا طَالِبٌWho are you? — I'm a student
أَنْتَ طَالِبٌ جَدِيدٌ ؟ — نَعَمْAre you a new student? — Yes

No word for "am/are" — أَنَا طَالِبٌ = "I am a student". ("hoon" ka koi lafz nahin.)

Rule 4 · عِنْدَ & الَّذِي

عِنْدَ = "at / with" — next noun takes kasra

هُوَ عِنْدَ الْمُدِيرِ

"He is at the director's."

Joins the family: تَحْتَ · أَمَامَ · خَلْفَ · عِنْدَ

الَّذِي = "who / which" (recognise only)

الرَّجُلُ الطَّوِيلُ الَّذِي خَرَجَ

"the tall man who left"

Like a "super-adjective": describes with a whole clause. Just recognise it — building these is Lesson 12.

الَّذِي = "jo/jis" — sirf pehchaan, banana baad mein.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

The adjective (النَّعْت) is everywhere.

Ek definite, ek indefinite — dono aap parh sakte ho.

Surah Al-Fātiḥah · 1:6 (definite النَّعْت)

اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

"Guide us to the straight path."

الصِّرَاطَ (the path) + الْمُسْتَقِيمَ (the straight) — both have الـ → a description. You recite it 17× a day!

Surah Yūsuf · 12:2 (indefinite النَّعْت)

إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا عَرَبِيًّا

"Indeed We sent it down as an Arabic Qur'an."

قُرْآنًا + عَرَبِيًّا — dono par الـ nahin → "an Arabic Qur'an". Lesson ka الْعَرَبِيَّةُ لُغَةٌ سَهْلَةٌ yaad aaya?

  3. أَنَا / أَنْتَ — I / you (no word for "am/are")
  4. عِنْدَ (at/with, + kasra) · الَّذِي (who/which — recognise)
