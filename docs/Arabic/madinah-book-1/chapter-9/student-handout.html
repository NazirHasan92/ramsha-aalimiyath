الدَّرْسُ التَّاسِعُ

Chapter 9 — Student Handout

النَّعْت (the adjective) · أَنَا / أَنْتَ · عِنْدَ · الَّذِي — describing things

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Describe a noun with an adjective (النَّعْت) — placed after the noun: بَيْتٌ جَدِيدٌ (a new house).
  2. Make the adjective agree in gender, الـ, and ending.
  3. Tell apart الْمُدَرِّسُ جَدِيدٌ ("the teacher is new") and الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ ("the new teacher").
  4. Say "I" (أَنَا) and "you" (أَنْتَ), and use عِنْدَ ("at someone's place").
  5. Recognise الَّذِي ("who/which") when you read it.

Time needed: this is a big lesson — about 75–90 minutes of class (or two shorter classes) + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · النَّعْت — the adjective comes after the noun

In English/Urdu the adjective comes first ("a new house" / "naya ghar"). In Arabic it comes after:

بَيْتٌ جَدِيدٌ  =  "a house — new"  =  "a new house"

The adjective (النَّعْت) copies its noun (المَنْعُوت) in three ways:

AgreementExampleMeaning
Genderفَاكِهَةٌ لَذِيذَةٌa delicious fruit (both feminine — ة)
Definiteness (الـ)الْمَدِينَةُ الْكَبِيرَةُthe big city (both have الـ)
Endingفِي الْغُرْفَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِin the big room (both take kasra)

⚠️ Adjective goes AFTER — and matches gender

✅ بَيْتٌ جَدِيدٌ  ·  ❌ جَدِيدٌ بَيْتٌ. And a feminine noun needs a feminine adjective: ✅ لُغَةٌ سَهْلَةٌ  ·  ❌ لُغَةٌ سَهْلٌ. "Sifat ism ke peeche, aur uske kapde pehnti hai."

Rule 2 · الْمُدَرِّسُ جَدِيدٌ vs الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ (the big one)

The little الـ on the adjective changes everything:

الْمُدَرِّسُ جَدِيدٌ"The teacher is new." → a SENTENCE (only the noun has الـ)
الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ"the new teacher" → just a PHRASE (both have الـ) — needs finishing!
الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ فِي الْفَصْلِ"The new teacher is in the classroom." → complete again ✓

💡 Remember Chapter 8's "open switch"

This is the same idea as هَذَا الْبَيْتُ. Matched الـ (both have it) = a description, a phrase — still open. Mismatched الـ (only the noun has it) = a sentence ("the teacher is new"). "Dono par الـ = sifat/phrase; sirf ek par الـ = jumla."

Rule 3 · أَنَا (I) & أَنْتَ (you)

After هُوَ / هِيَ (he/she, Ch 5), here are the people talking:

أَنَاI (man or woman) — main
أَنْتَyou (to a male) — tum / aap
مَنْ أَنْتَ ؟أَنَا طَالِبٌWho are you? — I am a student.
أَنْتَ طَالِبٌ جَدِيدٌ ؟نَعَمْ، أَنَا طَالِبٌ جَدِيدٌAre you a new student? — Yes, I am a new student.

⚠️ No word for "am/are"

أَنَا طَالِبٌ = "I am a student" — there's no separate word for "am". Just like هُوَ طَالِبٌ and هَذَا بَيْتٌ.

Rule 4 · عِنْدَ (at / with) & الَّذِي (who / which)

عِنْدَ = "at someone's place / with". It joins the location family — the next word takes kasra:

أَيْنَ الْمُدَرِّسُ ؟ — هُوَ عِنْدَ الْمُدِيرِWhere is the teacher? — He is at the director's.

الَّذِي = "who / which". It begins a describing phrase. Just recognise it for now — you'll learn to build these in a later lesson.

الْكِتَابُ الَّذِي عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ لِلْمُدَرِّسِThe book which is on the desk is the teacher's.
الرَّجُلُ الطَّوِيلُ الَّذِي خَرَجَ مِنَ الْمَدْرَسَةِthe tall man who left the school

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Build the phrase (adjective after the noun)

Write the noun then the adjective, agreeing in gender. Example: house + new → بَيْتٌ جَدِيدٌ.

  1. a small bird (طَائِر + صَغِير) →
  2. a delicious fruit (فَاكِهَة + لَذِيذ) →
  3. a rich merchant (تَاجِر + غَنِيّ) →
  4. an easy language (لُغَة + سَهْل) →

Exercise 2 · Sentence or phrase?

Write S (a complete sentence) or P (just a phrase). Then translate.

  1. الْمُدَرِّسُ جَدِيدٌ —
  2. الْمُدَرِّسُ الْجَدِيدُ —
  3. الْمَدِينَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ —
  4. السَّيَّارَةُ الْجَمِيلَةُ —

Exercise 3 · Fill in the adjective (النَّعْت)

Put a suitable adjective in the blank, agreeing with the noun (lesson Exercise 2).

  1. خَدِيجَةُ طَالِبَةٌ
  2. الْعَرَبِيَّةُ لُغَةٌ
  3. الْعُصْفُورُ طَائِرٌ
  4. الْقَاهِرَةُ مَدِينَةٌ

Exercise 4 · Fill in the noun (المَنْعُوت)

Put a suitable noun in the blank so the adjective describes it (lesson Exercise 3).

  1. الْعَرَبِيَّةُ سَهْلَةٌ
  2. عَمَّارٌ غَنِيٌّ
  3. هَذَا مَكْسُورٌ
  4. فَيْصَلٌ كَسْلَانُ

Exercise 5 · أَنَا / أَنْتَ / عِنْدَ

Complete each sentence.

  1. Who are you? — مَنْ ؟   I am a student. —
  2. Where is the new teacher? — He is at the director's. — هُوَ
  3. Add the ending after عِنْدَ: الْكِتَابُ عِنْدَ (الْمُدَرِّس)

Exercise 6 · Translate both ways

(a) Into Arabic:

  1. The new teacher is in the classroom. —
  2. Arabic is an easy language. —
  3. I am a hardworking student. —

(b) Into English:

  1. عَبَّاسٌ تَاجِرٌ غَنِيٌّ —
  2. الْكُرْسِيُّ الْمَكْسُورُ هُنَاكَ —
  3. الطَّالِبُ الَّذِي خَرَجَ مِنَ الْفَصْلِ مِنَ الْهِنْدِ —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Build 6 noun + adjective phrases (3 masculine, 3 feminine) — remember the adjective goes after and copies the gender.
  2. Write 4 minimal pairs: الْـ…ُ …ٌ (sentence) vs الْـ…ُ الْـ…ُ (phrase). Mark which is which.
  3. Write 4 sentences about yourself with أَنَا (e.g. أَنَا طَالِبٌ مُجْتَهِدٌ).
  4. Use عِنْدَ in 3 sentences (mind the kasra).
  5. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
