الدَّرْسُ الْعَاشِرُ

Chapter 10 — Student Handout

Attached pronouns (اسْمِي · اسْمُكَ · اسْمُهُ · اسْمُهَا) · أَبُوكَ/أَخُوكَ · "to have" (عِنْدَ / لِـ) · مَعَ

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Say "my / your / his / her" by gluing a tail onto a noun: اسْمِي (my name), اسْمُكَ (your name), اسْمُهُ (his name), اسْمُهَا (her name).
  2. Remember a tailed noun is definite — so no الـ and no tanween.
  3. Say "your father / your brother": أَبُوكَ / أَخُوكَ (but "my" = أَبِي / أَخِي).
  4. Say "I have" two ways: عِنْدِي for things, لِي for people.
  5. Say "with me / with you": مَعِي / مَعَكَ.

Time needed: this is a big lesson — about 75–90 minutes of class (or two shorter classes) + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · The four tails — ـِي / ـكَ / ـهُ / ـها

To say "my / your / his / her", glue a little tail onto the end of the noun:

TailMeansExample with اسْم (name)
ـِيmy (mera)اسْمِي — my name
ـكَyour, to a male (tera/aapka)اسْمُكَ — your name
ـهُhis (uska)اسْمُهُ — his name
ـهاher (uski)اسْمُهَا — her name

⚠️ A tail = "the" already — no الـ, no tanween

"My name" is already a specific name. So never add الـ or tanween: ✅ بَيْتِي · ❌ الْبَيْتِي. And ـهُ = his, ـها = her — the tail follows the owner's gender: Hamza → اسْمُهُ, Zaynab → اسْمُهَا.

Rule 2 · أَبُوكَ / أَخُوكَ — the long و

Most nouns just take the tail (بَيْتُكَ). But "father" and "brother" grow a long و first:

أَبِي · أَبُوكَ · أَبُوهُ · أَبُوهَاmy / your / his / her father
أَخِي · أَخُوكَ · أَخُوهُ · أَخُوهَاmy / your / his / her brother

💡 Only "my" is different

"My father / my brother" drop the و: أَبِي, أَخِي. Everything else keeps it: أَبُوكَ, أَخُوهُ. "Sirf 'mera' alag hai — baaki sab mein lamba و."

Rule 3 · "I have" — عِنْدِي (things) & لِي (people)

Arabic has no verb "to have". It uses two words + a tail — and they split the job:

Things (car, pen, book)عِنْدِي · عِنْدَكَ · عِنْدَهُعِنْدِي سَيَّارَةٌ — I have a car
People (brother, child)لِي · لَكَ · لَهُ · لَهالِي أَخٌ — I have a brother

⚠️ Never عِنْدِي أَخٌ

A brother is not a possession! The book's own rule (p. 59): say لِي أَخٌ ✓ and عِنْدِي كِتَابٌ ✓ — but not عِنْدِي أَخٌ ✗. Cheez ke liye عِنْدَ, rishte ke liye لِـ.

Rule 4 · مَعَ — "with"

مَعَ = "with". It takes the same four tails, or sits before a visible noun:

مَعِي · مَعَكَ · مَعَهُ · مَعَهاwith me / you / him / her
مَنْ مَعَكَ ؟ — مَعِي زَمِيلٌWho is with you? — A colleague is with me.
خَرَجَ حَامِدٌ مَعَ خَالِدٍHamid went out with Khalid.

⚠️ مَعَهُ, not مَعَ هُوَ

For "with him", the pronoun attaches as a tail: مَعَهُ ✓ — don't say مَعَ هُوَ ✗.

  1. هَذِهِ الْبِنْتُ طَالِبَةٌ . اسْمُ زَيْنَبُ .

Exercise 4 · Father & brother (lesson Exercise 8)

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Pick 4 of your own things (pen, book, bag, house) and write each with all four tails (…ِي / …ُكَ / …ُهُ / …ُهَا).
  2. Write about your family in 4 sentences using لِي (e.g. لِي أَخٌ وَاحِدٌ, لِي أُخْتٌ).
  3. Write 3 "have a thing" sentences with عِنْدِي — and check none of them should be لِـ!
  4. Practise the father/brother grid out loud: abī · abūka · abūhu · abūhā (and أَخِي…).
  5. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
