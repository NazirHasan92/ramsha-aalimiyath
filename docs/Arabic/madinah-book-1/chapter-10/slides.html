By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Say "my / your / his / her" with a tail: اسْمِي · اسْمُكَ · اسْمُهُ · اسْمُهَا.
    dum lagao — mera/tera/uska/uski
  2. Say "your father / your brother": أَبُوكَ · أَخُوكَ.
    lamba و — lekin 'mera' = أَبِي
  3. Say "I have" two ways: عِنْدِي (things) / لِي (people).
    cheez vs rishta
  4. Say "with me / with you": مَعِي · مَعَكَ.
    ke saath

📋 Quick recap

  • Detached pronouns: هُوَ · هِيَ (Ch 5), أَنَا · أَنْتَ (Ch 9)
  • Iḍāfa — owner after the thing: بَيْتُ الْمُدَرِّسِ (Ch 4)
  • لِـ = for / belonging to (Ch 4) · عِنْدَ = at / with (Ch 9)

Today: tiny tails for "my / your / his / her" — and how to say "I have".

Rule 1 · The four tails

ـِيmy · mera · اسْمِي
ـكَyour (m) · tera · اسْمُكَ
ـهُhis · uska · اسْمُهُ
ـهاher · uski · اسْمُهَا

A tail glues onto the end of the noun.

A tail = "the" already

بَيْتِي · بَيْتُكَ · بَيْتُهُ · بَيْتُهَا

No الـ, no tanween: ✅ بَيْتِي · ❌ الْبَيْتِي

Dum khud "the" ka kaam karti hai. ـهُ=uska (mard), ـها=uski (aurat) — maalik ke gender se.

Owner's gender picks the tail

اسْمُهُ حَمْزَةُhis name is Hamza (a man → ـهُ)
اسْمُهَا زَيْنَبُher name is Zaynab (a woman → ـها)

Urdu ka "uska" dono ke liye — Arabic mein nahin!

Rule 2 · أَبُوكَ / أَخُوكَ — the long و

fatherأَبِي · أَبُوكَ · أَبُوهُ · أَبُوهَا
brotherأَخِي · أَخُوكَ · أَخُوهُ · أَخُوهَا

Most nouns just take the tail (بَيْتُكَ) — but أَب/أَخ grow a و.

Only "my" drops the و

أَبِي · أَخِي

"my father / my brother" = أَبِي / أَخِي (no و). Everything else keeps it: أَبُوكَ, أَخُوهُ.

Chant: abī · abūka · abūhu · abūhā — sirf pehla alag.

Rule 3 · "I have" — two ways

THINGS · عِنْدَ

عِنْدِي سَيَّارَةٌ

"I have a car"

عِنْدِي · عِنْدَكَ · عِنْدَهُ

PEOPLE · لِـ

لِي أَخٌ

"I have a brother"

لِي · لَكَ · لَهُ · لَها

No verb "to have" in Arabic — use عِنْدَ or لِـ + a tail.

⚠️ Never عِنْدِي أَخٌ

✅ لِي أَخٌ · ✅ عِنْدِي كِتَابٌ · ❌ عِنْدِي أَخٌ

The book's own rule (p. 59): a relative is not a possession.

Cheez ke liye عِنْدَ, rishte ke liye لِـ.

Rule 4 · مَعَ — "with"

مَعِي · مَعَكَ · مَعَهُ · مَعَها

with me / with you / with him / with her

Same four tails — on مَعَ now.

In a sentence

مَنْ مَعَكَ ؟ — مَعِي زَمِيلٌWho is with you? — A colleague is with me
خَرَجَ حَامِدٌ مَعَ خَالِدٍHamid went out with Khalid

"with him" = مَعَهُ ✓ — not مَعَ هُوَ ✗ (dum chipakti hai)

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

The attached tails are everywhere.

Har dum jo aaj seekhi — Qur'an mein milegi.

Surah Al-ʿAlaq · 96:1 — the tail ـكَ ("your")

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ

"Read in the name of your Lord who created."

رَبِّكَ = رَبّ + ـكَ ("your"). The very first revealed words!

Surah Aṣ-Ṣaff · 61:6 — the tail ـهُ ("his")

… اسْمُهُ أَحْمَدُ

"… whose name is Ahmad" ﷺ.

اسْمُهُ = "his name" — bilkul lesson ke اسْمُهُ حَمْزَةُ jaisa.

Surah Ṭā-Hā · 20:25 — ـِي ("my") & لِي

رَبِّ اشْرَحْ لِي صَدْرِي

"My Lord, expand for me my chest." (Mūsā's duʿā ﷺ)

صَدْرِي = "my chest" (ـِي) · لِي = "for me" — today's tails exactly.

📋 Recap — what we covered

  1. The four tails: ـِي · ـكَ · ـهُ · ـها (my/your/his/her) — no الـ, no tanween
  2. أَبُوكَ / أَخُوكَ grow a و — but "my" = أَبِي / أَخِي
  3. "I have": عِنْدِي (things) vs لِي (people) — never عِنْدِي أَخٌ
  4. مَعَ + tail — مَعِي · مَعَكَ · مَعَهُ
