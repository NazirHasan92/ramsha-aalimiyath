الدَّرْسُ الرَّابِعَ عَشَرَ

Chapter 14 — Student Handout

"You all" & "we" · أَنْتُمْ / نَحْنُ · the tails ـكُمْ ("your") / ـنَا ("our") · ذَهَبْتُمْ / ذَهَبْنَا ("you all / we went")

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

Last lesson we learned to talk about a group — "they / their" (هُمْ / هُنَّ). Today we learn to talk to a group — "you all" (أَنْتُمْ) — and to speak as a group — "we" (نَحْنُ). By the end you will be able to:

  1. Say "you all" — أَنْتُمْ — and "your" — the tail ـكُمْ (بَيْتُكُمْ = your house). This is the ـكُمْ of السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ!
  2. Say "we" — نَحْنُ — and "our" — the tail ـنَا (بَيْتُنَا = our house).
  3. Say "you all went" — ذَهَبْتُمْ — and "we went" — ذَهَبْنَا.
  4. Recite the beautiful sentence نَحْنُ مُسْلِمُونَ . اللهُ رَبُّنَا … ("We are Muslims. Allah is our Lord…").

Time needed: one class (≈60 min) + about 30 minutes of practice at home.

🧭 The one big idea — two new "people"

Each new "person" comes as a little family of three: a pronoun, a tail ("your / our"), and a verb ending ("…went"). Notice the rhyme — "you all" carries …م, "we" carries …نا:

FamilyPronounTail "your / our"Verb "…went"Roman Urdu
"you all"أَنْتُمْـكُمْذَهَبْتُمْtum sab · tumhara · tum sab gaye
"we"نَحْنُـنَاذَهَبْنَاhum · hamara · hum gaye

Rule 1 · "You all" = أَنْتُمْ, and "your" = ـكُمْ

When you speak to more than one person, أَنْتَ ("you", one man) becomes أَنْتُمْ ("you all"). And "your" is the tail ـكُمْ stuck on the end of a word:

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
مَنْ أَنْتُمْ ؟who are you (all)?tum sab kaun ho?
كَيْفَ حَالُكُمْ ؟how are you (all)?tum sab kaise ho?
أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُمْ ؟where is your house?tumhara ghar kahan hai?
أَبُوكُمْ صَدِيقِيyour father is my friendtumhara baap mera dost hai

💡 You already know ـكُمْ!

It is the ـكُمْ of السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ — "peace be upon you all." Yeh wahi "kum" hai jo rozana "as-salamu alaykum" mein bolte ho. One note: on "father / brother" it becomes أَبُوكُمْ / أَخُوكُمْ (the long و from Chapter 10).

Rule 2 · "We" = نَحْنُ, and "our" = ـنَا

ـنَا is the easy tail — it is the same for men, women, and mixed groups (no gender to worry about):

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
نَحْنُ مُسْلِمُونَwe are Muslimshum musalman hain
هِيَ أُخْتُنَاshe is our sisterwoh hamari behan hai
بَيْتُنَا قَرِيبٌ مِنَ الْمَطَارِour house is near the airporthamara ghar airport ke qareeb hai
اللهُ رَبُّنَاAllah is our LordAllah hamara Rabb hai

⚠️ Keep ـكُمْ and ـنَا apart

ـكُمْ = "your" (point at them); ـنَا = "our" (hand on your own chest). "Kum" = tumhara; "naa" = hamara. Practise: someone asks أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُمْ ؟, you answer بَيْتُنَا قَرِيبٌ …

Rule 3 · "You all went" / "we went" — ذَهَبْتُمْ / ذَهَبْنَا

The verb changes its ending to match who did it. Build on the verbs you know (ذَهَبَ went, خَرَجَ went out, جَلَسَ sat):

WhoEndingExampleRoman Urdu
you (one man)ـتَأَنْتَ ذَهَبْتَtu gaya
you allـتُمْأَنْتُمْ ذَهَبْتُمْtum sab gaye
weـنَانَحْنُ ذَهَبْنَاhum gaye

💡 From the dialogue

أَذَهَبْتُمْ إِلَى الْمُسْتَشْفَى ؟ — نَعَمْ ، ذَهَبْنَا . — "Did you all go to the hospital? — Yes, we went." Verb ka ending zameer se milta hai: "tum sab" → ـتُمْ, "hum" → ـنَا.

🕌 The sentence of the lesson — نَحْنُ مُسْلِمُونَ …

This sentence is built from "our" (ـنَا) — six times! Learn it by heart:

نَحْنُ مُسْلِمُونَ . اللهُ رَبُّنَا وَالإِسْلَامُ دِينُنَا وَالنَّبِيُّ مُحَمَّدٌ ﷺ رَسُولُنَا وَالقُرْآنُ الكَرِيمُ كِتَابُنَا وَالكَعْبَةُ قِبْلَتُنَا وَالعَرَبِيَّةُ لُغَتُنَا .

naḥnu muslimūn. allāhu rabbunā wa-l-islāmu dīnunā wa-n-nabiyyu muḥammadun ﷺ rasūlunā wa-l-qurʾānu l-karīmu kitābunā wa-l-kaʿbatu qiblatunā wa-l-ʿarabiyyatu lughatunā.

"We are Muslims. Allah is our Lord, Islam is our religion, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is our Messenger, the Noble Qur'an is our Book, the Kaʿbah is our qiblah, and Arabic is our language."

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Add the tail ـكُمْ ("your") then ـنَا ("our") — lesson Exercise 3

Write each word twice: once with ـكُمْ, once with ـنَا. Example: بَيْتٌ → بَيْتُكُمْ · بَيْتُنَا.

  1. أُمٌّ → ·
  2. صَدِيقٌ → ·
  3. مَدْرَسَةٌ → ·
  4. دِينٌ → ·
  5. أَبٌ → ·  (careful — five nouns!)

Exercise 2 · ـكُمْ or ـنَا?

Choose the right tail for the meaning. Tip: "your" = ـكُمْ, "our" = ـنَا.

  1. our Lord — رَبُّ
  2. your school — مَدْرَسَتُ
  3. our language — لُغَتُ
  4. where is your house? — أَيْنَ بَيْتُ ؟

Exercise 3 · Make the verb "you all" then "we" — lesson Exercise 6

Rewrite each sentence with أَنْتُمْ ("you all"). Example: أَنْتَ ذَهَبْتَ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ → أَنْتُمْ ذَهَبْتُمْ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.

  1. أَنْتَ خَرَجْتَ مِنَ الْفَصْلِ . →
  2. أَنْتَ جَلَسْتَ فِي الْفَصْلِ . →

Now answer with "we": أَذَهَبْتُمْ إِلَى السُّوقِ ؟ — نَعَمْ ، .

Exercise 4 · Answer about yourselves (lesson Exercise 1) — use نَحْنُ / ـنَا

Answer each question as a group ("we / our"):

  1. مَنْ أَنْتُمْ ؟ —
  2. أَيْنَ بَيْتُكُمْ ؟ —
  3. مَنْ رَبُّكُمْ ؟ —
  4. مَا لُغَتُكُمْ ؟ —

Exercise 5 · Read the names aloud (lesson Exercise 5)

Read each name correctly — a plain ـُ at the end, no tanween. Tick each Prophet you recognise.

إِبْرَاهِيمُ · إِسْمَاعِيلُ · إِسْحَقُ · يَعْقُوبُ · أَيُّوبُ · سُلَيْمَانُ · دَاوُدُ · يُونُسُ · إِدْرِيسُ

Exercise 6 · Translate both ways

(a) Into Arabic:

  1. We are Muslims; Allah is our Lord. —
  2. Where is your (all) new house? —

(b) Into English:

  1. نَحْنُ أَبْنَاءُ الدُّكْتُورِ مُوسَى . —
  2. أَذَهَبْتُمْ إِلَى الْمُسْتَشْفَى ؟ — نَعَمْ ، ذَهَبْنَا . —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the creedal sentence نَحْنُ مُسْلِمُونَ . اللهُ رَبُّنَا … and be ready to recite it.
  2. Write 4 things with ـكُمْ ("your") and the same 4 with ـنَا ("our") — e.g. رَبُّكُمْ / رَبُّنَا.
  3. Write the verb pair ذَهَبْتُمْ / ذَهَبْنَا for خَرَجَ and جَلَسَ too.
  4. Read the Prophets' names aloud until they flow — no tanween at the end.
  5. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
