📋 Quick recap — what we can already do

  • Point: هَذَا · ذَلِكَ · هَذِهِ · تِلْكَ (Ch 1–7)
  • Say "this is a house": هَذَا بَيْتٌ (Ch 1)
  • Location word + kasra: تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ (Ch 5)
  • Prepositions: فِي · عَلَى · إِلَى · مِنْ · لِـ (Ch 3–4)

Today: we let them all run together — and add three new things.

Rule 1 · هَذَا الْبَيْتُ is NOT a sentence

no الـ = closed ✓

هَذَا بَيْتٌ

"This is a house." — complete

+ الـ = open ⏳

هَذَا الْبَيْتُ

"this house…" — waiting

Add الـ → the sentence opens and needs finishing.

Finish the phrase with a predicate

هَذَا الْبَيْتُ جَدِيدٌ

"This house is new." ✓ complete again

هَذَا الْبَيْتُ لِلتَّاجِرِ

"This house is for the merchant." ✓

💡 Urdu already does this!

هَذَا بَيْتٌ = "yeh ek ghar hai" ✓

هَذَا الْبَيْتُ = "yeh ghar…" ⏳ (yeh ghar kya?)

هَذَا الْبَيْتُ جَدِيدٌ = "yeh ghar naya hai" ✓

الـ ek switch hai jo jumla khol deta hai — band karne ke liye khabar lagao.

The lesson drills this (Exercise 3, p. 43)

هَذَا كِتَابٌ

↓ add الـ + a predicate

هَذَا الْكِتَابُ لِمُحَمَّدٍ

"this book is Muhammad's"

Rule 2 · أَمَامَ & خَلْفَ

أَمَامَ

in front of · saamne

خَلْفَ

behind · peeche

Two new location words — they work just like تَحْتَ (Ch 5).

The next word takes kasra

أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدِin front of the mosque
خَلْفَ الْمَدْرَسَةِbehind the school

Add them to the family: فِي · عَلَى · إِلَى · مِنْ · لِـ · تَحْتَ · أَمَامَ · خَلْفَ

⚠️ Don't drop the kasra

✅ أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدِ

❌ أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدُ

Wahi Chapter 3 ka rule — jagah-wale lafz ke baad zer.

From the lesson: السَّبُّورَةُ أَمَامَ الطَّالِبِ وَهِيَ خَلْفَ الْمُدَرِّسِ

Rule 3 · Countries + فِي/مِنْ/إِلَى

with الـ → kasra

الْيَابَان · الْهِنْد · الصِّين

مِنَ الْيَابَانِ

foreign → frozen

أَمْرِيكَا · فَرَنْسَا · أَلْمَانِيَا

مِنْ أَمْرِيكَا

Two kinds of country name — one changes, one doesn't.

⚠️ No tanween on foreign names

✅ مِنْ أَمْرِيكَا · إِلَى فَرَنْسَا

❌ مِنْ أَمْرِيكَاً

Bahar ke naam fix rehte hain — kuch add nahin hota. (Poora rule baad mein.)

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

Both new ideas appear in famous verses.

هَذَا الْقُرْآنَ aur خَلْفَ — dono Quran mein.

Surah Al-Isrāʾ · 17:9

إِنَّ هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ يَهْدِي لِلَّتِي هِيَ أَقْوَمُ

inna hādhā l-qurʾāna yahdī…

"Indeed this Qur'an guides to that which is most upright."

هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ = exactly Rule 1 — a demonstrative + a definite noun ("this Qur'an").

Ayat al-Kursī · Al-Baqarah 2:255

يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ

…wa-mā khalfahum — "…and what is behind them."

خَلْفَ (aaj ka lafz) Ayat al-Kursī mein. ـهُمْ ("them") wala ending Lesson 10 mein aayega.
