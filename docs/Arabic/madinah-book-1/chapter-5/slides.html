Rule 1 · هُوَ & هِيَ — the first pronouns

هُوَ

huwa

"he / it" (masculine)

woh (mardaana)

هِيَ

hiya

"she / it" (feminine)

woh (zanaana)

The gender rule — same as Ch 3's هَذِهِ

Match the pronoun to the noun's gender:

  • Masculine noun (no ة) → هُوَ
  • Feminine noun (ة-ending) → هِيَ

Urdu mein "woh" har cheez ke liye chalti hai. Arabic mein nahin — har lafz ki gender pakdani padti hai.

Gender pairing chart

Masculine → هُوَ

كِتَابٌ · بَيْتٌ
قَلَمٌ · مَسْجِدٌ
مُحَمَّدٌ · وَلَدٌ

Feminine → هِيَ

حَقِيبَةٌ · سَيَّارَةٌ
مَدْرَسَةٌ · غُرْفَةٌ
خَدِيجَةُ · شَجَرَةٌ

Pair-drill: "كِتَاب — هُوَ", "سَيَّارَة — هِيَ", "مَسْجِد — هُوَ"...

The pattern — هُوَ/هِيَ as subject

QuestionAnswer
أَيْنَ كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ ؟ هُوَ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ
"Where is Muhammad's book? — It is on the desk."
أَيْنَ حَقِيبَةُ الْمُدَرِّسِ ؟ هِيَ تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ
"Where is the teacher's bag? — It is under the desk."

⚠️ هُوَ is NOT "is"

Arabic has no "to be" in present tense. هُوَ = subject (he/it), not link (is).

  • ✅ الْكِتَابُ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ — subject = noun
  • ✅ هُوَ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ — subject = pronoun
  • ❌ الْكِتَابُ هُوَ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ — two subjects, wrong!

Ya naam ya pronoun — dono ek saath nahin.

Rule 2 · قَلَمُ مَنْ ؟ — "Whose?" via iḍāfa

قَلَمُ مَنْ ؟

qalamu man?

= "Whose pen?"

= "Kis ka qalam?"

An iḍāfa with مَنْ as the 2nd noun

It's just an iḍāfa with a question word

Ch 4 iḍāfa قَلَمُ مُحَمَّدٍ "Muhammad's pen"
Ch 4 iḍāfa قَلَمُ الْمُدَرِّسِ "the teacher's pen"
Ch 5 NEW قَلَمُ مَنْ "whose pen?"

Same iḍāfa rules (Ch 4). Only the 2nd noun changes — to a question word.

Wahi iḍāfa, par doosre lafz par مَنْ baith gaya.

Q&A pattern

قَلَمُ مَنْ هَذَا ؟

"Whose pen is this?" · "Kis ka qalam hai yeh?"

⬇️
هَذَا قَلَمُ الْمُدَرِّسِ

"This is the teacher's pen" · "Yeh ustaad ka qalam hai"

Two doors, same room

Ch 4 way (لِـ)Ch 5 way (iḍāfa)
لِمَنْ هَذَا الْقَلَمُ ؟ قَلَمُ مَنْ هَذَا ؟
هَذَا الْقَلَمُ لِمُحَمَّدٍ هَذَا قَلَمُ مُحَمَّدٍ
Both = "Whose pen is this? — Muhammad's."

Both correct. Both used by native speakers.

Dono sahi hain — dono ka matlab same hai.

More examples

كِتَابُ مَنْ هَذَا ؟ هَذَا كِتَابُ حَامِدٍ
بَيْتُ مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟ ذَلِكَ بَيْتُ الْإِمَامِ
سَيَّارَةُ مَنْ هَذِهِ ؟ هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدَرِّسِ
حَقِيبَةُ مَنْ هَذِهِ ؟ هَذِهِ حَقِيبَةُ خَدِيجَةَ

Rule 3 · تَحْتَ + هُنَاكَ — new location words

تَحْتَ

taḥta

"under"

neeche

هُنَاكَ

hunāka

"there"

wahaan

تَحْتَ — joins the kasra family

Same kasra rule as Ch 3 prepositions.

فِي · عَلَى · إِلَى · مِنْ · لِـ · تَحْتَ

→ next noun takes kasra

Chhe lafz — sab ek hi rule. Aage wala ism mein zer.

تَحْتَ in action

تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ taḥta l-maktabi under the desk
تَحْتَ السَّرِيرِ taḥta s-sarīri under the bed
تَحْتَ الشَّجَرَةِ taḥta sh-shajarati under the tree
تَحْتَ الْكِتَابِ taḥta l-kitaabi under the book

هُنَاكَ — stands alone

Unlike تَحْتَ, هُنَاكَ doesn't take a noun after it.

الْكِتَابُ هُنَاكَ "The book is there"
هُوَ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ هُنَاكَ "It is on the desk there"

Pairs with هُنَا (here) / هُنَاكَ (there).

Rule 4 · مُضَاف / مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ — formal names

Last week you learned iḍāfa as "first / second noun".

Today: the parts get their formal Arabic names.

Same rules. Just new labels.

Wahi pichhle hafte wali iḍāfa — bas asli naam mil rahe hain.

The diagram

سَيَّارَةُ
·
الْمُدَرِّسِ
مُضَاف مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ

مُضَاف (muḍāf) = 1st noun (owned) · مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ (muḍāfun ilayhi) = 2nd noun (owner)

The 3 iḍāfa rules — restated

  1. The مُضَاف (1st noun) takes no الـ.
  2. The مُضَاف (1st noun) takes no tanween.
  3. The مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ (2nd noun) is in kasra.

Teen rules same — sirf naam upgrade huye.

Rule 5 · هَمْزَة الوَصْل — the connecting alif

A reading rule, not a writing rule.

The initial vowel of الـ, اِبْن, اِسْم drops when something precedes them in speech.

Shuruaati alif ki "i" beech mein gum ho jaati hai.

Same word, two positions

SentenceRead as
ابْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ فِي الْعِرَاقِ ibnu Muḥammadin fi l-ʿIrāq
"Ibn" at start — full i
خَرَجَ ابْنُ الطَّبِيبِ kharaja-bnu ṭ-ṭabīb
"Ibn" mid-sentence — i swallowed
مَا اسْمُ الْمُدَرِّسِ ؟ ma-smu l-mudarris?
"Ism" mid-sentence — i swallowed

You've seen this before!

The dropping-alif phenomenon has been with you since Ch 3:

  • Ch 3: مِنَ الْـ · "fi l-bayt", "ʿalā l-maktab" — alif silent in speech
  • Ch 4: لِلْـ · alif dropped in writing too
  • Ch 5: ابن، اسم · same phenomenon, now named

Phenomenon purana — sirf naam aaj mila.

Read-aloud — model and mimic

Listen to the teacher, then repeat:

  1. خَرَجَ ابْنُ الطَّبِيبِ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ
    kharaja-bnu ṭ-ṭabībi mina l-bayt
  2. مَا اسْمُ الْمُدَرِّسِ ؟
    ma-smu l-mudarris?
  3. اسْمُ الْمُهَنْدِسِ فَيْصَلٌ
    ismu l-muhandisi Fayṣal (start → full i)

🕌 Three sacred sentences

Every word in these is now known grammar.

Each = subject noun + iḍāfa as predicate.

Teen jumle — saari iḍāfa aur nominal sentence ek saath.

Sentence 1

الْكَعْبَةُ بَيْتُ اللَّـهِ

al-Kaʿbatu baytu llāhi

"The Ka'ba is the House of Allah"

"Ka'ba Allah ka ghar hai"

Subject: الْكَعْبَةُ · Predicate (iḍāfa): بَيْتُ اللَّـهِ

Sentence 2

مُحَمَّدٌ ﷺ رَسُولُ اللَّـهِ

Muḥammadun rasūlu llāhi

"Muhammad ﷺ is the Messenger of Allah"

"Muhammad ﷺ Allah ke rasool hain"

Subject: مُحَمَّدٌ · Predicate (iḍāfa): رَسُولُ اللَّـهِ

Sentence 3

الْقُرْآنُ كِتَابُ اللَّـهِ

al-Qurʾānu kitaabu llāhi

"The Qur'an is the Book of Allah"

"Qur'an Allah ki kitaab hai"

Subject: الْقُرْآنُ · Predicate (iḍāfa): كِتَابُ اللَّـهِ

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ

"Allah — there is no deity except Him."

"It is peace until the break of dawn."

Feminine هِيَ — referring back to لَيْلَة الْقَدْر (the Night of Decree — feminine).

Surah Al-Fātiḥah · 1:2 (review with new vocabulary)
