Rule 1 · تِلْكَ — "that" (far, feminine)

تِلْكَ

til-ka — "that" for a far feminine thing or person.

هَذِهِ ki door wali behen · ذَلِكَ ki feminine behen.

From the lesson — near vs far

مَنْ هَذِهِ ؟ — هَذِهِ آمِنَةُ
"Who is this? — This is Aamina." (near)
وَمَنْ تِلْكَ ؟ — تِلْكَ فَاطِمَةُ
"And who is that? — That is Faatima." (far)

⚠️ Urdu's one "woh" will trick you

Urdu = one word woh for man or woman. Arabic splits it:

far man → ذَلِكَ  ·  far woman → تِلْكَ

Urdu mein ek "woh" — Arabic mein mard ذَلِكَ, aurat تِلْكَ.

Read it right: til-ka

تِلْكَ

Clean ت (t) — not ذ (dh).

Hook for the two far-words:

ذَلِكَ = dh (masculine) · تِلْكَ = t (feminine)

Both end in ك — the mark of "far".

Two questions pick the word

1. Near or far?
near → هـ words (هَذَا / هَذِهِ) · far → ك words (ذَلِكَ / تِلْكَ)

2. Masculine or feminine?
masc → هَذَا / ذَلِكَ · fem → هَذِهِ / تِلْكَ

(1) paas/door? (2) mard/aurat? — bas, ek lafz mil gaya.

Rule 3 · Drill the two axes

Change one thing at a time.

Gender axis (fix distance) · Distance axis (fix gender)

Gender axis — only ذَلِكَ/تِلْكَ changes

ذَلِكَ دِيكٌ وَتِلْكَ دَجَاجَةٌrooster / hen
ذَلِكَ جَمَلٌ وَتِلْكَ نَاقَةٌcamel / she-camel

Same distance — only the gender flips.

Distance axis — only near/far changes

هَذِهِ طَبِيبَةٌ وَتِلْكَ مُمَرَّضَةٌdoctor / nurse
هَذِهِ طَوِيلَةٌ وَتِلْكَ قَصِيرَةٌtall / short

Same gender — only the distance flips.

⚠️ Keep the ة under تِلْكَ

✅ تِلْكَ مُمَرَّضَةٌ  ·  ❌ تِلْكَ مُمَرَّضٌ

تِلْكَ points at a female → the predicate is feminine too.

Chapter 6 ka rule wahi — ة nahin chhootna chahiye.

Rule 4 · تِلْكَ in questions & iḍāfa

Nothing new behind it — all review.

Peeche kuch naya nahin — sab purana.

Questions with تِلْكَ

مَنْ تِلْكَ ؟ — تِلْكَ فَاطِمَةُWho is that? — Faatima
مَا تِلْكَ ؟ — تِلْكَ بَيْضَةٌWhat is that? — an egg
أَتِلْكَ دَجَاجَةٌ ؟ — لَا، تِلْكَ بَطَّةٌA hen? — No, a duck

Iḍāfa with تِلْكَ

"That is the director's car."

✅ تِلْكَ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ

❌ تِلْكَ السَّيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ

Iḍāfa ka pehla lafz bare rehta hai — الـ/tanween nahin.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

تِلْكَ opens whole surahs.

تِلْكَ se kayi suraton ki shuruaat hoti hai.

Surah Yusuf · 12:1

الٓر ۚ تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ

tilka āyātu l-kitābi l-mubīn

"These are the verses of the clear Book."

You can read تِلْكَ (today) + آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ (an iḍāfa, Ch 4). Here تِلْكَ points at a whole group of verses — the "group" use comes fully in Lesson 13.

Surah Al-Baqarah · 2:229

تِلْكَ حُدُودُ اللَّهِ فَلَا تَعْتَدُوهَا

tilka ḥudūdu llāhi fa-lā taʿtadūhā

"These are the limits of Allah, so do not transgress them."

تِلْكَ (aaj) + حُدُودُ اللَّهِ (iḍāfa) — dono parh sakte ho.
