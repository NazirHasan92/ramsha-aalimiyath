Chapter 7 — Student Handout

تِلْكَ — "that" (far, feminine) · completing the demonstrative grid

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Use تِلْكَ ("that") for a far feminine thing or person.
  2. Choose the right "this/that" by asking two questions — near/far and masculine/feminine.
  3. Contrast ذَلِكَ ↔ تِلْكَ (gender) and هَذِهِ ↔ تِلْكَ (distance).
  4. Ask and answer: مَنْ تِلْكَ ؟, مَا تِلْكَ ؟, أَتِلْكَ ... ؟.
  5. Keep everything from Chapter 6 (agreement, the ة, iḍāfa) working with تِلْكَ.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 20 minutes of self-practice. This is a short lesson — one new word.

Rule 1 · تِلْكَ — "that" for a far feminine thing

You already know three pointing words. There was one gap — a "that" for a far feminine thing. Today's word fills it.

هَذِهِ (this — near)  →  تِلْكَ (that — far)

tilka — read it "til-ka", with a clean ت (t).

💡 From the lesson — near vs far

مَنْ هَذِهِ ؟ — هَذِهِ آمِنَةُ . وَمَنْ تِلْكَ ؟ — تِلْكَ فَاطِمَةُ

"Who is this? — This is Aamina. And who is that? — That is Faatima."

Roman: "آمِنَة paas hai → هَذِهِ; فَاطِمَة door hai → تِلْكَ."

⚠️ Urdu's one "woh" will trick you

Urdu uses one word — woh — for "that", man or woman. Arabic splits it: a far man is ذَلِكَ, a far woman/feminine thing is تِلْكَ. "Urdu mein ek 'woh' — Arabic mein mard ذَلِكَ, aurat تِلْكَ."

Rule 2 · The complete grid — two questions pick the word

With تِلْكَ added, there are now four pointing words. To choose, ask two questions: (1) near or far? and (2) masculine or feminine?

Masculine مُذَكَّرFeminine مُؤَنَّث
Near — "this"هَذَاهَذِهِ
Far — "that"ذَلِكَتِلْكَ ← new

💡 The hook

Near words start with هـ (هَذَا, هَذِهِ). Far words end with ك (ذَلِكَ, تِلْكَ). And of the two far-words: ذَلِكَ starts with ذ (dh, masculine), تِلْكَ starts with ت (t, feminine). "Door = ك par khatam; mard ذ se, aurat ت se."

Rule 3 · The two contrasts — drill these

The whole lesson is built on side-by-side pairs. Read each one aloud.

Gender contrast (same distance — only ذَلِكَ/تِلْكَ changes):

ذَلِكَ دِيكٌ وَتِلْكَ دَجَاجَةٌThat is a rooster and that is a hen.
ذَلِكَ جَمَلٌ وَتِلْكَ نَاقَةٌThat is a camel and that is a she-camel.

Distance contrast (same gender — only near/far changes):

هَذِهِ طَبِيبَةٌ وَتِلْكَ مُمَرَّضَةٌThis is a doctor and that is a nurse.
هَذِهِ طَوِيلَةٌ وَتِلْكَ قَصِيرَةٌThis (one) is tall and that (one) is short.

⚠️ Keep the ة on the describing word

The Chapter 6 rule still holds: تِلْكَ points at a female, so the predicate is feminine too.
✅ تِلْكَ مُمَرَّضَةٌ  ·  ❌ تِلْكَ مُمَرَّضٌ.

Rule 4 · تِلْكَ in questions & iḍāfa (all review)

Nothing new behind تِلْكَ — your old questions and iḍāfa work exactly the same.

مَنْ تِلْكَ ؟تِلْكَ فَاطِمَةُWho is that? — That is Faatima.
مَا تِلْكَ ؟تِلْكَ بَيْضَةٌWhat is that? — That is an egg.
أَتِلْكَ دَجَاجَةٌ ؟لَا، تِلْكَ بَطَّةٌIs that a hen? — No, that is a duck.
سَيَّارَةُ مَنْ تِلْكَ ؟تِلْكَ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِWhose car is that? — That is the director's car.

⚠️ The iḍāfa rule still holds with تِلْكَ

✅ تِلْكَ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ  ·  ❌ تِلْكَ السَّيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ. The first noun of the iḍāfa stays bare — no الـ, no tanween.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · هَذِهِ or تِلْكَ? (near or far)

Both are feminine — choose by distance. Write هَذِهِ (near) or تِلْكَ (far).

  1. (near) سَاعَةٌ
  2. (far) نَافِذَةٌ
  3. (far) بَطَّةٌ
  4. (near) بِنْتٌ
  5. (far) مُمَرَّضَةٌ
  6. (near) حَدِيقَةٌ

Exercise 2 · ذَلِكَ or تِلْكَ? (masculine or feminine)

Both are far — choose by gender. Write ذَلِكَ (m) or تِلْكَ (f).

  1. دِيكٌ (rooster)
  2. دَجَاجَةٌ (hen)
  3. جَمَلٌ (camel)
  4. نَاقَةٌ (she-camel)
  5. مُؤَذِّنٌ (muezzin)
  6. عَيْنٌ  (careful — no ة, but feminine!)

Exercise 4 · Complete the contrast (follow the example)

Example: هَذِهِ طَبِيبَةٌ → وَتِلْكَ مُمَرَّضَةٌ.

  1. هَذِهِ نَاقَةٌ → وَذَلِكَ (camel)
  2. هَذِهِ طَوِيلَةٌ → وَتِلْكَ (short)
  3. ذَلِكَ دِيكٌ → وَتِلْكَ (hen)
  4. هَذِهِ مِنَ الْهِنْدِ → وَتِلْكَ (from Japan)

Exercise 5 · Find and correct the mistake

Each sentence has one error (wrong gender or wrong distance). Rewrite it correctly.

  1. ذَلِكَ بَطَّةٌ →
  2. تِلْكَ مُمَرَّضٌ →
  3. تِلْكَ السَّيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ →
  4. مَنْ ذَلِكَ ؟ — ذَلِكَ فَاطِمَةُ →

Exercise 6 · Translate both ways

(a) Into Arabic:

  1. That (far) is a nurse. —
  2. That is a rooster and that is a hen. —
  3. Who is that (far woman)? — That is Zaynab. —
  4. That is the director's car. —

(b) Into English:

  1. هَذِهِ طَبِيبَةٌ وَتِلْكَ مُمَرَّضَةٌ —
  2. أَتِلْكَ دَجَاجَةٌ ؟ — لَا، تِلْكَ بَطَّةٌ —
  3. ذَلِكَ جَمَلٌ وَتِلْكَ نَاقَةٌ —
  4. هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدَرِّسِ وَتِلْكَ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the four-cell grid — هَذَا · ذَلِكَ · هَذِهِ · تِلْكَ. Be able to write it from memory.
  2. Drill the gender axis: build 5 pairs like ذَلِكَ ... وَتِلْكَ ... (same distance, masc → fem).
  3. Drill the distance axis: build 5 pairs like هَذِهِ ... وَتِلْكَ ... (same gender, near → far).
  4. Ask and answer the three تِلْكَ questions (مَنْ تِلْكَ؟ · مَا تِلْكَ؟ · أَتِلْكَ ...؟) about objects far from you.
  5. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
