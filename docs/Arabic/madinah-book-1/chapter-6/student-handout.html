الدَّرْسُ السَّادِسُ

Chapter 6 — Student Handout

The feminine (التَّأْنِيث) · the ة marker · agreement · body parts

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Make a masculine word feminine by adding ة (طَبِيب → طَبِيبَة).
  2. Match an adjective/profession to a feminine subject: هِيَ طَبِيبَةٌ, دَرَّاجَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ.
  3. Use هَذِهِ ("this", feminine) correctly.
  4. Recognise the few body parts that are feminine without a ة (عَيْن، أُذُن، يَد، رِجْل).
  5. Build feminine iḍāfa phrases (بِنْتُ يَاسِرٍ, سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ) and ask "whose?" about a feminine thing.
  6. Know the new vocabulary — family, household objects, and body parts.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · التَّأْنِيث — the ة makes a word feminine

Arabic has only two genders — masculine (مُذَكَّر) and feminine (مُؤَنَّث). There is no "it". The usual way to make a word feminine is to add the letter ة (taa marbuuta) to the end.

طَبِيب  +  ة  =  طَبِيبَة

ṭabīb (male doctor) → ṭabībah (female doctor)

💡 The "switch" — add ة

Take any masculine word, add ة, and it becomes feminine:

  • مُسْلِم → مُسْلِمَة (a Muslim, m → f)
  • مُهَنْدِس → مُهَنْدِسَة (engineer, m → f)
  • كَبِير → كَبِيرَة (big)
  • جَدِيد → جَدِيدَة (new)

Roman: "Mardaana lafz le lo, aakhir mein ة laga do — feminine taiyaar."

⚠️ Urdu gender will trick you

Urdu gender does not match Arabic. kitaab is feminine in Urdu, but كِتَابٌ is masculine in Arabic (هَذَا, هُوَ). "Urdu wali gender bhool jao — Arabic mein ة dekho ya yaad karo."

Rule 2 · Agreement — the ة flows through the sentence

When the subject is feminine, the words that describe it (a profession or an adjective) also take the ة. And we use هَذِهِ (not هَذَا) for a feminine "this".

💡 One switch lights the whole line

Once the subject is feminine, the ة must reach every describing word:

هَذِهِ دَرَّاجَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ

hādhihi darrājatun jadīdatun — "this is a new bicycle" — three feminine markers in a row.

From the lesson — read aloud:

أَهِيَ مُهَنْدِسَةٌ ؟ — لَا، هِيَ طَبِيبَةٌIs she an engineer? — No, she is a doctor.
هَذِهِ سَاعَةُ عَلِيٍّ. هِيَ جَمِيلَةٌ جِدًّاThis is Ali's watch. It is very beautiful.
هَذِهِ دَرَّاجَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ، وَدَرَّاجَةُ أَنَسٍ قَدِيمَةٌThis is a new bicycle, and Anas's bicycle is old.

⚠️ Don't forget the ة on the adjective!

✅ هِيَ طَبِيبَةٌ  ·  ❌ هِيَ طَبِيبٌ (masculine form on a feminine subject).
✅ سَيَّارَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ  ·  ❌ سَيَّارَةٌ جَدِيدٌ.

Rule 3 · A few words are feminine without a ة

Most feminine words have a ة — but not all. A handful must simply be memorised. In this lesson they are the body parts that come in pairs.

Masculine → هَذَاFeminine (no ة) → هَذِهِ
أَنْفٌ (nose) · فَمٌ (mouth)عَيْنٌ (eye) · أُذُنٌ (ear)
يَدٌ (hand) · رِجْلٌ (leg/foot)

💡 Memory hook — "paired organs are feminine"

Two eyes, two ears, two hands, two feet — all feminine in Arabic, so all take هَذِهِ:

هَذِهِ عَيْنٌ · هَذِهِ أُذُنٌ · هَذِهِ يَدٌ · هَذِهِ رِجْلٌ

Roman: "Jism ke jo ange do-do hote hain woh feminine — هَذِهِ. Naak aur mooh masculine — هَذَا."

The family words بِنْت (girl), أُخْت (sister) and أُمّ (mother) are also feminine without a ة — but that's easy, since they obviously refer to females.

Rule 4 · The feminine in iḍāfa & "Whose?"

Nothing new here — the iḍāfa (Ch 4) and "whose?" questions (Ch 4–5) work exactly the same with feminine nouns. When a feminine word is the first noun of an iḍāfa, the old rules still hold: no الـ, no tanween — just ـةُ.

Feminine iḍāfa — from the lesson:

بِنْتُ يَاسِرٍbintu YāsirYasir's daughter
أُخْتُ الْمُهَنْدِسِukhtu l-muhandisthe engineer's sister
سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِsayyāratu l-mudīrthe director's car
بَقَرَةُ الْفَلَّاحِbaqaratu l-fallāḥthe farmer's cow

"Whose?" about a feminine thing:

سَيَّارَةُ مَنْ هَذِهِ ؟هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِWhose car is this? — This is the director's car.
لِمَنْ هَذِهِ ؟هَذِهِ لِخَالِدٍWhose is this? — This is Khalid's.
مَنْ هَذِهِ ؟هَذِهِ أُخْتُ عَبَّاسٍWho is this? — This is Abbas's sister.

⚠️ Keep the iḍāfa rules — even with feminine words

✅ سَيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ  ·  ❌ السَّيَّارَةُ الْمُدِيرِ  ·  ❌ سَيَّارَةٌ الْمُدِيرِ. The first noun stays bare.

Exercise 3 · Complete the agreement (follow the example)

Example: مُحَمَّدٌ طَالِبٌ → آمِنَةُ طَالِبَةٌ ("Muhammad is a student → Aminah is a student").

  1. حَامِدٌ طَبِيبٌ → فَاطِمَةُ
  2. هُوَ مُسْلِمٌ → هِيَ
  3. الْبَابُ مُغْلَقٌ → النَّافِذَةُ

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Drill the ة switch: take 10 masculine words and make each feminine. Speed up until it's automatic.
  2. Build 5 full-agreement feminine sentences — subject + adjective both feminine (e.g. هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةٌ جَمِيلَةٌ).
  3. Memorise the body-part demonstratives — which take هَذَا and which take هَذِهِ (remember: paired organs are feminine).
  4. Build 5 feminine iḍāfa phrases (no الـ/tanween on the first noun).
  5. Complete all 8 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
  6. Find the ة around you: list 5 words you already know that end in ة (e.g. الْكَعْبَة، الْفَاتِحَة، سُورَة) — they are all feminine.
