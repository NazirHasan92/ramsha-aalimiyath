الدَّرْسُ الثَّالِثَ عَشَرَ

Chapter 13 — Student Handout

Talking about a group · plural nouns · هَؤُلَاءِ / أُولَئِكَ ("these / those") · هُمْ / هُنَّ ("they") · ذَهَبُوا / ذَهَبْنَ ("they went")

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

So far we have talked about one person at a time. Today we talk about a group — "these students", "those women", "they went". When you go from one to many, four things change together. By the end you will be able to:

  1. Make a noun plural — by an ending (مُهَنْدِسُونَ / طَالِبَات) or by re-shaping it (رَجُل → رِجَال).
  2. Point at a group: هَؤُلَاءِ ("these") / أُولَئِكَ ("those") — one word for men or women.
  3. Say "they": هُمْ (men) / هُنَّ (women), and "their": ـهُمْ / ـهُنَّ.
  4. Say "they went": ذَهَبُوا (men) / ذَهَبْنَ (women).

Time needed: this is the longest lesson — your teacher will likely split it into two classes (men first, then women + "those"). Practice ≈ 30 minutes at home.

🧭 The one big idea — four things go plural together

When you talk about a group of people, all four of these switch to plural at once (everything matches gender — except the pointer, which is the same for both):

one→ many men→ many women(Urdu)
طَالِبٌ / طَالِبَةٌطُلَّابٌطَالِبَاتٌtalib · talaba · talibaat

Rule 1 · Plural nouns — by ending, or by re-shaping

Arabic makes a plural in two ways:

WayHowExamplesRoman Urdu
Sound (add an ending)ـُونَ (men) · ـَات (women)مُهَنْدِسُونَ · مُسْلِمُونَ · طَالِبَات · مُدَرِّسَاتsaalim — ending lago: mard ـُونَ, aurat ـَات
Broken (re-shape the word)no ending — the word changes insideرَجُل→رِجَال · وَلَد→أَوْلَاد · كَبِير→كِبَار · أَخ→إِخْوَةtoota hua — lafz andar se badle; yaad karo

💡 Broken plurals are like English "man → men"

You can't guess them — you learn them with the word, just like foot → feet or mouse → mice. Inko yaad karna parta hai, predict nahin hota.

Rule 2 · هَؤُلَاءِ / أُولَئِكَ — "these / those"

To point at a group, use one word — the same for men or women:

ArabicMeaningRoman Urdu
هَؤُلَاءِthese (near)yeh (saare)
أُولَئِكَthose (far)woh (saare)
هَؤُلَاءِ طُلَّابٌ · هَؤُلَاءِ طَالِبَاتٌthese are students (m · f)yeh talaba · yeh talibaat

⚠️ A silent و in أُولَئِكَ

You write the و but you do not say it: read أُولَئِكَ as "u-lā-ʾi-ka", not "ul-wā…". No separate "feminine these" — هَؤُلَاءِ and أُولَئِكَ work for everyone.

Rule 3 · "They" = هُمْ (men) / هُنَّ (women)

And "their" is the tail ـهُمْ / ـهُنَّ (the group version of Ch 10's ـهُ/ـهَا):

ArabicMeaning (English)Meaning (Urdu)
هُمْ مُجْتَهِدُونَthey (m) are hard-workingwoh (mard) mehnati hain
هُنَّ زَمِيلَاتِيthey (f) are my colleagueswoh (aurtein) meri saheliyan hain
بَعْضُهُمْ مِنَ الصِّينِsome of them are from Chinaunmein se kuchh China se hain
أَبُوهُنَّ الشَّيْخُ بِلَالٌtheir father is Shaykh Bilalunka baap Shaykh Bilal hai

⚠️ Not ـكُمْ yet

Today is "they / their" (هُمْ/هُنَّ). The "you all" of السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ (ـكُمْ) comes in the next lesson — not now.

Rule 4 · "They went" — ذَهَبُوا / ذَهَبْنَ

The verb matches the doer in number and gender:

onemany (3+)
men
mard		ذَهَبَ
gaya		ذَهَبُوا
gaye
women
aurtein		ذَهَبَتْ
gayi		ذَهَبْنَ

💡 The ا in ذَهَبُوا is silent

Read ذَهَبُوا as "dhahabū" — the final ا is written but not pronounced. ذَهَبُوا إِلَى الْمَطْعَمِ = "they (m) went to the restaurant"; الطَّبِيبَاتُ خَرَجْنَ = "the (female) doctors went out".

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Make it plural (lesson Exercise 1) — حَوِّلِ الْمُبْتَدَأَ إِلَى جَمْعٍ

Change the start of the sentence to plural. Examples: هَذَا طَالِبٌ → هَؤُلَاءِ طُلَّابٌ · هَذِهِ بِنْتٌ → هَؤُلَاءِ بَنَاتٌ.

  1. هَذَا تَاجِرٌ . →
  2. هَذَا رَجُلٌ . →
  3. هَذَا شَيْخٌ . →
  4. هَذِهِ طَالِبَةٌ . →
  5. هَذِهِ مُسْلِمَةٌ . →
  6. هَذِهِ أُخْتٌ . →

Exercise 2 · Write the plural (lesson Exercise 6) — اُكْتُبْ جَمْعَ الْكَلِمَاتِ

Write the plural of each word. Tip: some take an ending (sound), some re-shape (broken).

  1. كَبِيرٌ →  ·  مُسْلِمٌ →  ·  رَجُلٌ →
  2. ضَيْفٌ →  ·  حَاجٌّ →  ·  أَخٌ →
  3. طَبِيبَةٌ →  ·  زَوْجَةٌ →  ·  بِنْتٌ →

Exercise 3 · Point with the right word

Put هَذَا / هَذِهِ / هَؤُلَاءِ (near) or ذَلِكَ / تِلْكَ / أُولَئِكَ (far) in the gap to match the word.

(a) near (this/these):

  1. رِجَالٌ .
  2. طَالِبَاتٌ .
  3. أُمِّي .

(b) far (that/those):

  1. تُجَّارٌ .
  2. طَبِيبَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ .
  3. أُمَّهَاتُ الطَّالِبَاتِ .

Exercise 4 · Fill in the pronoun (lesson Exercise 5) — هُوَ · هِيَ · هُمْ · هُنَّ

Put the right "he / she / they (m) / they (f)" in the gap.

  1. مَنْ هَذَا الرَّجُلُ ؟ — مُدَرِّسُنَا .
  2. أَيْنَ الطَّالِبَاتُ ؟ — فِي الْفَصْلِ .
  3. مَنْ هَؤُلَاءِ الْفِتْيَةُ ؟ — أَبْنَاءُ الْمُدَرِّسِ .
  4. مَنْ هَذِهِ الْفَتَاةُ ؟ — بِنْتُ الْمُدَرِّسَةِ .

Exercise 5 · Make the verb plural (lesson Exercises 4 & 3)

Rewrite with a plural doer. Examples: الطَّالِبُ ذَهَبَ → الطُّلَّابُ ذَهَبُوا · زَيْنَبُ خَرَجَتْ → زَيْنَبُ وَمَرْيَمُ خَرَجْنَ.

  1. الطَّالِبُ جَلَسَ فِي الْفَصْلِ . →
  2. التَّاجِرُ ذَهَبَ إِلَى السُّوقِ . →
  3. الْمُدَرِّسَةُ ذَهَبَتْ إِلَى الْفَصْلِ . →

Exercise 6 · Translate both ways

(a) Into Arabic:

  1. These are new students; they are from America. —
  2. Those women are my (paternal) aunts. —

(b) Into English:

  1. أَيْنَ التُّجَّارُ ؟ — ذَهَبُوا إِلَى السُّوقِ . —
  2. الطَّبِيبَاتُ خَرَجْنَ مِنَ الْمُسْتَشْفَى . —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Read the three dialogues aloud (men · women · "those") until they flow. Watch the silent و of أُولَئِكَ and the silent ا of ذَهَبُوا.
  2. Learn 10 broken plurals by heart (رِجَال · أَوْلَاد · إِخْوَة · نِسَاء · أَطِبَّاء · شُيُوخ · ضُيُوف · كِبَار · صِغَار · جُدُد).
  3. Write 4 sentences with هَؤُلَاءِ and 4 with أُولَئِكَ — two of each for men, two for women.
  4. Write the four verb cells for خَرَجَ and جَلَسَ (one man / many men / one woman / many women).
  5. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
