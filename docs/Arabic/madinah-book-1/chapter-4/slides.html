By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Use in, on, from, to (فِي · عَلَى · مِنْ · إِلَى).
    Chaar place-words istemaal karna
  2. Change the ending to zer (kasra) after them.
    Un ke baad ending zer banana
  3. Ask "where?" and "where from?"
    "kahaan?" aur "kahaan se?" poochna
  4. Use went & went out + place words.
    "gaya / nikla" aur jagahon ke lafz

Rule 1 · Four little place-words

They go before a noun and tell us where:

فِيinmein
عَلَىٰonpar
مِنْfromse
إِلَىٰtotak / ki taraf

Say عَلَىٰ / إِلَىٰ with a long "aa"

عَلَىٰ = ʿalā  (not "ʿalay")

إِلَىٰ = ilā  (not "ilay")

The tail ـى is a hidden alif — it sounds "aa".

Aakhir mein lambi "aa" — "ay" nahin.

Rule 2 · The big rule — ending becomes ـِ (zer)

Put a place-word in front → the ending changes to a kasra:

الْبَيْتُ  →  فِي الْبَيْتِ

al-baytu → fī l-bayti

"the house → in the house"

pesh (ُ) se zer (ِ)

The one rule to remember

Preposition aaya → ending par zer.

فِي الْبَيْتِ  ✅ fī l-baytI

فِي الْبَيْتُ  ❌ fī l-baytU

Chaar lafz (في، على، من، إلى) ke baad hamesha zer.

More — read them aloud

عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِon the deskmez par
فِي الْمَسْجِدِin the mosquemasjid mein
إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِto the schoolmadrasa ki taraf

مِنْ + الـ = "mina-l"

When مِنْ meets الـ, the ن takes a little fatḥa:

مِنَ الْهِنْدِ

mina l-hindi (not "min al-hindi")

مِنَ الصِّينِ · مِنَ الْيَابَانِ

"min-al" nahin — "mina-l". Ek saans mein.

Rule 3 · Asking "where?"

أَيْنَ ؟

"where (is)?" — a place

أَيْنَ الْكِتَابُ ؟

ayna l-kitābu?

هُوَ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ.

"It is on the desk."

مِنْ أَيْنَ ؟

"where from?" — origin

مِنْ أَيْنَ أَنْتَ ؟

min ayna anta?

أَنَا مِنَ الْيَابَانِ.

"I am from Japan."

⚠️ مَنْ (who) is NOT مِنْ (from)

مَنْ = man = who (kaun)

مِنْ = min = from (se)

Both in one line: مَنْ مِنَ الصِّينِ ؟

man mina ṣ-ṣīni? — "Who is from China?"

Rule 4 · Two action words

ذَهَبَ

dhahaba

"he went"

partner → إِلَى (to)

(woh) gaya

خَرَجَ

kharaja

"he went out / left"

partner → مِنْ (from)

(woh) bāhar niklā

Put them together

خَرَجَ مِنَ الْفَصْلِ
وَذَهَبَ إِلَى الْمُدِيرِ.

kharaja mina l-faṣli wa-dhahaba ilā l-mudīri

"He left the classroom and went to the director."

Woh kamre se nikla aur director ke paas gaya.

One picture to remember

Come out of (مِنْ) one place …

… and go to (إِلَى) another.

خَرَجَ → مِنْ  ·  ذَهَبَ → إِلَى

(These stay as "he went / he went out" — we don't change them yet.)

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

The four words you learned today are everywhere in the Qur'an —
each followed by a noun in kasra.

Aaj ke chaar lafz Qur'an mein har jagah hain.

فِي — "in" · at-Tīn 95:4

… فِي أَحْسَنِ تَقْوِيمٍ

fī aḥsani taqwīm — "… in the best of forms."

فِي + أَحْسَنِ → kasra. Today's rule!

مِنْ — "from" · an-Nās 114:6

مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ

mina l-jinnati wa-n-nās — "… of jinn and mankind."

The مِنَ الْـ smoothing live — and a kasra on الْجِنَّةِ. The last āyah of the Qur'an.

عَلَىٰ & إِلَىٰ — "on" & "to"

عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

ʿalā kulli shayʾin qadīr — "powerful over all things" (e.g. 2:20)

وَإِلَى اللَّهِ تُرْجَعُ الْأُمُورُ

wa-ilā-llāhi turjaʿu l-umūr — "to Allah all matters return" (e.g. 3:109)
