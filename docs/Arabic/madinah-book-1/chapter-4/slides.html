Rule 1 · لِـ — "for / belongs to"

لِـ

li-

= "for" / "belongs to" (possession)

= "ke liye" / "ka / ki / ke"

The 5th preposition — but with a twist

Two facts:

  1. لِـ is a single letter — written attached to the next word. No space.
    Agle lafz se judi — alag space nahin
  2. Same kasra rule as Ch 3 — noun after لِـ takes kasra.
    Aage wale ism ke aakhir mein zer — Ch 3 wala rule

Compare with Ch 3 prepositions:

فِي · عَلَى · إِلَى · مِنْ (separate words)
لِـ (attached prefix)

Example — with a name

لِمُحَمَّدٍ

li-Muḥammadin

"for Muhammad" / "Muhammad's"

"Muhammad ke liye / Muhammad ka"

Notice: kasra-tanween on Muhammad (ـٍ) — the "-in" doubling.

"-un" hat gaya, "-in" aa gaya — doubling barqaraar.

Rule 2 · The big spelling rule

لِـ + الْ = لِلْـ

The alif of الـ disappears in writing.

لِـ aur الـ milte hain → alif gum, sirf do لَامs back-to-back.

What it looks like

لِـ + الْوَلَدُ = لِلْوَلَدِ

li- + al-waladu = lil-waladi

Cross out the alif when you first write it — make its disappearance visible to your eye.

Likhte waqt pehle alif ko pencil se cross karo — phir pakka likho.

Rule 3 · Names take tanween (surprise!)

مُحَمَّدٌ

Muḥammadun

"Muhammad" — definite, but with tanween

"Muhammad" — khaas, par tanween phir bhi lagi

Three routes to being "definite"

Route 1: الـ

الْوَلَدُ

al-waladu

"the boy"

(Ch 1)

Route 2: Name

مُحَمَّدٌ

Muḥammadun

"Muhammad"

(Ch 4 — today!)

Route 3: Iḍāfa

كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ

kitaabu Muḥammadin

"Muhammad's book"

(Ch 4 — today!)

Teen door — ek hi kamre tak jaate hain. Sab "khaas" hone ke alag tareeqe.

The rule for names

  • ✅ Names are definite by themselves — no الـ needed.
  • ✅ Names still carry tanween (مُحَمَّدٌ, عَلِيٌّ).
  • ❌ Never add الـ to a name (no الْمُحَمَّدُ).
  • After لِـ: kasra-tanween (لِمُحَمَّدٍ).

Yeh classical Arabic ka usool hai — accept karo. Naam alag category mein hain.

Rule 4 · Asking "Whose?"

لِمَنْ ؟

li-man?

= "Whose? / For whom?"

= "Kis ka? / Kis ke liye?"

= لِـ (today) + مَنْ (Ch 2)

The pattern

لِمَنْ هَذَا الْكِتَابُ ؟

"Whose book is this?" · "Yeh kitaab kis ki hai?"

⬇️
هَذَا الْكِتَابُ لِمُحَمَّدٍ

"This book is Muhammad's" · "Yeh kitaab Muhammad ki hai"

Question and answer both use لِـ — symmetry!

"Whose car is this? — This car is the imam's."

Rule 5 · Iḍāfa — possession without a preposition

إِضَافَة

iḍāfa

= "joining" — two nouns glued to express possession

= "jodne wala rishta" — bina preposition possession

The biggest idea in this chapter — possibly in Book 1.

The example you'll use forever

كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ

kitaabu Muḥammadin

"Muhammad's book" / "the book of Muhammad"

"Muhammad ki kitaab"

Word order — flipped from English/Urdu!

English "Muhammad's book" owner first
Urdu "Muhammad ki kitaab" owner first
Arabic كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ ("book + Muhammad") OWNED first!

Urdu's "kitaab-e-Muhammad" actually uses Arabic order — the "-e-" is the Arabic kasra!

The 3 pakka rules of iḍāfa

Take any iḍāfa, like كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ. Three things must be true:

  1. 1st noun: NO الـ. Ever.
  2. 1st noun: NO tanween (single ending vowel only).
  3. 2nd noun: in kasra (yes, without a preposition!).

Teen rules: pehla par الـ nahin, pehla par tanween nahin, doosra zer mein. Drill mein teenon ko ek saath bolo.

Diagram it

كِتَابُ → مُحَمَّدٍ
1st noun = owned · مُضَاف · single ḍamma · no الـ, no tanween
2nd noun = owner · مُضَاف إِلَيْهِ · in kasra

Examples with names

كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ Muhammad's book
بَيْتُ عَلِيٍّ Ali's house
قَلَمُ حَامِدٍ Hamid's pen
ابْنُ يَاسِرٍ Yasir's son
عَمُّ خَالِدٍ Khalid's paternal uncle

Examples with الـ-nouns

كِتَابُ الْوَلَدِ the boy's book
بَيْتُ الْإِمَامِ the imam's house
قَلَمُ الْمُدَرِّسِ the teacher's pen
سَيَّارَةُ الْإِمَامِ the imam's car

Second noun: single kasra (no doubling, because الـ-nouns don't take tanween).

⚠️ The classic iḍāfa mistakes

  • ❌ الْكِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ — الـ on the first noun. Wrong!
  • ❌ كِتَابٌ مُحَمَّدٍ — tanween on the first noun. Wrong!
  • ❌ كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٌ — second noun not in kasra. Wrong!
  • ✅ كِتَابُ مُحَمَّدٍ — correct! All 3 rules satisfied.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

Today's grammar is on every page — iḍāfa is one of the most common Qur'anic constructions.

Iḍāfa Qur'an ke har page par hai — aaj seekhi grammar fauran pehchaan mein aayegi.

Surah Al-Fatihah · 1:2

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

"All praise is for Allah, Lord of the worlds."

"Sab tareef Allah ke liye hai, jahaanon ka Rabb."

You see:

  • لِلَّهِ — the لِـ + الْ = لِلْـ rule, in action!
  • رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ — a classic iḍāfa (no الـ/tanween on first, kasra on second).

Surah Al-Fatihah · 1:4

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

"Master of the Day of Recompense."

"Roz-e-jaza ka maalik."

A double iḍāfa — three nouns chained! مَالِكِ (Master) of يَوْمِ (the Day) of الدِّينِ (Recompense). All in kasra. Preview of Ch 5+.

Surah Al-Fatihah · 1:1

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

"In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful."

Inside بِسْمِ ٱللَّـهِ is an iḍāfa: اسْم (name) + اللَّـه (Allah). First noun no الـ/tanween, second in kasra. Every part of this opening line is Ch 4 grammar.

(بِـ ek aur preposition hai — aage chapter mein milegi. Aaj sirf iḍāfa pakdaayi gayi.)

Surah Al-Baqarah · 2:284 (opening)

لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ

"To Allah belongs whatever is in the heavens..."

"Allah hi ka hai jo aasmaanon mein hai."

Pure possession with لِـ — "X li-Y" = "X belongs to Y". Today's rule, exactly.
