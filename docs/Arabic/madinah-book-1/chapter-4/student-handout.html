الدَّرْسُ الرَّابِعُ

Chapter 4 — Student Handout

in · on · from · to  ·  "where?" & "where from?"  ·  went / went out

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Use the four small words in, on, from, to (فِي · عَلَى · مِنْ · إِلَى).
  2. Change a word's ending to a kasra (zer) after one of these words.
  3. Ask and answer "where is it?" (أَيْنَ ؟) and "where are you from?" (مِنْ أَيْنَ ؟).
  4. Use "went" (ذَهَبَ) and "went out" (خَرَجَ), and learn place + country words.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · Four little words for place — حُرُوف الجَرّ

These four words go before a noun and tell us where:

ArabicRead itEnglishRoman Urdu
فِيfīinmein
عَلَىٰʿalāonpar
مِنْminfromse
إِلَىٰilāto / towardstak / ki taraf

💡 Say it right

عَلَىٰ and إِلَىٰ end in a long "aa" sound — ʿalā, ilā (NOT "ʿalay / ilay"). The tail ـى is a hidden alif.

Rule 2 · The big rule — the ending becomes ـِ (zer)

When you put one of these four words in front of a noun, the noun's ending changes to a kasra (zer):

الْبَيْتُ  →  فِي الْبَيْتِ

al-baytu → fī l-bayti · "the house → in the house" · pesh se zer

عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِʿalā l-maktabion the deskmez par
فِي الْمَسْجِدِfī l-masjidiin the mosquemasjid mein
إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِilā l-madrasatito the schoolmadrasa ki taraf
مِنَ الْبَيْتِmina l-baytifrom the houseghar se

⚠️ Two things to remember

1. The ending becomes zer after the four words — fī l-baytI, not fī l-baytU.
2. When مِنْ meets الـ, say it as one smooth sound: "mina-l" — مِنَ الْهِنْدِ (mina l-hindi), not "min al-hindi".

Rule 3 · Asking "where?" — أَيْنَ ؟ and مِنْ أَيْنَ ؟

Two question words. Both are answered using one of the four place-words.

أَيْنَ ؟ — "where (is)?"

Ask about a place. Answer with فِي / عَلَىٰ.

أَيْنَ الْكِتَابُ ؟

ayna l-kitābu? — "Where is the book?"

هُوَ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ.

huwa ʿalā l-maktabi — "It is on the desk."

مِنْ أَيْنَ ؟ — "where from?"

Ask about origin. Answer with مِنْ.

مِنْ أَيْنَ أَنْتَ ؟

min ayna anta? — "Where are you from?"

أَنَا مِنَ الْيَابَانِ.

anā mina l-yābāni — "I am from Japan."

⚠️ مَنْ (who) is NOT مِنْ (from)

Same letters, different vowel!
مَنْ (man, with zabar) = who  ·  مِنْ (min, with zer) = from
Example with both: مَنْ مِنَ الصِّينِ ؟ — man mina ṣ-ṣīni? — "Who is from China?"

Rule 4 · Two action words — ذَهَبَ (went) & خَرَجَ (went out)

Each action word has its own partner from the four place-words:

ذَهَبَdhahabahe went goes with إِلَى (to)
خَرَجَkharajahe went out / left goes with مِنْ (from)

خَرَجَ مِنَ الْفَصْلِ وَذَهَبَ إِلَى الْمُدِيرِ.

kharaja mina l-faṣli wa-dhahaba ilā l-mudīri
"He left the classroom and went to the director." · Woh kamre se nikla aur director ke paas gaya.

💡 An easy picture

You come out of (مِنْ) one place, and go to (إِلَى) another. خَرَجَ → مِنْ; ذَهَبَ → إِلَى.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Add the preposition and fix the ending

Put the place-word in front and change the ending to a kasra (zer). The first is done for you.

  1. الْبَيْتُ + فِي → فِي الْبَيْتِ (fī l-bayti)
  2. الْمَكْتَبُ + عَلَىٰ →
  3. الْمَسْجِدُ + إِلَىٰ →
  4. الْفَصْلُ + مِنْ →
  5. الْغُرْفَةُ + فِي →
  6. السُّوقُ + إِلَىٰ →

Exercise 2 · Choose the right preposition

Fill the blank with one of: مِنْ · إِلَى · فِي · عَلَى

  1. الْكِتَابُ الْمَكْتَبِ. (the book is ___ the desk)
  2. الطَّالِبُ الْفَصْلِ. (the student is ___ the classroom)
  3. ذَهَبَ حَامِدٌ الْجَامِعَةِ. (Hamid went ___ the university)
  4. خَرَجَ الْمُدِيرُ الْمَدْرَسَةِ. (the director left ___ the school)
  5. ذَهَبَ مُحَمَّدٌ الصِّينِ الْيَابَانِ. (Muhammad went ___ China ___ Japan)

Exercise 3 · مَنْ or مِنْ? Circle the right one

One means "who", the other means "from". Read the sentence and decide.

  1. ( مَنْ / مِنْ ) فِي الْغُرْفَةِ ؟ — "___ is in the room?"
  2. هُوَ ( مَنْ / مِنْ ) الْهِنْدِ. — "He is ___ India."
  3. ( مَنْ / مِنْ ) مِنَ الصِّينِ ؟ — "___ is from China?"
  4. أَنَا ( مَنْ / مِنْ ) الْفِلِبِّيْنِ. — "I am ___ the Philippines."

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the 15 new words — places, countries, and the two action words.
  2. Read out loud every example sentence in this handout — at least 3 times each, putting the kasra (zer) on the noun after each place-word.
  3. Do all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
  4. Look around your home: write 3 sentences saying where things are — "the X is in/on the Y" — with فِي or عَلَىٰ and the kasra ending.
