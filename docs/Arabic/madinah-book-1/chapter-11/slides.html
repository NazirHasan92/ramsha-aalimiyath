By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Say "in it": فِيهِ (m) / فِيهَا (f).

  2. Read a whole paragraph — بَيْتِي ("My House").
    poora paragraph parhna
  3. Keep "my" words bare — no الـ (بَيْتِي, not الْبَيْتِي).

📋 Quick recap — you already know all this

  • The tails: اسْمِي · اسْمُكَ · اسْمُهُ · اسْمُهَا (Ch 10) — no الـ
  • iḍāfa — owner after the thing: غُرْفَةُ أَخِي (Ch 4)
  • Demonstratives: هَذِهِ (near) · تِلْكَ (far) (Ch 7)
  • أَمَامَ · عَلَى · تَحْتَ + kasra (Ch 3/5/8) · لِي أَخٌ (Ch 10)

Today: we put it all together and read.

📖 The passage — بَيْتِي

هَذَا بَيْتِي . بَيْتِي أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدِ . بَيْتِي جَمِيلٌ . فِيهِ حَدِيقَةٌ صَغِيرَةٌ .

"This is my house. My house is in front of the mosque. My house is beautiful. In it is a small garden."

Har lafz pehchaan'te ho — bas ek saath parho.

My room

هَذِهِ غُرْفَتِي . فِيهَا نَافِذَةٌ كَبِيرَةٌ وَمِرْوَحَةٌ جَمِيلَةٌ . سَاعَتِي وَقَلَمِي وَكِتَابِي عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ . وَحَقِيبَتِي تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ .

"This is my room. In it is a big window and a beautiful fan. My watch, pen, and book are on the desk. And my bag is under the desk."

My brother & sister

هَذِهِ غُرْفَةُ أَخِي . وَتِلْكَ غُرْفَةُ أُخْتِي . لِي أَخٌ وَاحِدٌ اسْمُهُ أُسَامَةُ ، وَلِي أُخْتٌ وَاحِدَةٌ اسْمُهَا سُعَادُ .

"This is my brother's room, and that is my sister's room. I have one brother — his name is Usama; and I have one sister — her name is Su'ad."

Rule 1 · فِيهِ / فِيهَا — "in it"

فِيهِ — in it (m)

فِي + ـهُ

for a masculine word

فِيهَا — in it (f)

فِي + ـها

for a feminine word

The Ch 10 tail — now on فِي.

Read it "fee-hi" — not "fee-hu"

مَنْ فِي الْبَيْتِ ؟ — فِيهِ حَامِدٌ

✅ فِيهِ (fee-hi, kasra) · ❌ فِيهُ

فِي ke baad ـهُ ka pesh → zer ban jaata hai. Bas awaaz yaad karo: fee-hi, fee-hā.

Answer without repeating the noun

مَاذَا فِي الْحَقِيبَةِ ؟ — فِيهَا كِتَابِيWhat's in the bag? — In it is my book
مَا فِيهِ أَحَدٌthere is no one in it

Rule 2 · "My" words & iḍāfa

بَيْتِي · غُرْفَتِي · سَرِيرِي · كِتَابِي

A "my" word = "the" already → no الـ: ✅ بَيْتِي · ❌ الْبَيْتِي

Dum khud "the" ka kaam karti hai.

غُرْفَةُ أَخِي — "my brother's room"

غُرْفَةُ أَخِي

iḍāfa (Ch 4): first word غُرْفَةُ is bare (no الـ, no tanween), owner أَخِي comes second.

"Cheez phir maalik" — غُرْفَةُ أَخِي = mere bhai ka kamra.

Rule 3 · أُحِبُّ … — "I love …"

أُحِبُّ

"I love"

It's a verb — but for now just read it (like ذَهَبَ/خَرَجَ). Verbs come much later.

Abhi banao mat — sirf parho.

The love-phrases

أُحِبُّ اللَّهَ · أُحِبُّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ

"I love Allah · I love the Prophet ﷺ"

أُحِبُّ اللُّغَةَ الْعَرَبِيَّةَ

"I love the Arabic language"

The word after أُحِبُّ wears a fatḥa (zabar) — just read it as written.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

فِيهِ/فِيهَا and "love" are everywhere.

Aaj ke lafz — Qur'an mein.

Surah Al-Baqarah · 2:2 — فِيهِ

ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ

"That is the Book about which there is no doubt."

فِيهِ = "in it" — today's word, opening the longest surah.

Surah Al-Qadr · 97:4 — فِيهَا

… وَالرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم

"… and the Spirit descend therein by the permission of their Lord."

فِيهَا = "us mein" (Laylat al-Qadr) — فِيهِ ka feminine jodi.

Surah Āl ʿImrān · 3:31 — the root of "love"

قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ اللَّهَ فَاتَّبِعُونِي يُحْبِبْكُمُ اللَّهُ

"Say: if you love Allah, then follow me; Allah will love you."

Same root ح‑ب‑ب as أُحِبُّ — recognition only (those are advanced verb forms).

📋 Recap — what we covered

  1. فِيهِ / فِيهَا = "in it" — the tail on فِي (read "fee-hi")
  2. The passage بَيْتِي — "my" words + iḍāfa, no الـ
  3. غُرْفَةُ أَخِي — iḍāfa: bare first word, owner second
  4. أُحِبُّ … — "I love" (read it, don't conjugate)
