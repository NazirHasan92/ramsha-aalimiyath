الدَّرْسُ الْحَادِيَ عَشَرَ

Chapter 11 — Student Handout

بَيْتِي ("My House") — a reading lesson · فِيهِ / فِيهَا ("in it") · أُحِبُّ … ("I love …")

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

Today is a reading lesson — we read a whole paragraph of real Arabic about a boy's house, using everything you've already learned. By the end you will be able to:

  1. Say "in it": فِيهِ (for a masculine word), فِيهَا (for a feminine word).
  2. Read the passage بَيْتِي ("My House") fluently — full of "my / his / her" words.
  3. Remember a "my / your / his / her" word takes no الـ and no tanween (بَيْتِي, not الْبَيْتِي).
  4. Read the beautiful phrases أُحِبُّ اللَّهَ ("I love Allah"), أُحِبُّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ ("I love the Prophet ﷺ").

Time needed: a comfortable lesson — about 60 minutes of class + 20 minutes of reading practice at home.

📖 The reading passage — بَيْتِي ("My House")

Read it aloud, slowly. Almost every word is something you already know.

هَذَا بَيْتِي . بَيْتِي أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدِ . بَيْتِي جَمِيلٌ . فِيهِ حَدِيقَةٌ صَغِيرَةٌ .
هَذِهِ غُرْفَتِي . فِيهَا نَافِذَةٌ كَبِيرَةٌ وَمِرْوَحَةٌ جَمِيلَةٌ . هَذَا سَرِيرِي وَهَذَا كُرْسِيٌّ وَمَكْتَبِي . سَاعَتِي وَقَلَمِي وَكِتَابِي عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ . وَحَقِيبَتِي تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ . نَافِذَةُ غُرْفَتِي مَفْتُوحَةٌ .
هَذِهِ غُرْفَةُ أَخِي . وَتِلْكَ غُرْفَةُ أُخْتِي . غُرْفَةُ أَخِي كَبِيرَةٌ وَغُرْفَةُ أُخْتِي صَغِيرَةٌ . غُرْفَةُ أَخِي أَمَامَ غُرْفَتِي وَغُرْفَةُ أُخْتِي أَمَامَ الْمَطْبَخِ .
لِي أَخٌ وَاحِدٌ اسْمُهُ أُسَامَةُ ، وَلِي أُخْتٌ وَاحِدَةٌ اسْمُهَا سُعَادُ .
أَبِي وَأُمِّي فِي تِلْكَ الْغُرْفَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ .
أَنَا أُحِبُّ أَبِي وَأُمِّي . وَأُحِبُّ أَخِي وَأُخْتِي .

This is my house. My house is in front of the mosque. My house is beautiful. In it is a small garden. This is my room. In it is a big window and a beautiful fan. This is my bed, and this is a chair and my desk. My watch, my pen, and my book are on the desk. And my bag is under the desk. The window of my room is open. This is my brother's room, and that is my sister's room. My brother's room is big and my sister's room is small. My brother's room is in front of my room, and my sister's room is in front of the kitchen. I have one brother — his name is Usama; and I have one sister — her name is Su'ad. My father and mother are in that big room. I love my father and my mother. And I love my brother and my sister.

Rule 1 · فِيهِ / فِيهَا — "in it"

To say "in it" without repeating the noun, add the tail ـهُ/ـها to فِي ("in"):

WordMeansUse it for…
فِيهِin it (us mein)a masculine word (الْبَيْتُ → فِيهِ)
فِيهَاin it (us mein)a feminine word (الْحَقِيبَةُ → فِيهَا)

⚠️ Read it "fee-hi" — not "fee-hu"

After فِي, the "his" tail reads with a kasra: فِيهِ ✓ (fee-hi), not فِيهُ ✗. فِيهَا stays the same. Example: مَنْ فِي الْبَيْتِ ؟ — فِيهِ حَامِدٌ ("Who is in the house? — In it is Hamid").

Rule 2 · "My / his / her" words take no الـ

The passage is full of "my" words. A word with a tail is already "the" — so never add الـ or tanween:

بَيْتِي · غُرْفَتِي · سَرِيرِي · كِتَابِيmy house / room / bed / book
غُرْفَةُ أَخِي · نَافِذَةُ غُرْفَتِيmy brother's room · the window of my room

💡 غُرْفَةُ أَخِي = "my brother's room"

This is iḍāfa (Chapter 4): the first word (غُرْفَةُ) is bare — no الـ, no tanween — and the owner (أَخِي) comes second. "Cheez phir maalik."

Rule 3 · أُحِبُّ … — "I love …"

أُحِبُّ = "I love". For now, just read these phrases — don't worry about how verbs work (that comes much later):

أُحِبُّ أَبِي وَأُمِّيI love my father and my mother
أُحِبُّ زَمِيلِي · أُحِبُّ أُسْتَاذِيI love my colleague · I love my teacher
أُحِبُّ اللَّهَ · أُحِبُّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺI love Allah · I love the Prophet ﷺ
أُحِبُّ اللُّغَةَ الْعَرَبِيَّةَI love the Arabic language

💡 The word after أُحِبُّ wears a "zabar"

Notice اللَّهَ, النَّبِيَّ, اللُّغَةَ end in a fatḥa (zabar) — because they are the thing being loved. Just read it as written; you'll learn the reason much later.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Answer with فِيهِ or فِيهَا (lesson Exercise 1)

Answer each question, choosing فِيهِ (m) or فِيهَا (f), like the example. Example: مَنْ فِي الْبَيْتِ ؟ — فِيهِ حَامِدٌ .

  1. مَاذَا فِي الْحَقِيبَةِ ؟ — كِتَابِي وَقَلَمِي .
  2. مَنْ فِي السَّيَّارَةِ ؟ — أَبِي وَأُمِّي .
  3. مَنْ فِي هَذِهِ الْغُرْفَةِ ؟ — الْمُدِيرُ .
  4. مَنْ فِي مَسْجِدِ الْجَامِعَةِ الْآنَ ؟ — مَا أَحَدٌ .

Exercise 2 · Read & understand the passage

Read بَيْتِي aloud, then answer in Arabic.

  1. أَيْنَ بَيْتُهُ ؟ (Where is his house?) →
  2. مَا فِي غُرْفَتِهِ ؟ (What is in his room?) →
  3. مَا اسْمُ أَخِيهِ ؟ (What is his brother's name?) →
  4. مَا اسْمُ أُخْتِهِ ؟ (What is his sister's name?) →

Exercise 3 · Spot the iḍāfa

Underline the two words that make an iḍāfa, and translate. Example: غُرْفَةُ أَخِي = "my brother's room".

  1. نَافِذَةُ غُرْفَتِي →
  2. غُرْفَةُ أُخْتِي →
  3. مَسْجِدُ الْجَامِعَةِ →

Exercise 4 · Fix the mistake

Each sentence has one error (a tailed noun with الـ, or a wrong "in it"). Cross it out and write the correct form.

  1. هَذَا الْبَيْتِي . →
  2. مَنْ فِي الْبَيْتِ ؟ — فِيهُ حَامِدٌ . →
  3. هَذِهِ الْغُرْفَةُ أَخِي . →

Exercise 5 · Read the love-phrases (lesson Exercise 2)

Read each phrase aloud, then match it to its meaning.

  1. أُحِبُّ اللَّهَ —
  2. أُحِبُّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ —
  3. أُحِبُّ أُسْتَاذِي —
  4. أُحِبُّ اللُّغَةَ الْعَرَبِيَّةَ —

Exercise 6 · Translate both ways

(a) Into Arabic:

  1. This is my room. In it is a big window. —
  2. I have one sister; her name is Su'ad. —

(b) Into English:

  1. بَيْتِي أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدِ ، فِيهِ حَدِيقَةٌ صَغِيرَةٌ —
  2. حَقِيبَتِي تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ ، وَكِتَابِي عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ —

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Read the passage بَيْتِي aloud three times — until it flows smoothly.
  2. Write 4 sentences about your own room using فِيهِ/فِيهَا and "my" words (e.g. هَذِهِ غُرْفَتِي . فِيهَا سَرِيرِي).
  3. Write 2 iḍāfa phrases like غُرْفَةُ أَخِي about your family (e.g. كِتَابُ أُخْتِي).
  4. Copy the love-phrases أُحِبُّ اللَّهَ · أُحِبُّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ · أُحِبُّ اللُّغَةَ الْعَرَبِيَّةَ neatly, with their meanings.
  5. Complete all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
