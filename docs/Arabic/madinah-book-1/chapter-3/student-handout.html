الدَّرْسُ الثَّالِثُ

Chapter 3 — Student Handout

Prepositions · "Where?" · feminine "this"

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Use the 4 basic prepositions: فِي (in), عَلَى (on), إِلَى (to), مِنْ (from).
  2. Change the ending of a noun after a preposition — to kasra (ـِ or ـٍ).
  3. Ask "Where is ___?" using أَيْنَ.
  4. Use the feminine "this" — هَذِهِ — for words ending in ة.
  5. Tell apart the twin-words: مَنْ (who?) vs مِنْ (from).
  6. Know about 12 new Arabic words.

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · The four prepositions

A preposition is a small word that locates or directs something. English: "in, on, to, from". Urdu: "mein, par, tak, se". Arabic has its own little family:

ArabicRead asEnglishRoman Urdu
فِيfeein / insidemein
عَلَىʿalāon / uponpar
إِلَىilāto / towardstak / ki taraf
مِنْminfrom / out ofse

💡 Three pronunciation notes

  1. عَلَى and إِلَى both end in ى (alif-maqsoorah) — it looks like ي but sounds like long "aa". Say ʿa-laa, not "ʿa-lay". Say i-laa, not "i-lay".
  2. مِنْ ends in silent ن. Say crisp: min. No phantom vowel.
  3. When مِنْ meets الـ, the ن takes a fatḥa: مِنْ + الْبَيْتِ → مِنَ الْبَيْتِ ("minal-bayti").

Rule 2 · The ending of the noun changes to kasra

This is the most important rule of today. After one of these four prepositions, the noun's ending changes — the pesh (ـُ) becomes a zer (ـِ).

For definite nouns (with الـ): the single pesh becomes a single zer.

Default formAfter a prepositionMeaning
الْبَيْتُفِي الْبَيْتِal-baytu → fil-bayti (in the house)
الْمَكْتَبُعَلَى الْمَكْتَبِal-maktabu → ʿalal-maktabi (on the desk)
الْمَدْرَسَةُإِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِal-madrasatu → ilal-madrasati (to the school)
السُّوقُمِنَ السُّوقِas-sūqu → minas-sūqi (from the market)

For indefinite nouns (without الـ): the doubled pesh (ـٌ = "-un") becomes a doubled zer (ـٍ = "-in"). The doubling stays.

Default formAfter a prepositionMeaning
بَيْتٌفِي بَيْتٍbaytun → fee baytin (in a house)
قَلَمٌعَلَى قَلَمٍqalamun → ʿalā qalamin (on a pen)
سُوقٌإِلَى سُوقٍsūqun → ilā sūqin (to a market)
جَبَلٌمِنْ جَبَلٍjabalun → min jabalin (from a mountain)

⚠️ The most important habit to build

Every time you put one of these 4 prepositions in front of a noun — switch the ending to zer. Pesh becomes zer. Do-pesh becomes do-zer. This is not optional. If you forget, the Arabic sentence is grammatically wrong.

Roman Urdu mein: "`فِي`, `عَلَى`, `إِلَى`, `مِنْ` ke baad noun ke aakhir mein **zer** — pesh nahin."

Full sentence examples — read each aloud:

الْكِتَابُ فِي الْحَقِيبَةِ al-kitaabu fil-ḥaqeebati The book is in the bag Kitaab bag mein hai
الْقَلَمُ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ al-qalamu ʿalal-maktabi The pen is on the desk Qalam mez par hai
الْإِمَامُ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ al-imaamu fil-masjidi The imam is in the mosque Imam masjid mein hai
هَذَا مِنَ السُّوقِ haadhaa minas-sūqi This is from the market Yeh bazaar se hai

Rule 3 · أَيْنَ ؟ — "Where?"

To ask "Where is ___?", put أَيْنَ (ayna) at the start of the question.

أَيْنَ الْكِتَابُ ؟

ayna l-kitaabu? · "Where is the book?" · Kitaab kahaan hai?

The answer uses today's prepositions:

الْكِتَابُ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ

al-kitaabu ʿalal-maktabi
"The book is on the desk" · "Kitaab mez par hai"

More examples:

أَيْنَ الْقَلَمُ ؟الْقَلَمُ فِي الْحَقِيبَةِWhere is the pen? — The pen is in the bag.
أَيْنَ الْإِمَامُ ؟الْإِمَامُ فِي الْمَسْجِدِWhere is the imam? — The imam is in the mosque.
أَيْنَ السَّرِيرُ ؟السَّرِيرُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِWhere is the bed? — The bed is in the room.
أَيْنَ الْوَلَدُ ؟الْوَلَدُ فِي الْحَدِيقَةِWhere is the boy? — The boy is in the garden.

⚠️ Twin words — be careful

Two words look almost the same. Don't mix them up:

مَنْman"who?"Ch 2 — for asking about a person
مِنْmin"from"Ch 3 — a preposition

Same letters (م + ن) — only the vowel on the م is different (fatḥa for man, kasra for min). Always write the vowels so you don't get confused.

Rule 4 · هَذِهِ — "this" (for feminine nouns)

You already know هَذَا (haadhaa) = "this is..." for masculine nouns (from Ch 1).

This week you learn its partner — هَذِهِ (pronounced haadhihi) = "this is..." for feminine nouns.

هَذِهِ غُرْفَةٌ

haadhihi ghurfatun · "This is a room" · Yeh ek kamra hai

💡 How do you spot a feminine noun?

The most common sign: the noun ends in ة (called taa marbuuta — "the tied ta"). Look at the end:

  • غُرْفَةٌ, مَدْرَسَةٌ, حَدِيقَةٌ — all end in ة → all feminine → use هَذِهِ.
  • بَيْتٌ, قَلَمٌ, مَكْتَبٌ — no ة → masculine → use هَذَا.

Compare — same job, different gender:

Masculine (with هَذَا)Feminine (with هَذِهِ)Meaning
هَذَا بَيْتٌهَذِهِ غُرْفَةٌthis house / this room
هَذَا كِتَابٌهَذِهِ حَقِيبَةٌthis book / this bag
هَذَا مَسْجِدٌهَذِهِ مَدْرَسَةٌthis mosque / this school
هَذَا جَبَلٌهَذِهِ حَدِيقَةٌthis mountain / this garden

Questions also follow the gender:

مَا هَذِهِ ؟هَذِهِ حَدِيقَةٌWhat is this? — This is a garden.
مَا هَذِهِ ؟هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةٌWhat is this? — This is a car.

  2. (عَلَى) الْمَكْتَبُ →
  3. (إِلَى) الْمَدْرَسَةُ →
  4. (مِنْ) السُّوقُ →
  5. (فِي) الْغُرْفَةُ →

Exercise 3 · مَنْ or مِنْ? Twin-word check.

For each blank, decide which of the two twin-words fits.

  1. "Who is this?" — هَذَا ؟
  2. "This is from the market." — هَذَا السُّوقِ
  3. "Who is the imam?" — الْإِمَامُ ؟
  4. "The pen is from the desk." — الْقَلَمُ الْمَكْتَبِ
  5. "Who is that?" — ذَلِكَ ؟
  6. "He is from the school." — هُوَ الْمَدْرَسَةِ

Exercise 6 · Make your own — look and say

Look around the room. Write 3 "where is ___?" questions and answers using today's prepositions (فِي / عَلَى are easiest). Also write 2 sentences using هَذِهِ for feminine objects you can see (bag, car, tree, room — anything with ة).

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the 4 prepositions with their Urdu meanings: فِي=mein, عَلَى=par, إِلَى=tak, مِنْ=se.
  2. Drill the kasra rule — take 5 nouns from Ch 1 (bayt, qalam, kitaab, masjid, walad) and put each one after each of the 4 prepositions. Say aloud. fil-bayti, ʿalal-bayti, ilal-bayti, minal-bayti. Then move to the next noun.
  3. Practice haadhihi aloud 20 times with feminine nouns: haadhihi ghurfatun, haadhihi madrasatun, haadhihi ḥaqeebatun — the demonstrative is glued to the noun.
  4. Do all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
  5. Find 5 objects at home — write a "where is ___?" question and answer for each, using the right preposition.
  6. Re-read Chapters 1 + 2 vocabulary — Ch 4 will use them again.
