الدَّرْسُ الثَّالِثُ

Chapter 3 — Student Handout

"The ___" · describing things · sun & moon letters

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Add the word "the" (الـ) to any noun.
  2. Describe something — "the pen is broken", "the door is open".
  3. Know which letters make the "L" of "al-" silent (sun letters) and which keep it (moon letters).
  4. Learn about 24 new words — mostly describing words (adjectives).

Time needed: about one hour of class + 30 minutes of self-practice.

Rule 1 · الـ means "the"

Attach الـ (pronounced "al-") to the front of any noun to make it specific:

Indefinite ("a ___") Definite ("the ___") Meaning
بَيْتٌالْبَيْتُa house → the house
قَلَمٌالْقَلَمُa pen → the pen
كِتَابٌالْكِتَابُa book → the book
جَمَلٌالْجَمَلُa camel → the camel

💡 Notice the ending

The doubled ending ـٌ (-un) drops to a single ـُ (-u) when you add الـ.

baytun → al-baytu (NOT al-baytun) · "ek ghar" → "woh ghar"

Rule 2 · Describing things — "the X is Y"

Put a describing word (adjective) after a definite noun, and you have made a whole sentence:

الْقَلَمُ مَكْسُورٌ

al-qalamu maksūrun · "The pen is broken" · Qalam toota hua hai

Just like Lesson 1, there is no word for "is" — the two words sit side by side.

الْبَابُ مَفْتُوحٌal-bābu maftūḥunThe door is openDarwaaza khula hai
اللَّبَنُ حَارٌّal-labanu ḥārrunThe milk is hotDoodh garam hai
الْمَاءُ بَارِدٌal-māʼu bāridunThe water is coldPaani thanda hai
الْقَمَرُ جَمِيلٌal-qamaru jamīlunThe moon is beautifulChand khoobsoorat hai

⚠️ Keep the describing word bare

Put الـ on the thing only — never on the describing word.
✅ الْقَلَمُ مَكْسُورٌ   (the pen is broken)
The describing word مَكْسُورٌ keeps its doubled ending (tanween). Don't add الـ to it.

Rule 3 · Sun letters & Moon letters (pronunciation)

When you attach الـ, sometimes the ل is pronounced and sometimes it goes silent. It depends on the next letter.

☀️ Sun letters (the L is silent)

The L disappears. The next letter doubles (shadda).

الشَّمْسُ

ash-shams
("the sun" — NOT "al-shams")

The 14 sun letters:

ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل ن

🌙 Moon letters (the L is pronounced)

The L is heard clearly.

الْقَمَرُ

al-qamar
("the moon")

The 14 moon letters:

ء ب ج ح خ ع غ ف ق ك م هـ و ي

💡 Easy way to remember

The Sun is loud — it eats the L. The Moon is gentle — it lets the L through.

Look for the shadda (ـّ) on the next letter — that doubling means the L is silent.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1 · Add الـ and write the ending correctly

Make each word definite. Remember: the doubled ending drops to a single one.

  1. بَيْتٌ → (e.g. al-baytu)
  2. قَلَمٌ →
  3. كِتَابٌ →
  4. مَسْجِدٌ →
  5. حَجَرٌ →
  6. وَلَدٌ →

Exercise 2 · Sun or Moon? Mark each ☀️ or 🌙

Decide whether the first letter swallows the L (sun ☀️) or keeps it (moon 🌙). Then say it aloud.

  1. الْقَمَرُ —
  2. الشَّمْسُ —
  3. الرَّجُلُ —
  4. الْبَابُ —
  5. النَّجْمُ —
  6. التُّفَّاحُ —
  7. الدِّيكُ —
  8. الْفَمُ —

Exercise 3 · Fill the blank with a suitable describing word

Choose from: جَمِيلٌ · وَسِخٌ · مَفْتُوحٌ · حَارٌّ · ثَقِيلٌ · خَفِيفٌ

  1. الْحَجَرُ (the stone is ___)
  2. الْبَابُ
  3. الْقَمَرُ
  4. الْوَرَقُ
  5. الْمِنْدِيلُ
  6. اللَّبَنُ

Exercise 4 · Translate into Arabic

  1. The book is new. —
  2. The shop is open. —
  3. The water is cold. —
  4. The apple is sweet. —
  5. The student is sick. —
  6. The merchant is rich. —

Exercise 5 · Translate into English

  1. الْكِتَابُ جَدِيدٌ وَالْقَلَمُ قَدِيمٌ —
  2. الْبَيْتُ قَرِيبٌ وَالْمَسْجِدُ بَعِيدٌ —
  3. الطَّالِبُ مَرِيضٌ —
  4. الطَّبِيبُ طَوِيلٌ وَالْمَرِيضُ قَصِيرٌ —

Exercise 6 · Look around — describe it!

Find 4 things near you. For each, write a describing sentence in Arabic: the [thing] is [adjective]. Put الـ on the thing, keep the adjective bare.

🏠 Homework before next class

  1. Memorise the 24 new words (mostly adjectives) with their meanings.
  2. Read out loud every example sentence in this handout — at least 3 times each, applying the sun/moon rule.
  3. Do all 6 exercises above. Bring your answers to next class.
  4. Recite Surah al-Fātiḥah and find one moon-letter الـ (الْحَمْد) and one sun-letter الـ (الرَّحْمٰن). Notice the difference in the L.
