Rule 1 · Adding "the"

الـ

al-

= "the" (makes a noun specific)

= "the" / khaas wala

Just stick it to the front

a ___
(aam · any)		 the ___
(khaas · specific)
بَيْتٌالْبَيْتُ
قَلَمٌالْقَلَمُ
كِتَابٌالْكِتَابُ

Notice the ending

بَيْتٌ ends in ـٌ (un — doubled)

الْبَيْتُ ends in ـُ (u — single)

When you add al-, the doubling drops.

baytun → al-baytu (not al-baytun)

"al-" lagaane se tanween (doubling) hat jaati hai.

Rule 2 · Describing things

Put a describing word after a definite noun:

الْقَلَمُ مَكْسُورٌ

al-qalamu maksūrun

"The pen is broken"

"Qalam toota hua hai"

Still NO word for "is"

English: "the pen / is / broken" (3 words)

Urdu: "qalam / toota hua / hai" (3 words)

Arabic: الْقَلَمُ مَكْسُورٌ (only 2 words!)

The silence between the words IS the "is".

Khaali jagah hi "hai" ka kaam karti hai.

One rule: keep the describing word bare

الْقَلَمُ the thing → takes الـ
مَكْسُورٌ the describing word → stays bare (tanween)

الـ on the thing only — never on the describing word.

Sirf cheez par "al-"; sifat bare rehti hai.

Rule 3 · Sun & Moon Letters

When you say "al-", sometimes the L is heard, sometimes silent.

It depends on the next letter.

"al-" ke baad ka harf decide karta hai — L sunaayi degi ya gaayab.

Two examples — same "al-", different sound

🌙 Moon · chand

الْقَمَرُ

al-qamar

"the moon"

The L is pronounced.
L saaf sunaayi deti hai

☀️ Sun · sooraj

الشَّمْسُ

ash-shams

"the sun"

The L is silent, ش doubles.
L gaayab, ش double ho gayi

How to remember

☀️ The Sun is loud —
it eats the L.

The Moon 🌙 lets the L through.

Sooraj zor se bolta hai — L kha jaata hai. Chand naram hai — L guzar jaane deta hai.

The 14 Moon letters (L is heard)

ء · ب · ج · ح · خ · ع · غ · ف · ق · ك · م · هـ · و · ي

The 14 Sun letters (L silent, next doubles)

ت · ث · د · ذ · ر · ز · س · ش · ص · ض · ط · ظ · ل · ن

Sun letters — moonh ke saamne wale (zubaan daaton ke paas). Moon letters — gale/holonth wale.

The cue: look for the shadda

الشَّمْسُ — shadda on ش → L silent (sun)

الْقَمَرُ — sukūn on ل → L heard (moon)

Shadda (ـّ) agle harf par = L silent. Yeh aankhon ki nishaani hai.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

Today's sun/moon rule is a recitation rule!

Aaj ka sun/moon rule Qur'an ki tilāwat ka rule hai.

Surah Al-Fātiḥah 1:2 — Moon letter 🌙

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

"All praise is for Allah, Lord of the worlds."

ح is a moon letter → L heard: al-ḥamd.

Surah Al-Fātiḥah 1:3 — Sun letter ☀️

الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

"The Most Compassionate, the Most Merciful."

ر is a sun letter → L swallowed, ر doubles: ar-raḥmān (NOT al-raḥmān).

Isi liye hum "ar-Raḥmān" padhte hain, "al-Raḥmān" nahin.
