⚠️ Pronunciation watch — عَلَى and إِلَى

The ending ى is alif-maqsoorah — it sounds like long "aa", NOT "y".

  • عَلَى = ʿa-laa  ❌ NOT "ʿa-lay"
  • إِلَى = i-laa  ❌ NOT "i-lay"

Aakhir wali ى ya jaisi dikhti hai par sunaayi "aa" deti hai — yeh alif-maqsoorah hai. Vocalised Qur'an mein dagger-alif ke saath dikhayi deti hai: عَلَىٰ

⚠️ Pronunciation watch — مِنْ

The final ن is silent (sukoon). Say crisp: min. No phantom vowel.

But when مِنْ meets الـ, the ن takes a fatḥa:

مِنْ + الْبَيْتِ → مِنَ الْبَيْتِ

min + al-bayti → minal-bayti

"Min" + "al-" = "minal-" — ن par zabar aa jaati hai. Yeh pair yaad rakho.

Examples — feel them in sentences

الْكِتَابُ فِي الْحَقِيبَةِ The book is in the bag
"Kitaab bag mein hai"
الْقَلَمُ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ The pen is on the desk
"Qalam mez par hai"
هَذَا مِنَ السُّوقِ This is from the market
"Yeh bazaar se hai"

Rule 2 · The big rule of Chapter 3

After one of the 4 prepositions,
the noun's ending becomes kasra.

Preposition ke baad noun ke aakhir mein zer (ـِ) aa jaati hai. Pesh nahin — zer.

This is the most important rule of today — if you forget it, the Arabic is grammatically wrong.

Definite nouns — ـُ becomes ـِ

DefaultAfter preposition
الْبَيْتُفِي الْبَيْتِ
al-baytu → fil-bayti
الْمَكْتَبُعَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ
al-maktabu → ʿalal-maktabi
الْمَدْرَسَةُإِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ
al-madrasatu → ilal-madrasati

Indefinite nouns — ـٌ becomes ـٍ

The doubling stays. Pesh becomes zer.

DefaultAfter preposition
بَيْتٌفِي بَيْتٍ
baytun → fee baytin
قَلَمٌعَلَى قَلَمٍ
qalamun → ʿalā qalamin
سُوقٌإِلَى سُوقٍ
sūqun → ilā sūqin

"-un" hat gaya — "-in" aa gaya. Doubling baqi — par direction badal gayi (upar se neeche).

Side by side — pesh family vs zer family

Default (pesh / pesh-tanween)

بَيْتٌ · الْبَيْتُ

baytun · al-baytu

Mubtada' / subject form

After preposition (zer / zer-tanween)

فِي بَيْتٍ · فِي الْبَيْتِ

fee baytin · fil-bayti

After فِي / عَلَى / إِلَى / مِنْ

⚠️ The mistake to catch on day one

❌ Wrong: "fee baytun", "ʿalal-maktabu", "minal-baytu"

✅ Right: "fee baytin", "ʿalal-maktabi", "minal-bayti"

Do chapters tak pesh-pesh padhi hai — aaj se preposition ke baad zer. Pakka rule.

Rule 3 · Asking "Where?"

أَيْنَ

ayna

= "where?" — to ask for a location

= "kahaan?" — jagah poochne ke liye

The pattern

أَيْنَ الْكِتَابُ ؟

"Where is the book?" · "Kitaab kahaan hai?"

⬇️
الْكِتَابُ عَلَى الْمَكْتَبِ

"The book is on the desk" · "Kitaab mez par hai"

The answer uses today's prepositions + the kasra rule!

More Q&A examples

أَيْنَ الْقَلَمُ ؟ الْقَلَمُ فِي الْحَقِيبَةِ
"Where is the pen? — The pen is in the bag."
أَيْنَ الْإِمَامُ ؟ الْإِمَامُ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ
"Where is the imam? — The imam is in the mosque."
أَيْنَ السَّرِيرُ ؟ السَّرِيرُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ
"Where is the bed? — The bed is in the room."

⚠️ Twin words — مَنْ vs مِنْ

Two words, same letters, different vowel — and different meanings!

How do you spot a feminine noun?

Look at the end: ة (taa marbuuta).

If a noun ends in ة → feminine → use هَذِهِ.

Aakhir mein ة (gol-mol wali "h" jaisi) dikhi? Feminine hai. هَذِهِ lagao.

✅ Feminine (with ة)

غُرْفَةٌ · مَدْرَسَةٌ · حَدِيقَةٌ

room · school · garden

هَذِهِ goes with these

✅ Masculine (no ة)

بَيْتٌ · قَلَمٌ · مَكْتَبٌ

house · pen · desk

هَذَا goes with these

Examples — read aloud

هَذِهِ غُرْفَةٌ This is a room Yeh ek kamra hai
هَذِهِ مَدْرَسَةٌ This is a school Yeh ek school hai
هَذِهِ حَقِيبَةٌ This is a bag Yeh ek bag hai
مَا هَذِهِ ؟ — هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةٌ What is this? — This is a car.

🎯 Putting it all together

A sentence using all four of today's concepts:

أَيْنَ الْحَقِيبَةُ ؟ — هَذِهِ الْحَقِيبَةُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ

ayna l-ḥaqeebatu? — haadhihi l-ḥaqeebatu fil-ghurfati

"Where is the bag? — This bag is in the room."

"Bag kahaan hai? — Yeh bag kamre mein hai."

In one sentence: أَيْنَ, هَذِهِ, the preposition فِي, and the kasra rule (on الْغُرْفَةِ).

📿 Connect to the Qur'an

Today's prepositions are everywhere in the Qur'an — you'll start recognising them right away.

Aaj ki prepositions Qur'an mein har jagah aati hain — aap fauran pehchaan lenge.

Surah Al-Falaq 113:1–2

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۝ مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ

"...from the evil of what He created."

"Us cheez ke shar se jo Us ne paida ki."

You see مِنْ — and the kasra ending on شَرِّ (after the preposition).

Surah An-Nas 114:6

مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ

"From [among] the jinn and mankind."

You see the مِنَ الْ bridge (ن with a fatḥa), plus kasra endings on الْجِنَّةِ and النَّاسِ.

Surah Ash-Shura 42:52 (ending)

لَتَهْدِي إِلَىٰ صِرَاطٍ مُّسْتَقِيمٍ

"...you guide to a straight path."

You see إِلَى (with the dagger-alif on top, showing the "aa" sound), and the indefinite kasra-tanween ("-in") on صِرَاطٍ.

Surah At-Takwir 81:26

فَأَيْنَ تَذْهَبُونَ

"Where then are you going?"

"To phir tum kahaan jaa rahe ho?"

You see أَيْنَ — the "where?" question word, just learned today!
