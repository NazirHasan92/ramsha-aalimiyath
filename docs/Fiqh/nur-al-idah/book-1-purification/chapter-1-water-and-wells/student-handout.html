Chapter 1 — Water & Wells · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · My first fiqh chapter

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

This is the very first chapter of fiqh — and it's about water. Why? Because every act of worship — prayer, tawaf, even touching the Mushaf — requires you to be in a state of purity. And the cause of that purity is water.

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. Define tahara (purification) — paaki ki qaanooni haalat.
  2. Name the 7 sources of pure water — from memory.
  3. Tell apart the 5 types of water — and give an example of each.
  4. Decide whether water is still usable when something falls in it.
  5. Know what to do with leftover water (su'r) from animals — cat, dog, horse, etc.
  6. Know the well rules — when to throw out all the water, and when not to worry.

Source: everything in this handout comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text).

Rule 1 · الطَّهَارَة & the 7 Sources of Water

Tahara (paaki) has two meanings:

  • General: cleanliness — physical or spiritual. (The Qur'an talks of purifying the soul too.)
  • Legal (fiqhi): the legal state required for prayer — of your body, clothes, and the place where you pray. The way you reach it: clean water (or, when no water is available, clean earth for tayammum).

Urdu mein: Tahara aam zubaan mein "paaki" ya "safaai" hai. Fiqh mein yeh ek qaanooni haalat hai — namaz ke liye zaroori. Teen jagah: badan, kapde, jagah. Pohonchne ka zariya: paani (ya jab paani na ho — mitti, tayammum).

Allah says in the Qur'an:

"He caused rain to descend from the heaven, to clean you therewith." (Q 25:48)

💡 Note — modern tap water

Tap water ultimately comes from one of these 7 sources (usually a river or well). Bottled mineral water comes from a spring or well. So in everyday life, both are valid for wudu and ghusl.

🕊️ The master hadith of the chapter — remember this

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Water is pure, and is not rendered impure by anything, except a thing that alters its colour, taste or smell."

Three things to check: colour · taste · smell (rang · zaayqa · boo). If none of them changed → water is still pure.

Rule 2 · The 5 Types of Water

This is the heart of the chapter. The book divides water into 5 types, based on two separate questions:

  1. Is the water itself pure? (Kya paani khud paak hai?)
  2. Can it purify other things? (Kya yeh doosri cheezon ko paak kar sakta hai?)
Type Pure? Purifying? Example
1
mutlaq		 ✅ Yes ✅ Yes Tap water, rain, river — plain water
2
disliked		 ✅ Yes ✅ Yes — but disliked Small saucer the cat drank from
3
musta'mal		 ✅ Yes ❌ No The water dripping in your wudu basin
4
najis		 ❌ Filthy ❌ No A small bucket of still water with a drop of blood in it
5
mashkuk		 ✅ Yes ❓ Doubtful Water a donkey or mule has drunk from

Urdu mein: Paach qism: (1) mutlaq — khaalis paani, sab kuch karo. (2) makruh — bili ka peeya, paak hai par doosra paani ho to wahi behtar. (3) musta'mal — istemaal ho chuka, paak hai par wudu nahin. (4) najis — gandga gir gayi, chhoro. (5) mashkuk — gadhe/khachchar ka peeya, wudu phir tayammum phir namaz.

💡 The big insight

"Pure" and "purifying" are two different things. A water can be pure (clean, no filth) but no longer purifying (it has already done its job). This is the key to understanding Type 2, 3, and 5.

Battery wali misaal: charged thi, ek dafa use ho gayi, ab khaali hai. Paak hai par 'kaam karne wali' nahin rahi.

⚠️ Important — used water (Type 3)

Water that has already been used for wudu or ghusl is called musta'mal. It is still PURE — you can use it to wash filth off your body or clothes (urine, blood, wine). But you cannot use it for another wudu or ghusl.

When does water become "used"? Right after the drop falls off your hand into the basin.

Rule 3 · When water gets contaminated

Water can have things fall into it. The book gives three separate tests:

When a solid mixes in

Ask: does the water still flow thin and naturally?

  • Yes → still water. Wudu OK. (Even if the colour changes — e.g. saffron water.)
  • No (e.g. cooked-down lentils, thickened) → no longer water.

Urdu mein: Solid mile to bahaav ka test. Agar patli dhaar mein behta hai — paani. Agar gaadha ho gaya (jaise daal pakaane se) — paani nahin raha.

🕊️ Hadith on lotus leaves (Bukhari 1268)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said about a man who died during Hajj: "Wash him with water and lotus leaves, and shroud him in two garments."

The Prophet (ﷺ) called for water with leaves — proof that solids don't disqualify water as long as it still flows.

When a liquid mixes in

The rule depends on how many characteristics (colour, taste, smell) the liquid has:

  • 2-characteristic liquid (e.g. milk — colour + taste): if 1 appears in water → overwhelmed (no longer water).
  • 3-characteristic liquid (e.g. vinegar — colour + taste + smell): if 2 appear in water → overwhelmed.

Mnemonic: "Aik mein se aik, ya teen mein se do."

Urdu mein: Doodh jaisi liquid (rang + zaayqa) mile to ek nishaani aane par paani kharab. Sirka jaisi liquid (rang + zaayqa + boo) mile to do nishaaniyaan aane par kharab.

When filth (najasa) falls in — the small/large/running rule

(10 × 10 arms or more)		 Filthy only if colour, taste, or smell changes.

💡 Measurement note

1 arm (dhirā') = from fingertip to elbow ≈ 48 cm. So 10 arms ≈ 4.8 metres. A pond bigger than 5m × 5m is "large still water".

Urdu mein: Chhota paani (najuk) — koi bhi najasa girne par napaak, chaahe nazar aaye ya nahin. Bara paani (raham wala) aur behta paani — sirf rang/zaayqa/boo badle to napaak. Yaad: ek hath ≈ 48 cm; 10 hath x 10 hath ≈ 5 metre x 5 metre.

Rule 4 · السُّؤْر · Leftover water from animals

When a small quantity of water has been drunk from by an animal, what remains is called su'r (jhoota paani). The ruling depends on the animal.

Note: river or ocean water is never su'r — only small quantities count.

Urdu mein: Jhoota paani sirf chhote bartan ka hota hai — nadi/samandar ka paani su'r nahin kehlaata, chaahe hazaaron log peeyen.

Category Status Animals
1 ✅ Pure & purifying Human (no filth in mouth) · Horse · Halal-meat animals (camel, cow, sheep, goat)
2 ❌ Filthy — forbidden Dog · Pig · Predatory land animal (wolf, fox, cheetah)
3 😐 Disliked when plain water is available Cat · Stray chicken · Predatory bird (eagle, falcon, glede) · House rat / snake.
(Scorpion is NOT disliked.)
4 ❓ Doubtful in purifying — do wudu + tayammum + pray Mule · Donkey

💡 Why predatory birds differ from predatory land animals

Predatory land animals drink with their tongue — wet with saliva (impure) → leftover filthy.

Predatory birds drink with their beak — beaks are bone, which is pure → leftover only disliked.

⚠️ Note on the dog

Only the dog's saliva makes water filthy. The dog's body otherwise is pure. So: if a dog jumps into a well, exits alive, and its tongue didn't touch the water → the water is still pure.

⚠️ Note on chickens

Stray chicken (roaming free, might eat filth) → leftover disliked. Caged chicken → leftover NOT disliked (Category 1).

Urdu mein quick yaadasht: Bili → makruh · Kutta → najis · Ghoda → paak · Gadha → mashkook · Kabootar (caged) → paak · Baaz → makruh · Bheriya → najis · Bichchu → paak (makruh nahin!). Yaad rakhne ki sab se aham nukta: shikari zameeni jaanwar (zubaan se peete) napaak; shikari parinde (chonch se peete) sirf makruh.

Rule 5 · الْآبَار · Wells & their purification

A well is considered "small water" by default, so contamination is taken seriously. The book gives three tiers of response.

Tier A — ALL water must be removed

In any of these cases, every drop of water must be withdrawn from the well:

  1. Any impurity falls into a small well — even a single drop of blood or alcohol.
  2. A pig falls in — even if it comes out alive and its tongue didn't touch the water (because all parts of a pig are impure).
  3. A dog, sheep, or human DIES in the well.
  4. Any bloated dead animal is in the well — even a small one (bloating means it has been there a while; filth has spread).

Precedent: When a man died in Zamzam, Ibn Abbas and Ibn al-Zubair (RA) ordered all the water to be withdrawn — many Companions were present, none objected.

Urdu mein: "Saara paani nikalo" wale 4 cases yaad rakho: (1) chhoti najasa chhote kuwein mein · (2) sooar (zinda ya murda) · (3) kutta / bakri / insaan ki maut · (4) phoola hua jaanwar.

Tier B — Bucket counts (when you can't drain it all)

What diedBuckets to expel
Bloated animal (when "all water out" is impossible)200
Chicken, cat, or similar40
Rat or similar20

Buckets are average size — usually the well's own bucket. This action also purifies the bucket, rope, and the cleaner's hand.

Urdu mein: Phoola hua jaanwar → 200 dol, murghi / bili → 40 dol, chuha → 20 dol. Yeh numbers Imam Abu Yusuf aur Imam Hasan ki ijtihad hai — directly Quran/Hadith mein nahin. Asool yeh hai: jitni badi cheez gir gayi, utna zyada paani nikaalo.

Tier C — Things that do NOT ruin well water

  1. Small amount of livestock dung (camel, sheep, horse, cow, mule, donkey) — wells are uncovered; wind blows it in.
  2. Droppings of halal birds (pigeons, sparrows).
  3. Death of bloodless creatures — fish, frog, mosquito, fly, wasp, scorpion, aquatic creatures.
  4. Any human or halal-meat animal falling in — provided it exits alive and had no filth on its body when it fell.
  5. Mule, donkey, or predatory bird falling in — provided it exits alive.

🕊️ The pigeon hadith (Tabarani 20/443)

The Prophet (ﷺ) thanked the pigeon and said: "Verily, it nested on the door of the cave until I was safe, and may Allah reward it with the masjid as its home."

And Ibn Mas'ud (RA) wiped away the droppings of a bird with his hand.

This is the textual basis for why halal-bird droppings don't ruin well water.

The "unknown duration" rule

If a dead animal is found in your well and nobody knows when it fell in:

  • Not bloated → water ruled impure for 1 day & 1 night. People who used it for wudu must repeat 1 day + night of prayer.
  • Bloated → water ruled impure for 3 days & 3 nights. People who used it for wudu/ghusl must repeat 3 days + nights of prayer.
  • Exception: if the water was only used for washing filthy clothes (not wudu/ghusl) → just wash the clothes again, no prayer repetition.

Urdu mein: Murda jaanwar mile, waqt ka pata nahin — agar phoola hua nahin to 1 din-raat ki namaz dohraao; phoola hua ho to 3 din-raat ki. Sirf kapde dhone mein use kiya tha to nayi se dho lo — namaz dohrane ki zaroorat nahin. Yeh rule kuwein ko regularly check karne ki adat dalwane ke liye hai.
