# Part A · Understanding the chapter

Before you can teach this chapter, you need to see what it is *actually* doing. On the surface it looks like a list of mechanical rules — "200 buckets if a bloated animal dies", "20 buckets for a rat", "throw out all the water if a pig falls in". Underneath, the book is silently teaching a **coherent system of purity** with five deep ideas.

**Yaad rakho:** Chapter dekhne mein bohot saare alag-alag qawaaneen jaise lagti hai — par andar se yeh ek behad logical, mantiqi nizaam hai. Tumhe rules ratta nahin maarna — tumhe samajhna hai *kyun* har rule wahin hai jahaan hai. Phir cohort ko sirf wajib hisse batao, baqi *"yeh aage ki taʿleem hai"*.

Work through the six concepts below at your own pace. For each, ask yourself: *can I explain this to someone who has never read fiqh before?* (*Kya main yeh kisi aam shakhs ko samjha sakti hoon jisne fiqh kabhi nahin padhi?*) If yes — move on. If not — re-read.

## 1 · The big picture — why "purification" is the first chapter of fiqh

Nur al-Idah's very first book is **Kitab al-Tahara** — *The Book of Purification* (*tahara — paaki / safaai*). Not prayer. Not fasting. Purification.

Why? Because **every act of worship — salah, tawaf, touching the Mushaf — has tahara as its precondition.** If the foundation is broken, the building above it collapses. Nur al-Idah's author opens with this on purpose.

The text defines **tahara** in two senses:

- **Linguistic (lughwi):** cleanliness — physical or spiritual. The Qur'an says *"Take from their wealth so that you might purify and sanctify them"* (Q9:103). Here, *tahara* is purifying the *soul*.
- **Juristic (shar'i):** "the ruling given to the place where prayer is concerned (the body, the clothes, the area where one prays!), the cause being **clean water** or **clean earth** which is deemed suitable for tayammum when there is no water." (Nur al-Idah)

So in fiqh, *tahara* isn't a vague feeling of cleanliness. It is a **legal state** of the body, clothes, and prayer-area. And the **cause** that gets you into that state is one of two things: water, or — in its absence — clean earth.

**Yaad rakho:** Tahara aam zubaan mein "safaai" hai. Fiqh mein yeh ek **legal status** hai — namaz padhne ki ijazat ke liye zaroori. Aur is status tak pohonchne ke do hi raaste hain: paani (asal), ya phir mitti (jab paani na ho — tayammum). Yeh Chapter 1 ka asli mauzu hai — *paani*.

## 2 · The 7 sources of permissible water

Nur al-Idah opens with: *"The types of water permissible for purification are seven:"*

> Rain water · Ocean water · River water · Well water · Melted snow · Melted hail · Spring water.
>
> *(Maa' al-matar · maa' al-bahr · maa' al-nahr · maa' al-bi'r · maa' al-thalj · maa' al-barad · maa' al-'ayn)*

The proof is two-fold:

1. **Qur'an (Q25:48):** *"He (Allah) caused rain to descend from the heaven, to clean you therewith."*
2. **Two Hadith of the Prophet (ﷺ):**
   - *"Water is pure and is not rendered impure by anything, except a thing that alters its colour, taste or smell."*
   - On ocean water specifically: *"Its water is pure, and the dead things in it are permissible."*

Notice what the book is doing: it defines purifying water **positively** — these 7 natural sources — not negatively (*"water is anything not chemical"*). The list is finite and clear.

### The hidden principle

All 7 sources share one feature: they are **naturally occurring, mineral-free water that has not been processed** for any other purpose. Tap water in a modern home? Yes — it flows from a well or river ultimately. Bottled mineral water? Yes — it's spring or well water. **Rose water, juice, broth?** No — these are not in the 7.

**Yaad rakho:** Saat asal paani hain — barish, samandar, nadi, kuwaan, baraf, ola, chashma. Inhi se asal tahara hoti hai. Sharbat, gulaab-jal, soup, juice — yeh paani nahin hain. Aaj kal nal ka paani in saat mein se hi aata hai, isliye theek hai.

### The hadith you must remember

> *"Water is pure and is not rendered impure by anything, except a thing that alters its colour, taste or smell."*

This single hadith governs **the entire chapter**. It says: water stays pure unless **colour / taste / smell** changes. This three-fold test (*lawn · ta'm · reeh*) comes back again and again throughout the chapter — for running water, for wells, for large bodies of water. **Burn this into your memory.**

**Yaad rakho:** Hadith mein teen cheezein hain: **rang · zaayqa · boo**. Jab tak yeh teen mein se ek bhi nahin badli, paani paak hai. Yeh teen-naam ki nishani har jagah cheque karni hai chapter mein. Yaad kar lo: *rang-zaayqa-boo*.

## 3 · The 5 types of water — the heart of the chapter

After the 7 sources, the book divides water into **5 types** based on its **purifying capacity**. This is where students get confused, because the 5 types use a two-dimensional grid that the book never draws explicitly. Let me draw it for you.

There are **two separate questions** to ask about any water:

| Question | Possible answer |
|---|---|
| **A.** Is the water itself pure? *(taahir fee nafsihi — apni zaat mein paak)* | Yes / No |
| **B.** Can it purify other things? *(mutahhir li-ghayrihi — doosri cheezon ko paak karne wala)* | Yes / No / Disliked / Doubtful |

A water can be **pure** without being **purifying**. Read that sentence again — this is the single biggest insight of the chapter, and most Urdu-speaking adults trip over it.

### The full grid

| Type | Pure? | Purifies others? | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — fully | Plain unmixed water (*ma' mutlaq*) — rain, river, etc. |
| **2** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — but **disliked** (*makruh*) | Small water a cat (or similar) drank from |
| **3** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No | **Used water** (*ma' musta'mal*) — already used for wudu/ghusl |
| **4** | ❌ Filthy | ❌ No | Small still water with impurity fallen in |
| **5** | ❓ Doubtful (for *purifying* — not for *purity itself*) | ❓ Doubtful | Leftover water of a mule or donkey |

### Type 1 — plain water (ma' mutlaq)

This is the default. *Mutlaq* literally means "unrestricted, absolute" (*be-qaid · khaalis · jis mein kuch mila hua nahin*). When you turn on the tap, fill a bucket from a well, collect rain — this is Type 1. Use it freely.

### Type 2 — pure & purifying, but disliked

A cat (or similar small animal — see §5 below) drinks from a **small** quantity of water. The water is still pure. Wudu with it is still valid. But it is **disliked** (*makruh*) to use it if plain water is available.

**Yaad rakho:** Bili ne thoda paani peeya — paani abhi bhi paak hai, wudu sahi hai. Lekin agar doosra saaf paani available hai, to is se wudu karna *makruh* hai. Mehez "be-adabi" nahin — fiqhi hukm hai.

### Type 3 — used water (ma' musta'mal) · the trickiest concept

This is **water that has already done its purification job**. Once you've used water to wash your face in wudu — *that water*, which drips off, is now *musta'mal*. It is:

- **Pure in itself** (taahir) — you wouldn't say it's "dirty"
- **NOT purifying** (ghayr mutahhir) — you cannot use it for wudu or ghusl again

**When does water become "used"?** *"Water becomes used right after it separates from the body."* So the water on your hands during wudu is still fine — but the moment a drop falls off and lands in a basin, that drop is now musta'mal.

There's a beautiful nuance: musta'mal water **can still clean physical filth** (*najasa haqiqiyya*) like urine, blood, wine from your clothes or body. It just can't lift *ritual impurity* (*hadath*) — wudu and ghusl.

> Used water is fit for the purification of physical filth (*najasa haqiqiyya*) from the body or clothes, such as urine, pus and wine — but is not purifying for major or minor impurity (*hadath*), and therefore unfit for wudu and ghusl.

There's also a subtle rule about **wudu upon wudu**: if you already have wudu and renew it intending the pleasure of Allah, fine — but the second wudu **must be in a different place** from the first (i.e. fresh water, fresh location). If you redo it in the same spot, the second water is **not** counted as musta'mal — but doing so is *disliked*.

**Yaad rakho:** Musta'mal paani sab se ulajh dene wala concept hai. **Paak hai** — gandga nahin. **Magar wudu/ghusl nahin ho sakti** isse. *Haan*, kapdon par urine lagaya to **isse dho sakte ho** — woh kaam ho jaayega. Bas ritual purification (*hadath uthana*) nahin ho saktee. Tum agar khud yeh confuse ho jaao to class mein bhi confuse ho jaayegi — yaad rakho: "pure but not purifying".

### Type 4 — filthy water (najis)

A **small** quantity of **still** water with impurity (*najis*) fallen in. The water itself is now filthy — even if no trace of the filth appears in it.

The definition of "small" and "large" is geometric (see §4 below). For now: a household basin or small pond is "small"; a large tank or lake is "large".

You **cannot** use filthy water to remove **either** physical filth **or** ritual impurity. It's just dead water.

### Type 5 — doubtful water

A mule (*khachchar*) or donkey (*gadha*) drinks from water. The water's *purity* isn't in doubt — it's the *purifying capacity* that's doubtful.

The ruling here is **precautionary**: if no other water is available, use this water for wudu, **then** perform tayammum, **then** pray. The doubled procedure covers the doubt — if the water *does* purify, your wudu suffices; if it doesn't, your tayammum suffices.

**Yaad rakho:** Khachchar/gadhe ka jhoota paani — agar aur kuch nahin to **dono karo**: pehle is se wudu, phir tayammum, phir namaz. Donon mein se ek tum ko cover kar legi. Yeh "ihtiyaat" (precaution) ka asool hai.

### The big insight you must take from §3

Most Urdu-speaking learners assume "paani ya to paak hai ya napaak". The chapter is teaching you **purity and purifying-capacity are separate**. A water can be pure but spent (Type 3). A water can be pure but disliked (Type 2). A water can be pure but doubtful (Type 5). This **two-axis** thinking is the chapter's biggest conceptual gift.

**Yaad rakho:** Paani ke baare mein do alag-alag sawaal poochho — (1) **Khud paak hai ya napaak?** (2) **Doosri cheezon ko paak kar sakta hai ya nahin?** Pehle sawaal ko *zaat ki paaki* kaha jaata hai, doosre ko *paak karne ki sifat*. Yeh do mehwar (axes) hain — har Type in dono ka combination hai.

## 4 · What overwhelms / changes water — the "is it still water?" test

Water mixes with other things constantly. Lentils boil in it. Saffron colours it. Soap dissolves in it. Used water mixes with fresh. **When does water stop being water?** That is what this section answers.

### Rule of thumb: water must keep "flowing thin"

The author writes: *"The nature of water is to be **thin and easy flowing**. If it loses this nature through cooking or due to a substance defeating the water, then this change renders the water unfit for wudu."*

The test for whether water is still "water": **can it still flow naturally?**

### Mixing with **solids** — judged by flow

| Situation | Status |
|---|---|
| Leaves, fruit, saffron fall in — water still flows thin | Still water. Wudu OK. **Even if colour/taste/smell change.** |
| Lentils cooked until water thickens / dries | No longer water. Not OK for wudu. |

**Hadith proof:** The Prophet (ﷺ) said about a man who fell from his camel and died (during Hajj): *"Wash him with water and lotus leaves, and shroud him in two garments."* (Bukhari 1268)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said *"water and lotus leaves"* — the leaves don't ruin the water as long as it stays flowing.

**Yaad rakho:** Solid cheez mile to **bahaav ka test** karo. Agar paani abhi bhi patli dhaar mein behta hai — paak. Agar gaadha ho gaya (jaise daal pakaane se) — paani nahin raha.

### Mixing with **liquids** — judged by 3 characteristics (taste, colour, smell)

This is more delicate. Some liquids have **2** characteristics; some have **3**.

| Liquid type | Example | Trigger for "overwhelmed" |
|---|---|---|
| 2 characteristics (no smell) | Milk (colour + taste) | If **1** of the 2 appears in water |
| 3 characteristics | Vinegar (colour + taste + smell) | If **2** of the 3 appear in water |

**The principle:** "*one of two*", or "*two of three*". *(Aik mein se aik, ya teen mein se do.)*

### Mixing with liquids that **have no characteristic** — judged by weight

What about a liquid that's clear, tasteless, odourless? Like:

- *Used water* (musta'mal — but that's still water-like, debate aside)
- *Rose water that has lost its smell*

The book gives a precise weight rule: if **2 ratls** of such liquid mix with **1 ratl** of plain water, wudu is **not** permitted. If the opposite (plain water majority), wudu **is** permitted.

- **1 ratl (Baghdad)** = 384.240 grams
- **2 ratls** ≈ 769 grams

**Yaad rakho:** Be-rang, be-zaayqa, be-boo liquid mile to **wazn** ka muqaabla — agar mila hua zyada (2:1) to paani nahin raha. Agar paani zyada (1:2) — paak. Roz-marra mein yeh case kam aata hai — bas yaad rahe ke "wazn ka rule" exist karta hai.

### Mixing with **filth** (najis) — the small vs large distinction

Now we get to the chapter's most-quoted rule. When **filth** falls into water:

| Water | Still water | Ruling |
|---|---|---|
| **Small** still water (< 10×10 arms) | Filth falls in | **Filthy — even if no trace appears.** |
| **Large** still water (≥ 10×10 arms) | Filth falls in | **Not filthy unless taste/colour/smell appears.** |
| **Running water** (any size) | Filth falls in | **Not filthy unless taste/colour/smell appears.** |

**Definitions:**
- **Arm's length** (*dhirā'*) = from fingertips to elbow = ~48 cm
- **Small** = less than 10×10 arms (square), or circumference < 36 arms (circular)
- **Large** = the depth is such that *if you scoop water, the base of the pool doesn't appear*
- **Running water** = *"if you scoop once and scoop again, it's not the same water"*

So a pond ~5×5 m square is exactly at the threshold (10 arms × 48 cm ≈ 4.8 m). Anything bigger = large.

**Yaad rakho:** Bara paani (10×10 hath) — sirf rang/zaayqa/boo badle to napaak. Chhota paani — koi bhi najasa giri to napaak, dikhe ya na dikhe. Behta paani — bade paani ki tarah; sirf nishaani badle to napaak. **Ek hath** ≈ ungli se kohni tak ≈ 48 cm.

### A side-note: the Shafi'i 216-litre rule

Imam Shafi'i defined "small water" differently — as **less than qullatayn** (≈ 216 litres). The Hadith: *"If the water reaches qullatayn (or 216 litres), it does not carry filth."* (Reported by the five, and Ibn Habban graded it as sound — the book's spelling of Ibn Ḥibbān.)

The Hanafis explain this hadith by noting that *the narrations conflict* — some say *one bulla*, some say *three*, some say *forty*. So they don't take qullatayn as a fixed threshold and use the 10×10 arms rule instead.

**Yaad rakho (apne liye — class mein zaroorat na ho to mat batao):** Shafi'i log "qullatayn" (216 litre) ke aas-paas chalein hain — Hanafi log 10×10 hath par chalein hain. Yeh ek classical farq hai jo tumhare class ke ahel-hadith-paksh waale classmates poochh sakte hain. Jawab: *"Hanafi madhhab ki dalil 10×10 hath hai; qullatayn ki riwayat conflict karti hain."*

## 5 · Su'r — the leftover water rule · 4 animal categories

After an animal drinks from a **small** quantity of water, the remaining water is called **su'r** (*remnant · jhoota paani*). Note the precise definition: *"river or ocean water cannot be termed su'r even if thousands of people or animals drink from it"* — because those are not *small* quantities.

Su'r is divided into 4 categories. Each animal-class fits in one category:

### 4 categories

| # | Category | Animals | Why? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Pure & purifying** | Human · Horse · Halal-meat animals (camel, cow, sheep, goat…) | Their saliva is pure. Wudu OK. |
| **2** | **Filthy — forbidden** | Dog · Pig · Predatory land animals (cheetah, wolf, fox) | Saliva is impure; pig is wholly impure. |
| **3** | **Disliked when plain water available** | Cat · Stray chicken · Predatory birds (eagle, falcon, glede) · House rat / snake | See reasoning below |
| **4** | **Doubtful (in purifying)** | Mule · Donkey | Wudu + tayammum + pray |

### Notable details (the "kyun" — the why)

- **Human exception:** if there is *filth in the human's mouth* (e.g. they just drank alcohol), their su'r is no longer Type 1. Otherwise — pure.
- **Horse:** included because the horse is honoured in Sacred law. (Even though horse meat is debated.) The book says: *"the leftover water of the horse is pure according to the majority."*
- **Predatory land animal vs predatory bird:** Critical distinction.
  - Predatory **land animal** (wolf, fox, cheetah) drinks with **tongue** — wet with saliva — *saliva impure* → leftover **filthy**.
  - Predatory **bird** (eagle, falcon) drinks with **beak** — beaks are **bone, which is pure** → leftover only **disliked**.
- **Cat:** the cat is "house-roaming" (*ghar mein ghoomne wali*). Its mouth contacts unknown things. So *disliked*, not filthy.
- **Stray chicken vs caged chicken:** Free chicken roams → might eat filth → leftover **disliked**. **Caged chicken** → leftover **not disliked** (Type 1).
- **Scorpion** — surprisingly, **not** disliked. The book singles it out: *"there is no dislike in using the leftover water of a scorpion."*
- **Mule and donkey (Type 4):** The doubt is about whether their saliva purifies others, not about its purity. Hence: use it, then tayammum, then pray — covers both possibilities.

**Yaad rakho:** Su'r ki char categories: **(1) Insaan / ghoda / halal jaanwar** — paak. **(2) Kutta / sooar / shikari jaanwar** — napaak. **(3) Bili / awara murghi / shikari parinde / ghar ke chuhe** — *makruh* (paak hai par disliked). **(4) Khachchar / gadha** — *mashkook* (wudu phir tayammum phir namaz). Sab se aham distinction: shikari **zameeni** jaanwar (zubaan se) vs shikari **parinde** (chonch se — haddi se).

### Quick desi example to bring it to class

Your aunt's home cat drinks from your saucer. Is the water now usable for wudu? **Yes — but disliked if plain water is available.** Throw it out, get a fresh glass. Not because the water is filthy, but because the *Prophet's followers* prefer the cleaner option when both are available.

Your neighbour's dog laps from a bucket. Is the water usable? **No — it is filthy.** Different category entirely.

## 6 · Wells — the precautionary structure

Wells (*bi'r — kuwaan*) get their own section because, historically, the Muslim community lived around wells. A contaminated well was a public crisis. The rules are graded by **what fell in** and **what happened**.

### The "ALL water out" cases

| Case | Ruling |
|---|---|
| Any impurity (even a drop of blood or alcohol) falls in a **small** well | ALL water out |
| **Pig** falls in (even alive, tongue didn't touch water) | ALL water out — *because all parts of the pig are impure* |
| **Dog, sheep, or human dies** in well | ALL water out |
| **Any bloated dead animal** in well (even small) | ALL water out — *because being bloated means it was in there a while, filth has spread* |

The Companions' precedent: *when a man died in Zamzam water, Ibn Abbas and Ibn al-Zubair ordered ALL the water to be withdrawn, and many Companions were present with no objection.*

### The exception in "ALL water out"

A **small amount of dung** (from livestock) is **ignored** — because wells are typically uncovered and the wind blows cattle dung in. The community could not function if this triggered "all water out". **But** if the amount is excessive, or if it appears in every bucket — back to "all water out".

### The bucket-count system

When you cannot physically empty all the water (deep well, fast-replenishing), Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Hasan gave a graded scale:

| Animal that died | Buckets to expel |
|---|---|
| Bloated animal (when you can't drain it all) | **200** |
| Chicken, cat, or similar | **40** |
| Rat or similar | **20** |

Buckets must be **average size** — usually the well's own bucket. This action purifies *the well, the bucket rope, AND the hand of the person cleaning*. Elegant detail — you don't need a separate cleansing afterward.

**Yaad rakho:** "Sab paani nikalna" wale cases yaad karo: **chhoti najasa chhote kuwein mein** · **sooar (zinda ya murda)** · **kutta / bakri / insaan ki maut** · **phoola hua jaanwar**. Phir bucket counts: **200 (phoola hua, agar saara nahin nikal sakta)** · **40 (murghi/bili)** · **20 (chuha)**.

### What does **NOT** ruin well water (very important — practical)

This list is generous and often surprising:

1. **Dung of camels, sheep, horses, mules, donkeys, cows** — small amounts ignored (see exception above)
2. **Droppings of halal birds** — pigeons, sparrows
3. **Death of a bloodless creature** — fish, frog, mosquito, fly, wasp, scorpion, aquatic creatures
4. **Human falling in alive** (no filth on body when fell in)
5. **Halal-meat animal falling in alive** (camel, cow, sheep)
6. **Mule, donkey, predatory bird falling in alive** (soundest view)

### The beautiful pigeon hadith

The book quotes: *"The Prophet (ﷺ) thanked the pigeon and said: 'Verily, it nested on the door of the cave until I was safe, and may Allah reward it with the masjid as its home.'"* (Tabarani 20/443) — *And Ibn Mas'ud wiped away the droppings of a bird with his hand.*

This is *why* pigeon droppings don't ruin water. The principle isn't arbitrary — it traces to the Prophet's (ﷺ) own honouring of the pigeon during the Hijra. Tell this story in class; it lands well.

### The saliva-test rule (it's elegant)

> *"If the saliva of the animal touches the water, then the water is judged according to the animal."*

In other words: water inherits the animal's su'r-category. Sheep saliva → water remains pure. Dog saliva → water becomes filthy. This is *one principle* that generates the whole su'r table — the chapter is internally consistent.

### The "unknown duration" rule

If you find a dead animal in your well and **don't know how long it's been there**:

| Condition of animal | Days/nights water is ruled impure | What people must repeat |
|---|---|---|
| Not bloated | **1 day and 1 night** | Repeat 1 day+night of prayers performed with that wudu |
| **Bloated** | **3 days and 3 nights** | Repeat 3 days+nights of prayers (and ghusl if used) |

*Exception:* if people only used the water to **wash filthy clothes** (not for wudu/ghusl) — they only need to wash the clothes again; no prayer repetition.

**Yaad rakho:** Yeh "unknown duration" rule kuwaan check karne ki **adat** dalwane ke liye hai — agar tum apna kuwaan regularly check nahin karte, to maut padi rahe phoole jaanwar ki — saari hafte ki namaz qaza! Yeh ek practical protection hai.

## The big picture — what this chapter is silently teaching

Step back. Chapter 1 of Nur al-Idah teaches the rulings of water — but underneath, it has handed you the **architecture of purity** in Hanafi fiqh:

1. **Purity is a legal state**, not a feeling. Body, clothes, and prayer-area are the three loci. (*Tahara ek qaanooni haalat hai — body, kapde, jagah — teen mehwar.*)
2. **Cause-and-effect, not magic.** Water purifies *because* the Lawgiver said so. When water loses its essential nature (cooked, dominated, used), it loses its causal capacity. (*Paani jadu nahin, asbaab-o-asaraat hai — apni asal sifat khoyi to capacity bhi gayi.*)
3. **Quantity matters.** Small water is fragile; large water is forgiving; running water self-cleans. Proportion is built into the law. (*Maqdaar maa'ne rakhti hai — chhota nazuk, bada raham wala, behta apne aap saaf.*)
4. **Categories are knowable, not vague.** The 5 types, the 4 su'r categories, the 7 sources — every Muslim should be able to recite these. (*Categories *waadeh* hain — ratta nahin, samajh ke yaad karne wali.*)
5. **Precaution is built in.** Type 5 (doubtful) → do both wudu and tayammum. Unknown-duration animal → repeat prayers. The law assumes the worst case. (*Shariat ihtiyaat ke saath kaam karti hai — agar shak hai to zyada karo, kam nahin.*)

**Yaad rakho:** Imam Hasan Shurunbulali ne is chapter mein paani ke baare mein har sawaal ka jawab diya hai — ratta maarne wali zubaan mein nahin, balki **principles** ki zubaan mein. Tumhe yeh principles samjhana hai cohort ko, na ke 50 alag-alag rules. Agar principle samajh aa gaya, har naye case par apne aap apply ho jaayega.

# Part B · Teaching the chapter

Now you understand the chapter. This section is how to *deliver* it to your classmates — adult, mostly Urdu-speaking Muslims learning practical fiqh.

---

## The five landmines for Urdu-speaking adult learners

### Landmine 1 · "Pure but not purifying" feels contradictory

Type 3 (musta'mal) and Type 2 (cat-su'r) both have this property. Adult learners object: *"Agar paak hai to wudu kyun nahin?"* (*If it's pure, why can't I do wudu with it?*).

**Counter-strategy:** Use a household analogy. *"Aap dishwasher mein use ho chuke saaf paani se barten dho lo — paani saaf hai par 'fresh' nahin. Wahin se daubara dhona ajeeb lagta hai. Bilkul wahi musta'mal paani ka maamla hai — paak hai par ritual purification ka kaam ek baar ho chuka."*

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Paani ke do alag sawaal hain — pehla: kya khud paak hai? Doosra: kya doosron ko paak kar sakta hai? Musta'mal mein pehla jawab haan, doosra jawab nahin. Jaise battery — charged thi, ab discharge ho gayi — battery 'tooti' nahin, sirf 'khaali' hai."*

### Landmine 2 · The 10×10 arms vs 216 litres confusion

Some classmates may have heard the qullatayn (216 litre) figure on YouTube/khutbahs and bring it up. Don't deny the hadith — explain the madhhab difference.

**Counter-strategy:** *"Yeh hadith hai, lekin Hanafi imam farmate hain ke iski narrations conflict karti hain — kuch riwayat 'ek bulla', kuch 'teen', kuch 'chaalis' kehti hain. Isliye Hanafi madhhab mein hum 10 hath x 10 hath ka pemana lete hain. Shafi'i bhai 216 litre lete hain. Donon ki dalil hai — hum apni madhhab par chalein ge."*

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Madhhab follow karne ka faaida yeh hai ke tumhe har waqt khud ijtihad nahin karna padta. Imam Abu Hanifa, Imam Abu Yusuf, Imam Muhammad — yeh log saari riwayat dekh ke 10×10 hath par pohonche hain. Tum apni madhhab par chalo."*

### Landmine 3 · "Dog is dirty so everything dog-related is filthy"

Pop-Islam often paints dogs as totally impure. Nur al-Idah is more precise: **only the dog's saliva and what touches it is filthy. The dog's body otherwise is pure.** This matters for the "dog jumps in well, exits alive, tongue didn't touch water" exception.

**Counter-strategy:** Clearly draw the distinction on the board:

- ✅ Dog's body — *paak*
- ❌ Dog's saliva / tongue — *napaak*
- → If dog falls in well, exits alive, tongue didn't touch water → water *still pure*

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Kutte ke baare mein loag kehte hain 'sab kuch napaak' — yeh sahi nahin. Kutte ka **lu'aab** (saliva) napaak hai, badan paak hai. Isi liye agar kutta gir gaya kuwein mein, lekin paani mein zubaan nahin lagi — paani paak rehta hai. Yeh fiqh ka dilchasp nuance hai."*

### Landmine 4 · "Yeh saare numbers (200, 40, 20) kahaan se aaye?"

Classmates with engineering / scientific background may struggle with the specific numbers. They feel arbitrary.

**Counter-strategy:** Be honest. These come from the **ijtihad of Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Hasan** — practical-application guidance, not direct Qur'an/hadith text. The principle is: *contamination → remove enough water to be confident the well is clean.* The specific numbers are scholars' considered judgment.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Yeh 200/40/20 directly Quran ya Hadith mein nahin hain — yeh Imam Abu Yusuf aur Imam Hasan ki ijtihad hai. Asool yeh hai: jitni badi cheez gir gayi, utna zyada paani nikaalo — itna ke yaqeen ho jaaye ke kuwaan paak hai. Khaali aankhon par bharosa nahin, scholarly judgment par."*

### Landmine 5 · Spiritual heaviness vs practical fiqh

Some classmates may feel overwhelmed: *"Itni saari paak/napaak ki cheezein! Kya har waqt sochna padega?"*

**Counter-strategy:** Reassure them. The chapter exists precisely so that they **don't** have to overthink in daily life. Once you know:
- Tap water = Type 1 = use freely
- Water with cat in it = Type 2 = use other water if available
- Water in your wudu basin = Type 3 = don't redo wudu in it
- Visibly filthy water = Type 4 = avoid

…you're 95% covered for daily life. The exotic cases (mule's su'r, bloated animal in well) are *encyclopedic* — you learn them once, then they rest in your memory until needed.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Ghabraao mat. Chapter ka maqsad yeh nahin ke har waqt 'paak-napaak' ki ratti maaron — ulta, maqsad yeh hai ke ek dafa samajh lo, phir zindagi mein bina sochay kaam ho jaaye. Imam-i-akram ne yeh tafseel isliye di hai ke ek dafa parh ke maatam karke khatam karo — har rooz nahin sochna."*

## Suggested 75-minute class plan

*Class shuru karne se pehle:* board par 3 cheezein likho — Chapter title (*Kitab al-Tahara — Bab al-Miyah*), the master overview chart (one diagram), and a small whiteboard space reserved for the **5 types table** that you'll build with the class.

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–5 min | Greetings · why purification is the FIRST chapter of fiqh (3 sentences) |
| 5–15 min | The definition of *tahara* (lughwi vs shar'i) + the **7 water sources** drill (rain/ocean/river/well/snow/hail/spring — say them together aloud, 3 times) |
| 15–35 min | The **5 types of water** — build the table on the board, type by type. Use real examples: tap water, cat saucer, wudu basin, dirty bucket, donkey's drinking trough |
| 35–45 min | The "what overwhelms water" rule — solid (flow test) vs liquid (1-of-2 / 2-of-3) vs filth (small/large/running) |
| 45–60 min | **Su'r** — drill the 4 categories with the animal table. Quiz them: *"Bili → ?"* (makruh) *"Kutta → ?"* (najis) *"Ghoda → ?"* (paak) — make it a game |
| 60–70 min | **Wells** — the "ALL water out" cases + bucket counts (200/40/20) + the pigeon hadith |
| 70–75 min | Wrap up · Q&A · assign handout exercises |

**Yaad rakho:** Pacing zaroori hai — Section 3 (5 types) sab se aham hai, isliye time us ko dene mein hesitate mat karna. Agar koi section short karna pade, "what overwhelms water" ko thoda compress karo — saara nahin hata na — kyunki cohort ko **practical** cases zyada chahiye. Pigeon hadith zaroor sunaao Wells section mein — yeh emotional connect deti hai.

## What you should NOT teach in Chapter 1

This is critical for staying on-scope. Each sub-topic in Book I of Nur al-Idah has its own chapter — don't bleed into them.

- ❌ **Istinja** (cleaning after toilet) — **Chapter 2 of Book I**
- ❌ **Lavatory etiquettes** — **Chapter 2 of Book I**
- ❌ **Wudu — pillars, sunnas, nullifiers** — **Chapter 3 of Book I**
- ❌ **Ghusl** — **Chapter 4 of Book I**
- ❌ **Tayammum** — **Chapter 5 of Book I**
- ❌ **Mash on khuffs** (wiping over leather socks) — **Chapter 6 of Book I**
- ❌ **Hayd / Nifas** (menstruation / postnatal) — **Chapter 7 of Book I**
- ❌ **Najasah** in detail (heavy vs light filth) — **Chapter 8 of Book I**

If a classmate asks *"agar wudu ke baare mein bataao?"* — say: *"Wudu pooray ek chapter par based hai — woh hum agle hafte/session mein dekhenge. Aaj sirf paani ka mauzu."*

**Yaad rakho:** Class ko pre-load mat karo. Adult learners ka biggest sign of overwhelm — woh waapas nahin aate next session. Sirf aaj ka content covered karo, fayda mand cases bata do, baqi par *"yeh aage seekhenge"*.

## Self-check before teaching

Before the class, make sure:

- [ ] You can list the **7 water sources** confidently in 5 seconds — without a notebook. *(7 paani ke zaraye 5 second mein, bina dekhe.)*
- [ ] You can draw the **5-types grid** on the board without a reference. *(5 types ki table khud bana sako.)*
- [ ] You can give a household example for each type — Type 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 — without notes. *(Har type ke liye ghar ka example bina sochay de sako.)*
- [ ] You can recite the master Hadith — *"Water is pure and is not rendered impure by anything, except a thing that alters its colour, taste or smell"* — and explain why this single hadith governs the chapter. *(Asal hadith zubaani sunao aur batao ke yeh poori chapter ki bunyaad kyun hai.)*
- [ ] You can fill in the **4 su'r categories** with at least one animal each. *(4 su'r categories — har ek mein kam se kam ek jaanwar.)*
- [ ] You can answer *"Why does the dog's su'r differ from the dog's body status?"* in one Urdu sentence. *("Kutte ke lu'aab aur badan mein farq kyun" — ek Urdu jumlay mein.)*

If yes to all six — *tum tayar ho.*

## After class — diagnostic checklist

For each classmate, watch for:

1. **Are they confusing "paak" with "saaf"?** *(Paani ka rang saaf hai* ≠ *paani paak hai.)* — A bucket of clear used water is "looks-clean" but is musta'mal.
2. **Are they over-applying "dog is impure"?** *(Kya woh kutte ke badan ko bhi napaak keh rahe hain?)*
3. **Are they remembering the running-water exception?** *(Behta paani — sirf nishaani badle to napaak.)*
4. **Can they recall the master hadith?** *(Rang, zaayqa, boo — teenon yaad hain?)*
5. **Are they treating the 200/40/20 as religious vs scholarly?** *(Inhe Quran-Hadith ka rule samajh rahe ya Imam-i-akram ki ijtihad?)*

**Yaad rakho:** Agar 3+ items mein cohort phans rahi hai — ek aur revision session karwao Chapter 2 (Istinja) par jaane se pehle. Bunyaad mazboot honi chahiye — paani ki samajh agar mazboot nahin, baqi saari taharaat (wudu, ghusl, tayammum) hilegi.
