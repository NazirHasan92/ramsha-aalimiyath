🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the student will be able to:

  1. Define الطَّهَارَة (tahara) in both its linguistic and juristic senses.
  2. List the 7 sources of pure water from memory.
  3. Distinguish the 5 types of water by their purity and purifying capacity, and give one household example for each.
  4. Apply the rule of "colour, taste, smell" to decide if a body of water has become filthy.
  5. Distinguish small still / large still / running water and the corresponding contamination rules.
  6. Categorise an animal's su'r (leftover water) into one of the 4 categories: pure · filthy · disliked · doubtful.
  7. Apply the well-purification rules: when to withdraw all water, when 200/40/20 buckets, and what does NOT ruin well-water.

1 · الطَّهَارَة & the 7 Sources of Pure Water

The book opens here. Tahara is not vague cleanliness — it is a precise legal state, and water (or earth, in its absence) is its cause.

Where this fits: the very first node of the master map. Everything else in Book I (wudu, ghusl, etc.) presupposes you understand water.

الطَّهَارَة · Tahara

Meaning
Purification — a legal state of cleanliness required before prayer. Two senses: linguistic (general cleanliness, even spiritual) and juristic (a specific ruling on body, clothes, and prayer-area).
Roman Urdu
Tahara = "paaki" (general meaning) ya "shariʿat ke mutaabiq paak hone ki haalat" (fiqhi meaning).
From the book
"The ruling given to the place where prayer is concerned (one's body, clothes, and area where one prays), the cause being clean water or clean earth which is deemed suitable for tayammum when there is no water." — Nur al-Idah

The 7 Sources of Purifying Water

List
Rain · Ocean · River · Well · Melted snow · Melted hail · Spring water.
Roman Urdu
Saat paani: baarish · samandar · nadi · kuwaan · pighli baraf · pighla ola · chashma.
Rule
These are the ONLY waters in their natural state that fully purify. Rose water, juice, broth, etc. are not in this list — they may have other uses, but they cannot perform wudu/ghusl.
Today's relevance
Tap water ultimately comes from one of these 7 (usually well or river). Bottled mineral water = spring or well. Both fine.

📿 Qur'anic proof — Surah Al-Furqan 25:48

وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءً طَهُورًا

"He (Allah) caused rain to descend from the heaven, to clean you therewith." (Q 25:48, as quoted in Nur al-Idah)

🕊️ The Master Hadith of the chapter

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Water is pure, and is not rendered impure by anything, except a thing that alters its colour, taste or smell." (as quoted in Nur al-Idah)

And on ocean water specifically: "Its water is pure and the dead things in it are permissible."

This single hadith governs the entire chapter. The three-fold test — colour · taste · smell (lawn · ta'm · reeh) — comes back for running water, large bodies of water, and wells.

💡 Teaching tip

Drill the 7 sources orally 3 times with the whole class. Then ask each student to name them solo. By minute 15 the cohort should be saying them automatically: "baarish, samandar, nadi, kuwaan, pighli baraf, pighla ola, chashma". This becomes the foundation — every later rule references "is this Type 1 water?"

2 · The 5 Types of Water — أَقْسَامُ الْمِيَاه

The heart of the chapter. Two separate questions about any water: (a) is it pure in itself? (b) can it purify other things?

Where this fits: after the 7 sources, the book classifies all water into 5 types based on its purifying capacity. Spend the most time here.

Type 1 · طَاهِرٌ مُطَهِّرٌ · Pure & Purifying

What
Plain unmixed water (ma' mutlaq) in its natural state. No dislike, no doubt.
Roman Urdu
Mutlaq paani — be-qaid, khaalis. Tap ka paani, kuwaan ka paani, baarish — sab Type 1.
Use
Wudu, ghusl, washing filth — everything.

Type 2 · Pure & Purifying — but disliked (مَكْرُوه)

What
Water from which a cat (or similar small animal) has drunk — only when it is a small quantity.
Roman Urdu
Bili ne thoda paani peeya — paani abhi bhi paak hai, wudu sahi ho jaayegi. Lekin agar aur saaf paani available hai, to is se wudu karna makruh hai.
Rule
Use it only if no plain (Type 1) water is available. If Type 1 is at hand, prefer it.

Type 3 · الْمَاءُ الْمُسْتَعْمَل · Used Water

What
Water that has already done its job — i.e. water that drips off your body during wudu, or water used to lift a state of minor or major impurity.
Roman Urdu
Musta'mal = woh paani jo wudu/ghusl ke kaam mein ek dafa istemaal ho chuka. Paak hai par "thakka hua".
Becomes "used" when…
"Right after it separates from the body" — i.e. the moment the drop falls off your hand into the basin, it is musta'mal.
Can be used for…
Cleaning physical filth (najasa haqiqiyya) like urine, pus, or wine from clothes/body.
Cannot be used for…
Wudu or ghusl. It cannot lift ritual impurity (hadath).

Type 4 · الْمَاءُ النَّجِس · Filthy Water

What
A small quantity of still water into which an impurity has fallen — filthy even if no trace appears.
Roman Urdu
Chhote thehre paani mein agar najasa gir gayi — paani napaak, chaahe nazar aaye ya nahin.
Threshold for "small"
Less than 10 × 10 arms (square), or circumference less than 36 arms (circular). 1 arm ≈ 48 cm (fingertip to elbow). So roughly less than 5 m × 5 m.
Use
Cannot be used to remove physical or legal filth. Discard.

Type 5 · الْمَاءُ الْمَشْكُوك · Doubtful Water

What
Water that a mule or donkey has drunk from. The doubt is not about its purity — that is fine — but about its capacity to purify others.
Roman Urdu
Khachchar/gadhe ka jhoota paani. Khud paak hai par doosron ko paak karne mein shak hai.
Ruling (precautionary)
If no other water is available: perform wudu with this water, then perform tayammum, then pray. The doubled procedure covers both possibilities.

⚠️ The biggest landmine here

"Pure but not purifying" (Type 3) sounds contradictory to adult learners. They will object: "Agar paak hai to wudu kyun nahin?" Explain with a battery analogy: "Yeh paani charged thi, ek dafa kaam kar chuki — ab khaali ho gayi. Paak hai, par 'fresh' nahin. Aur ek baar use nahin ho sakti wudu ke liye." Re-emphasise: two separate questions — (a) is it pure? (b) can it still purify others?

💡 Teaching tip

Draw the 5-types table on the whiteboard with the class. Don't pre-fill it — ask them: "Type 1 — koi misaal de sakta hai?" and let them think. When they say "tap water", write it in. Build the table together. They will remember a table they constructed 5× better than one you handed them.

3 · What Overwhelms / Contaminates Water — مَا يَغْلِبُ عَلَى الْمَاء

When does water stop being water? The book gives three separate tests — for solids, for liquids, and for filth.

Where this fits: a water can be Type 1 yesterday and Type 4 today if something fell into it. This section is how you tell the difference in real time.

Mixing with a solid

Test
Does the water still flow thin and naturally? If yes → still water. If no → not water.
Roman Urdu
Solid (jaise saffron, patte, phal) mile to bahaav ka test. Agar patli dhaar mein behta hai — paani. Agar gaadha ho gaya (daal pakaane se) — paani nahin.
Important
Even if the colour or smell changes — as long as the water still flows like water, it is still water. Saffron-water is still water.

Mixing with a liquid (with characteristics)

2-trait liquid
e.g. Milk — colour + taste (no smell). If 1 of the 2 appears in the water, the water is overwhelmed.
3-trait liquid
e.g. Vinegar — colour + taste + smell. If 2 of the 3 appear in the water, the water is overwhelmed.
Mnemonic
"Aik mein se aik, ya teen mein se do."

Mixing with a characterless liquid — by weight

Example
Used water, or rose water that has lost its smell. Such a liquid has no colour/taste/smell to test for.
Rule
If 2 ratls of such liquid mix with 1 ratl of plain water → wudu not permitted. If the opposite (plain water majority) → permitted.
Roman Urdu
Be-rang, be-zaayqa, be-boo liquid mile to wazn ka muqaabla. Agar mila hua zyada (2:1) — paani nahin raha. Agar paani zyada (1:2) — paak.
Conversion
1 ratl (Baghdad) ≈ 384 g · 2 ratls ≈ 769 g.

The small / large / running rule (for filth)

Small still water
Less than 10×10 arms (≈ 5m × 5m). If filth falls in → filthy even if no trace appears.
Large still water
10×10 arms or more. If filth falls in → filthy only if colour/taste/smell change.
Running water
"If you scoop once and scoop again, it's not the same water." Same as large still — filthy only if traits change.
Mnemonic
Chhota — najuk (har najasa napaak). Bara — raham wala (sirf nishaani badle to napaak). Behta — apne aap saaf (sirf nishaani badle to napaak).

🕊️ Hadith — the lotus-leaves story

The Prophet (ﷺ) said about a man who fell from his camel during Hajj and died: "Wash him with water and lotus leaves, and shroud him in two garments." (Bukhari 1268, as quoted in Nur al-Idah)

Lesson: the Prophet (ﷺ) called for water with lotus leaves — leaves don't disqualify the water as long as it stays flowing. This is the textual proof for the "solid mixing" rule.

💡 Teaching tip

Draw a pond, a basin, and a stream on the whiteboard. Drop an imaginary "X" of filth into each one. Walk through the three rulings out loud with the class. This visual contrast — small/large/running — locks the rule in within 5 minutes.

After an animal drinks from a small quantity of water, the remaining water is called su'r. Its ruling depends entirely on the animal.

Defining su'r

Definition
"The small quantity of water which remains after a human or animal has drunk."
Important
River or ocean water is never su'r, even if thousands drink from it. Su'r requires a small quantity.
Roman Urdu
Jhoota paani — chhote bartan / chhote taalaab ka paani jisme se kisi insaan ya jaanwar ne peeya hai. Bade tank ya nadi ka paani su'r nahin hota.

Category 1 · Pure & purifying ✅

Animals
Human (provided no filth in mouth, e.g. just drank alcohol) · Horse · Halal-meat animal (camel, cow, sheep, goat, etc.)
Why
Their saliva is pure. The water remains Type 1.
Roman Urdu
Insaan, ghoda, halal jaanwar (oont, gaay, bakri) — agar kuch peeya hai to bacha hua paani paak. Wudu ho sakti hai bila ghibrahat.

Category 2 · Filthy — forbidden ❌

Animals
Dog · Pig · Predatory LAND animal (cheetah, wolf, fox).
Why
Their saliva is impure. The pig is wholly impure. The water becomes Type 4 (najis).
Roman Urdu
In jaanwaron ne agar bartan se peeya — paani napaak, phenk do.

Category 3 · Disliked when plain water available 😐

Animals
Cat · Stray chicken · Predatory BIRD (eagle, falcon, glede) · Rat / snake (around the home). Scorpion is NOT disliked.
Why predatory birds differ from predatory land animals
Land predators drink with their tongue — wet with saliva (impure) → leftover filthy. Birds drink with their beak — beaks are bone (pure) → leftover only disliked.
Roman Urdu
Bili, awara murghi, shikari parinde (baaz, etc.), ghar ke chuhe, saanp — agar peeya to paani paak hai par makruh. Agar saaf paani available hai, woh use karo. Bichchu mustasna hai — uska jhoota makruh nahin.
Caged vs stray chicken
Caged chicken → leftover NOT disliked (Type 1). Stray chicken → leftover disliked (it may have eaten filth roaming).

Category 4 · Doubtful (in purifying others) ❓

Animals
Mule · Donkey (their leftover water).
Ruling
If no other water is available: perform wudu with this, then perform tayammum, then pray. Precautionary doubling.
Roman Urdu
Khachchar / gadhe ka jhoota paani — agar koi doosra paani nahin to dono karo: pehle is se wudu, phir tayammum, phir namaz. Donon mein se ek tum ko cover kar legi — yeh "ihtiyaat" ka asool hai.
Why
The doubt is not about purity of the water — that is fine. The doubt is about its purifying capacity. Hence wudu (if water purifies) + tayammum (if it doesn't) → one of the two will validate prayer.

⚠️ Important nuance — the dog

Many classmates assume everything related to the dog is filthy. Nur al-Idah is more precise: only the dog's saliva and tongue make water filthy. The dog's body otherwise is pure. So: if a dog jumps in a well, exits alive, and its tongue didn't touch the water → the water is still pure.

💡 Teaching tip — turn it into a quiz

After laying out the 4 categories, call out animals randomly and ask the class to call back the category: "Bili → ?" "Makruh!" · "Kutta → ?" "Najis!" · "Ghoda → ?" "Paak!" · "Gadha → ?" "Mashkook!" · "Kabootar (cage mein) → ?" "Paak!" · "Cheeta → ?" "Najis!" · "Baaz / eagle → ?" "Makruh!" Loud, group-response. 2 minutes of this and they'll never forget.

5 · الْآبَار · Wells & Their Purification

Why wells get their own section: in the classical Muslim community, a contaminated well was a public crisis. The rulings are graded by what fell in and what happened.

Where this fits: a well is "small water" by default — so anything that contaminates it is serious. The book gives a precise, graded response system.

"ALL water out" cases

Rule
In these 4 cases, ALL the water of the well must be withdrawn:
1.
Any impurity in a small well — even a single drop of blood or alcohol. (Exception: a small amount of cattle dung is ignored — wells are uncovered, wind blows dung in. But excessive dung → all water out.)
2.
Pig falls in, even if it exits alive and its tongue did not touch the water — because all parts of the pig are impure.
3.
A dog, sheep, or human DIES in the well. Precedent: Ibn Abbas and Ibn al-Zubair ordered this when a man died in Zamzam.
4.
Any BLOATED dead animal, even small — because being bloated indicates it was there a while, filth has spread.
Roman Urdu
Saara paani nikalo jab: chhoti najasa chhote kuwein mein · sooar gir gaya (zinda ya murda) · kutta/bakri/insaan ki maut · phoola hua jaanwar. Yaad kar lo — yeh 4 cases hain.

Bucket counts (when you cannot drain all water)

Bloated animal
200 buckets — when "all water out" is the rule but physically impossible.
Chicken / cat / similar
40 buckets.
Rat / similar
20 buckets.
Bucket spec
Average size, usually the well's own bucket. This action purifies the well, the bucket rope, AND the hand of the person cleaning — no separate cleansing needed.
Source
Verdict of Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Hasan (as recorded in Nur al-Idah).
Roman Urdu
Yaad rakho: phoola hua jaanwar = 200 dol, murghi/bili = 40 dol, chuha = 20 dol. Yeh Imam Abu Yusuf aur Imam Hasan ki ijtihad hai — directly Quran/Hadith mein nahin. Bucket, rassi, aur cleaner ka haath sab paak ho jaata hai isi amal se.

What does NOT ruin well water

1.
Dung of halal-meat livestock (camel, sheep, horse, mule, donkey, cow…) — provided not excessive.
2.
Droppings of halal birds (pigeons, sparrows).
3.
Death of a creature with no flowing blood — fish, frog, mosquito, fly, wasp, scorpion, aquatic creatures.
4.
A human, halal-meat animal, mule, donkey, or predatory bird falling in and exiting alive — provided no filth was on their body when they fell in.
Roman Urdu
Paani kharab NAHIN hota in se: (1) mawesheyon ka thoda goobar (kuwein khule hote hain, hawa daal deti hai) · (2) halal-paasrindon ki beat (kabootar, chidiya) · (3) be-khoon ke jaanwar ki maut (machhli, mendak, makkhi, machhar, bichchu) · (4) koi insaan ya halal jaanwar gir gaya par zinda nikla (badan par najasat na ho).

The unknown-duration rule

Situation
A dead animal is found in the well and nobody knows how long it has been there.
Not bloated
Water ruled impure for 1 day and 1 night. People who used it for wudu must repeat 1 day + night of prayer.
Bloated
Water ruled impure for 3 days and 3 nights. People who used it for wudu/ghusl must repeat 3 days + nights of prayer.
Exception
If they only used the water to wash filthy clothes (not wudu/ghusl) — they just wash the clothes again. No prayer repetition.
Roman Urdu
Murda jaanwar mile, waqt ka pata nahin: agar phoola hua nahin to 1 din-raat ki namaz dohraao; phoola hua ho to 3 din-raat ki. Sirf kapde dho hain to nayi se dho lo, namaz dohrane ki zaroorat nahin. Yeh rule kuwein ko regularly check karne ki adat dalwane ke liye hai.

🕊️ The pigeon hadith — why halal-bird droppings are forgiven

The Prophet (ﷺ) thanked the pigeon and said: "Verily, it nested on the door of the cave until I was safe, and may Allah reward it with the masjid as its home." (Tabarani 20/443, as quoted in Nur al-Idah)

And Ibn Mas'ud (RA) wiped away the droppings of a bird with his hand.

This is the textual basis for why pigeon/sparrow droppings do not ruin well water. The principle is not arbitrary — it traces to the Prophet's (ﷺ) own honouring of the pigeon during the Hijra.

⚠️ The numbers are scholarly ijtihad — be honest about it

200 / 40 / 20 are not directly from the Qur'an or hadith. They come from Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Hasan's ijtihad — considered practical-application judgments. The underlying principle is: contamination → expel enough water to be confident the well is clean. If a classmate asks "where do these numbers come from?", explain this honestly.

💡 Teaching tip

For modern, urban classmates who have never seen a well — explain that this section is also the conceptual basis for how to handle contamination of any small enclosed water source: an aquarium, a water cooler, a household tank. The principle scales.

⚠️ Common Student Mistakes — Watch For These

1. Confusing "paak" with "saaf"

Students assume that if water looks clean, it must be pure for wudu. They forget about musta'mal (used water — Type 3). A bucket of crystal-clear water from your wudu basin is still musta'mal — clean-looking but not purifying.

2. "Dog is entirely impure"

Folk-Islam paints the dog as totally filthy. Nur al-Idah is precise: only the dog's saliva makes water filthy. The body otherwise is pure. Hence the well exception: dog falls in, exits alive, tongue didn't touch water → water stays pure.

3. Applying the small-water rule to large bodies

Students panic when filth touches any water — even an Olympic pool. Remind them: 10 × 10 arms is the threshold. Anything bigger is large still water — only filthy if colour/taste/smell change.

4. Thinking 200/40/20 are direct Qur'an/Hadith

These are Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Hasan's ijtihad as recorded in Nur al-Idah — practical-application guidance. The principle (expel enough to be confident) is sacred; the specific numbers are scholarly judgment.

5. Mixing up "predatory bird" with "predatory land animal"

Eagle is Category 3 (disliked) — drinks with beak (bone, pure). Wolf is Category 2 (filthy) — drinks with tongue (saliva impure). Drill this distinction; it is the chapter's most-tested nuance.

6. Forgetting the scorpion exception

Most "creepy animals around the home" → su'r disliked. But Nur al-Idah explicitly singles out the scorpion: "there is no dislike in using the leftover water of a scorpion." Scorpion-su'r is Category 1 (pure).

📿 Connect to the Qur'an & Hadith

Every verse and hadith below is quoted by Nur al-Idah itself. Use 1–2 of these in class to anchor the rulings in the texts.

Surah Al-Furqan 25:48 — the verse on rain

وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءً طَهُورًا

"He (Allah) caused rain to descend from the heaven, to clean you therewith."

Students recognise: the word طَهُورًا (tahooraa) — "purifying" — which is the very name of this chapter (tahara).

The Master Hadith — colour, taste, smell

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Water is pure, and is not rendered impure by anything, except a thing that alters its colour, taste or smell."

This single hadith governs the entire chapter. Whenever a student asks "is this water still OK?", the answer comes from this hadith: check lawn · ta'm · reeh.

Hadith on ocean water

The Prophet (ﷺ) said about the ocean: "Its water is pure and the dead things in it are permissible."

This is why ocean water is one of the 7 sources — and also why dead fish in the ocean are permissible to eat.

Hadith on lotus leaves (Bukhari 1268)

About a man who fell from his camel during Hajj and died, the Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Wash him with water and lotus leaves, and shroud him in two garments."

This is the textual proof that solids (lotus leaves) mixing with water do not disqualify it, as long as the water still flows naturally.

Hadith on the pigeon (Tabarani 20/443)

The Prophet (ﷺ) thanked the pigeon and said: "Verily, it nested on the door of the cave until I was safe, and may Allah reward it with the masjid as its home."

The textual basis for why halal-bird droppings do not ruin well water.
