Book I — Chapter 8: an-Najāsa (filth) & its purification

The two kinds of filth, heavy vs light, how much is excused, and how to clean each surface · Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (Hanafi)

Book I ka aakhri chapter — cheezon ki paaki

Press → to begin · Press F for fullscreen

From "purify myself" to "purify my world"

All the earlier chapters cleaned you — wudū, ghusl, tayammum, the bloods. That was ḥadath (a state).

Today: cleaning your WORLD — clothes, body-surface, ground, leather. This is khubth.

This is the last chapter of Book I — and the most everyday. It opens the door to Prayer (a pure body, dress & place).

Paaki ke do hisse — andar (ḥadath) aur baahar (khubth). Yeh baahar wala.

Three keys to the whole chapter

1 · Two categories
khubth (substance → wash) vs ḥadath (state → wudū/ghusl); khubth sorted heavy vs light
2 · The excused amount
heavy = up to a dirham · light = under a quarter
3 · The traces decide
colour · smell · taste — essence transforms = pure (istiḥāla)

Learn these first — almost every ruling turns on one of them.

Do najāsatein, naap ke saath maaf, aur nishaan faisla karta hai.

Rule 1 · The two kinds of filth

KindWhat it isRemoved by
Khubth (real)a substance — urine, wine, blood, stool…washing (this chapter)
Ḥadath (legal)a state on the limbs (minor/major)wudū / ghusl (earlier)

You can be in both at once — broke wudū AND blood on the sleeve → do both.

Khubth = daag → dho; ḥadath = haalat → wudū/ghusl.

Rule 2 · Heavy vs light filth

Heavy (ghalīẓa)
alcohol · flowing blood · dead-animal meat & hide · unlawful-meat urine (human/fox/wolf) · dog/predator dung · chicken/duck/swan droppings · body discharges that break wudū
Light (khafīfa)
horse urine · lawful-meat-animal urine (sheep) · droppings of non-edible flying birds (hawk, eagle)

"Flowing" blood = leaves its place. Blood in meat / flea / fish / martyr = pure.

Behta khoon = jagah chhoR de; gosht/pissoo/machhli/shaheed ka khoon paak.

The two tables — don't guess, look

Table 1 · Bird droppingsVerdict

Non-edible flying (hawk)Light

Andaaza mat lagao — table dekho.

Rule 3 · How much is excused?

Heavy filth
excused up to the size of a dirham
(more → not excused)
Light filth
excused if less than a quarter of the clothing/body
(reaches ¼ → not excused)

Plus: pin-head urine specks are excused. "A quarter" = of the minimum prayer-clothing (navel → below knees).

"Excused" = the prayer is valid — NOT that the filth is pure. Still clean it when you can.

Bhaari dirham tak, halki chौthaai se kam. Maaf = namaz ho jaayegi.

Rule 4 · Does the filth actually move?

A clean thing is impure ONLY if a trace (colour/smell/taste) actually transfers onto it.

Stays pure
wind carrying a smell · a dry cloth in a non-dripping filthy cloth · a wet cloth on dry soil with no trace
Becomes impure
only when a visible trace appears (colour, smell, taste)

Boo se nahin — nazar aane wali gandagi se. Table 3: na Tapke to saaf.

Rule 5 · How to purify filth

FilthHow
Visible (has a body)remove the substance — even one wash
Invisible (no body)three washes + wring each time

Purifies with water OR any cleansing liquid — vinegar, rose water.

Nazar aane wali: ek baar. Na-nazar: 3 baar. Paani ya sirka/gulaab-jal.

The special surfaces

Khuff
rub on the ground if filth is dried (Abū Dāwūd 385 & 650)
Sword / metal
wipe with soil or cloth
Ground / plants
pure on drying — pray on it, but NO tayammum
Istiḥāla — essence transforms → pure (wine → vinegar)
Sperm (manī) — dry → scratch off; wet → wash

Zameen sookh kar paak (par tayammum nahin); sharab→sirka paak.

Rule 6 · The hide of a dead animal

The meat stays filth — but the hide can be reclaimed by tanning (real or legal), and slaughter purifies the skin too.

"Hide which has been tanned is pure."

— At-Tirmidhī 1728

EXCEPT
the pig (never) · the human skin (dignity)
Pure parts
hair · feathers · horn · hoof · dry bone (nerves impure) · musk & civet pure

Khaal dabaaghat/zibah se paak — suar aur insaan mustasnā.

Recap

Classify first:
khubth (wash) vs ḥadath (wudū/ghusl) · then heavy vs light (Tables 1 & 2)
Then the ruling:
excused — heavy dirham / light quarter · purify — visible 1 wash / invisible 3 · hide → tanning

Classification first, ruling second — almost every mistake is a wrong classification.

Pehle pehchaan, phir hukm. Asal kaam classify karna hai.

🏠 Homework

  1. Memorise the three keys (two categories · excused amount · traces decide)
  2. Learn the two thresholds (heavy = dirham, light = quarter)
  3. Learn Tables 1 & 2 (bird droppings, urine)
  4. Learn visible vs invisible purification (1 wash / 3 washes)
  5. Learn how the dead-animal hide is purified (except pig & human)
  6. Complete all 6 exercises on the handout
