# Chapter 7 — Deep Notes · al-Ḥayḍ wa'n-Nifās (menstruation, postnatal bleeding & chronic discharge)

**Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · Hasan Shurunbulali (Hanafi)**
*This file is Ramsha's personal learning material. Read **Part A** first to understand the chapter yourself, then **Part B** when you're ready to teach it to your classmates.*

*Reading guide: technical explanations are in English; key takeaways and counter-strategies are flagged with **Yaad rakho:** boxes in Roman Urdu. Skim the Urdu boxes for a fast revision pass.*

*Source constraint: every ruling, hadith, and verse below is drawn **only** from Nur al-Idah (book pp. 94–106). Nothing has been imported from outside fiqh sources or general knowledge.*

> **A word on the subject-matter — read this before anything else.** This is the most intimate chapter in Book I. The book itself sets the tone: it treats blood, the body, and intercourse in a plain **clinical, legal register** — *"the topic of menstrual periods is one of the most important issues based on the fact that a vast amount of rules are derived from it."* Your job is to **match that register exactly**: name things at the book's level, in clean medical-legal language, and **stop there**. Don't euphemise into vagueness (the cohort needs the actual ruling), and don't elaborate beyond the page. Several rulings (the intercourse-timing details, the "area beneath the navel" rule) belong in Part A for *your* understanding — Part B marks clearly which of these you **teach only if a classmate asks**. Modesty here is professionalism, not embarrassment.

> **Where this sits in Book I.** Ch 4 (Ghusl) named *ḥayḍ ending* and *nifās ending* as two of the causes that **obligate the major bath** — but it deliberately deferred the *detail* to here. This chapter is that detail: **what** these bloods are, **how long** each lasts, **what they forbid**, and the special **istiḥāḍa/excused** machinery for blood that is *not* ḥayḍ. It draws on everything before it — wudū (Ch 3), ghusl (Ch 4), tayammum (Ch 5) — because the whole point is *when a woman is cut off from worship and when she returns to it*. Teach it only once the cohort is solid on ghusl.

# Part A · Understanding the chapter

The book opens by naming the **three bloods that exit the female**, and immediately tells you why this is no minor topic: *"a vast amount of rules are derived from it such as divorce, lineage, the permissibility of intercourse, prayer, fasting, the recitation of Quran, iʿtikāf (religious retreat), the entering of a mosque, ṭawāf around the kaʿbah and maturity."* So this single biological reality decides a woman's worship, her marriage, her waiting-period — it is load-bearing for half of fiqh. *(The book even sketches the three kinds of divorce — ḥasan, aḥsan, bidʿī — to show how tightly divorce is tied to the menstrual cycle. That sketch is **background**; the divorce rules themselves are not this chapter and you do **not** teach them — see Part B.)*

The whole chapter rests on **one engine**: telling the three bloods apart. Get the engine right and every ruling falls out of it. The engine has three master-keys:

1. **The numbers decide — read every bleeding against its clock.** Each blood has a fixed window of *days*, and the **day-count**, not how the woman feels, classifies the blood:
   - **Ḥayḍ (menstruation):** minimum **3 days** (and their nights), average **5**, maximum **10**.
   - **Nifās (postnatal bleeding):** maximum **40 days**; **no minimum** (it can stop any time).
   - **Istiḥāḍa (chronic discharge):** *the leftover category* — any blood **shorter than the minimum** (under 3 days) **or longer than the maximum** (over 10 for ḥayḍ, over 40 for nifās). Anything outside the legal windows is **not** ḥayḍ/nifās; it is istiḥāḍa.
   - Between two periods there must be a **minimum 15 pure days**.

2. **During the days, anything-but-pure-white is menses.** Within the ḥayḍ window, the *colour* doesn't matter — *"the red, yellow and dark coloured fluid… is menstruation, until she sees pure white."* ʿĀʾishah's rule: *whatever is other than pure-white = menses.* The signal that purity has returned is the **white discharge (ṭuhr)**, not a particular colour stopping. (This is why the book records ʿĀʾishah telling women *"do not be hasty until you see a white discharge."*)

3. **The great reversal — ḥayḍ/nifās STOP worship; istiḥāḍa does NOT.** This is the payoff of the whole chapter. **Ḥayḍ and nifās** lift a woman *out* of prayer, fasting, Qur'an, the mosque, ṭawāf and intercourse (8 forbidden things). **Istiḥāḍa is the opposite** — it is treated *"like that of a permanent nose bleed"*: it does **not** prevent prayer, fasting or intercourse. The istiḥāḍa-sufferer is an **excused person (maʿdhūr)** who simply **renews wudū at each prayer-time** and carries on. So the same blood-from-the-same-place can either suspend all worship or barely interrupt it — the *classification* (key 1) is what decides which.

**Yaad rakho:** Teen chaabiyaan. (1) **Ginti faisla karti hai** — har khoon ki *din* ki had hoti hai: **ḥayḍ 3–10 din** (kam-se-kam 3, zyada-se-zyada 10, औsat 5), **nifās zyada-se-zyada 40** (kam ki koi had nahin), aur jo in **windows se bahar** ho woh **istiḥāḍa**. Do periods ke darmiyaan **kam-se-kam 15 din paaki**. (2) **Dinon ke andar safed ke alaawa har rang = ḥayḍ** — laal/zard/maila/kaala sab ḥayḍ, jab tak **safed (ṭuhr)** na dikhe. (3) **Bada palaT** — **ḥayḍ/nifās ibadat rok dete hain** (8 cheezein haraam), magar **istiḥāḍa nahin rokta** (woh "musalsal naak ke khoon" jaisa hai — har namaz ke waqt wudū karo aur parho)। Yaani ek hi jagah ka khoon kabhi sab ibadat band kar de aur kabhi mushkil se rokay — sab ka faisla **classification** par hai.

## 1 · The three bloods — the map and the clock

The book's first heading is **"The types of blood"** (*anwāʿ ad-dimāʾ*): *"The types of blood that exit from the female are menstrual periods [ḥayḍ], postnatal bleeding [nifās] and chronic vaginal discharge [istiḥāḍa]."* Everything else in the chapter is a footnote to telling these three apart, so anchor the **clock table** before any detail:

| Blood | What it is | Minimum | Maximum |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ḥayḍ** (menstruation) | natural blood from a healthy uterus | **3 days** (+ nights) | **10 days** (+ nights) · *avg 5* |
| **Nifās** (postnatal) | blood **after giving birth** | **no minimum** (can stop anytime) | **40 days** |
| **Istiḥāḍa** (chronic discharge) | *the leftover* — blood outside the legal window | — | — (it is "permanent") |

The key conceptual move: **istiḥāḍa is defined by exclusion.** The book: *"Istiḥāḍa is the discharge of blood that stops in **less than three days** or blood in **excess of ten days** of menstrual bleeding. Equally, the excess of blood after **forty days** of postnatal bleeding is regarded as istiḥāḍa."* So you never identify istiḥāḍa directly — you identify it by ruling out ḥayḍ and nifās on the **numbers**.

**Yaad rakho:** Pehle **naqsha** zehan mein bithao: teen khoon — **ḥayḍ** (3 se 10 din), **nifās** (paidaish ke baad, max 40, min koi nahin), **istiḥāḍa** (jo bach jaaye)। Istiḥāḍa ko **seedha nahin pehchaante** — ginti se ḥayḍ/nifās ko **khaarij** karke jo bache woh istiḥāḍa.

## 2 · Ḥayḍ up close — who, the pillar, the colours, the clock

**The definition (who has ḥayḍ).** *"Menstrual bleeding is blood that discharges (forcefully) from the uterus of a female who has **attained puberty**, who is **not ill or pregnant** and **has not reached the age of menopause** (which is fifty-five years old; and the fatwa issued is based on this view)."* So three bracketing conditions: she is **bāligha** (mature — and the book, on Ibn ʿĀbidīn's note, says *no girl matures before nine*), she is **not ill/pregnant** (so blood from illness or pregnancy isn't ordinary ḥayḍ), and she is **under 55** (the fatwa age of menopause). Blood from a girl under nine, or from a menopausal woman, is treated as **istiḥāḍa**, not ḥayḍ — *"this is the strongest view."*

**The pillar (rukn) of ḥayḍ.** *"The pillar of menstruation is the **emergence of blood from the uterus**; that is, the appearance of blood to the **immediate part outside the vagina**. If the blood remains in the inner part… then it is **not** deemed menses."* This is the fatwā position (per Quhustānī). *(Muḥammad held it is based on the woman **feeling** it — a recorded second view; not the fatwā.)* The practical upshot: ḥayḍ "counts" from when blood **appears**, not while it is still internal.

**The clock (the numbers).** *"The minimum days of menstrual periods are **three days and three nights**. The average is **five days** and the maximum is **ten days**."* The book is precise about the units: the minimum is *"three days and their nights (the nights have been added… to clarify the extent of time in hours, which is **seventy-two astronomical hours**)"* and the maximum is *"ten days and their nights (or **240 hours**)."* Two refinements:
- **Continuity is not required.** *"It is **not** conditional for the blood to continue throughout… the discontinuation for an hour or two or more does **not** invalidate."* So a dry spell mid-period doesn't reset the count.
- **Short-of-minimum = istiḥāḍa.** *"That which is short of the minimum duration (of three days or seventy-two hours) **even by a small margin** is regarded as chronic vaginal bleeding [istiḥāḍa] and not menstruation."* The proof is the Prophet ﷺ's report (Darquṭnī; al-Zaylaʿī): *"The minimum period for ḥayḍ in the case of a virgin girl or deflowered women is **three days** and its accompanying nights, while the maximum is **ten days**"* — explicitly *contrary to* Imām Shāfiʿī, who fixes the minimum at one day and one night.

**The colours (master-key 2).** *"The red, yellow and dark coloured fluid that a woman sees during the period of menses **is** menstruation, until she sees **pure white**."* ʿĀʾishah *"deemed whatever was other than pure white as menses."* And the **characteristic** of menstrual blood: *"its colour is red but closer to being **black**, and… it has a **foul smell**."* The yellow/muddy/green seen *during* the menses-days is judged as menstrual blood by the majority. The book's anchor is the ḥadīth in the Muwaṭṭaʾ of Imām Mālik: women would send ʿĀʾishah cotton cloths with a yellowness on them, asking about prayer, and she said *"Do not be hasty until you see a white discharge"* — *"by this she meant purity from menses."*

**One practical recommendation.** From *Sharḥ al-Wiqāya*: it is **recommended** for a woman to use **cotton wool / a pad / old cloth** during her menses to cover the private part; it is **disliked** to place something **inside** the internal area (as with a tampon).

**Yaad rakho:** **Ḥayḍ kis ko:** bāligha (9 saal ke baad), jo **na beemar na haamla**, aur **55 saal se kam** (sin-e-yaas)। Is se bahar (9 se chhoti / 55 ke baad) ka khoon = **istiḥāḍa**. **Rukn:** khoon ka **bahar zaahir hona** (andar reh jaaye to ḥayḍ nahin)। **Ginti:** min **3 din-raat (72 ghante)**, औsat 5, max **10 din-raat (240 ghante)**; beech mein **ruk jaaye to count reset nahin** hota; **72 ghante se thoda bhi kam = istiḥāḍa**. **Rang:** dinon ke andar **laal/zard/kaala/maila sab ḥayḍ** jab tak **safed** na dikhe (ʿĀʾishah: safed dekhne tak jaldi mat karo)। **Mashwara:** rui/pad jaayiz; andar rakhna (tampon jaisa) **makrooh**.

## 3 · Nifās, istiḥāḍa, and the pure days between periods

**Nifās (postnatal bleeding).** *"Postnatal bleeding (nifās) is blood that discharges **after giving birth**. The **maximum** days of postnatal bleeding is **forty days**. There is **no fixed limit on the minimum** — the bleeding may stop at any time before the forty days."* The proof is Anas (RA): the Prophet ﷺ *"had prescribed **forty days** for women in the state of nifās, **except if she is purified before this**"* (Ibn Mājah 649). If blood continues **past forty days**, the excess is **istiḥāḍa** (per Abū Ḥanīfa — Shāfiʿī says past *sixty*; the book records Abū Ḥanīfa's forty).

> Note the asymmetry with ḥayḍ: ḥayḍ has **both** a floor (3) and a ceiling (10); nifās has **only a ceiling** (40) — there is no minimum, because a woman may give birth and bleed only briefly, or not at all.

**Istiḥāḍa (chronic vaginal discharge) — the overflow category.** Already defined by exclusion (§1). The book then handles the realistic complication: a woman with a **known routine (ʿāda / habit)** of regular days. The rule of thumb: *"her regular days are that which she sees last,"* and a habit becomes established **even after one occurrence**. Two scenarios the book gives:
- If her bleeding **stays within ten days** but the *number* changes (e.g. her usual 5 days becomes 7), the **new number becomes her habit**.
- If her bleeding **exceeds ten days** and she has a known habit of less (e.g. 7), she **falls back on her habit (7)** and *the excess beyond it is istiḥāḍa*. (The same fallback logic applies to nifās against the 40-day ceiling.)

The teaching point is not the casuistry but the **principle**: when blood overflows the legal maximum, you **return the woman to her known habit** and label the surplus istiḥāḍa. *(The detailed worked cases — half-the-sun-risen, the Ikhtiyār "ten days each month" view — are Part-A depth; do not drill them, see Part B.)*

**The pure days between two periods.** *"The **minimum** days of purification that separates two menstrual periods is **fifteen days**. There is **no time limit on the maximum** — it may extend to a year or two."* So 15 clean days is the floor that must separate one ḥayḍ from the next; a woman could then stay pure indefinitely. *(Exception the book notes: a girl who has just reached puberty and is suffering istiḥāḍa is **assigned** ten days ḥayḍ / fifteen days pure by calculation. Edge case — Part-A only.)* And Ibn ʿĀbidīn's nuance: if a short **stoppage** of blood happens *within* the menses, it is treated *"like the continual flow of blood"* (i.e. the count runs through it — this is the same "discontinuation doesn't reset" point from §2).

**Yaad rakho:** **Nifās:** paidaish ke baad ka khoon — **max 40 din**, **min koi nahin** (kabhi bhi ruk sakta hai; Anas — Ibn Mājah 649)। 40 ke baad ka = **istiḥāḍa**. (Ḥayḍ ki tarah farsh-o-chhat dono nahin — nifās ki sirf **chhat**, 40.) **Istiḥāḍa:** jo bach jaaye; jab khoon max se **barh jaaye** to aurat ko uski **purani aadat (ʿāda)** par lauTa do, aur **zyaadati istiḥāḍa**. **Paaki ke din:** do periods ke beech **kam-se-kam 15 din** paaki (zyada ki koi had nahin)।

## 4 · The 8 things forbidden during ḥayḍ and nifās

This is the heart of the chapter for a learner — *what changes in her worship.* The book: *"**Eight** things are forbidden for a woman during menses or postnatal bleeding."* (Ḥayḍ and nifās share the **same** forbidden list — that's why the book treats them together.)

1. **Prayer (ṣalāh).** Forbidden — the Prophet ﷺ: *"When the menstruation begins, **abandon prayer**, and when it is finished **take a bath and pray**"* (Abū Dāwūd 286). *(And — see §6 — she does **not** make up the missed prayers.)*
2. **Fasting (ṣawm),** whether obligatory or optional. *(But fasts **are** made up later — §6.)*
3. **Reciting a verse of the Qur'an** — *"whether… from memory or… from the **muṣḥaf**."* The ḥadīth of Ibn ʿUmar: *"The woman in the state of menses is **not to recite anything of the Quran** and neither the sexually impure"* (Tirmidhī 131, Ibn Mājah 596). **The exception:** words that come out as **duʿā / praise / dhikr** — *tasbīḥ, takbīr, tahlīl,* "innā lillāhi…", "Bismillāh", "O Allah forgive me" — are **permitted** (there is consensus that *dhikr* and *ṣalawāt* are allowed in this state). The Mālikīs further permit her to recite/touch *for the purpose of learning and teaching* — because, unlike the junub, *she cannot simply bathe to lift the state.*
4. **Touching the Qur'an** — unless with a **separate covering**. Allah says *"No-one can touch it, except those who are pure"* (al-Wāqiʿa 56:79); the Prophet ﷺ: *"No one besides the person in a state of purification is to touch the Qur'an."* (One may turn pages with a pen/stick; a tafsīr book that is mostly commentary is treated differently.)
5. **Entering a mosque.** Umm Salama reported the Prophet ﷺ: *"The masjid is lawful **neither for a man in a state of janāba nor a menstruating female**"* (Ibn Mājah 645). Nifās takes the same ruling.
6. **Ṭawāf around the Kaʿba** — even if performed, *"because purity is conditional to its perfect performance."* ʿĀʾishah was told: *"Perform that which the pilgrim of ḥajj performs, **though do not make ṭawāf until you become clean**"* (Bukhārī 305). *(The book adds the expiation if ṭawāf is done in this state — a sacrifice — but that is ḥajj-detail, Part-A only.)*
7. **Sexual intercourse (jimāʿ)** is unlawful with a menstruating or postnatally-bleeding woman.
8. **Sexual enjoyment of the area between the navel and the knees.** The ḥadīth (Zaid ibn Aslam): a man asked what was permissible of his wife in her menses; the Prophet ﷺ said *"She should put on her **izār** (the cloth wrapping the lower half), then your concern is with her **upper half**."* (Muwaṭṭaʾ of Imām Muḥammad 75.) *(ʿĀʾishah's easier view, also recorded: he should simply avoid the place of blood.)*

**Yaad rakho:** **Ḥayḍ/nifās mein 8 cheezein haraam** (dono ki list aik hi): (1) **namaz**, (2) **roza**, (3) **Quran ki aayat parhna** (zabani ya muṣḥaf se) — magar **duʿā/tasbīḥ/dhikr jaayiz**, aur seekhne-sikhaane ki ijaazat (Mālikī); (4) **Quran chhoona** (bila-ghilaaf nahin — al-Wāqiʿa 56:79); (5) **masjid mein daakhil hona** (Ibn Mājah 645); (6) **ṭawāf** (Bukhārī 305); (7) **jimāʿ**; (8) **naaf se ghutne tak** lutf uthaana (izār pehna kar upar wala hissa — Muwaṭṭaʾ Muḥammad 75)। Yaad: namaz **qaza nahin**, roza **qaza hai** (§6)।

## 5 · The two smaller impurity-states — janāba (5) and ḥadath (3)

After the big list, the book sets two **shorter** forbidden-lists beside it — and the elegant thing is they are **nested subsets** of the eight. Teach them as **shrinking circles**:

- **Janāba (major ritual impurity — needs ghusl): 5 things forbidden.** *(Janāba = the state after intercourse or seminal emission — "distance," because one is kept *distant* from worship until ghusl.)* The five: **(1) prayer** *(al-Nisā 4:43)*, **(2) reciting Qur'an** *(the same Ibn Mājah 596 ḥadīth)*, **(3) touching the Qur'an** without a covering *(al-Wāqiʿa 56:79)*, **(4) entering a mosque**, **(5) ṭawāf.** Notice: this is the eight **minus** fasting, intercourse, and the navel-to-knee rule (those are ḥayḍ-specific).
- **Ḥadath (minor impurity — needs wudū): 3 things forbidden.** *"Prayer, ṭawāf around the kaʿba, and to touch the Qur'an without a covering."* The smallest circle — the three things that even a broken wudū blocks.

So the structure is **8 ⊃ 5 ⊃ 3**: the worse the impurity, the more it shuts off. Ḥayḍ/nifās (8) shut off the most; janāba (5) less; minor ḥadath (3) least. *(Why does janāba allow fasting while ḥayḍ forbids it? Because the junub can **bathe and lift the state**, but the menstruating woman cannot remove her state at will — a theme the book returns to.)*

**The two rulings that hang off this section:**

**(a) Make up the fasts, not the prayers.** *"A woman with menstrual periods or postnatal bleeding must **repeat the days of fasting** she missed, **not prayer**."* ʿĀʾishah: *"We were encountered with it… thereupon we were ordered to **make up fasting**, but **were not** ordered to make up prayer"* (Muslim 335). The reason the book gives: prayers recur five times daily — making up a week of them would be *"great difficulty"* — so Allah eased it; fasting comes once a year and is manageable. *(And it is **recommended** for her to keep her prayer-routine alive: make wudū at each prayer-time, sit on her prayer-mat, and do dhikr — so she doesn't lose the habit. Radd al-Muḥtār.)*

**(b) When may intercourse resume?** Two cases, turning on the verse al-Baqarah 2:222 — *"Do not approach them until they are pure."* The verse-word **يَطْهُرْنَ** has *two readings*, and the book uses both:
- **If the bleeding stops at the maximum** (ḥayḍ reaches its 10th day, or nifās its 40th): intercourse is permitted **immediately, even without ghusl** — because at the maximum, blood beyond it is *no longer ḥayḍ/nifās at all.* (This rests on the *"until they are pure"* reading.)
- **If the bleeding stops short of the maximum** (she finished her **regular days** but is under the ceiling): intercourse is **not** permitted until **one** of three things happens — *(i)* she makes **ghusl**, *or (ii)* she is unable to and makes **tayammum + prays**, *or (iii)* **a prayer-time fully passes** over her with enough time to have bathed. (This rests on the *"until they purify themselves"* reading — purification is required.)

The clean takeaway: **stopped at the max → free immediately; stopped early → she must purify (or a prayer-time must lapse) first.**

**Yaad rakho:** Do chhoti lists, baRi list ke **andar**: **janāba = 5 haraam** (namaz, Quran parhna, Quran chhoona, masjid, ṭawāf — yaani 8 me se roza/jimāʿ/naaf-ghutna nikaal do), **ḥadath (chhoti na-paaki) = 3 haraam** (namaz, ṭawāf, Quran chhoona)। **8 ⊃ 5 ⊃ 3.** Do faisle: (a) **roze ki qaza haan, namaz ki nahin** (Muslim 335 — namaz baar baar aati hai, mushkil); (b) **jimāʿ kab?** — agar khoon **max par ruka** (ḥayḍ 10 / nifās 40) to **foran, bina ghusl bhi**; agar **max se pehle** (apni aadat ke din poore) ruka to **ghusl** (ya tayammum+namaz, ya ek namaz ka waqt guzar jaaye) ke baad (al-Baqarah 2:222 — do qiraaten)।

## 6 · Istiḥāḍa is no excuse from worship — and the "excused" (maʿdhūr)

Here the chapter delivers its **reversal** (master-key 3) and generalises it into a whole category of chronically-leaking worshippers.

**Istiḥāḍa behaves like a permanent nosebleed.** *"The blood of istiḥāḍa is like that of a **permanent nose bleed**. It does **not** prevent prayer, fasting, or intercourse."* So everything ḥayḍ/nifās forbade, istiḥāḍa **permits** — she prays, fasts, may be approached. The only requirement: *"The one suffering from chronic vaginal discharge is required to **perform ablution at the time of each obligatory prayer**."* The Prophet ﷺ: *"The women with extended bleeding is to **perform wudu for each prayer**"* [book's wording] (Ibn Mājah). With that one wudū she may pray *"as many obligatory and optional prayers as she likes"* — and the flowing blood **during** the prayer does not break it.

**The general category: the excused (maʿdhūr / ʿudhr).** The book widens the rule beyond women: *"The same applies for anyone who suffers a chronic condition (ʿudhr)"* — a person who **cannot stop intermittent drops of urine**, a **perpetual nosebleed**, **constant breaking of wind**, a **wound that won't stop flowing**. *"The term **excused [maʿdhūr]** applies to a person who suffers from a chronic ailment for a **complete prayer time** and is therefore unable to maintain wudū for the time needed to offer the prayer."* They are **excused** and may pray *with* the illness present.

**The three conditions that govern the excuse** — the book gives them as three sub-headings; learn them as **start / continue / end**:
- **When is one *deemed* excused (start)?** *"One is not deemed excused until he suffers from the illness for a **full prayer time**… and in that time there is **no let up** from the illness that would allow him enough time to perform ablution and offer the prayer."* (The illness must own a *whole* prayer-time once.)
- **What is required to *remain* excused (continue)?** *"the illness must occur in **every prayer time** after it has been established even if it only occurs once."* (One occurrence per prayer-time keeps the status alive.)
- **What *nullifies* the excuse (end)?** *"The **absence** of the illness for a **complete prayer time**"* — a whole prayer-time passes with no occurrence → she is no longer excused and returns to normal rules.

**When the excused person's wudū breaks.** *"The ablution of those who are excused is nullified **only when the time of the obligatory prayer ends**."* So her wudū is tied to the **prayer-time**, not to the leaking: ẓuhr-time wudū dies when ʿaṣr enters. Two riders the book gives: *(i)* if she is excused **for one cause** (say, constant wind) but a **different**, non-chronic ḥadath occurs (she accidentally cuts herself and bleeds), **that** breaks her wudū normally; *(ii)* fajr-time ends at **sunrise**, not at ẓuhr — so the excused person's fajr wudū dies at sunrise.

**Yaad rakho:** **Istiḥāḍa ibadat se chhutkaara nahin** — woh "musalsal naak ke khoon" jaisa hai: namaz, roza, jimāʿ sab **jaayiz**; bas **har farḍ namaz ke waqt naya wudū** (Ibn Mājah), aur ek wudū se jitni chaaho farḍ-o-nafl parho; namaz ke darmiyaan khoon behne se namaz **nahin tootti**. Yahi **maʿdhūr** ka aam usool hai (musalsal peshaab/naak ka khoon/hawa/zakhm). **Teen shartein — shuru/jaari/khatam:** (1) **shuru:** poora ek namaz ka waqt bina mohlat ke beemari rahe; (2) **jaari:** har namaz ke waqt mein kam-se-kam **ek baar** aaye; (3) **khatam:** poore ek waqt **bilkul na aaye** to ʿudhr khatam. Maʿdhūr ka wudū **waqt khatam hone par** tootta hai (alag gair-mustaqil ḥadath bhi tor de; fajr ka waqt **tulūʿ** par khatam)।

---

## The big picture — what Chapter 7 is silently teaching

1. **Worship bends to the body — by Allah's design, not by neglect.** A woman is *commanded* to leave prayer in ḥayḍ; obeying the rukhṣa is itself obedience. The cohort (and Ramsha) should feel the chapter as **mercy and order**, never as exclusion or shame. *(Ḥayḍ mein namaz chhoRna bhi Allah ka hukm maan-na hai — yeh raħmat aur nizaam hai, mahroomi nahin.)*
2. **The classification is the whole game.** One blood, three legal identities — and the day-count decides which. Almost every error in real life is a *mis-classification* (calling istiḥāḍa "a period," or counting beyond ten days as ḥayḍ). Teach the numbers, and the rulings follow. *(Asal kaam khoon ko **pehchaan-na** hai; ginti theek to hukm theek.)*
3. **The Law eases what is genuinely hard, and holds what is manageable.** Prayers are forgiven (five a day, a heavy makeup) but fasts are made up (once a year). Istiḥāḍa is downgraded to a mere wudū-renewal so a chronically-bleeding woman is not locked out of worship for life. *(Mushkil cheez maaf — namaz; mumkin cheez baaqi — roza ki qaza.)*
4. **Purity is a state with an on/off logic, and the maʿdhūr rule keeps the chronically-impure inside worship.** Rather than excluding those who *cannot* stay pure, the Law gives them a **prayer-time-bound** purity. *(Jo paaki qaaim nahin rakh sakta, use waqt-bandhi paaki di gayi — ibadat se bahar nahin kiya.)*

**Yaad rakho:** Poora chapter: **teen khoon, ginti se pehchaan**; **ḥayḍ/nifās ibadat rokte hain (8), istiḥāḍa nahin (maʿdhūr — har waqt wudū)**; **namaz qaza nahin, roza qaza**; aur sab kuchh **Allah ka hukm, raħmat aur nizaam** — sharm ya mahroomi nahin.

# Part B · Teaching the chapter

You now understand Chapter 7. This section is how to **deliver** it to the same adult, mostly Urdu-speaking cohort — who already know wudū (3a/3b), ghusl (4), tayammum (5) and mash (6). This is the cohort's **most practically important** purification chapter (it governs the daily worship of every woman present, and every man's marriage), but also the most **sensitive**. Your three jobs: (1) hold a **calm, clinical, dignified register** so no one is embarrassed; (2) make the **classification engine** (the day-counts) rock-solid, because every real-life mistake is a mis-classification; and (3) be disciplined about **what you teach to the whole room vs. what you answer one-to-one when asked.**

---

## A note on register and audience — read before you plan the class

The cohort is **adult**; many are **married women** for whom this is lived reality, and there may be **men** present for whom parts are about their wives. So:

- **Match the book's clinical tone.** Say "menstruation," "intercourse," "the area between the navel and the knees" plainly — the way a doctor or the book does. Vagueness *helps no one*; a married woman needs to know exactly when she may pray again or be approached.
- **Separate "everyone needs this" from "answer if asked."** The **classification, the 8 forbidden things, the make-up rule, and the istiḥāḍa/wudū rule** are for the whole room — these are needed weekly. The **intercourse-timing details (case b), the navel-to-knee rule, the expiation-for-ṭawāf, the worked istiḥāḍa habit-cases** are best handled **briefly, and expanded only on a direct question** — ideally women-to-women, or "come ask me after."
- **Frame it as mercy and obedience from the first minute** — so the room receives it as dignified fiqh, not as something to be awkward about.

**Yaad rakho:** Lehja **saaf, ba-waqaar, clinical** — bilkul kitaab jaisa; gol-mol baat se kisi ka faaida nahin (shaadi-shuda aurat ko theek waqt jaanna zaroori)। **Sab ke liye:** classification, 8 haraam cheezein, qaza ka usool, istiḥāḍa/wudū. **Sirf poochhne par (alag se):** jimāʿ ka waqt (case b), naaf-se-ghutna, ṭawāf ka kaffāra, istiḥāḍa ke tafseeli aadat-cases. Shuru se **raħmat aur farmaanbardaari** ke taur par pesh karo.

---

## The five landmines for this cohort

### Landmine 1 · Calling istiḥāḍa "a period" — the classification error

The single most consequential mistake. A woman who bleeds 12 days assumes all 12 are ḥayḍ and skips prayer the whole time — but **only up to 10 is ḥayḍ**; day 11–12 is **istiḥāḍa**, during which she **must pray** (those skipped prayers were obligatory and are now owed). The reverse error: spotting for one day and skipping prayer, when **under 3 days isn't ḥayḍ at all** — it is istiḥāḍa, and she **must** pray.

**Counter-strategy:** Put the **clock table** on the board and never take it down: ḥayḍ **3–10**, nifās **max 40**, anything outside = **istiḥāḍa = you still pray.** Drill 3–4 day-count scenarios out loud.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Har khoon 'period' nahin. **Ḥayḍ sirf 3 se 10 din.** 10 ke baad ka khoon **istiḥāḍa** hai — us mein **namaz farḍ** hai, chhoRi to qaza. Aur **3 din se kam** bhi ḥayḍ nahin — woh bhi istiḥāḍa, namaz parho. Ginti se pehchaano, andaaze se nahin."*

### Landmine 2 · "Coloured discharge means it's over"

Women often think the period ends when the blood turns brown/yellow. The book is the opposite: **during the days, every colour except pure white is still menses.** Stopping prayer too early (on yellow) or resuming too early is the error.

**Counter-strategy:** Teach ʿĀʾishah's rule: *wait for the **white discharge (ṭuhr)**.* Yellow/brown/black within the window = still ḥayḍ.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Period khatam tab nahin jab rang badle — balki jab **safed paani (ṭuhr)** dikhe. Dinon ke andar **zard/bhura/kaala sab ḥayḍ**. ʿĀʾishah ne kaha: **safed dekhne tak jaldi mat karo.**"*

### Landmine 3 · "She makes up the missed prayers too"

By analogy with fasting, learners assume missed prayers are made up. They are **not** — fasts yes, prayers no.

**Counter-strategy:** State it as a clean pair and give the reason: prayers recur 5× daily (a week's makeup = great hardship → forgiven); fasting is once a year (manageable → made up). Anchor with ʿĀʾishah (Muslim 335).

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"**Roze ki qaza hai, namaz ki nahin.** Namaz din mein 5 baar — hafte bhar ki qaza bohot mushkil, is liye maaf; roza saal mein ek baar — qaza mumkin. (ʿĀʾishah, Muslim 335.)"*

### Landmine 4 · "When the blood stops, intercourse is fine right away"

True **only** if it stopped at the *maximum* (10 / 40). If it stopped **early** (her regular days, under the ceiling), intercourse waits until she **makes ghusl** (or tayammum+prays, or a full prayer-time lapses).

**Counter-strategy:** Two boxes on the board — *"stopped at the max → free now"* vs *"stopped early → purify first."* Keep this **brief in the full room**; expand only if asked.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Khoon ruk-na kaafi nahin. Agar **max par ruka** (10/40) — to **foran** theek. Agar **apne dinon par jaldi ruka** — to pehle **ghusl** (ya tayammum+namaz, ya ek namaz ka waqt guzar jaaye), phir."*

### Landmine 5 · Putting ḥayḍ's "stop all worship" onto istiḥāḍa

After learning the 8 forbidden things, learners wrongly think *any* vaginal bleeding stops prayer. Istiḥāḍa is the **opposite** — she **prays and fasts**, with a fresh wudū each prayer-time.

**Counter-strategy:** Hammer the reversal: *istiḥāḍa = permanent nosebleed = you keep worshipping (wudū per prayer-time).* This is the maʿdhūr rule; tie it to anyone with a chronic leak (urine drops, wind, a wound).

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"**Istiḥāḍa namaz nahin rokta.** Woh 'musalsal naak ka khoon' jaisa — namaz, roza sab parho, bas **har namaz ke waqt naya wudū**. Yahi **maʿdhūr** ka qaida (musalsal peshaab/hawa/zakhm waale bhi)。"*

## Suggested 70-minute class plan

*Class shuru karne se pehle:* board par do cheezein taiyaar rakho — (a) the **clock table** (ḥayḍ 3–10 · nifās max 40 · istiḥāḍa = outside) and (b) the **three shrinking circles** (ḥayḍ/nifās = 8 ⊃ janāba 5 ⊃ ḥadath 3). And open with one sentence of **framing**: *yeh raħmat aur nizaam hai.*

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–6 min | Framing + bridge: ghusl (Ch 4) ne ḥayḍ/nifās ke **khatam** ko ghusl ka sabab kaha — ab uski **tafseel**. Set the dignified, clinical tone. |
| 6–14 min | The **three master-keys** — *numbers decide* · *anything-but-white = menses* · *the great reversal (ḥayḍ stops worship, istiḥāḍa doesn't)*. Draw the **clock table**. |
| 14–24 min | **§1–2 Ḥayḍ** — who (bāligha, not ill/pregnant, under 55) · the pillar (blood appears) · **3/5/10** · colours (wait for white). Land Landmines 1 & 2. |
| 24–32 min | **§3 Nifās & istiḥāḍa & pure days** — nifās max 40/no min (Ibn Mājah 649) · istiḥāḍa = overflow → fall back on habit · 15 pure days. |
| 32–44 min | **§4 the 8 forbidden** — prayer, fasting, recite, touch, mosque, ṭawāf, intercourse, navel-to-knee. Keep #7/#8 brief & dignified. |
| 44–52 min | **§5 the two smaller lists** (janāba 5, ḥadath 3 — shrinking circles) + **make-up: fasts yes, prayers no** (Muslim 335). Land Landmine 3. *Intercourse-timing (case b): one line; expand only if asked* (Landmine 4). |
| 52–64 min | **§6 istiḥāḍa & the maʿdhūr** — permanent-nosebleed → keep worshipping, wudū per prayer-time (Ibn Mājah); the 3 conditions (start/continue/end); wudū dies at prayer-time's end. Land Landmine 5. |
| 64–70 min | Q&A (offer one-to-one for sensitive questions) · assign handout · preview Chapter 8 (Najāsa — filth). |

**Yaad rakho:** Do board-tools: **clock table** aur **teen ghatti circles (8⊃5⊃3)**. Pehle teen chaabiyaan, phir sab aasaan. Sab se ahem teen jagah: **classification** (L1), **roza-qaza/namaz-nahin** (L3), aur **istiḥāḍa ibadat nahin rokta** (L5)। Lehja shuru se ba-waqaar.

## What you should NOT teach (to the full room) in Chapter 7

- ❌ **The three kinds of divorce** (ḥasan / aḥsan / bidʿī) on p.94. The book mentions them only to show *why* ḥayḍ matters — they are a **divorce chapter**, not this one. One line ("ḥayḍ even decides divorce and ʿidda timing") is plenty; don't teach the rules.
- ❌ **The worked istiḥāḍa habit-cases** (half-the-sun-risen, "increased by one-sixth," the Ikhtiyār "ten days each month / nineteen pure" view, the just-pubescent assigned 10/15). Teach the **principle** (overflow → fall back on the known habit; surplus = istiḥāḍa) and stop. The arithmetic is Part-A depth for *you*.
- ❌ **The ṭawāf expiation** (sacrifice a cow/camel/sheep for ṭawāf in impurity) — that is **ḥajj** detail. Name only that ṭawāf is forbidden in this state.
- ❌ **The intercourse-timing casuistry (case b) and the navel-to-knee rule in graphic detail** — state them once, plainly, and **expand only on a direct (ideally private) question.** Don't dwell.
- ❌ **The two-readings tajwīd discussion of يَطْهُرْنَ** as a tajwīd lesson — name that the verse grounds the two cases; don't run the qirāʾāt.
- ❌ **Najāsa (filth) detail** — that is **Chapter 8**. Don't pre-teach. If asked where "impure blood" fits: *"Chapter 8 mein."*
- ❌ **Comparative fiqh as debate** — the Shāfiʿī minimum (one day/night) and nifās-60 are named in the book only as contrasts; mention *that* they differ, then return to the Ḥanafī rule. Don't argue it.

**Yaad rakho:** Chhod do: **talaaq ki 3 qismein** (yeh chapter nahin), **istiḥāḍa ke tafseeli hisaab**, **ṭawāf ka kaffāra** (ḥajj), **jimāʿ ka tafseel** (sirf poochhne par, alag se), **يطهرن ki qiraaten** (tajwīd nahin), **najāsat** (Ch 8). Usool parhao, footnote nahin.

## Self-check before teaching

Before class, make sure you can:

- [ ] State the **three master-keys** (numbers decide · anything-but-white = menses · the great reversal) and explain how each decides cases.
- [ ] Recite the **clock table** from memory: ḥayḍ **3 / 5 / 10**, nifās **max 40 / no min**, istiḥāḍa = **outside the window**, **15** pure days minimum.
- [ ] State **who has ḥayḍ** (bāligha · not ill/pregnant · under 55) and the **pillar** (blood appears outside).
- [ ] List the **8 forbidden** things and attach the right ḥadīth/verse to at least prayer, recite, touch, mosque, ṭawāf.
- [ ] Explain the **nested lists** (8 ⊃ janāba 5 ⊃ ḥadath 3) and *why* janāba allows fasting (she can bathe).
- [ ] State the **make-up rule** (fasts yes, prayers no — Muslim 335) and its reason.
- [ ] State the **two intercourse-resumption cases** (stopped at max → now; stopped early → purify first).
- [ ] Explain **istiḥāḍa = maʿdhūr** (permanent nosebleed → keep worshipping, wudū per prayer-time) and the **three conditions** (start / continue / end) + when the excused's wudū breaks (prayer-time ends).

If yes to all eight — *tum tayyar ho.*

## After class — diagnostic checklist

For each classmate, watch for:

1. **Can they classify by the numbers?** *(12 days bleeding → kitne din ḥayḍ, kitne istiḥāḍa? 2 days spotting → ḥayḍ ya nahin?)* — the single most important test.
2. **Do they know to wait for the white discharge, not a colour-change?**
3. **Do they know prayers are NOT made up, but fasts ARE?**
4. **Do they know istiḥāḍa does NOT stop prayer (wudū per prayer-time)?**
5. **Do they hold the dignified, matter-of-fact attitude** — receiving this as mercy and law, not embarrassment?

**Yaad rakho:** Agar 2+ classmates abhi bhi **10 din se zyada ko ḥayḍ** ginte hain, ya **istiḥāḍa mein namaz chhoRte** hain — to Chapter 8 se pehle 5-minute ka **clock-table + reversal** refresher lo. Yeh chapter tab kaamyaab hai jab cohort **ginti se classify** kar le aur **istiḥāḍa ko ibadat-rok na samjhe** — aur poora maahaul ba-waqaar rahe.
