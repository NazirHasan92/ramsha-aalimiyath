From ghusl to the three bloods

In Ghusl (Ch 4) we said: ḥayḍ ending and nifās ending make the major bath obligatory.

Today: the detail — what these bloods are, how long, and what they change.

The book calls this "one of the most important issues" — a vast amount of fiqh (worship, marriage, lineage) turns on it.

Yeh raħmat aur nizaam hai — saaf, ba-waqaar baat.

Three keys to the whole chapter

1 · The numbers decide
ḥayḍ 3–10 · nifās max 40 · outside = istiḥāḍa
2 · Anything-but-white
within the days, every colour = menses until the white discharge
3 · The great reversal
ḥayḍ/nifās STOP worship; istiḥāḍa does NOT

Learn these first — almost every ruling turns on one of them.

Ginti se pehchaan, safed = paaki, aur istiḥāḍa ibadat nahin rokta.

Rule 1 · The three bloods & the clock

BloodMinimumMaximum
Ḥayḍ (menstruation)3 days10 days (avg 5)
Nifās (postnatal)none40 days
Istiḥāḍa (chronic)the leftover — outside the window

You never spot istiḥāḍa directly — rule out ḥayḍ/nifās by the numbers; what's left is istiḥāḍa.

Ḥayḍ 3–10, nifās max 40 (min nahin), baaqi = istiḥāḍa.

Rule 2 · Ḥayḍ — who & how long

Who
bāligha (≥9) · NOT ill/pregnant · under 55 (menopause)
How long
min 3 days (72h) · avg 5 · max 10 (240h) — a dry spell doesn't reset

Outside this (under 9, past 55, under 3 days, over 10 days) → istiḥāḍa.

Ḥayḍ: 9 ke baad, na beemar na haamla, 55 se kam; 3 se 10 din.

The colours — wait for the white

Within the days, red, yellow, muddy, green, black — all are menses.

Purity = the pure-white discharge (ṭuhr).

ʿĀʾishah: "Do not be hasty until you see a white discharge."

— Muwaṭṭaʾ of Imām Mālik 99

Rang badalna kaafi nahin — safed paani dekho.

Rule 3 · Nifās, istiḥāḍa & the pure days

Nifās
after birth · max 40 · NO minimum (Ibn Mājah 649)
Istiḥāḍa overflow
past the max → fall back on her known habit; surplus = istiḥāḍa
Pure days
min 15 between two periods (no maximum)

Ḥayḍ has a floor AND a ceiling; nifās has only a ceiling (40).

Nifās max 40, min nahin; barh jaaye → aadat par lauTo; 15 din paaki.

Rule 4 · The 8 forbidden in ḥayḍ & nifās

Worship
1 · prayer · 2 · fasting
The Qur'an
3 · reciting · 4 · touching
Sacred places
5 · mosque · 6 · ṭawāf
Intimacy
7 · intercourse · 8 · navel↔knee

Duʿā & dhikr stay permitted · prayer NOT made up, fasting IS.

8 haraam (4 jore). Duʿā jaayiz. Namaz qaza nahin, roza qaza.

Rule 5 · The shrinking circles (8 ⊃ 5 ⊃ 3)

State#Which
Ḥayḍ / Nifās8the full list
Janāba (ghusl)5prayer · recite · touch · mosque · ṭawāf
Ḥadath (wudū)3prayer · ṭawāf · touch Qur'an

Janāba allows fasting — the junub can bathe and lift the state.

8 ⊃ 5 ⊃ 3. Janāba mein roza jaayiz (ghusl se haalat uth jaaye).

Make-up + resuming intercourse

ʿĀʾishah: "We were ordered to make up fasting, but were not ordered to make up prayer."

— Muslim 335

Stopped at the MAX (10 / 40)
intercourse permitted now, even before ghusl
Stopped EARLY (regular days)
wait for ghusl (or tayammum+pray, or a prayer-time lapses)

Roza qaza, namaz nahin. Jimāʿ: max par → foran; jaldi ruka → ghusl ke baad.

Rule 6 · Istiḥāḍa & the excused (maʿdhūr)

Istiḥāḍa is "like a permanent nosebleed" — it does NOT stop prayer, fasting or intercourse.

Just make a fresh wudū at each prayer-time (Ibn Mājah) — then pray as many as you like.

Start
ailment fills a whole prayer-time (no gap to pray)
Continue
occurs every prayer-time after (even once)
End
a full prayer-time passes with none

Istiḥāḍa ibadat nahin rokta — har waqt naya wudū. Yahi maʿdhūr ka qaida.

Recap

Classify by the numbers:
ḥayḍ 3–10 · nifās max 40 (no min) · outside = istiḥāḍa · 15 pure days · wait for the white discharge
The consequences:
ḥayḍ/nifās forbid 8 (⊃ janāba 5 ⊃ ḥadath 3) · fasts made up, not prayers · istiḥāḍa = keep worshipping (wudū per prayer-time)

The classification is the whole game — get the numbers right and the rulings follow.

Asal kaam khoon pehchaan-na hai; ginti theek to hukm theek.

🏠 Homework

  1. Memorise the three keys (numbers decide · white = purity · the reversal)

  3. Learn the 8 forbidden things in four pairs
  4. Learn the make-up rule (fasts yes, prayers no)
  5. Learn why istiḥāḍa does not stop worship
