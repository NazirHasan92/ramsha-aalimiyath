Chapter 7 — al-Ḥayḍ wa'n-Nifās (menstruation & postnatal bleeding) · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · The three bloods, how long each lasts, what they change in worship, and the "excused" rule

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

In the Ghusl chapter we said that ḥayḍ ending and nifās ending make the major bath obligatory. Today we learn the detail. The book names three bloods that exit the female body — and the whole topic is about telling them apart, because each one changes a woman's worship differently. We answer:

  1. The three bloods — and the day-counts that classify them.
  2. Ḥayḍ — who has it, how long, and the colour rule.
  3. Nifās & istiḥāḍa — postnatal blood and the "leftover" category.
  4. The 8 things forbidden during ḥayḍ/nifās.
  5. The two smaller lists (janāba, ḥadath) + making up fasts (not prayers).
  6. Istiḥāḍa & the excused — why this blood does not stop worship.

A note on the topic: this is intimate but everyday fiqh — receive it the way the book gives it, as mercy and law, calmly and plainly. (Yeh raħmat aur nizaam hai — sharm ki baat nahin.)

Source: everything here comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text), the Chapter on Menstruation, Postnatal & Chronic Bleeding (book pp. 94–106). No outside sources have been added.

The three keys to the whole chapter

Before the lists, learn these three. Almost every ruling turns on one of them:

KeyWhat it means
1 · The numbers decideEach blood has a day-window: ḥayḍ 3–10 days, nifās max 40 (no minimum), and anything outside the window is istiḥāḍa. The day-count, not how it feels, classifies the blood.
2 · Anything-but-white = mensesWithin the days, red, yellow, muddy, green, black all count as ḥayḍ — until the pure-white discharge appears. Wait for the white, not a colour-change.
3 · The great reversalḤayḍ & nifās STOP worship (8 forbidden things). Istiḥāḍa does NOT — it is like a permanent nosebleed; she keeps praying/fasting and just renews wudū each prayer-time.

Urdu mein: Teen chaabiyaan: (1) ginti faisla karti hai (ḥayḍ 3–10, nifās max 40, bahar = istiḥāḍa); (2) safed ke alaawa har rang = ḥayḍ jab tak safed na dikhe; (3) bada palaT — ḥayḍ/nifās ibadat rokte hain, istiḥāḍa nahin.

Rule 1 · The three bloods & the clock

BloodWhat it isMinimumMaximum
Ḥayḍ (menstruation)natural blood from the uterus3 days (+ nights)10 days · avg 5
Nifās (postnatal)blood after giving birthnone40 days
Istiḥāḍa (chronic)the leftover — blood outside the windowunder 3 · over 10 (ḥayḍ) · over 40 (nifās)

You never spot istiḥāḍa directly — you classify by the numbers, rule out ḥayḍ and nifās, and what is left over is istiḥāḍa.

Urdu mein: Teen khoon: ḥayḍ (3–10), nifās (max 40, min koi nahin), istiḥāḍa (jo bach jaaye). Ginti se ḥayḍ/nifās khaarij karke jo bache woh istiḥāḍa.

Rule 2 · Ḥayḍ — who, how long, the colours

QuestionThe answer
Who has it?A girl who has reached puberty (no girl before 9), who is not ill or pregnant, and who is under 55 (menopause). Outside this → istiḥāḍa.
When does it "count"?From when the blood appears outside (the pillar) — not while it is still internal.
How long?Minimum 3 days (72 hours), average 5, maximum 10 days (240 hours). A dry spell mid-period does not reset the count. Under 3 days = istiḥāḍa.
Which colours?Within the days, every colour but pure white is menses (red, yellow, muddy, green, black). Purity = the white discharge.

💡 Wait for the white discharge

ʿĀʾishah told the women: "Do not be hasty until you see a white discharge." A change to brown or yellow does not mean the period is over.

Urdu mein: Ḥayḍ: bāligha (9 ke baad), na beemar na haamla, 55 se kam. Min 3 din (72 ghante), max 10 (240 ghante). Safed ke alaawa har rang ḥayḍ — safed dekhne tak intezaar.

Rule 3 · Nifās & istiḥāḍa & the pure days

TopicThe rule
NifāsBlood after birth. Maximum 40 days; no minimum (can stop any time). Past 40 days = istiḥāḍa. (Anas — Ibn Mājah 649.)
Istiḥāḍa overflowWhen blood goes past the maximum, the woman falls back on her known habit (ʿāda) — and the surplus is istiḥāḍa.
Pure daysAt least 15 days of purity must separate two periods. There is no maximum (she may be pure for a year or more).

Urdu mein: Nifās: max 40, min nahin (Ibn Mājah 649). Khoon barh jaaye → apni aadat par lauTo, zyaadati istiḥāḍa. Do periods ke beech kam-se-kam 15 din paaki.

Rule 4 · The 8 things forbidden during ḥayḍ & nifās

Ḥayḍ and nifās share the same list of eight. Learn them in four pairs:

PairThe two forbidden things
Worship-acts1. Prayer (ṣalāh) · 2. Fasting (ṣawm) — obligatory or optional
The Qur'an3. Reciting a verse (from memory or muṣḥaf) · 4. Touching the Qur'an without a covering
Sacred places5. Entering a mosque · 6. Ṭawāf around the Kaʿba
Intimacy7. Sexual intercourse · 8. Enjoyment of the area between the navel and the knees

⚠️ Two things to get right

(a) She may still say duʿā, dhikr, tasbīḥ, takbīr and "Bismillāh" — only reciting Qur'an as Qur'an is forbidden. (b) Prayer is NOT made up; fasting IS made up later.

Urdu mein: 8 haraam (4 jore): namaz+roza · Quran parhna+chhoona · masjid+ṭawāf · jimāʿ+naaf-se-ghutna. Duʿā/dhikr jaayiz. Namaz qaza nahin, roza qaza.

Rule 5 · The two smaller lists + making up worship

The same acts are forbidden in lesser impurity-states too — but fewer of them. The worse the impurity, the more it shuts off (8 ⊃ 5 ⊃ 3):

StateHow many forbiddenWhich
Ḥayḍ / Nifās8the full list above
Janāba (major impurity — needs ghusl)5prayer · reciting · touching the Qur'an · mosque · ṭawāf
Ḥadath (minor impurity — needs wudū)3prayer · ṭawāf · touching the Qur'an

💡 Make up the fasts, not the prayers

A woman makes up the fasts she missed, but not the prayers — because prayers recur five times daily (a week's makeup is too heavy → forgiven) while fasting is once a year (manageable). (ʿĀʾishah — Muslim 335.)

When may intercourse resume? If the blood stopped at the maximum (ḥayḍ day 10 / nifās day 40) → permitted right away, even before ghusl. If it stopped early (her regular days) → not until she makes ghusl (or tayammum and prays, or a full prayer-time passes).

Urdu mein: 8 ⊃ 5 ⊃ 3. Janāba = 5, ḥadath = 3. Roza qaza, namaz nahin (Muslim 335). Jimāʿ: max par ruka → foran; jaldi ruka → ghusl ke baad.

Rule 6 · Istiḥāḍa & the excused (maʿdhūr)

Istiḥāḍa blood is "like a permanent nosebleed" — it does not prevent prayer, fasting, or intercourse. The one requirement: make a fresh wudū at the time of each obligatory prayer, and with it pray as many prayers as she likes; blood flowing during the prayer does not break it.

This is the general rule of the excused person (maʿdhūr) — anyone with a chronic leak (constant urine drops, a perpetual nosebleed, constant wind, a wound that won't stop). Three conditions:

ConditionThe rule
Start (become excused)The ailment fills a whole prayer-time with no gap long enough to make wudū and pray.
Continue (stay excused)It occurs in every prayer-time after that — even just once.
End (no longer excused)A full prayer-time passes with no occurrence at all.

The excused person's wudū breaks when the prayer-time ends — not from the leaking itself. (A different, non-chronic event — like a fresh cut — breaks it normally.)

Urdu mein: Istiḥāḍa ibadat nahin rokta — har namaz ke waqt naya wudū (Ibn Mājah). Yahi maʿdhūr ka qaida. Wudū waqt khatam hone par tootta.
