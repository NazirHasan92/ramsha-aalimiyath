> **Why this is "Chapter 3a" and not the whole of Chapter 3.** The Wudū chapter in Nur al-Idah is the **densest chapter in Book I** — roughly twenty pages, twelve distinct topics. Teaching it in one sitting would drown an adult cohort. So we split it cleanly down the middle:
> - **Chapter 3a (this file)** — *Performing wudū*: the opening, the 4 pillars, the conditions, the sub-rules of fulfilment, the 18 sunnas, the 14 etiquettes, the 6 disliked acts. This is everything you **do** when you make wudū, arranged as a four-tier quality gradient.
> - **Chapter 3b (next session)** — *The status of wudū*: the 3 types of wudū, the 12 nullifiers, and the 10 non-nullifiers. This is the **legal life** of a wudū once you have it.
>
> Teach 3a first. 3b only makes sense once the cohort can already perform wudū.

# Part A · Understanding the chapter

Chapter 1 was about **water**. Chapter 2 was about **istinja** — the first use of that water. Now we reach the act that all of those were preparing for: **wudū**, the ablution that unlocks prayer.

The genius of how Imam Shurunbulali organises this chapter is that he doesn't give you a flat list of "things to do in wudū." He gives you a **four-tier gradient of obligation** — and once you see the gradient, the whole chapter organises itself:

| Tier | Arabic | Weight | Count in the book | If you omit it… |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Farḍ** (pillars / *arkān*) | Obligatory | **4** | Wudū is **invalid** — must redo |
| 2 | **Sunna** | Confirmed practice | **18** | Wudū is valid, but **deficient / less reward** |
| 3 | **Adab** (*mandūb* / etiquette) | Recommended | **14** | Wudū is valid; you simply **missed extra reward** |
| 4 | **Makrūh** (disliked) | To be avoided | **6** | Wudū is valid, but you did something **disliked** |

This is the same logical move the book made in Chapter 2 with *ḥarām vs makrūh* — except here it is stretched across **four** levels instead of two. **The single most important thing your cohort must leave with is this gradient.** Everything else is detail that hangs on it.

**Yaad rakho:** Pura wudū ka chapter ek **chaar-manzila imārat** hai. Sab se neeche **farḍ (4)** — yeh na ho to wudū hi nahin. Uske oopar **sunna (18)** — chhoot jaaye to wudū ho jaata hai par naaqis. Phir **adab (14)** — sawaab ki baat. Sab se oopar **makrūh (6)** — karne se bachna. Agar cohort sirf yeh chaar darje samajh le, baaqi sab apne aap jagah par baith jaata hai.

Work through the seven sections below in order.

## 0 · The opening — sins falling away, and the Qur'anic command

Before a single ruling, the book opens with a **hadith of mercy**. The Prophet ﷺ said:

> *"When a person performs ablution and rinses his mouth, his wrong deeds fall from it. As he rinses his nose, his wrong deeds fall from it. When he washes his face, his wrong deeds fall from it until they fall from beneath his eyelashes. When he washes his hands, his wrong deeds fall from them until they fall from beneath the fingernails. When he wipes his head, his wrong deeds fall from it until they fall from his ears. When he washes his feet, his wrong deeds fall from him until they fall from beneath his toenails. Then his walking to the masjid and his prayer give him extra reward."* (Mālik, an-Nasāī and Ibn Mājah)

Then the book grounds wudū in the **Qur'an** — the foundational verse for the entire act:

> *"O you who believe! When you prepare for prayer wash your faces and your hands (and arms) to the elbows; Rub your head (with water) and (wash) your feet to the ankles."* (al-Māʾidah 5:6)

Notice how perfectly the verse maps onto the four pillars you are about to learn: **wash the face · wash the hands+arms to the elbows · rub (wipe) the head · wash the feet to the ankles.** The pillars are not the jurist's invention — they are read straight out of the āyah.

**Yaad rakho:** Wudū ki bunyaad **do nasoos** par hai — ek **hadith** (gunaah dhulte hain — har aʿzaa ke saath) aur ek **āyat** (Sūrah al-Māʾidah 5:6). Class ki shuruaat issi se karo: pehle dil ko bata do ke wudū sirf "paani lagana" nahin — **gunaahon ka jhadna** hai. Phir āyat dikhao — taa ke cohort dekhe ke chaaron arkān seedha Qur'an se nikle hain.

## 1 · The four pillars (al-arkān / al-farāʾiḍ)

The pillars are the **non-negotiable core**. Miss any one — even partly — and there is no wudū. The book lists exactly four:

### Pillar 1 · Wash the face

> *"The first of these is to wash the face… and its limit length wise, is from the start of the top of the forehead (typically from the hairline) to the bottom of the chin. And breadth wise, its limit is the area between the two ear lobes."*

So the boundary of the face for wudū is a precise rectangle:
- **Length** (top to bottom): hairline → bottom of the chin.
- **Breadth** (side to side): from one earlobe to the other.

And what does "washing" actually mean? The book gives a sharp definition: ***"at least a couple of drops should flow from the limb."*** Merely *wetting* the skin (wiping a damp hand over it) is **not** washing. Water must **flow**. This definition governs every "wash" in wudū.

### Pillar 2 · Wash the hands and arms, including the elbows

> *"The second is to wash the hands with the arms (and elbows) once."*

The elbows are **included** — they are part of what must be washed, not a boundary you stop before. ("To the elbows" in the verse means *up to and including*.)

### Pillar 3 · Wash the feet, including the ankles

> *"The third is to wash the feet with the ankles."*

The ankles are included, exactly like the elbows. And the book attaches a stern warning hadith — the Prophet ﷺ saw people whose **heels were left dry** and said:

> *"Woe to the dry heels from Hellfire. Perform ablution in full."* (Abū Dāwūd 97)

The heel is the spot people most commonly miss. *"Perform ablution in full"* — no dry patches.

### Pillar 4 · Wipe a quarter of the head

> *"The fourth pillar is to wipe a quarter of the head as the Prophet ﷺ wiped the front of his head."*

This is the pillar people most often get **wrong in two opposite directions**:
- The **farḍ** is to wipe **one quarter** of the head — not the whole head.
- Wiping the **whole** head is a *sunna* (you'll meet it in §4), not the obligation.

So a person who wipes only a quarter has fulfilled the pillar. A person who wipes the whole head has done the pillar *and* a sunna. Note also the verb: the head is **wiped** (*masḥ*), not **washed** (*ghasl*) — only a wet hand passing over it, no flowing water required.

**Yaad rakho:** Chaar arkān — **(1) chehra dhona** (baalon ki jadh se thodi tak, ek kaan ki lau se doosri tak; "dhona" ka matlab paani **baha** — sirf geela karna nahin), **(2) haath + bāzu kuhniyon samet**, **(3) paaon + takhno samet** ("sukhi edi ke liye jahannam" — edi sab se zyada chhootti hai), **(4) sar ke chauthayi hissa ka masah**. Sab se bada confusion pillar 4 par: **farḍ sirf chauthayi hai, pura sar sunna hai.** Aur sar **dhoya nahin jaata — masah hota hai.**

### The big insight you must take from §1

The four pillars come **straight from the verse** (al-Māʾidah 5:6), in the verse's own order. Three of them are **washing** (flowing water: face, arms, feet) and one is **wiping** (a wet hand: the head). The definition of "washing" — *at least a couple of drops must flow* — is the quiet hinge of the whole act: if water did not flow over a spot, that spot was not washed, and the wudū is deficient there. Drill the four pillars until the cohort can recite them in order without notes; everything after this is refinement on top of these four.

## 2 · The reason, and the two sets of conditions

Before the sunnas, the book pauses to answer three structural questions: *Why* do we make wudū? *Who* is obligated to? *What* makes a given wudū legally valid? Keep these three apart in your head — students blur them.

### The reason for wudū (al-sabab)

> *"Its reason is to make lawful those actions that cannot be valid except with wudu. This is its ruling pertaining to this life, while in the hereafter; it is the attainment of rewards."*

So wudū has a **this-worldly function** (it makes prayer and other acts *lawful / valid*) and an **otherworldly function** (reward). Wudū is not an end in itself — it is a **key** that unlocks acts of worship.

**Yaad rakho:** Wudū **maqsad nahin, chaabi hai**. Duniya mein: namaz waghaira ko **jaiz** banata hai. Aakhirat mein: **sawaab**. Yeh framing zaroori hai — warna cohort wudū ko ek alag "ritual" samajh leta hai, jabke woh namaz ka darwaza hai.

### Conditions that OBLIGATE wudū (shurūṭ al-wujūb)

These decide **whether wudū is even demanded of a person**. The book lists:

1. **Sanity, maturity, and Islam.** A child, an insane person, and a non-Muslim are not addressed by this command. The book cites: *"The pen has been lifted from three: a sleeping man until he wakes, an immature person until he attains maturity, and an insane person until he is restored to his senses."* (Ibn Mājah 2041). On maturity: it is reached by a **wet dream**, or — failing that — by **age fifteen** (the view of the two disciples, Abū Yūsuf and Muḥammad, and also Shāfiʿī and Aḥmad; another view says eighteen, from Ibn ʿAbbās). **The fatwa is on fifteen.**
2. **The ability to use a sufficient amount of water** to wash the required limbs at least once. No water, or unable to reach it (e.g. a well with no rope), means one is "like one who has no water."
3. **Being in a state of minor ritual impurity** (*ḥadath*) — and the **absence of menses and postnatal bleeding**. (One already in wudū is not obligated to renew it.)
4. **Inadequate time left** for the obligatory prayer. When the prayer time is *cramped*, wudū becomes immediately obligatory.

### Conditions that VALIDATE wudū (shurūṭ al-ṣiḥḥa)

These decide **whether a wudū you actually performed counts**. The book lists exactly three:

1. **Pure water must reach the complete outer skin.** *"If the area of a pin hole remains in which water does not touch from the parts that are obligatory to wash, wudu is not valid."* A single dry pin-prick on a farḍ limb invalidates the whole wudū.
2. **Anything incompatible with wudū must cease before you begin.** Menses, postnatal bleeding, or active minor impurity (e.g. urine or blood still flowing) must stop first; wudū performed *while* it flows is invalid.
3. **All barriers to water must be removed** — *"wax or fatty grease… nail polish, paint, or glue."* Anything that forms a film over the skin stops water reaching it, and so invalidates.

**Yaad rakho:** **Do alag-alag list hai — gaD-mad mat karo.**
- *Shurūṭ al-wujūb* (kis par wudū **farḍ hota** hai): ʿaql, bulūgh, Islam · paani ki istitāʿat · ḥadath ki haalat / hayd-nifās ka na hona · waqt ka tang hona.
- *Shurūṭ al-ṣiḥḥa* (wudū **sahih kab hota** hai): paani pure khaal tak pahunche (**sui ki nok jitni bhi sukhi jagah na ho**) · maniʿ haalat (hayd/nifās/ḥadath) khatam ho · har **rukāwat** (nail polish, mom, glue) hata di jaaye.

### The big insight you must take from §2

"Obligates" and "validates" answer two different questions. *Wujūb* asks **"is this person even required to make wudū right now?"** — it is about the **person and the moment**. *Ṣiḥḥa* asks **"did this particular wudū legally succeed?"** — it is about the **act itself**. The nail-polish problem is a *ṣiḥḥa* problem (the act fails); a sleeping child is a *wujūb* problem (no obligation yet). Keep the two columns in separate mental boxes.

## 3 · Fulfilling the rulings — the fine print of "reaching the skin"

This short section is the book tying up loose ends about **how water reaches the skin** when the body has obstacles — hair, nails, rings. Every rule here is really an application of validity-condition #1 (water must reach the skin) and #3 (remove barriers).

- **Thick beard** (one so dense it hides the skin): it is obligatory to wash only the **visible (outer) part** — *"this is the soundest view upon which the fatwa is given."* Because the dense hair has "taken the place of the skin," the obligation passes from skin to hair surface. *(Side-note the book makes: in the Arabic the author wrote* wājib *here, but he means* farḍ/obligatory*, as he clarified in Marāqī al-Falāḥ.)*
- **Sparse / small beard** (skin visible through it): water **must reach the skin** beneath, because that is easy and "poses no hardship."
- **Hair stretching outside the border of the face**, and the **part of the lips hidden when the mouth is closed**: water is **not** required to reach these — *"what is unseen is linked to the mouth and what is apparent is linked to the face."*
- **Fingers held tightly together, nails grown long over the fingertips, or dough under the nails:** it becomes **obligatory to wash them after removing the obstruction** — water must get in between and underneath.
- **Insect droppings, the dung of fleas, and the like:** these do **not** prevent water from reaching the skin; the washing is valid even with them present.
- **A tight ring:** *"It is necessary to move around the tight ring during wudu… and when the Prophet ﷺ performed wudu, he moved his ring."* (Ibn Mājah 449)
- **Medicine on a wound/cut on the feet:** if washing the cut would cause harm or pain, it is **permitted to pass water over the medicine** placed on the cut (don't injure yourself to wash).
- **No re-doing:** once the hair of the head has been wiped, you do **not** repeat the wiping if you later shave; nor repeat the washing after cutting your nails or moustache.

**Yaad rakho:** Yeh poora section ek hi usool ki tafseel hai — **paani khaal tak pahunchna chahiye.** Ghani daadhi: sirf oopri hissa farḍ (baal khaal ki jagah le leta hai). Halki daadhi: neeche khaal tak paani lazmi. Tight angoothi: **ghumao** (Nabi ﷺ ne ghumayi). Zakhm par dawaa aur dhona takleef de: paani **oopar se gujaar do**. Nails ke neeche aata/lamba nakhun: rukāwat hata kar dhona farḍ.

## 4 · The eighteen sunnas of wudū

Now we move up one tier: from **farḍ** (do-or-it's-invalid) to **sunna** (the Prophet's confirmed practice — omit and the wudū is valid but **deficient**). The book lists eighteen. Don't drown the cohort in all eighteen as separate items; **group them** so they're memorable.

**Group A — Before you start (the openers):**
1. **Washing the hands to the wrists** before beginning. (Muslim 252 — *"If one of you awakes from sleep, he should not dip his hand in the container until he washes it three times…"*)
2. **The tasmiyah** — saying *"Bismillāh ar-Raḥmān ar-Raḥīm."* (Abū Dāwūd 101 — *"there is no wudu for one who does not mention the name of Allah on it"*)
3. **The siwāk** — brushing the teeth (even with the finger if no stick). (Muslim 487 — *"Would it not be a burden on my nation, I would have ordered them to use the siwak at every prayer"*)

**Group B — The mouth and nose:**
4. **Rinsing the mouth three times** (*maḍmaḍah*), even from a single handful. (Abū Dāwūd 144)
5. **Drawing water into the nostrils three times** (*istinshāq*), with three fresh handfuls.
6. **Going deeper** (*mubālagha*) when rinsing mouth and nose — **provided one is not fasting** (a faster must not risk water going down).

**Group C — Reaching the hidden places:**
7. **Combing the fingers through a large beard** (*takhlīl al-liḥyah*) with water on the palm. (Abū Yūsuf held this sunna; Abū Ḥanīfah held it merely permissible.)
8. **Interlacing the fingers** (*takhlīl al-aṣābiʿ*) — and toes. (at-Tirmidhī 39; Ṭabarānī 22/64 — *"Whoever does not interlace his fingers in water, Allah will interlace them in Hellfire…"*)

**Group D — The threefold washing and the head:**
9. **Washing each limb three times.** (Ibn Mājah 420 — the Prophet ﷺ performed it once = the obligation, twice = double reward, thrice = *"this is my wudu, the wudu of the Prophets before me."*)
10. **Wiping the entire head** (*istīʿāb*). (Bukhārī 185)
11. **Wiping the ears**, even with the leftover head-water. (Abū Dāwūd 134 — *"The ears are from the head."*)

**Group E — Quality of the washing:**
12. **Rubbing the limbs** with the water (*dalk*).
13. **Washing the limbs successively** (*muwālāt*) — without pausing long enough for the previous limb to dry.

**Group F — The "last four" (sunna, but the book notes some call them mandūb):**
14. **The intention** (*niyyah*) — **this is sunna, not farḍ, in the Ḥanafī school.** (Contrast Mālik and Shāfiʿī, who make it obligatory; the Ḥanafī argument is that al-Māʾidah 5:6 commands the *washing* with **no mention of intention**, and the water is itself a purifying substance.)
15. **The chronological order** (*tartīb*) as the verse lists them — sunna, not farḍ.
16/17. **Beginning with the right** limb, and **starting** the washing from the fingertips/toes and the wiping from the front of the head.
18. **Wiping the neck** (not the throat).

> The book ends with a crucial footnote-in-the-text: ***"It has been said that the last four mentioned acts (namely point 15, 16, 17, and 18) are recommended (mandūb), and not sunna."*** So even within the list, tartīb / starting-right / neck sit on the borderline between sunna and adab.

**Yaad rakho:** Atthaarah sunna ko alag-alag ratta mat lagao — **groups mein yaad karo**: (A) shuru ke teen — haath dhona, Bismillah, miswāk · (B) muh-naak — kulli ×3, naak ×3, gehraai (roze mein nahin) · (C) chhupi jagahein — daadhi ka khilāl, ungliyon ka khilāl · (D) teen baar dhona + pura sar + kaan · (E) malna (dalk) + lagaataar (muwālāt) · (F) aakhri chaar — **niyyah, tartīb, daahne se, gardan**. **Sab se bada nukta: Ḥanafī mein niyyah SUNNA hai, farḍ nahin** — yeh landmine hai, aage Part B mein.

### The big insight you must take from §4

Two facts about this list change how the cohort prays for the rest of their lives:
1. **Niyyah and tartīb are sunna in the Ḥanafī school — not farḍ.** (Muwālāt is also a sunna — it is listed as sunna #13 — but the book makes its explicit *sunna-not-farḍ* argument, from al-Māʾidah 5:6, only for niyyah and tartīb.) This means a Ḥanafī's wudū is technically valid even if they forgot the intention or washed the limbs out of order. (It is *deficient* and you should never do it on purpose — but it is not *invalid*.) Students who came up thinking these were obligatory will be surprised; this is the doctrinal heart of the lesson.
2. The Prophet ﷺ modelled the threefold wash himself and called it *"the wudu of the Prophets before me."* The sunnas are not optional extras bolted on — they are how the Prophet ﷺ actually made wudū. Teach them as **the complete picture**, with the four pillars as the irreducible minimum inside it.

## 5 · The fourteen etiquettes (al-ādāb / al-mandūbāt)

Tier three. The book defines *adab* precisely: ***"that which the Prophet performed once or twice"*** (as opposed to a sunna, which he kept to consistently). *"The one who undertakes its performance is rewarded though it is not blameworthy for its omission."* So adab is **pure bonus** — reward if you do it, no blame if you don't. Fourteen of them:

1. **Sit on an elevated place** (so the used water flows away and doesn't splash back).
2. **Face the qibla** (better for the acceptance of supplication).
3. **Not to seek assistance** from others (rise in worship by your own hand).
4. **Abstain from worldly talk** during wudū (it distracts from supplication).
5. **Unite the intention of the heart with the tongue** (say it and mean it together).
6. **Make the transmitted supplications** of the Prophet, Companions, and Tābiʿīn.
7. **Say "Bismillāh…" at the washing/wiping of each limb.**
8. **Insert the little finger into the opening of the ears** (to widen the wiped area).
9. **Move around a wide, loose ring** (a *tight* one was obligatory to move — §3; a loose one is only adab).
10. **Gargle and draw water into the nose with the right hand; remove the nasal mucus with the left.**
11. **Perform wudū before the prayer time enters** — *except for a person who is "excused"* (one with a chronic condition such as constant wind/incontinence; their wudū is tied to the prayer time).
12. **Recite the two shahādahs after finishing, while standing.** (Abū Dāwūd 169 — *"…all the eight doors of Paradise will be opened for him…"*)
13. **Drink from the leftover wudū water while standing**, facing the qibla. (Bukhārī 1637 — the Prophet ﷺ "drank standing from the excess of wudu, the water of Zamzam.")
14. **Say:** *"Allāhumma ijʿalnī min at-tawwābīn wa-jʿalnī min al-mutaṭahhirīn"* — *"O Allah, make me of those who repent and of those who purify themselves."*

**Yaad rakho:** Adab = ***Nabi ﷺ ne ek-do baar kiya*** (sunna = hamesha kiya). Karne par sawaab, na karne par **koi gunaah nahin**. Atthaarah sunna se in chaudah adab ko alag rakho — varna cohort har cheez ko "lazmi" samajhne lagega. Adab ki **rooh**: wudū ko ek **dhikr aur du'a** ka lamha bana do — qibla rukh, har aʿzaa par Bismillah, aakhir mein shahādatain aur tawwābīn waali du'a.

## 6 · The six disliked acts (al-makrūhāt)

The top tier — what to **avoid**. These don't invalidate wudū (it's still valid), but they're disliked and cost you. Exactly six:

1. **Wasting water** (*isrāf*). The Prophet ﷺ passed Saʿd making wudū and said *"What is this wastage, O Saʿd?"* Saʿd asked, *"Is there wastage in wudu?"* He replied: ***"Yes, even if you were at a flowing river."*** (Ibn Mājah 425)
2. **Using too little water** (*taqtīr*) — so stingy that washing nearly becomes mere wiping. *"The best of matters is the middle course."* (al-Bayhaqī 261)
3. **Slapping water onto the face** — *"the face has honour, and it should be performed gently… Do not hit the face."* (ʿAbd ar-Razzāq 9/333)
4. **Speaking worldly talk** during wudū (it distracts from supplication — the mirror of adab #4).
5. **Seeking help from others without a need.** (ʿUmar said he once moved to pour water for the Prophet ﷺ, who said: *"Stop, O ʿUmar — for verily I do not need anyone to help me with my prayers."*)
6. **Wiping the head three times with fresh water each time** — *"because wiping is only performed once."*

Notice the **two-sided symmetry**: wasting water (too much) *and* using too little (too stingy) are **both** makrūh — Islam wants the **middle path**. And worldly talk appears as both a *makrūh* (#4 here) and the inverse of an *adab* (avoiding it, adab #4) — the book reinforces it from both directions.

**Yaad rakho:** Chhe makrūhāt — yeh wudū ko **batil nahin** karte, bas naa-pasandeeda hain. Sab se yaad-rakhne-wala: **paani ka israaf — "behte darya par bhi"** (zyada) aur **taqtīr** (bohot kam) — **dono** makrūh. Beech ka raasta. Chehre par paani maarna mat (chehre ki izzat), bina zaroorat doosre se madad mat lo, sar ka masah teen baar naye paani se mat karo.

### The big insight you must take from §§5–6

The adab and the makrūhāt are **two faces of the same refinement**: the adab tell you what *adds* beauty and reward to a valid wudū; the makrūhāt tell you what *subtracts* from it. Together with the pillars (the floor) and the sunnas (the Prophet's normal practice), they complete the picture of a wudū that is not just *valid* but *excellent* (*iḥsān*). When your cohort can place any given action of wudū into the right tier — farḍ, sunna, adab, or makrūh — they have genuinely understood this chapter.

## The big picture — what Chapter 3a is silently teaching

Step back. The whole of Chapter 3a is a single architectural lesson in disguise:

1. **Worship has a floor and a ceiling.** *(Ibādat ki ek farsh hai aur ek chhat.)* The floor is the four pillars — below it, there is no wudū at all. The ceiling is the sunnas, adab, and avoidance of makrūhāt — the climb from *valid* to *excellent*. Most of the religion lives in that climb.
2. **The minimum is small; the excellence is rich.** Only **four** things are obligatory. Eighteen sunnas + fourteen adab show that Allah left enormous room for a believer to *beautify* a simple act. Wudū is generous: easy to satisfy, endless to perfect.
3. **The evidence is right there in the verse.** The four pillars are read straight out of al-Māʾidah 5:6 — the jurists did not invent them. This builds the cohort's trust: fiqh is *the unpacking of revelation*, not a parallel system.
4. **Even washing is wrapped in dhikr.** Bismillah at the start, the name of Allah on each limb, the two shahādahs and the *tawwābīn* du'a at the end. Like the lavatory in Chapter 2, even the basin is a place of remembrance.

**Yaad rakho:** Yeh chapter sirf "wudū kaise karein" nahin sikhaata — yeh sikhaata hai ke **har ibādat mein ek kam-se-kam (farḍ) hai aur ek kamāl (sunna + adab) hai**, aur asal deen us **darmiyaani safar** mein hai. Sirf chaar cheezein farḍ — magar atthaarah sunna aur chaudah adab keh rahe hain: *"is chhoti si cheez ko bhi khoobsoorat bana sakte ho."*

# Part B · Teaching the chapter

You now understand Chapter 3a. This section is how to **deliver** it to your classmates — adult, mostly Urdu-speaking Muslims, almost all of whom *already make wudū every day*. That last fact is the key to teaching this chapter well.

## A note on the cohort's starting point

Unlike Chapter 2 (istinja), where much of the content was new, **everyone in your class already performs wudū**. They have muscle-memory and inherited habits — some correct, some not, many of unknown rank. Your job is **not** to teach them wudū from zero. It is to **sort what they already do into the four tiers** and correct two or three specific misconceptions.

This changes your tone: you are not an instructor handing down a new skill, you are a **guide helping them understand what they already do.** Lean into that. Ask *"Aap log abhi wudū mein kya-kya karte ho?"* and sort their answers live onto a four-tier board (farḍ / sunna / adab / makrūh).

**Yaad rakho:** Cohort wudū **pehle se karta hai** — naya skill nahin sikhana, balke unke **maujooda amal ko chaar darjon mein** chaanT-na hai. Board par chaar column banao aur unke jawaab live us mein daalo. Yeh sab se asar-daar tareeqa hai is chapter ko parhaane ka.

## The five landmines for Urdu-speaking adult learners

### Landmine 1 · "Niyyah farḍ hai" — the intention misconception

Almost everyone has been taught that the **intention** (*niyyah*) is obligatory for wudū. In the **Ḥanafī school it is a sunna, not a farḍ.** A classmate may insist: *"Bina niyyat ke wudū hi nahin hota!"*

**Counter-strategy:** Don't dismiss them — explain the *madhhab* basis. Nur al-Idah's own argument: the Qur'an commands the *washing* (al-Māʾidah 5:6) with **no mention of intention**, and the water itself is already a purifying substance (unlike the soil of tayammum, where intention *is* required because soil is not an inherent purifier). So a Ḥanafī who fell into a river and came out with every limb washed has — technically — a valid wudū. **But** stress immediately: *this is about validity in an edge case, not about dropping the intention.* You should always make the intention; it is the Prophet's practice and it carries the reward. We are simply being precise about its **rank**.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Ḥanafī madhhab mein wudū ki niyyah **sunna** hai, farḍ nahin — yeh kitaab (Nur al-Idah) khud kehti hai. Daleel: Qur'an ne sirf 'dhona' ka hukm diya, niyyat ka zikr nahin kiya; aur paani khud paak karne waali cheez hai. Iska matlab yeh **nahin** ke niyyat chhod do — niyyat hamesha karo, woh Nabi ﷺ ka tareeqa hai aur sawaab usi mein hai. Hum sirf yeh keh rahe hain ke uska **darja** sunna hai, farḍ nahin. Isi tarah tartīb aur muwālāt bhi sunna hain — farḍ nahin."*

### Landmine 2 · "Pura sar dhona/masah farḍ hai" — the head confusion

Many believe the **whole head** must be wiped as the obligation, or even confuse wiping with *washing* the head.

**Counter-strategy:** Two corrections in one. (a) The **farḍ** is to wipe **a quarter** of the head; wiping the **whole** head is a *sunna*. (b) The head is **wiped** (*masḥ*), never washed — a wet hand passing over it, no flowing water. So: minimum = wet quarter; ideal (sunna) = wet whole head; *washing* the head is neither required nor part of the wudū's design.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Sar ka **farḍ** sirf **chauthayi** ka masah hai — pura sar nahin. Pura sar karna **sunna** hai (behtar), magar farḍ chauthayi se ada ho jaata hai. Aur sar **dhoya nahin jaata — masah hota hai**: geela haath pher do, paani bahane ki zaroorat nahin. To kam-se-kam: geele haath se chauthayi. Kamāl: pura sar. Dhona: na farḍ, na sunna."*

### Landmine 3 · Treating every sunna as if it were farḍ (the over-stresser)

The opposite danger to Landmine 1: a conscientious classmate who, on learning the 18 sunnas, starts treating *all* of them as make-or-break — and becomes anxious that a wudū where they forgot the siwāk or didn't rinse three times is "invalid."

**Counter-strategy:** This is exactly why the **four-tier gradient** is the spine of your lesson. Put it on the board and keep returning to it. A missed *sunna* = valid wudū, less reward. A missed *farḍ* = invalid wudū, redo it. Give them the relief: *"Agar aap teen baar ki jagah ek baar dho lein — wudū **ho gaya**, bas kamāl chhoot gaya."* Precision in **both** directions — don't drop farḍ, don't panic over sunna.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Sunna chhoot jaaye to wudū **batil nahin hota** — bas sawaab kam, kamāl adhoora. Sirf **chaar farḍ** aise hain ke chhoot jaayein to wudū dobaara karna padega. Baaqi sab — sunna, adab — wudū ko khoobsoorat banate hain, lekin uski **sehat** chaar arkān par hai. Na farḍ chhodo, na sunna par ghabraao."*

### Landmine 4 · "Wujūb" vs "ṣiḥḥa" — two different question-words

Adult learners often collapse "what makes wudū *obligatory*" and "what makes wudū *valid*" into one list, then get confused when, e.g., nail polish appears under "validity" but not "obligation."

**Counter-strategy:** Teach them as **two different questions**, not two lists to memorise:
- *Shurūṭ al-wujūb* answers: **"Kya is shakhs par abhi wudū farḍ bhi hai?"** (person + moment: sane? mature? Muslim? in ḥadath? prayer-time tight?)
- *Shurūṭ al-ṣiḥḥa* answers: **"Jo wudū kiya, woh **gina** bhi jaayega?"** (the act: paani har jagah pahuncha? rukāwat hati? maniʿ haalat khatam?)

Use the nail-polish example to make it click: nail polish is never a question of *whether you must* make wudū — it's a question of *whether the wudū you made counts.* So it lives under *ṣiḥḥa*.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Do alag sawaal hain. Pehla — **'kis par wudū farḍ hai?'** — yeh shakhs aur waqt ke baare mein hai (ʿaql, bulūgh, Islam, ḥadath, waqt ka tang hona). Doosra — **'jo wudū kiya woh sahih hai ya nahin?'** — yeh amal ke baare mein hai (paani sui-ki-nok jitni jagah bhi na chhode, rukāwat hat jaaye). Nail polish pehle sawaal ka jawaab nahin badalti — woh doosre sawaal mein aati hai: us se wudū **count nahin hota**."*

### Landmine 5 · The dry heel and the pin-hole — "I thought I was clean"

The validity condition is unforgiving: *a single pin-hole of dry skin on a farḍ limb invalidates the wudū.* The most commonly missed spots are the **heels** (the Prophet's "Woe to the dry heels"), between the **toes** and **fingers**, and under a **ring**. Classmates may have been making deficient wudū for years without knowing.

**Counter-strategy:** Don't induce guilt — induce **attention**. Frame it as a gift: *"Aaj ke baad aapka wudū pehle se behtar hoga."* Demonstrate the three danger-zones physically: rub the heels, comb between the toes/fingers (sunna #8 *takhlīl*), move the ring (§3). Tie it to the hadith — the Prophet ﷺ didn't warn about the heels by accident; it's the spot water skips.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Sehat ki shart yeh hai ke **sui ki nok jitni bhi sukhi jagah** farḍ aʿzaa par na rahe. Sab se zyada chhootne waali jagahein: **ediyaan** ('sukhi edi ke liye jahannam' — Nabi ﷺ ka irshaad), **ungliyon ke darmiyaan** (haath aur paaon dono), aur **angoothi ke neeche**. Yeh ghabraane ki baat nahin — aaj ke baad aapka wudū aur mukammal hoga. Edi malo, ungliyon ka khilāl karo, angoothi ghumao."*

## Suggested 75-minute class plan

*Class shuru karne se pehle:* board par ek **chaar-manzila imārat** banao — neeche se oopar: **FARḌ (4) · SUNNA (18) · ADAB (14) · MAKRŪH (6)**. Yeh poore chapter ka naqsha hai; har section ke baad uski cheezein apni manzil par likhte jao.

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–7 min | Greetings · the **opening hadith** (sins fall away with each limb) + **al-Māʾidah 5:6** · why wudū is a *key*, not an end |
| 7–10 min | Draw the **four-tier board** (farḍ / sunna / adab / makrūh) — the map for the whole class |
| 10–25 min | **The 4 pillars** — recite in order; physically demonstrate each; nail the two head-corrections (quarter, not whole; wipe, not wash) and the heel warning |
| 25–35 min | **Conditions** — two columns on the board: *obligate* (person+moment) vs *validate* (the act). Use nail polish to separate them |
| 35–42 min | **Fulfilling the rulings** — beard (thick vs sparse), ring (move it), wound (water over medicine), pin-hole/heel danger-zones |
| 42–57 min | **The 18 sunnas** — teach in the **six groups**, not as 18 items. Flag: niyyah/tartīb/muwālāt are sunna, not farḍ (Landmine 1 + 3) |
| 57–65 min | **14 adab** (reward-only) + **6 makrūhāt** (avoid; israaf "even at a river"). Place each on the board |
| 65–72 min | **Recap on the four-tier board** — quiz: call out an action, class shouts the tier |
| 72–75 min | Q&A · assign handout exercises · preview Chapter 3b (types + nullifiers) |

**Yaad rakho:** Is chapter ka **dil** chaar-manzila board hai — usi par class shuru karo, usi par khatam karo. Sab se zyada waqt (15 min) arkān ko do, kyunki wahi sehat ki bunyaad hai. 18 sunna ko **groups** mein parhao warna cohort doob jaayega. Aakhri quiz (action bolo, tier batao) sab se mazboot consolidation hai.

## What you should NOT teach in Chapter 3a

- ❌ **The 3 types of wudū** (farḍ / wājib / mandūb occasions) — that's **Chapter 3b** (next session). You may *mention* the word "types" but don't open the list.
- ❌ **The 12 nullifiers** (what breaks wudū — bleeding, sleep, vomiting, etc.) — **Chapter 3b**. If a classmate asks *"wudū kis cheez se toot-ta hai?"* say: *"Yeh agla hissa hai — Chapter 3b mein tafseel se aayega."*
- ❌ **The 10 non-nullifiers** — **Chapter 3b**.
- ❌ **Ghusl** (the major bath) — **Chapter 4**. (You may *reference* that menses/janāba require ghusl, established in Ch 2 — but don't teach the ghusl procedure.)
- ❌ **Tayammum** — **Chapter 5**. (You'll *mention* it only to explain *why* niyyah is farḍ there but sunna in wudū — that contrast is fair game, but don't teach tayammum's rules.)
- ❌ **Detailed fiqh of the "excused" person** (*maʿdhūr*) — only mention it exists (adab #11); its full rules are a later topic.

If a classmate races ahead to "what breaks wudū," gently hold the line: *"Pehle wudū karna mukammal seekh lein — todna agle hafte."*

**Yaad rakho:** 3a sirf **wudū karna** sikhata hai. **Todna, types, ghusl, tayammum — sab baad mein.** Cohort ko ek baar mein sab dena overwhelm karta hai — aur overwhelm ki sab se badi nishaani: woh waapas nahin aate.

## Self-check before teaching

Before class, make sure you can:

- [ ] Recite the **four pillars in order** without notes, with each limb's boundary (face: hairline→chin, earlobe→earlobe; arms incl. elbows; feet incl. ankles; quarter of head — *wipe*).
- [ ] State the **definition of "washing"** — *at least a couple of drops must flow*.
- [ ] Explain **why niyyah is sunna (not farḍ) in the Ḥanafī school** — the al-Māʾidah 5:6 argument + the water-vs-soil contrast.
- [ ] Keep **wujūb conditions** and **ṣiḥḥa conditions** in two separate columns, and place nail-polish correctly (ṣiḥḥa).
- [ ] Teach the **18 sunnas in six groups** rather than as a flat list.
- [ ] Define **adab** precisely (*"what the Prophet did once or twice; reward if done, no blame if omitted"*) and distinguish it from sunna.
- [ ] List the **6 makrūhāt**, leading with the two-sided water rule (isrāf *and* taqtīr).
- [ ] Recite the **opening hadith** and **al-Māʾidah 5:6** to anchor the lesson.

If yes to all eight — *tum tayyar ho.*

## After class — diagnostic checklist

For each classmate, watch for:

1. **Can they place an action in the right tier?** *(Koi bhi amal bolo — farḍ, sunna, adab, ya makrūh — sahih khaane mein daal sakte hain?)* This is the single test of whether the chapter landed.
2. **Did the niyyah-rank correction stick — without tipping into "so I'll drop it"?** *(Niyyah sunna hai — par chhodni nahin; yeh nuance samjhe?)*
3. **Do they wipe a quarter (not wash the whole) head correctly?** *(Sar: chauthayi, masah — dhona nahin?)*
4. **Are they checking the heel / between-toes / under-ring danger-zones?** *(Ediyaan, ungliyon ka darmiyaan, angoothi — dhyan de rahe hain?)*
5. **Do they keep wujūb and ṣiḥḥa apart?** *(Do alag sawaal — farḍ kis par, sahih kab — gaD-mad to nahin kar rahe?)*

**Yaad rakho:** Agar 2+ classmates har sunna ko farḍ samajhne lagein (ghabraahaT), ya niyyah-correction ko "to phir niyyat chhod do" samajh lein — to agle hafte (Chapter 3b se pehle) 5-minute ka **chaar-manzila refresher** lo. Wudū **karna** mazboot honi chahiye us se pehle ke hum wudū **todne** (3b) ki taraf jaayein — warna nawaqid ki list bunyaad ke baghair latakti rahegi.
