Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · The four pillars, conditions, sunnas, etiquettes & disliked acts

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

You already make wudu every day. Today we are not learning it from zero — we are sorting what you already do into four levels, and clearing up two or three common misunderstandings. By the end, your wudu will be a little more complete and a lot more understood.

The whole lesson hangs on one picture — a four-level ladder:

LevelArabicWeightIf you miss it…
1Farḍ (pillars)Obligatory · 4 things❌ Wudu is invalid — redo it
2SunnaConfirmed practice · 18 things⚠️ Valid, but deficient (less reward)
3Adab (etiquette)Recommended · 14 things✅ Valid — you just missed extra reward
4MakrūhDisliked · 6 things✅ Valid, but you did something disliked

Rule 1 · The four pillars (farāʾiḍ) — the foundation

These four are obligatory. Miss any one — even a dry spot the size of a pin-hole — and the wudu must be redone. They come straight from the Qur'an (al-Māʾidah 5:6).

#PillarThe detail you must know
1Wash the faceLength: hairline → bottom of chin. Breadth: earlobe → earlobe. "Washing" = at least a couple of drops must flow (not just wetting).
2Wash the hands & armsIncluding the elbows.
3Wash the feetIncluding the ankles. Don't miss the heels — "Woe to the dry heels from Hellfire" (Abū Dāwūd 97).
4Wipe a quarter of the headWipe (wet hand), not wash. Only a quarter is obligatory (the whole head is a sunna).

Urdu mein: Chaar farḍ: (1) chehra dhona (jadh-e-baal se thodi tak, kaan se kaan tak; paani bahe), (2) haath+bāzu kuhniyon samet, (3) paaon takhno samet (edi na chhoote), (4) sar ke chauthayi ka masah (dhona nahin). Pura sar = sunna, chauthayi = farḍ.

⚠️ The strict rule

A single dry pin-hole on a pillar limb makes the wudu deficient. The most-missed spots: heels, between the toes and fingers, under a ring.

Rule 2 · Two kinds of conditions — keep them apart

The book gives two different lists that answer two different questions. Don't mix them up.

Conditions that OBLIGATE wudu
"Is this person required to make wudu now?"		Conditions that VALIDATE wudu
"Did the wudu they made count?"
• Sanity, maturity, Islam (maturity: a wet dream, else age 15)
• Ability to use enough water
• Being in minor impurity; no menses/nifās
• The prayer time is cramped		 • Pure water reaches all the skin (a dry pin-hole → invalid)
• Any incompatible state (menses, nifās, active impurity) has stopped first
• All barriers removed: wax, grease, nail polish, paint, glue

Urdu mein: Do alag sawaal: (1) "kis par wudu farḍ hai?" — shakhs aur waqt. (2) "jo wudu kiya, sahih hai?" — amal. Nail polish doosre sawaal mein aati hai: us se wudu count nahin hota.

💡 Remember

Wudu is a key, not an end — it makes prayer lawful (this life) and earns reward (the hereafter).

Rule 3 · Making sure the water reaches the skin

The whole idea is one rule applied to the body's obstacles: water must reach the skin.

  • Thick beard (hides the skin): wash the visible part — the hair has taken the place of the skin.
  • Sparse beard (skin shows through): water must reach the skin beneath.
  • Tight ring: move it so water gets under it (the Prophet ﷺ moved his ring — Ibn Mājah 449).
  • Long nails / dough under nails / tightly-held fingers: clear the obstruction, then wash.
  • Insect droppings, flea dung: do not block water — wudu is valid.
  • A painful cut on the foot: pass water over the medicine on it — don't injure yourself.
  • No re-doing: wiped your head then shaved? No re-wipe. Cut your nails after wudu? No re-wash.

Urdu mein: Sab ka ek usool — paani khaal tak pahunche. Ghani daadhi: oopar dhona. Halki daadhi: neeche tak. Tight angoothi: ghumao. Zakhm: dawaa ke oopar se paani.

Rule 4 · The 18 sunnas — in six easy groups

Sunna = the Prophet's regular practice. Miss one and your wudu is still valid, just less complete. Learn them in groups, not as a list of 18:

GroupThe sunnas
A · Openers1 Wash hands to wrists · 2 Say "Bismillāh…" · 3 Use the siwāk
B · Mouth & nose4 Rinse mouth ×3 · 5 Water in nose ×3 · 6 Go deeper — but not when fasting
C · Hidden places7 Comb water through a large beard · 8 Interlace the fingers (and toes)
D · Three & head9 Wash each limb ×3 · 10 Wipe the whole head · 11 Wipe the ears ("the ears are from the head")
E · Quality12 Rub the limbs (dalk) · 13 Wash successively without pausing (muwālāt)
F · Last four14 The intention (niyyah) · 15 The order (tartīb) · 16/17 Begin with the right, from fingertips/toes · 18 Wipe the neck

⚠️ The big surprise — niyyah is a sunna here

In the Hanafi school, the intention (niyyah) and the order (tartīb) are sunnas — not obligatory (muwālāt, washing-without-pausing, is also a sunna — #13). The Qur'an (al-Māʾidah 5:6) commands the washing with no mention of intention. This does not mean drop the niyyah — always make it; it is the Prophet's way and carries the reward. We are only being precise about its level.

Urdu mein: Ḥanafī mein niyyah, tartīb — sunna hain, farḍ nahin (muwālāt bhi sunna hai). Matlab chhodna nahin — sirf darja samajhna. Pura sar (sunna #10) aur chauthayi (farḍ) ko gaD-mad mat karo.

Rule 5 · The 14 etiquettes (adab) & the 6 disliked acts (makrūh)

Adab = what the Prophet ﷺ did "once or twice" — reward if you do it, no blame if you don't. Makrūh = disliked; the wudu is still valid, but you've done something to avoid.

The 14 etiquettes (a selection to remember)

  • Sit on an elevated place; face the qibla.
  • Don't talk worldly talk; unite your heart's intention with your tongue.
  • Say "Bismillāh…" at each limb; make the transmitted supplications.
  • Little finger into the ear openings; move a loose ring.
  • Make wudu before the prayer time enters (except a person who is "excused").
  • After wudu, while standing: the two shahādahs (Abū Dāwūd 169 — "the eight doors of Paradise"), drink the leftover water, and say:
    "Allāhumma ijʿalnī min at-tawwābīn wa-jʿalnī min al-mutaṭahhirīn" — "O Allah, make me of those who repent and of those who purify."

The 6 disliked acts

#DislikedWhy
1Wasting water (isrāf)"…even if you were at a flowing river" (Ibn Mājah 425)
2Using too little water (taqtīr)It nearly becomes mere wiping — "the best of matters is the middle course"
3Slapping water on the face"The face has honour" — apply gently
4Worldly talk during wuduDistracts from supplication
5Seeking help without a need"I do not need anyone to help me with my prayers"
6Wiping the head ×3 with fresh waterWiping is done only once

Urdu mein: Paani ka israaf aur taqtīr — dono makrūh (beech ka raasta). Adab = sawaab ki baat, chhodne par gunaah nahin. Makrūh wudu ko batil nahin karte — bas na-pasandeeda hain.
