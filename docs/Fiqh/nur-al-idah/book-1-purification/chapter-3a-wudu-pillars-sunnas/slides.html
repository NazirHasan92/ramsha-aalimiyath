You already make wudu every day

Today we are not learning wudu from zero.

We are sorting what you already do into four levels — and clearing up two or three common misunderstandings.

By the end, your wudu will be more complete — and much better understood.

Naya skill nahin — maujooda amal ko chaar darjon mein samajhna.

Where wudu comes from

"When a person performs ablution and rinses his mouth, his wrong deeds fall from it… When he washes his feet, his wrong deeds fall from him until they fall from beneath his toenails."

— Mālik, an-Nasāī, Ibn Mājah

Wudu is not "putting water on the body" — it is the falling-away of sins.

Wudu = gunaahon ka jhadna.

The Qur'anic command

"O you who believe! When you prepare for prayer wash your faces and your hands (and arms) to the elbows; Rub your head (with water) and (wash) your feet to the ankles."

— al-Māʾidah 5:6

The four pillars are in this verse, in order. The jurists did not invent them.

Chaaron arkān seedha Qur'an se aaye hain.

Pillar 1 · The four pillars (farḍ)

Obligatory. Miss any one — even a dry pin-hole — and you redo the wudu.

#PillarKey detail
1Wash the faceHairline → chin; earlobe → earlobe
2Wash armsIncluding the elbows
3Wash feetIncluding the ankles (the heels!)
4Wipe the headA quarter — wipe, not wash

Two things people get wrong

① "Washing" means at least a couple of drops must flow — not just wetting.

② The head is wiped (masḥ), not washed — and only a quarter is farḍ (the whole head is a sunna).

"Woe to the dry heels from Hellfire. Perform ablution in full."

— Abū Dāwūd 97

"Dhona" = paani bahe. Sar = chauthayi ka masah (pura sar sunna).

Rule 2 · Two kinds of conditions

OBLIGATE
"Must this person make wudu now?"
Sane · mature · Muslim · enough water · in impurity · prayer-time tight
VALIDATE
"Did the wudu count?"
Water reaches all skin · impurity stopped first · barriers removed

Two different questions — don't mix them.

The nail-polish test

Does nail polish change whether you must make wudu? No.

Does it change whether the wudu counts? Yes — it blocks the water.

So nail polish is a VALIDATE issue, never an OBLIGATE issue.

Nail polish se wudu count nahin hota — yeh sehat ka masla hai, wujūb ka nahin.

Rule 3 · Water must reach the skin

Thick beard
Wash the visible part
Sparse beard
Water to the skin beneath
Tight ring
Move it (Prophet ﷺ did)
Painful cut
Water over the medicine

Danger-zones most often missed: heels between toes/fingers under a ring

Sab ka ek usool: paani khaal tak pahunche.

Rule 4 · The 18 sunnas — six groups

A · Openers
wash hands · Bismillah · siwāk
B · Mouth & nose
rinse ×3 · nose ×3 · go deeper*
C · Hidden
comb beard · interlace fingers
D · Three & head
each limb ×3 · whole head · ears
E · Quality
rub (dalk) · successively
F · Last four
niyyah · order · right · neck

*go deeper when rinsing — but not when fasting.

18 sunna ko groups mein yaad karo — list mein nahin.

⚠️ The big surprise

In the Hanafi school, the intention (niyyah) and the order (tartīb) are sunnas — not obligatory (muwālāt too is a sunna — #13).

The Qur'an commands the washing with no mention of intention (al-Māʾidah 5:6).

This does NOT mean drop the niyyah — always make it. We are only naming its level.

Niyyah/tartīb sunna hain — farḍ nahin (muwālāt bhi sunna). Chhodni nahin — darja samajhna.

Rule 5 · The 14 etiquettes (adab)

Adab = what the Prophet ﷺ did "once or twice" — reward if done, no blame if omitted.

  • Face the qibla · sit elevated · don't talk worldly talk
  • Say "Bismillāh" at each limb
  • After wudu, standing: the two shahādahs + drink the leftover water

"Allāhumma ijʿalnī min at-tawwābīn wa-jʿalnī min al-mutaṭahhirīn"
"O Allah, make me of those who repent and those who purify."

The 6 disliked acts (makrūh)

1 · Wasting water
"even at a flowing river"
2 · Too little water
nearly mere wiping
3 · Slapping the face
"the face has honour"
4 · Worldly talk
5 · Help without need
6 · Wiping head ×3
with fresh water

Both too much AND too little water are disliked — the middle path.

Makrūh wudu ko batil nahin karte — bas na-pasandeeda hain.
