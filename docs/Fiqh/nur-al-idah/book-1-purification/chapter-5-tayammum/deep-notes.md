# Chapter 5 — Deep Notes · Tayammum (the dry purification)

**Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · Hasan Shurunbulali (Hanafi)**
*This file is Ramsha's personal learning material. Read **Part A** first to understand the chapter yourself, then **Part B** when you're ready to teach it to your classmates.*

*Reading guide: technical explanations are in English; key takeaways and counter-strategies are flagged with **Yaad rakho:** boxes in Roman Urdu. Skim the Urdu boxes for a fast revision pass.*

*Source constraint: every ruling, hadith, and verse below is drawn **only** from Nur al-Idah (book pp. 74–86). Nothing has been imported from outside fiqh sources or general knowledge.*

> **Where this sits in Book I.** Chapters 3–4 gave you the two *water* purifications: **wudū** (minor) and **ghusl** (major). But what happens when there is **no water**, or water is there but you **cannot use it** (you're sick, it's freezing, the only water must be drunk)? Islam does not leave you stranded outside worship. This chapter is the **mercy clause**: **tayammum** — purifying with clean **earth** instead of water. It is the dry, emergency substitute (*badal*) for both wudū *and* ghusl. Teach it only after the cohort is solid on wudū and ghusl — tayammum is defined *against* them ("it stands in for them").

# Part A · Understanding the chapter

Tayammum is the simplest *act* in Book I — two strikes on the earth, wipe the face and arms — but it is one of the **richest in conditions**, because the whole institution rests on a single delicate idea: **it is a substitute that is only switched on in an emergency.** Get that idea, and the long list of conditions becomes obvious. The chapter answers **five plain questions**:

| § | The question | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **When am I *allowed* to do tayammum?** | The valid excuse — water **absent** or **unusable** (the book's 2nd condition, the heart of the chapter) |
| 2 | **What do I do it *with*, and *how*?** | Pure **earth**, **intention**, the **two strikes** (face + arms-to-elbows) — the book's 8 conditions + the 2 pillars |
| 3 | **What is the *complete* tayammum?** | The **7 sunan** — the Prophetic method |
| 4 | **How does a tayammum *live*?** | When to **delay** it · **requesting** water first · one tayammum for **many** prayers |
| 5 | **What *ends* it?** | The nullifiers — everything that breaks wudū **+** the return of usable water (the mirror) |

Before the lists, grab **three master-keys**. Almost every ruling in the chapter turns on one of them:

1. **Badal — a substitute, switched on only in need.** Tayammum is not an *equal* of water; it is a stand-in (*badal*) that the Sharīʿah permits **only** when water is genuinely unavailable or unusable. The book is explicit: *"the Sacred Law allowed tayammum as a substitute for water during its absence or when one is unable to use the water."* Two consequences flow from this all chapter long: (a) **no valid excuse → no tayammum**; (b) the instant usable water returns, the tayammum **dies** (§5).
2. **Niyyah is a *condition*, not just a sunna.** This is the chapter's biggest conceptual surprise *against* wudū. In wudū, intention is only a *sunna* — because **water purifies by its own essence**. But **soil is not a purifier by its essence** ("the essence of the soil is physically unclean and is not deemed a purifier except at times of necessity"). So earth only becomes a purifier **when you intend it**. Therefore **no intention → no valid tayammum.** (In wudū you can be valid without intention; in tayammum you cannot.)
3. **Two strikes, two limbs.** The physical act is radically simple: **face** and **the two arms up to and including the elbows** — done in **two strikes** on pure earth, with the whole hand. That's it. The hadith: *"Tayammum is two strikes, a strike for the face and a strike for the arms to his elbows."* (Hakim, al-Mustadrak 1/287)

**Yaad rakho:** Teen chaabiyaan. (1) **Badal** — tayammum paani ka *naa'ib* hai, sirf *zaroorat* mein chalta hai; jaayiz uzr na ho to tayammum nahin, aur paani qaabil-e-istemaal hote hi tayammum **toot** jaata hai. (2) **Niyyah shart hai** (sunnat nahin) — kyunki **mitti** apni zaat se paak-kun nahin (paani hai), is liye mitti tabhi paak karti hai jab **niyyat** karo. (3) **Do darb, do a'za** — **chehra** aur **dono baazu kohniyon samet**, do martaba mitti par haath maar kar. Hadith: "Tayammum do darb hai — ek chehre ke liye, ek baazuon ke liye kohniyon tak" (Hakim 1/287).

## 1 · When tayammum is *allowed* — the valid excuse

This is the heart of the chapter. The book lists **eight conditions that validate tayammum**, but the one that decides *whether you may reach for earth at all* is the **second**: you must have a **valid reason**. The book splits that reason into **two kinds of "absence" of water** — and this two-way split is the cleanest way to teach the whole thing.

### A · "Real absence" (ḥaqīqī) — there is no usable water within reach

The default case: *"being separated from water at the distance of a mile, even though one is in the city. If one is at a distance that is less than a mile, he must go for water."* So the trigger is **roughly a mile** away (see the measure note below) — water that is actually **not available or not found**.

- The duty to **search first:** *"one is to seek water if it is near-by, or there are people present who may be asked, or one has a predominant view it is present in a locality based on evidence such as **birds or a fruit tree**."* If none of that, *"one is not required to seek it"* — what counts is your **predominant belief**.

### B · "Legal absence" (ḥukmī) — water is right there, but you genuinely can't use it

This is the subtler half: the water exists, *"though one has no ability to use it."* The book's reasons:

- **Illness** — *"he may contract an illness by using the water, or that one's illness will become aggravated or delayed"* (fever, diarrhoea, slowed recovery).
- **Severe cold** — *"it is so cold that one fears harm to life or limb, or that he will become seriously ill from the use of the water."*
- **Fear of an enemy / a predator** by the water — *"fear from a predator, or another human, or fear for one's possessions or trusts."*
- **Fear of thirst** — needing the water for drinking (oneself, a **companion**, or a **beast**), *"or needing the water to make dough, for baking bread"* (you may keep the water for the dough and do tayammum — *"though one cannot use this water for soup, because there is no necessity for it"*).
- **No instrument to draw it** — *"the absence of an instrument by which to extract water such as a rope or bucket"* (water down a well, no means to reach it).

### C · The un-make-up-able prayers — Janāza & Eid

A special permission: *"if one fears he will miss the **Funeral** or **Eid** prayer, he is to make tayammum."* The reason: **these prayers cannot be made up later** if missed. The book even adds: if you start the Funeral/Eid prayer with the imam and your wudū breaks, you may **renew tayammum and complete the same prayer.** The proof: Ibn ʿAbbās reported, *"If the funeral prayer came and you feared you would miss it, pray with tayammum."* (Ibn ʿAdī, al-Kāmil 7/182)

### The flip-side — when fear of *missing a prayer* is **NOT** a reason

*"It is **not** a valid reason if one fears he will miss the **Friday** prayer or the **time of an obligatory prayer**."* Why? Because both have a fallback: Friday has a **substitute** (you pray ẓuhr instead), and the five obligatory prayers can be **made up as qaḍā**. Tayammum-for-fear-of-time is only for prayers with **no** fallback (Janāza, Eid).

> **The measure of "a mile."** The book's footnote works through it: Marāqī al-Falāḥ says one mile = 4000 arm-lengths (*dhirāʿ*) ≈ 1866 m; other Ḥanafī sources ≈ 1848 m; the modern statute mile ≈ 1609 m and a nautical mile ≈ 1852 m. **Don't teach the metres** — teach *"about a mile, or far enough that fetching water is a real burden."*

**Yaad rakho:** Tayammum ki ijaazat ka **dil** = jaayiz uzr (2nd shart). Do tarah ki **"paani ki ghair-maujoodgi"**: **(A) Haqeeqi** — paani hai hi nahin / ek meel ke andar nahin (pehle **talaash** karo agar qareeb ho ya log/nishaaniyaan — parinde, phaldaar darakht — hon; warna zaroori nahin). **(B) Hukmi** — paani maujood hai magar istemaal nahin kar sakte: **beemaari** (barhne/der ka khauf), **sakht sardi** (jaan/uzu ka khauf), **dushman/darinda** ka dar, **pyaas** (khud/saathi/jaanwar/aata gondhne ke liye), **rassi-dol** na hona. **(C)** **Janāza ya Eid** chhootne ka dar → tayammum jaayiz (yeh do qaza nahin ho saktin; Ibn ʿAbbās — Ibn ʿAdī 7/182). **Magar** **Jumu'ah** ya **farḍ namaz ka waqt** chhootne ka dar → tayammum **nahin** (Jumu'ah ka badal ẓuhr hai, farḍ ki qaza ho jaati hai).

## 2 · What you do it *with*, and *how* — the act

The remaining seven of the book's **eight validating conditions** describe the *substance* and the *action*. Plus the chapter names the **two pillars** (arkān) — the irreducible core.

### Condition 1 — Intention (niyyah), and what makes it valid

As master-key 2 said, **niyyah is a condition** here (not merely a sunna). *"Its reality is to have a firm resolve in the heart upon the action. The time for the intention is when one places his hands upon the substance."* So you **intend as you strike the earth.**

- **Three conditions for the intention itself:** *"(a) that one be **Muslim** — tayammum is not applicable to non-believers; (b) that one possess the **ability to differentiate and discriminate** [i.e. **sanity**] so that he comprehends what he intends; (c) that one have **knowledge** of what he intends."*
- **To validate the intention *for prayer*, you must intend one of three things** (the book gives a "Summary Diagram"): **(I)** purification from your unclean state (intending *purification alone* suffices — even for janāba); **(II)** to make **prayer permissible** for yourself; or **(III)** a **specific act of worship that requires purity** (e.g. the prostration of recital, or *"I intend tayammum for the prayer of janāza"*). *"A person cannot pray if he **only** intends tayammum"* without one of these three. *(A subtle one to understand, not to dwell on: intending tayammum merely to touch the Qur'an, enter a mosque, or give adhān does **not** make prayer permissible — those are not the "pillars" of prayer.)*

### Condition 3 — A pure **earth** substance

*"It must be from a pure earth substance, such as soil, stone, gypsum, limestone, kohl, clay and arsenic"* (the view of Abū Ḥanīfa and Muḥammad). **Not** valid with *"wood, silver, gold, or metal."* Also excluded: *"everything that turns to ashes upon burning, and everything made in fire."* *(Abū Yūsuf: only earth and sand. Al-Shāfiʿī: only earth in which things can grow.)* The simple rule for the cohort: **of the genus of the earth → yes; not of the earth → no.**

### Conditions 4–6 — The action: face + arms, whole hand, two strikes

- **(4) Completely wipe the parts:** *"the face and the hands up to the elbows. One must remove his ring, interlace or rub between his fingers, and wipe the entire skin of his face."* The proof: *"Tayammum is two strikes, a strike for the face and a strike for the arms to his elbows."* (Hakim, al-Mustadrak 1/287) — and the method hadith: *"He struck the ground with his palms, then raised them to his face; then he struck the ground again and wiped his arms, the inner part and the outer part, until he touched his elbows with his hands."* (Ad-Daraqutni, Sunan vol. 1, 179)
- **(5) Wipe with the *whole* hand or most of it:** *"it is not valid if a person wipes with only two fingers, even repeatedly."* (Contrast wudū, where the head may be wiped with two fingers.)
- **(6) Two strikes** with the inside of the palms on the soil — *"even if the two strikes are from the same place."* (If a dust-storm covers you and you wipe that dust over face and arms *with the intention*, that counts.)

### Conditions 7–8 — Clear the path first

- **(7) Incompatible states must end first:** *"things that are incompatible with tayammum must discontinue before proceeding, such as menstrual periods, postnatal bleeding, or minor impurity."* (You can't tayammum *during* ḥayḍ/nifās.)
- **(8) Remove obstacles** on the skin: *"such as wax or fatty grease — because if one wipes with these present, he will in fact be wiping the wax or fat, and not the skin."*

### The two pillars (arkān)

> *"There are **two pillars** of tayammum; they are to wipe the arms (with the elbows) and the wiping of the face. If a person neglects one of these, then his tayammum is invalid."*

So strip everything away and tayammum is just: **wipe the face + wipe the arms to the elbows.** The eight conditions are *what makes those two pillars count*.

> **What renders tayammum *obligatory*** is, the book says, *"the same as wudū"* — sanity, maturity, Islam, being in a state of minor impurity, and so forth. (You already taught this in the wudū conditions chapter; one line is enough.)

**Yaad rakho:** Act ki sharten: **(1) niyyah** — *shart* hai (mitti apni zaat se paak-kun nahin); niyyat ke 3 conditions: **Musalmaan**, **aqlmand (tameez)**, **ilm**; aur namaz ke liye 3 mein se ek niyyat: **tahaarat**, ya **namaz halaal karna**, ya **koi khaas paaki-waala amal** (jaise janāza). **(3) Paak mitti** ki jins — mitti, patthar, chuna, gachi, surma, geeli mitti (lakdi/sona/chandi/dhaat **nahin**). **(4-6) Amal:** **chehra + dono baazu kohniyon tak**, **poore haath** se (do ungliyon se nahin), **do darb**. **(7)** hayd/nifas/be-wuzu'i pehle khatam ho. **(8)** khaal par rukaawat (mom/charbi) hata do. **Arkan = 2:** chehra + baazu (kohni samet). Wujoob ki sharten wudū jaisi.

## 3 · The complete tayammum — the 7 sunan

> *"The sunan of tayammum are **seven**."*

Once the conditions and two pillars are met, tayammum is **valid**. The seven sunan describe the **Prophetic method** — teach them as a short sequence, the same way you taught the 12 sunnas of ghusl as a flow:

1. **Tasmiyah** — to say *"In the name of God, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate"* at the start.
2. **Tartīb** — order: *"to wipe the face before the hands (to the elbows)."*
3. **Muwālāt** — *"to perform the actions successively, without pausing between them."*
4. **Iqbāl** — dragging the palms **forward** on the soil (*"rub them forward"*).
5. **Idbār** — dragging them **backward** (*"and backward in the soil"*).
6. **Nafḍ** — **shaking** the hands after striking (to drop the excess dust — "bumping the index fingers").
7. **Tafrīj al-aṣābiʿ** — keeping the **fingers open and spread**, *"so the earth gets in between the fingers to ensure optimum cleaning."*

The method hadith again: *"He struck the ground with his palms, then raised them to his face, then struck the ground again and wiped his arms, the inner and outer part, until he touched his elbows."* (Ad-Daraqutni 1/179)

**Yaad rakho:** 7 sunan ek tarteeb mein: (1) **Bismillah**, (2) **tartīb** (chehra pehle, baazu baad mein), (3) **muwālāt** (bila-rukey), (4) haath mitti par **aage** kheencho, (5) **peeche**, (6) haath **jhaad** lo (zyada mitti gira do), (7) **ungliyaan khuli** rakho (mitti beech mein pahunche). Daleel: Daraqutni 1/179.

## 4 · How a tayammum *lives* — delay, requesting water, many prayers

Three practical rules govern a tayammum's life around the question *"is water really not coming?"*

### When to **delay** tayammum

- **Recommended (mandūb) to delay** *"when one expects, according to his predominant view, to attain water before the end of the prayer time."* (Abū Ḥanīfa and Abū Yūsuf: in that case it is **required** to delay.) The logic: if water is likely to come, wait — water is the real thing.
- **Wājib to delay if you've been *promised* water:** *"if one has been promised water, it is wajib to delay tayammum, even if one fears he will miss the prayer from its time"* — **provided** the water is available and **close by**. If it is *"not present with the person who promised it, or it is a mile away or more,"* delaying is not required.
- Same for being **promised clothes** to cover the ʿawra, or a **rope/water-skin** to draw water.

### **Requesting** water before you resort to earth

- *"It is **wājib to seek water the distance of 400 steps** if one believes it is nearby, provided it is safe to do so. If not, then he need not search."* If you have evidence water is close (*"a bird, fruit, or information received"*), you must seek it (300–400 steps) in that direction — *"it is not permitted for him to perform tayammum unless he has searched."* If you've **no** belief water is near, **no** obligation to search.
- **Ask your companion:** *"One is required to request water from an accompanying person… provided that one is in a place where people are not stingy."* If water is only given for payment, you must **buy** it at the **average price** *if* you have money to spare beyond your basic needs; at an **unreasonable price**, you need not. (Three conditions to require purchase: people not stingy, **standard price**, and you have **surplus means**.)

### One tayammum, **many** prayers

- *"One may offer **as many obligatory and voluntary prayers** as he desires with only **one** tayammum"* — *"though it is superior to repeat tayammum in order to remove oneself from the view of the Shāfiʿī school"* (Imām Shāfiʿī: a fresh tayammum for *every* obligatory prayer). The Ḥanafī reasoning: he is **in a state of purity as long as water is unavailable** — *"tayammum acts like wudū in that one is deemed pure until it is broken."*
- And *"it is **valid to perform tayammum before the entry of the prayer time**,"* just like wudū.

**Yaad rakho:** (a) **Taakheer:** agar waqt ke andar paani milne ki ummeed ho → **mandūb** ke taakheer karo (Imāmain: zaroori); paani ka **wada** mil jaaye aur qareeb ho → **wajib** taakheer (chaahe waqt nikalne ka dar ho); door (≥ meel) ho to nahin. (b) **Talab-e-aab:** **400 qadam** tak paani talaash karo agar qareeb hone ka gumaan ho; saathi se **maango**; ghair-munaasib daam par kharidna laazim nahin. (c) **Ek tayammum se kai namazein** (farḍ + nafl) — wudū ki tarah paak rehte ho jab tak paani na mile / tayammum na toote; waqt se pehle bhi durust.

## 5 · What *ends* a tayammum — the mirror

Tayammum is undone by **two things** — and this is the natural **mirror** of the chapter (the same device you used for the wudū nullifiers in 3b and ghusl's non-obligators in Ch 4):

1. **Everything that nullifies wudū** also nullifies tayammum (since tayammum stands in for wudū — passing wind, sleep with relaxed joints, flowing blood, and the rest of the 3b list).
2. **The return of usable water** — the moment the excuse ends. The book defines *"the ability to use the water"* as having *"a quantity sufficient for wudū, even if one has only enough to wash each limb once"*; and it adds that the excuse ends *"such as the enemy retreating, or one's sickness being alleviated, or it being no longer cold, or one having found an instrument by which to extract water."* In all these, *"tayammum is void."* This is the **unique** nullifier — the direct consequence of master-key 1 (*badal* is switched off the moment the need ends).

**The "sees water" refinement (when does it break?):** the book gives three cases. **Before** the prayer → tayammum is void (use water). **During** the prayer → in the Ḥanafī school the sighting **breaks** it (purification has ended); *(Shāfiʿī: it does not break once you've entered the prayer.)* **After** the prayer is finished → **no repeat**, by agreement. *(Teach the headline — "paani dikh gaya to tayammum khatam" — and keep the three-case detail for yourself.)*

> Hold this as the clean summary: **tayammum is "wudū/ghusl made of earth," valid only while water is out of reach — and it dies the moment water comes back within reach.**

**Yaad rakho:** Tayammum **do** cheezon se toot-ta hai: (1) **jo kuch wudū ko tor-ta hai** (3b wali poori list) — kyunki tayammum wudū ka *naa'ib* hai; aur (2) **qaabil-e-istemaal paani mil jaana** / uzr khatam hona (beemaar ki sehat-yaabi) — yeh tayammum ka **khaas** naaqis hai (chaabi 1 ka nateeja). Khulaasा: tayammum = "mitti ka wudū/ghusl", sirf tab tak jab tak paani door — paani aate hi khatam.

## 6 · The book's closing edge cases (understand — teach only if asked)

The chapter ends with a cluster of hard cases. **You** should understand them; for an adult intro cohort, mention them only if someone asks — don't make them the lesson.

- **The injured / wounded body.** *"If the majority or half of the body is injured, one is permitted to perform tayammum"* (the limbs assessed are the wudū-limbs). But *"if the majority of the body is sound, then one is to wash the unharmed area and wipe the injured part."* And *"one cannot combine the use of water and tayammum together"* for the *same* purification — e.g. water for half the arm and tayammum for the other half is not allowed; *"if one does not have enough water to complete washing, then he is to observe tayammum."*
- **Forgetting water you had.** If a traveller *"forgets water during his journey, performs tayammum and prays, then later remembers he had water"* — Abū Ḥanīfa and Muḥammad: he does **not** repeat the prayer; Abū Yūsuf: he repeats. *(A madhhab nuance — for your knowledge.)*
- **The one with no hands and no feet (faqid al-ṭahūrayn).** *"The one who has no hands and no feet and suffers a wound upon his face is permitted to pray without purification and does not need to repeat the prayer — and this is the soundest view."* More generally, the person who possesses **neither of the two purificatory elements** (*al-ṭahūrayn* = water **or** clean earth) — e.g. constrained in an unclean place, unable to reach either — has two reported positions: Abū Ḥanīfa says the **prayer is delayed**; the two companions say he should **"resemble" those who pray** (bow and prostrate facing the qibla, by indication if he cannot, **without** reciting) — *"and this is wajib."* Either way the resemblance **does not** lift the obligation: he **repeats** the prayer once he obtains water or clean earth (the book likens it to a menstruating woman who becomes pure mid-day in Ramaḍān — she abstains the rest of the day and makes up the fast later).

**Yaad rakho:** Aakhri mushkil soorten (sirf samajhne ke liye): (a) **zakhmi badan** — aadha/zyada zakhmi → tayammum; zyada saalim → saalim hissa dhoyo + zakhmi par mas; paani aur tayammum ek saath nahin. (b) **Safar mein paani bhool gaya** phir yaad aaya → Imāmain: dohraana nahin, Abū Yūsuf: dohraana. (c) **Jiske haath-paaon na hon** (faqid al-ṭahūrayn) — chehre par zakhm ho to bila-tahaarat namaz, dohraani nahin (asah); aam tor par jise na paani na paak mitti mile → Abū Ḥanīfa: namaz mu'akhkhar; saahibain: namaz jaisa "mushaabahat" karo (qibla ru, ishaare se, bila-qiraat) — phir paani/mitti milne par **dohraao**.

## The big picture — what Chapter 5 is silently teaching

Step back and the whole chapter rests on a few ideas worth handing the cohort:

1. **Islam never locks you out of worship.** The book frames tayammum as proof of *"the ease of the religion"*: earlier nations bore hardships *"that would not disconnect the servant from the Lord."* Tayammum exists so that *"the servant would not be barred from undertaking acts of worship or receiving their reward."* This is the emotional heart — teach it as **mercy**, not as a list. *(Yeh chapter Allah ki **aasaani** ka suboot hai — al-Mā'idah: "Allah tum par tangi nahin chahta.")*
2. **A substitute has a precise on/off switch.** Tayammum is *badal* — it turns **on** with a valid excuse and **off** the instant water is usable. Nearly every condition and the unique nullifier are just this one idea applied. *(Naa'ib ka ek switch hai: uzr par on, paani par off.)*
3. **The simpler the act, the more careful the gateway.** The action is trivial (two wipes), so the Sharīʿah is careful about *when* you're allowed to reach for it — hence eight conditions and the "search for water first" duty. Mercy is not laxity. *(Amal aasaan, lekin darwaaza ehtiyaat se — pehle paani dhoondo.)*
4. **Earth purifies only by intention.** Unlike water, soil isn't a purifier by nature — your *niyyah* is what makes it one. A quiet lesson that, in worship, **the heart's intention is itself an instrument of purity.** *(Mitti ko **niyyat** paak-kun banaati hai — dil ki niyyat khud ek aala-e-tahaarat hai.)*

**Yaad rakho:** Pura chapter: Allah ki **rahmat o aasaani** — ibaadat se kabhi mahroom nahin. Tayammum ek **badal** hai (uzr par on, paani par off). Amal aasaan, magar darwaaza ehtiyaat se. Aur mitti ko **niyyat** paak-kun banaati hai.

# Part B · Teaching the chapter

You now understand Chapter 5. This section is how to **deliver** it to the same adult, mostly Urdu-speaking cohort — who already know wudū (3a/3b) and ghusl (4). Tayammum is, after ghusl, an **easy and uplifting** chapter — the *act* is simple and the theme is mercy. Your main jobs are: (1) anchor the **substitute (badal)** idea so the conditions make sense, (2) clear up the most common myths (tayammum "any time you're lazy", or "any dust"), and (3) let the cohort feel the **ease of the dīn**.

---

## A note on the cohort's starting point

Two things to expect. First, this chapter is a **relief** after the sensitive ghusl material — keep the tone warm and hopeful; the headline is *"Allah does not wish to place you in difficulty."* Second, almost everyone "knows" tayammum loosely (rub hands on a wall, wipe the face) but is fuzzy on the **gateway** — *when* it is actually permitted, the duty to **search for water first**, and that it **breaks the moment water returns.** Most of your teaching value is at the gateway, not the wipe.

**Yaad rakho:** Yeh ghusl ke baad ek **aasaan, hauslā-afzā** chapter hai — lehja narm aur ummeed-bhara. Sab "tayammum karna" jaante hain magar **kab jaayiz hai**, pehle **paani dhoondna**, aur **paani aate hi toot** jaana — yahin sab se zyada parhaane ki cheez hai.

## The five landmines for Urdu-speaking adult learners

### Landmine 1 · "Tayammum jab chaho kar lo" — treating it as a convenience

The biggest myth: that tayammum is a free shortcut whenever water is a hassle. The book is strict — you need a **valid excuse** (condition 2), and if water is within about a mile or reachable, **you must go to it / search for it** (the 400-steps duty).

**Counter-strategy:** Teach the **badal** key first, then the two "absences" (real / legal). Stress the **search-first** duty: if there's a sign of water (a bird, a fruit tree, someone to ask), you must seek it before earth.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Tayammum koi **shortcut** nahin — yeh sirf **majboori** mein hai. Do haalat: ya paani **hai hi nahin** (ek meel ke andar nahin), ya paani **hai magar istemaal nahin kar sakte** (beemaari, sakht sardi, dushman, pyaas, rassi-dol na ho). Aur agar paani qareeb hone ki **nishaani** ho — parinda, phaldaar darakht, ya koi poochhne wala — to pehle **dhoondna** farḍ hai (~400 qadam tak). Bina uzr tayammum durust nahin."*

### Landmine 2 · "Tayammum breaks only like wudū" — forgetting the water-nullifier

Learners apply the 3b nullifier list and stop there — missing the **unique** nullifier: **finding usable water** ends the tayammum at once.

**Counter-strategy:** Teach the mirror in §5 as **two** lines: *(a) sab kuch jo wudū tor-ta hai, (b) PLUS paani mil jaana.* Hammer the second — it's the direct result of "badal off when need ends."

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Tayammum **do** tarah toot-ta hai. Ek — jo bhi cheez **wudū** ko tor-ti hai (hawa, neend, behta khoon… 3b wali list) — kyunki tayammum wudū ka **naa'ib** hai. Aur doosra, jo **khaas** hai: **qaabil-e-istemaal paani mil jaana** (ya beemaar ka theek ho jaana). Uzr khatam, tayammum khatam — agli namaz paani se."*

### Landmine 3 · "Koi bhi gard se ho jaata hai" — wrong substance / wrong action

Two related errors: using a non-earth substance (a metal surface, a painted/plastic table, ash), and the *action* — wiping with two fingers, one strike, or skipping the elbows.

**Counter-strategy:** Substance rule: **jins-e-zameen** (soil, sand, stone, lime, clay) — **not** wood/metal/gold/silver/ash. Action: **two strikes**, **whole hand**, **face + arms to the elbows** (remove the ring, wipe between fingers). Quote *"two strikes, one for the face and one for the arms to the elbows"* (Hakim 1/287).

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Cheez: **mitti ki jins** — mitti, ret, patthar, chuna, gachi. **Lakdi, dhaat, sona-chandi, raakh nahin.** Amal: **do darb** (do baar mitti par haath), **poore haath** se (do ungliyon se nahin), **chehra + dono baazu kohniyon samet** — anguthi nikaalo, ungliyon ke darmiyaan mas karo. Hadith: 'tayammum do darb hai, ek chehre ka, ek baazuon ka kohniyon tak' (Hakim 1/287)."*

### Landmine 4 · "Ek tayammum ek namaz" — the Shāfiʿī confusion

Many (especially those exposed to other schools) think a fresh tayammum is needed for every farḍ prayer. The Ḥanafī ruling is the opposite.

**Counter-strategy:** State it plainly: **one tayammum → as many farḍ and nafl prayers as you like**, as long as nothing breaks it. Mention the Shāfiʿī view *exists* (one per farḍ) so they recognise it, but the Ḥanafī rule is one-tayammum-many-prayers (you're "pure until broken," like wudū). Don't turn it into a comparative lecture.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Hanafi mein **ek tayammum** se jitni chaaho **farḍ aur nafl** namazein parh lo — jab tak koi cheez use tore nahin. (Shāfiʿī ke nazdeek har farḍ ke liye naya tayammum — yeh farq pehchaan lo, magar humara qaida: ek tayammum, kai namazein — wudū ki tarah 'paak jab tak toote nahin'.) Waqt se pehle bhi tayammum durust hai."*

### Landmine 5 · "Fear of missing any prayer = tayammum" — the Janāza/Eid limit

Learners over-extend the "fear of missing the prayer" permission to *all* prayers. The book restricts it to **Janāza and Eid** — and explicitly **excludes** Friday and the obligatory-time fear.

**Counter-strategy:** Give the logic, not just the rule: tayammum-for-fear-of-time is only for prayers with **no fallback**. **Janāza/Eid** can't be made up → tayammum allowed. **Friday** has ẓuhr as a substitute, and the five prayers have **qaḍā** → so fear of missing *them* is **not** a reason.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"'Namaz chhoot jaane ka dar' har namaz ke liye uzr **nahin**. Sirf **Janāza** aur **Eid** ke liye — kyunki yeh **qaza nahin** ho saktin (Ibn ʿAbbās: 'janāza ka dar ho to tayammum se parh lo' — Ibn ʿAdī 7/182). Magar **Jumu'ah** (badal ẓuhr hai) aur **farḍ ka waqt** (qaza ho jaati hai) — in ke chhootne ke dar par tayammum **nahin**."*

## Suggested 70-minute class plan

*Class shuru karne se pehle:* board par do cheezein taiyaar rakho — (a) ek **switch diagram** (UZR → tayammum ON; PAANI → tayammum OFF), aur (b) ek do-haisse ka panel: **KAB jaayiz** (real/legal absence) ↔ **KAB nahin** (Jumu'ah/waqt ka dar). Yeh poore chapter ka naqsha hai.

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–6 min | Bridge: *"Wudū aur ghusl **paani** se the. Agar paani **na** ho ya use **na** kar sako? Islam tumhe ibaadat se bahar nahin karta — **tayammum**: mitti se paaki."* Read al-Mā'idah ("Allah tum par tangi nahin chahta") |
| 6–14 min | The **three master-keys** — **badal** (switch on/off) · **niyyah is a condition** (mitti niyyat se paak-kun) · **two strikes, face + arms**. Everything hangs on these |
| 14–30 min | **§1 · When is tayammum allowed?** — real absence (≈1 mile, search-first) vs legal absence (illness/cold/enemy/thirst/no-rope) + Janāza/Eid (not Friday/time). Land Landmines 1 & 5 |
| 30–44 min | **§2 · What & how** — pure earth (jins-e-zameen), niyyah + its conditions, the **2 strikes / face + arms to elbows / whole hand**, clear barriers. The **2 pillars**. Land Landmine 3 |
| 44–52 min | **§3 · The 7 sunan** — the method as a flow (bismillah · tartīb · muwālāt · forward · back · shake · spread) |
| 52–62 min | **§4 · The life of a tayammum** — delay (hope/promise of water), requesting water (400 steps, ask companion), **one tayammum → many prayers**. Land Landmine 4 |
| 62–68 min | **§5 · What ends it — the mirror:** everything that breaks wudū **+** water returning. Land Landmine 2 |
| 68–70 min | Q&A · assign handout exercises · preview Chapter 6 (Mash on the khuffs — wiping over leather socks) |

**Yaad rakho:** Do board-tools: **switch** (uzr ON / paani OFF) aur **panel** (kab jaayiz ↔ kab nahin). Pehle **teen chaabiyaan**, phir sab aasaan. Lehja **rahmat** wala — yeh aasaani ka chapter hai. Sab se ahem do jagah: **gateway** (kab jaayiz + pehle paani dhoondo) aur **nullifier** (paani aate hi toot jaata hai).

## What you should NOT teach in Chapter 5

- ❌ **The metres of "a mile"** (1609 / 1848 / 1866 / 1852 m). Teach *"about a mile / far enough that fetching is a real burden."* The numbers are a footnote, not a class point.
- ❌ **The deep madhhab disputes** (Abū Yūsuf vs Abū Ḥanīfa/Muḥammad on the earth-substance; the intricate intention cases for touching-Qur'an / mosque / adhān). You understand them (Part A); teach the **clean rule** — earth-genus → yes; intend purification/prayer/a-pure-act.
- ❌ **The closing edge cases as a lesson** (§6 — the injured body, forgetting water, the one with no hands/feet / faqid al-ṭahūrayn). These are real and you should *know* them, but they will overwhelm an intro cohort — mention them **only if asked**, at the book's level.
- ❌ **Re-teaching the wudū nullifiers** — §5 only *references* the 3b list; a one-line reminder ("jo wudū tor-ta hai") is enough, don't re-run 3b.
- ❌ **Mash on khuffs** (wiping leather socks) — it begins right after this in the book, but it is **Chapter 6**. Don't pre-teach; just preview as "next."
- ❌ **Ḥayḍ/nifās detail** — condition 7 only says these *must end first*; the mechanics are **Chapter 7**. If asked: *"Chapter 7 mein tafseel."*
- ❌ **Turning the Shāfiʿī contrasts into a comparative-fiqh lecture** — name *that* they differ (one tayammum per prayer; intention for prayer obligation) so the cohort recognises it, then return to the Ḥanafī rule.

**Yaad rakho:** 5 sirf **tayammum** hai — kab jaayiz, kis se, kaise, kaise jeeता aur kaise toot-ta hai. **Meel ke metre, gehre ikhtilaaf, mash-on-khuffs (Ch 6), hayd/nifas tafseel (Ch 7)** — yeh sab chhod do. Saaf qaida parhao, footnote nahin.

## Self-check before teaching

Before class, make sure you can:

- [ ] State the **three master-keys** (badal / niyyah-is-a-condition / two-strikes-face-and-arms) and explain how each decides cases.
- [ ] Give the **two kinds of absence** (real vs legal) with 2–3 examples of each, and the **search-first** duty.
- [ ] Explain why **Janāza/Eid** allow tayammum-for-fear but **Friday / obligatory-time** do not.
- [ ] Name the **substance rule** (earth-genus yes; wood/metal/gold/silver/ash no).
- [ ] Walk the **act**: two strikes, whole hand, **face + arms to the elbows** — and quote *"two strikes…"* (Hakim 1/287).
- [ ] Name the **2 pillars** (face + arms) and the **7 sunan** as a sequence.
- [ ] State the **delay / requesting-water** rules and **one-tayammum-many-prayers**.
- [ ] State the **two nullifiers** — wudū-breakers **+** the return of usable water.

If yes to all eight — *tum tayyar ho.*

## After class — diagnostic checklist

For each classmate, watch for:

1. **Do they know tayammum needs a *valid excuse*, not just inconvenience?** *(Real/legal absence — aur pehle paani dhoondna?)* This is the test of whether the chapter landed.
2. **Can they name what tayammum is made *with* and the *two limbs*?** *(Mitti ki jins; chehra + baazu kohniyon tak; do darb?)*
3. **Do they know it breaks the moment usable water returns?** *(Paani milte hi tayammum khatam — yeh khaas naaqis?)*
4. **Do they know one tayammum covers many prayers?** *(Ek tayammum, kai farḍ + nafl?)*
5. **Do they restrict the fear-of-missing permission to Janāza/Eid (not Friday/time)?**

**Yaad rakho:** Agar 2+ classmates abhi bhi tayammum ko "shortcut" samajhte hain, ya "paani aate hi toot jaata hai" par atke hain — to Chapter 6 se pehle 5-minute ka **switch-diagram refresher** lo. Yeh chapter tab kaamyaab hai jab cohort **rahmat** ko mehsoos kare aur **gateway** (kab jaayiz) ko theek se jaane.
