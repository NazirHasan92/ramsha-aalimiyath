Chapter 5 — Tayammum (the dry purification) · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · When tayammum is allowed, what to do it with, how, and what ends it

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

You already know wudū and ghusl — both made with water. But what if there is no water, or water is there but you cannot use it (you're ill, it's freezing, the only water must be kept for drinking)? Islam does not lock you out of worship. Today: tayammum — purifying with clean earth. It is the dry substitute for both wudū and ghusl. We answer five questions:

  1. When am I allowed to do tayammum? — the valid excuse (no usable water).
  2. What do I do it with, and how? — pure earth, intention, the two strikes.
  3. What is the complete tayammum? — the 7 sunan.
  4. How does a tayammum live? — delay, requesting water, many prayers.
  5. What ends it? — everything that breaks wudū + the return of water.

Urdu mein: Wudū aur ghusl paani se the. Paani na ho ya use na kar sako → tayammum: mitti se paaki, paani ka badal. Paanch sawaal: (1) kab jaayiz, (2) kis se aur kaise, (3) kaamil tareeqa (7 sunan), (4) kaise jeeta hai, (5) kaise toot-ta hai.

Source: everything here comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text), the Chapter on Tayammum (book pp. 74–86). No outside sources have been added.

The three keys to the whole chapter

Before the lists, learn these three. Almost every ruling turns on one of them:

KeyWhat it means
1 · Badal (a substitute)Tayammum stands in for wudū/ghusl only when water is absent or unusable. No valid excuse → no tayammum. The moment usable water returns, the tayammum ends.
2 · Niyyah is a conditionSoil is not a purifier by its essence (water is) — so earth purifies only when you intend it. No intention → no valid tayammum.
3 · Two strikes, two limbsThe act is just the face and the arms up to the elbows, in two strikes on pure earth, with the whole hand.

Urdu mein: Teen chaabiyaan: (1) badal — uzr par ON, paani par OFF; (2) niyyah shart hai (mitti niyyat se paak-kun); (3) do darb — chehra + baazu kohniyon tak.

Rule 1 · When tayammum is allowed — the valid excuse

You need a real reason: water is either absent or there but unusable. Two kinds of "absence":

KindWhat it means
A · Real absenceNo water within about a mile / not found. If water is closer, you must go to it. If there is a sign of water (a bird, a fruit tree, someone to ask), you must search first (~400 steps).
B · Legal absenceWater is there but you can't use it: illness (worsening/delayed recovery) · severe cold · enemy / predator · thirst (self, companion, animal, or water needed for dough) · no rope/bucket to draw it.

⚠️ Fear of missing the prayer — only Janāza & Eid

If you fear missing the Funeral or Eid prayer, you may make tayammum — because these cannot be made up. But fear of missing the Friday prayer (substitute = ẓuhr) or the obligatory-prayer time (can be made up as qaḍā) is not a valid reason.

Urdu mein: Uzr chahiye. (A) Haqeeqi — paani ~meel ke andar nahin (pehle dhoondo). (B) Hukmi — paani hai magar istemaal nahin: beemaari, sardi, dushman, pyaas, rassi-dol na hona. Janāza/Eid chhootne ka dar → jaayiz; Jumu'ah/farḍ-waqt ka dar → nahin.

Rule 2 · What & how — the substance, the act, the 2 pillars

Once you have a valid excuse:

PartThe rule
IntentionA condition here (not just a sunna). Made when you place your hands on the earth. You must be Muslim, sane, and know what you intend.
SubstanceThe genus of the earth: soil, sand, stone, gypsum, lime, clay. Not wood, metal, gold, silver, or ash.
The actionTwo strikes on the earth with the inside of the palms; wipe the face, then the arms up to and including the elbows — with the whole hand (not two fingers). Remove your ring; wipe between the fingers.
Clear the pathḤayḍ / nifās / minor impurity must end first; remove obstacles on the skin (wax, grease).
★ The 2 pillarsWipe the face + wipe the arms to the elbows. Neglect one → invalid.

The proof: the Prophet ﷺ said, "Tayammum is two strikes, a strike for the face and a strike for the arms to his elbows." (Hakim, al-Mustadrak 1/287)

Urdu mein: Niyyah shart; jins-e-zameen (mitti/ret/patthar/chuna) — lakdi/dhaat/sona-chandi nahin; do darb: chehra + baazu kohniyon tak, poore haath se. Arkan = 2: chehra + baazu.

Rule 3 · The complete tayammum — the 7 sunan

Beyond the two pillars, the Prophetic method has seven sunan, in this order:

  1. Bismillah at the start
  2. Order (tartīb) — wipe the face before the arms
  3. Without pausing (muwālāt)
  4. Rub the palms forward on the soil
  5. Rub them backward
  6. Shake the hands (drop the excess dust)
  7. Spread the fingers (so earth reaches between them)

Urdu mein: 7 sunan: Bismillah · tartīb (chehra pehle) · muwālāt · haath aage · peeche · jhaad lo · ungliyaan khuli.

Rule 4 · How a tayammum lives, and what ends it

TopicThe rule
When to delayRecommended to delay if you expect water within the prayer time; obligatory (wajib) to delay if you've been promised water that is close by.
Requesting waterSeek water (~400 steps) if you believe it's near and it's safe; ask a companion; buy at the average price if you can afford it (not at an unreasonable price).
Many prayersOne tayammum → as many farḍ and nafl prayers as you wish, until something breaks it. Valid even before the prayer time enters.
What ends it(1) everything that breaks wudū, and (2) the return of usable water (or the excuse ending — e.g. the sick recover).

💡 The Ḥanafī rule on repeating

One tayammum covers many prayers (you are "pure until broken," like wudū). (The Shāfiʿī view is a fresh tayammum for every obligatory prayer — recognise it, but our rule is one-tayammum-many-prayers.)

Urdu mein: Ummeed ho to taakheer mandūb; paani ka wada qareeb ho to wajib. ~400 qadam dhoondo, saathi se maango. Ek tayammum → kai namazein. Toot-ta hai: (1) jo wudū ko tore, (2) paani mil jaana.
