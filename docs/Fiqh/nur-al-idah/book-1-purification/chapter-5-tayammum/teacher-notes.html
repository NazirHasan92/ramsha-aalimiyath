🤲 A chapter about mercy — keep the tone warm

After the sensitive ghusl chapter, tayammum is a relief: the act is simple and the theme is the ease of the dīn. The book frames it as proof that "Allah does not wish to place you in difficulty" and that "the servant would not be barred from acts of worship." Teach it as mercy, not as a dry list of conditions.

★ Master Overview — نَقْشَةُ الْبَاب

One bird's-eye map. Wudū (Ch 3) and ghusl (Ch 4) used water; tayammum is the dry substitute (badal) made of earth, switched on only when water is absent or unusable. The chapter answers five questions: when it is allowed, what & how (substance + act), the method (7 sunan), how it lives (delay, request water, many prayers), and what ends it (a mirror).

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the student will be able to:

  1. Explain tayammum as a substitute (badal) for wudū/ghusl, permitted only when water is absent or unusable, with the verse al-Māʾidah 5:6.
  2. Apply the three master-keys — badal (on/off switch), niyyah is a condition, and two strikes (face + arms) — to decide cases.
  3. Distinguish real absence of water (≈1 mile / not found — search first) from legal absence (illness, cold, enemy, thirst, no instrument).
  4. State when fear of missing a prayer permits tayammum (Janāza & Eid) and when it does not (Friday / obligatory-time).
  5. Name the substance rule (earth-genus), the act (two strikes, whole hand, face + arms to the elbows), and the 2 pillars.
  6. Walk the 7 sunan as a sequence, and state the delay / requesting-water rules and one tayammum → many prayers.
  7. State the two nullifiers — everything that breaks wudū plus the return of usable water.

0 · From Water (Wudū/Ghusl) to Earth (Tayammum) — the 3 master-keys

Open by linking back, then hand the cohort the three keys that decide almost every ruling in the chapter before any list.

Where this fits: the bridge. Wudū and ghusl were made of water; tayammum is the dry substitute made of earth, for when water is out of reach.

💡 The 60-second bridge

"Wudū aur ghusl paani se the. Agar paani na ho, ya ho magar istemaal na kar sako? Islam tumhe ibaadat se bahar nahin karta — tayammum: mitti se paaki, paani ka badal." The headline is mercy: "Allah does not wish to place you in difficulty."

⚠️ Teach these THREE master-keys first — everything hangs on them

1 · Badal (a substitute with an on/off switch): tayammum stands in for wudū/ghusl only when water is absent or unusable. No valid excuse → no tayammum; and the instant usable water returns, the tayammum dies.
2 · Niyyah is a CONDITION (not just a sunna): because soil is not a purifier by its essence (water is) — earth purifies only when you intend it. No intention → no valid tayammum.
3 · Two strikes, two limbs: the act is just the face and the arms up to the elbows, in two strikes on pure earth, with the whole hand.

Tayammum — definition & legality

Meaning
Lexically "purpose and intention" (al-qaṣd); legally "the wiping of the face and hands on soil which is pure." It is specific to this Ummah — "not introduced in any previous laws before it."
Why it exists
A mercy: "the Sacred Law allowed tayammum as a substitute for water during its absence, or when one is unable to use the water… so that the servant would not be barred from undertaking acts of worship or receiving their reward."
Qur'anic proof
"And if you find no water, then take for yourselves clean sand or earth and rub therewith your faces and hands. Allah does not wish to place you in difficulty." (al-Māʾidah 5:6)
Roman Urdu
Tayammum = lughat mein "irādah/qaṣd", shar'an "paak mitti par chehre aur haathon ka mas". Yeh paani ka badal hai — Allah ki aasaani (al-Mā'idah 5:6: "Allah tum par tangi nahin chahta").

1 · When Tayammum Is Allowed — the Valid Excuse (العُذْر)

The heart of the chapter. Of the book's eight validating conditions, the second decides whether you may reach for earth at all: you need a valid reason — and it comes in two kinds of "absence" of water.

Where this fits: the gateway. Almost all of teaching value is here — when is tayammum permitted, and the duty to search for water first.

A · Real absence (ḥaqīqī) — no usable water within reach

From the book
"One must have a valid reason, such as being separated from water at the distance of a mile, even though one is in the city. If one is at a distance that is less than a mile, he must go for water… The real absence is the actual water not being available or found."
Search first
You must seek water "if it is near-by, or there are people present who may be asked, or one has a predominant view it is present based on evidence such as birds or a fruit tree." If none of that, "one is not required to seek it" — what counts is your predominant belief.
★ The measure
"About a mile" (the footnote works through ≈1609–1866 m). Teach the idea, not the metres: far enough that fetching water is a real burden.
Roman Urdu
A: paani hai hi nahin / ~ek meel ke andar nahin. Pehle dhoondo agar qareeb ho ya log/nishaaniyaan (parinde, phaldaar darakht) hon; warna zaroori nahin — ghaalib gumaan ka aitbaar.

B · Legal absence (ḥukmī) — water there, but unusable

From the book
"The legal absence is that the water is available, though one has no ability to use it" — illness contracted/aggravated/delayed; "so cold that one fears harm to life or limb"; "fear from a predator, or another human, or for one's possessions"; "fear of thirst… upon oneself, companions or beast"; needing water "to make dough, for baking bread"; or "the absence of an instrument… such as a rope or bucket" to draw it.
The six reasons
1 illness · 2 severe cold · 3 enemy/human · 4 predator/possessions · 5 thirst (self/companion/beast, or water needed for dough) · 6 no rope/bucket to reach the water.
Roman Urdu
B: paani hai magar istemaal nahin kar sakte — beemaari, sakht sardi, dushman/insaan, darinda/maal ka dar, pyaas (khud/saathi/jaanwar/aata gondhna), rassi-dol na hona.

C · Janāza & Eid — and the Friday limit

From the book
"If one fears he will miss the Funeral or Eid prayer, he is to make tayammum… Even if one begins the prayer with the imam and inadvertently breaks wudu, he is permitted to renew tayammum and complete the same prayer." Ibn ʿAbbās: "If the funeral prayer came and you feared you would miss it, pray with tayammum." (Ibn ʿAdī, al-Kāmil 7/182)
Why — & the limit
Permitted because Janāza/Eid "cannot be made up later." But "it is not a valid reason if one fears missing the Friday prayer or the time of an obligatory prayer" — Friday has a substitute (ẓuhr) and the obligatory "can be made up as qaḍā."
Roman Urdu
C: Janāza/Eid chhootne ka dar → tayammum jaayiz (qaza nahin ho saktin; Ibn ʿAbbās — Ibn ʿAdī 7/182). Magar Jumu'ah (badal ẓuhr) aur farḍ ka waqt (qaza ho jaati hai) → tayammum nahin.

💡 Teaching tip — the "switch"

Draw a switch on the board: a valid excuse (real or legal absence) turns tayammum ON; usable water turns it OFF. Every condition and the nullifier in §5 are just this one idea. (Pre-empts Landmines 1 & 5.)

2 · What & How — Substance, Act, the 2 Pillars (أَرْكَان التَّيَمُّم)

The rest of the book's eight conditions describe the substance (pure earth), the intention, and the action (two strikes). Strip it all away and you reach the two pillars: wipe the face + wipe the arms to the elbows.

Where this fits: the act itself — once you have a valid excuse, what do you use and how do you do it?

Condition 1 · Intention (niyyah) — and what validates it

From the book
"The first condition that validates tayammum is the intention… The time for the intention is when one places his hands upon the substance. The reason it is conditional (as opposed to wudu) is that the essence of the soil is not deemed a purifier except at times of necessity… the soil does not act as a purifier except with an intention."
3 conditions of the intention
(a) be Muslim (not applicable to non-believers); (b) possess the ability to discriminate [sanity]; (c) have knowledge of what one intends.
For prayer — intend one of 3
(I) purification from one's unclean state (suffices alone, even in janāba); (II) to make prayer permissible; (III) a specific act requiring purity (e.g. "tayammum for the prayer of janāza"). One cannot pray if he only intends tayammum.
Roman Urdu
Niyyah shart hai (mitti niyyat se paak-kun banti hai). Niyyat ke 3: Musalmaan · aqlmand · ilm. Namaz ke liye 3 mein se ek: tahaarat / namaz halaal karna / koi khaas paaki-waala amal (janāza).

Condition 3 · A pure earth substance

From the book
"It must be from a pure earth substance, such as soil, stone, gypsum, limestone, kohl, clay and arsenic… not wood, silver, gold, or metal. Everything that turns to ashes upon burning, and everything made in fire, cannot be used." (Abū Ḥanīfa & Muḥammad; Abū Yūsuf: only earth & sand.)
The simple rule
Of the genus of the earth → valid (soil, sand, stone, lime, clay). Not of the earth → invalid (wood, metal, gold, silver, ash).
Roman Urdu
Jins-e-zameen → durust (mitti, ret, patthar, chuna, gachi). Lakdi/dhaat/sona-chandi/raakh → nahin.

Conditions 4–6 · The action — two strikes, whole hand

From the book
"To completely wipe the parts required: the face and the hands up to the elbows. One must remove his ring, interlace between his fingers, and wipe the entire skin of the face… It is not valid if a person wipes with only two fingers… It must be with two strikes with the inside of the palms on the soil."
The proof
"Tayammum is two strikes, a strike for the face and a strike for the arms to his elbows." (Hakim, al-Mustadrak 1/287). Method: "He struck the ground with his palms, raised them to his face, then struck again and wiped his arms — inner and outer — until he touched his elbows." (Ad-Daraqutni 1/179)
Roman Urdu
Do darb: ① chehra, ② baazu kohniyon samet — anguthi nikaalo, ungliyon mein khilaal. Poore haath se (do ungliyon se nahin). Hadith: "tayammum do darb hai" (Hakim 1/287).

Conditions 7–8 + the 2 pillars

From the book
(7) "Things incompatible with tayammum must discontinue first — menstrual periods, postnatal bleeding, or minor impurity." (8) "Remove obstacles that prevent wiping the skin, such as wax or fatty grease."
★ The 2 pillars
"There are two pillars of tayammum: to wipe the arms (with the elbows) and the wiping of the face. If one neglects one of these, his tayammum is invalid."
What makes it obligatory
The same as wudū — "sanity, maturity, Islam, being in a state of minor impurity, and so forth."
Roman Urdu
(7) hayd/nifās/be-wuzu'i pehle khatam ho. (8) khaal par mom/charbi hata do. Arkan = 2: chehra + baazu (kohni samet). Wujoob ki sharten wudū jaisi.

3 · The 7 Sunan — the Method (سُنَنُ التَّيَمُّم)

The book: "The sunan of tayammum are seven." They describe the Prophetic method — teach them as a short sequence, the way you taught the 12 sunnas of ghusl as a flow.

Where this fits: beyond the bare pillars — the complete, masnūn way to perform tayammum.

The 7 sunan — in order

From the book
"To say 'In the name of God, Most Merciful, Most Compassionate' at the start, and to perform the actions in chronological order — namely, to wipe the face before the hands… To perform the actions successively without pausing… When one places his hands on the earth, it is sunna to rub them forward and backward in the soil, after which he is to shake off his hands. In addition, the fingers are to be open and spread." (Ad-Daraqutni 1/179)
The seven
1 Tasmiyah (Bismillah) · 2 Tartīb (face before arms) · 3 Muwālāt (no pausing) · 4 rub the palms forward · 5 rub them backward · 6 shake the hands · 7 spread the fingers. The takeaway is the method, not a memorised numbering.
Roman Urdu
7 sunan ek bahaav: (1) Bismillah, (2) tartīb (chehra pehle), (3) muwālāt, (4) haath aage, (5) peeche, (6) haath jhaad lo, (7) ungliyaan khuli. Daleel: Daraqutni 1/179.

💡 Teaching tip

Demonstrate it once at the front: strike → forward/back → shake → wipe face → strike → wipe arms to the elbows. The cohort learns the flow by watching, not by memorising "1 to 7."

4 · How a Tayammum Lives — Delay, Request Water, Many Prayers

Three practical rules govern a tayammum's life around the question "is usable water really not coming?"

Where this fits: the working life of a valid tayammum — before, during, and across prayers.

When to delay tayammum

From the book
"It is recommended to delay tayammum when one expects to attain water before the end of the prayer time" (Abū Ḥanīfa & Abū Yūsuf: required). "If one has been promised water, it is wajib to delay tayammum, even if he fears missing the prayer from its time — provided the water is available and close by." If a mile or more away, delaying is not required.
The two levels
Mandūb to delay if you expect water within the time. Wājib to delay if you've been promised water that is close by (also: promised clothes for the ʿawra, or a rope/water-skin).
Roman Urdu
Ummeed ho paani milne ki → mandūb taakheer (Imāmain: zaroori). Paani ka wada + qareeb → wajib taakheer (chaahe waqt ka dar ho); door (≥meel) → nahin.

Requesting water before earth

From the book
"It is wajib to seek water the distance of 400 steps if one believes it is near-by, provided it is safe… it is not permitted for him to perform tayammum unless he has searched. One is required to request water from an accompanying person, provided people are not stingy. And if one is not given water unless it is paid for at the average price, then one must pay if he has extra money."
The rules
Search ~400 steps if you believe water is near and it's safe. Ask your companion. Buy at the average price if you have surplus means; at an unreasonable price, you need not.
Roman Urdu
~400 qadam tak paani dhoondo (agar qareeb hone ka gumaan + mehfooz); saathi se maango; munaasib daam par lo agar paisa fazil ho; ghair-munaasib daam par laazim nahin.

One tayammum, many prayers

From the book
"One may offer as many obligatory and voluntary prayers as he desires with only one tayammum… tayammum acts like wudu in that one is deemed pure until it is broken. It is valid to perform tayammum before the entry of the prayer time." (Shāfiʿī view: a fresh tayammum for every obligatory prayer.)
The Ḥanafī rule
One tayammum → as many farḍ + nafl prayers as you wish, until something breaks it (you are "pure until broken," like wudū). And tayammum is valid before the prayer time enters.
Roman Urdu
Ek tayammum → jitni chaaho farḍ + nafl namazein (jab tak toote nahin) — wudū ki tarah "paak jab tak toote nahin". Waqt se pehle bhi durust. (Shāfiʿī: har farḍ ke liye naya.)

5 · What Ends a Tayammum — the Mirror

Tayammum is undone by two things — the natural mirror of the chapter (the same device as the wudū nullifiers in 3b). The second is unique and follows straight from master-key 1.

Where this fits: the off-switch. A tayammum lasts until one of two things ends it.

The two nullifiers

① Like wudū
Everything that nullifies wudū also nullifies tayammum — because tayammum stands in for wudū. (Passing wind, sleep with relaxed joints, flowing blood, a mouthful of vomit, etc. — the whole Chapter 3b list.)
② The unique nullifier
The return of usable water — the book defines this as "a quantity sufficient for wudū, even if one has only enough to wash each limb once" — or the excuse otherwise ending ("the enemy retreating, one's sickness alleviated, no longer cold, or finding an instrument to extract water"). The moment the need is gone, the substitute switches off.
"Sees water" — 3 cases
Before the prayer → void. During the prayer → in the Ḥanafī school it breaks (purity has ended). After finishing → no repeat (agreed). (Teach the headline: paani dikh gaya → tayammum khatam.)
The clean summary
Tayammum is "wudū/ghusl made of earth," valid only while water is out of reach — and it dies the moment usable water comes back within reach.
Roman Urdu
Do naaqis: (1) jo kuch wudū ko tor-ta hai (3b ki list) — kyunki tayammum wudū ka naa'ib hai; (2) qaabil-e-istemaal paani mil jaana / uzr khatam hona (beemaar ki sehat) — yeh tayammum ka khaas naaqis hai. Khulaasa: paani aate hi tayammum khatam.

The book's closing edge cases (understand — teach only if asked)

Injured / wounded body
"If the majority or half of the body is injured, one is permitted to perform tayammum" (the limbs assessed are the wudū-limbs). But "if the majority is sound, one washes the unharmed area and wipes the injured part." And "one cannot combine water and tayammum" for the same purification.
No hands & no feet (faqid al-ṭahūrayn)
"The one who has no hands and no feet and a wound on his face is permitted to pray without purification and does not need to repeat — this is the soundest view." More generally, one who has neither water nor clean earth: Abū Ḥanīfa — the prayer is delayed; the two companions — he should "resemble" those who pray (qibla-facing, by indication, without reciting) and repeat it once he obtains water or earth.
Teach-level
Know these; teach only if asked. They are real rulings the chapter closes on, but they will overwhelm an intro cohort — keep them in reserve.
Roman Urdu
Aakhri mushkil soorten: zakhmi badan (aadha/zyada zakhmi → tayammum; warna saalim hissa dhoyo + zakhmi par mas; paani+tayammum ek saath nahin); jiske haath-paaon na hon / na paani na mitti → Abū Ḥanīfa: namaz mu'akhkhar, saahibain: "mushaabahat" karo phir paani/mitti par dohraao. Sirf poochhne par parhao.

⚠️ Don't forget the second nullifier

Learners apply the 3b wudū list and stop. The unique nullifier — finding usable water — is the whole point of "badal off when need ends." Hammer it: uzr khatam, tayammum khatam.

⚠️ Common Student Mistakes — Watch For These

1. Treating tayammum as a convenience

Tayammum needs a valid excuse (real or legal absence of water). If water is within ~a mile or reachable, you must go to it — and if there's a sign of water (a bird, a fruit tree, someone to ask), you must search first (~400 steps). No excuse → no tayammum.

2. Forgetting the second nullifier

Tayammum breaks by (a) everything that breaks wudū AND (b) the return of usable water. The second is unique — uzr khatam, tayammum khatam.

3. Wrong substance / wrong action

Use the genus of the earth (soil, sand, stone, lime, clay) — not wood/metal/gold/silver/ash. The action: two strikes, whole hand, face + arms to the elbows (remove the ring, wipe between fingers). Two fingers / one strike / skipping the elbows = invalid.

4. "A fresh tayammum for every prayer"

That is the Shāfiʿī view. The Ḥanafī rule: one tayammum → as many farḍ and nafl prayers as you like, until something breaks it (pure-until-broken, like wudū).

5. Over-extending "fear of missing the prayer"

That permission is only for Janāza and Eid (which can't be made up). It does not apply to Friday (substitute = ẓuhr) or to the obligatory-prayer time (which can be made up as qaḍā).

6. Treating niyyah as optional (as in wudū)

In wudū, intention is only a sunna. In tayammum it is a condition — soil purifies only when intended. No intention → no valid tayammum.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an & Hadith

Every text below is quoted by Nur al-Idah itself in this chapter. Use 1–2 in class to anchor the rulings in the sources.

al-Māʾidah 5:6 — the substitute, and the mercy

"And if you find no water, then take for yourselves clean sand or earth and rub therewith your faces and hands. Allah does not wish to place you in difficulty."

The book's proof for the legality of tayammum — and the headline of the whole chapter: purification by earth is Allah's ease, so the servant is never barred from worship.

Note: this edition's footnote prints the reference as "al-Māʾidah 7" (older numbering); the verse — including "Allah does not wish to place you in difficulty" — is al-Māʾidah 5:6 in the standard muṣḥaf, the same verse used in the Wudū and Ghusl chapters.

al-Baqarah 2:286 — no soul is burdened beyond capacity

"On no soul does God place a burden greater than it can bear," and "Our Lord! Lay not on us a burden greater than we have the strength to bear."

Cited by the book to show why tayammum exists — Islam removed the hardships borne by earlier nations so that worship is never out of reach.

"Tayammum is two strikes" (Hakim, al-Mustadrak 1/287)

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Tayammum is two strikes, a strike for the face and a strike for the arms to his elbows."

The basis for the act — two strikes, and the two pillars (the face and the arms up to and including the elbows).

The method of wiping (Ad-Daraqutni, Sunan 1/179)

It is reported that the Prophet ﷺ "struck the ground with his palms, then raised them to his face; then he struck the ground again and wiped his arms, the inner part and the outer part, until he touched his elbows with his hands."

The basis for the masnūn method and several of the 7 sunan (the order, the rubbing, reaching the elbows).

Ibn ʿAbbās — tayammum for the funeral prayer (Ibn ʿAdī, al-Kāmil 7/182)

Ibn ʿAbbās reported: "If the funeral prayer came and you feared you would miss it, pray with tayammum."

The basis for permitting tayammum out of fear of missing the Janāza (and Eid) prayer — prayers that cannot be made up later.
