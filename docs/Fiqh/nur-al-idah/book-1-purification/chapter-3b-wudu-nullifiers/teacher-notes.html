✂️ This is "Chapter 3b" — the second half of Wudu

Wudu is the densest chapter in Book I, so it is split in two. 3a = performing wudu: the 4 pillars, conditions, 18 sunnas, 14 etiquettes, 6 disliked acts. 3b (this file) = the status of wudu: the 3 types, the 12 nullifiers, the 10 non-nullifiers. Teach 3a first — 3b only makes sense once the cohort can already perform wudu.

📖 Source constraint

Every ruling, every verse, every hadith below is drawn only from Nur al-Idah (book pp. 56–64). Nothing has been added from outside sources. If a classmate asks a question whose answer is not in this chapter, say: "Yeh is hisse mein nahin hai — Chapter 4 (Ghusl) ya kisi aur kitaab mein khulega."

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter (Part 2), the student will be able to:

  1. Name the 3 types of wudu — farḍ, wājib, mandūb — with an example of each, and explain that the weight depends on the act the wudu is for.
  2. Recall that wudu is farḍ for prayer (incl. nafl & funeral), the prostration of recital, and touching the Qur'an; wājib for ṭawāf; and mandūb on many occasions.
  3. Recite the 12 nullifiers in four families (exits/flow · vomit · loss of control · prayer cases).
  4. Apply the master-key word "flowing" and the "loss of control" principle to decide unseen cases.
  5. State the 10 non-nullifiers as the mirror of the nullifiers, and use the principle behind each line.
  6. Correct the two common folk-rulings — any blood (→ only flowing) and touching a woman (→ does not break it in the Ḥanafī school).
  7. Ease waswasa in over-scrupulous learners: certainty is not lifted by doubt.

0 · From "Performing" (3a) to "Status" (3b)

Open by linking back. The cohort can now make wudu; this chapter is about the wudu they hold once it is made.

Where this fits: the bridge. 3a answered "how do I make wudu?"; 3b answers "how much does it weigh, and what breaks it?"

💡 The 90-second recap of 3a

Before opening 3b, redraw the 3a ladder once: farḍ (4) · sunna (18) · adab (14) · makrūh (6). Then pivot: "Ab humein wudu karna aata hai. Aaj uski zindagi — kab woh farḍ hai, kab wājib, kab sirf behtar; aur ek baar ho jaaye to kis cheez se toot-ta hai aur kis se nahin." Do not re-teach 3a — 90 seconds only.

⚠️ The single device that carries this chapter — the mirror

Sections 2 and 3 are twins: flowing blood breaks wudu ↔ non-flowing blood doesn't; a mouthful of vomit breaks it ↔ less doesn't; relaxed sleep breaks it ↔ firmly-seated sleep doesn't. Teach them on a two-column board (TOOTTA ↔ NAHIN TOOTTA), never as two separate lists. Once the cohort grasps the principle behind each line, they can place a brand-new situation correctly.

1 · The Three Types — أَقْسَامُ الْوُضُوء

The book opens: "Wudu is divided into three types." A ranking by legal weight — and the weight depends on the act the wudu is for.

Where this fits: the first of the chapter's three questions — how much does a given wudu weigh? The same washing carries three different weights.

Type 1 · Farḍ (obligatory)

From the book
"The first type is that which is obligatory upon one in a state of minor impurity who wishes to pray, even if the prayer is a voluntary one, or is a funeral prayer, for indeed it is still a prayer… It is obligatory for the prostration of recital and for one who wishes to touch the Quran, even if it is only one verse that is written on a paper, dirham or wall, as Allah said [None shall touch except those who are clean]."
Farḍ for three things
(1) Any prayer — including a voluntary prayer and even the funeral prayer (no rukūʿ/sujūd, yet still a prayer). (2) The prostration of recital (sajdat al-tilāwa). (3) Touching the Qur'an — even one verse on paper, a coin, or a wall.
Qur'anic proof
"None shall touch except those who are clean." (al-Wāqiʿa 56:79)
Roman Urdu
Farḍ: har namaz (nafl aur janāza bhi), sajda-e-tilāwat, aur Qur'an ko chhoona ("paak ke siwa koi na chhuye").

Type 2 · Wājib (necessary)

From the book
"The second type is that which is wajib for the tawaf of the ka'bah, as the Prophet ﷺ said [The tawaf around the ka'bah is like prayer except that you may speak, and whoever speaks should not speak unless it is good]." (at-Tirmidhī 960)
Just one case
For ṭawāf (circling the Kaʿba), wudu is wājib — one notch below farḍ. The proof is that ṭawāf is "like" prayer; since it is like, not identical, the rank is wājib rather than farḍ.
Roman Urdu
Wājib: sirf ṭawāf ke liye (Nabi ﷺ: "ṭawāf namaz jaisa hai" — magar "jaisa", "wahi" nahin, isi liye wājib).

Type 3 · Mandūb (recommended) — in 5 baskets

From the book
"The third type is that which is recommended on different occasions…" — then a long list. Group it; do not read 20 items.
A · Stay constantly pure
Refresh an existing wudu (in a different place; same-spot repeat = wastage); before sleep and on waking.
B · After a fault
After backbiting, lying, slander; after any sin ("wudu expiates small sins" + Āl ʿImrān 3:135); after laughing loudly outside prayer; in anger (Abū Dāwūd 4766).
C · Worship / recitation / ʿilm
For every prayer (at-Tirmidhī 58; Abū Dāwūd 62 — "ten virtuous deeds"); to recite Qur'an from memory; for hadith reading/narrating; for studying; for the adhān, iqāma, khuṭba.
D · Sacred contact & places
Touching books of Islamic law; before ghusl (Muslim 316); when junub wishes to eat/sleep/repeat intercourse (Muslim 307; Ahmad 3/21); after washing/carrying a corpse (Abū Dāwūd 3161); visiting the Prophet ﷺ; on ʿArafah & Ṣafā–Marwah.
E · After a disputed act
After camel meat (at-Tirmidhī 81 — Ḥanafī: recommended; Aḥmad: required); after touching a woman or one's private part (doesn't break wudu for Ḥanafīs, but recommended to exit the scholars' disagreement). This quietly previews the non-nullifiers (§3).
Roman Urdu
Mandūb ki lambi list ko 5 tokriyon mein baant lo: (A) hamesha bā-wuzu, (B) galti ke baad, (C) ibādat/ʿilm, (D) muqaddas chhuaao/jaghein, (E) ikhtilāfi kaam ke baad. Farḍ chhoote to amal nahin; mandūb chhoote to bas sawaab gaya.

💡 Teaching tip — the big idea of §1

Same washing, three weights, depending on what it unlocks: a non-negotiable key for prayer (farḍ), necessary for ṭawāf (wājib), a beautiful extra everywhere else (mandūb). The heart of the long third list is one idea — a believer tries to live in a constant state of wudu.

2 · The Twelve Nullifiers — نَوَاقِضُ الْوُضُوء

The heading is exact: "Twelve things nullify wudu, and they are…" Don't teach twelve loose items — teach four families.

Where this fits: the second question — what ends a wudu? The left column of your mirror board.

Family A · Exits / flow (#1–3)

From the book
"1/ Anything that exits from the front and back passage nullifies wudu except the air which comes out from the front passage (of a female)… 2/ Giving birth without seeing blood nullifies ablution… 3/ The flowing of an impurity such as blood or pus from anywhere else other than the two passages nullifies wudu… 'Ablution is from all types of flowing blood.'" (al-ʿAynī 1/262)
The three
(1) Anything from the two passages — the only exception being wind from a female's front passage ("most sound view"). (2) Birth without blood (breaks due to wetness, even if not classed as nifās). (3) Flowing blood/pus from elsewhere.
★ The hinge word
Flowing — it must move past where it emerged. Blood that merely appears but does not run does NOT break wudu (see non-nullifier #1). This one word separates half of §2 from half of §3.
Roman Urdu
A: (1) aage/peeche se kuch bhi (sirf aurat ki aagey ki hawa mustasna), (2) bila-khoon wiladat, (3) kisi aur jagah se khoon/peep ka behna. Markazi lafz: behna.

Family B · Vomit (#4–6)

From the book
"4/ Vomiting a mouthful of food and water, blood clots or bile nullifies wudu… the definition of a mouthful is that the mouth cannot be shut except with difficulty… 5/ If a person vomits a few times and the cause is the same… deemed as one occurrence… 6/ If one's saliva is overshadowed by blood… ablution is void." (at-Tirmidhī 87)
The threshold
A mouthful = the mouth cannot shut except with difficulty. Less than that does NOT break wudu (non-nullifier #6). Mucus from the head is pure and never a nullifier (by agreement; fatwa).
Roman Urdu
B: (4) muh-bhar qai (muh band karna mushkil), (5) ek hi sabab se thodi-thodi qai mila kar muh-bhar = ek baar, (6) thook jis par khoon ghaalib ho.

Family C · Loss of control — sleep & consciousness (#7–10)

From the book
"7/ To sleep in such a way that one's buttocks are not firmly set on the ground, renders the wudu void… the sleep itself does not invalidate wudu… rather it is due to the sleeping person not being able to remain free from that which breaks wudu… 8/ The elevation of one's buttocks from the floor while one is sleeping… nullifies the wudu even if one does not fall over. 9/10/ Losing consciousness (fainting) / temporary insanity / and drunkenness all nullify wudu." (Marāqī al-Falāḥ 55)
The principle
Sleep itself is not impurity — wudu breaks because the sleeper can no longer guarantee he is free of what breaks it. So wudu breaks only when joints relax: lying on the side, back, or hip, or buttocks not firm. Seated-upright / standing / bowing / prostration-pose sleep = intact (unless the body collapses). "Wudu is not imposed except on the one who sleeps lying down."
Deeper loss
Fainting, insanity, and drunkenness are a deeper loss of control than sleep — so they break wudu in any posture.
Roman Urdu
C: neend khud na-paaki nahin — khatra jod dheele hone ka hai. Karwat/peeth/kuolhe par leT kar neend = toot-ta; seedhe baithe, chuthad jamey = qaaim. (9/10) be-hoshi, junoon, nasha — har haalat mein todte hain.

Family D · Two special prayer cases (#11–12)

From the book
"11/ Ablution is void if a mature conscious person laughs loudly in a prayer that includes bowing and prostration even if he intentionally laughs… The same applies even if done forgetfully… smiling in prayer does not affect wudu or prayer. 12/ If a man rubs a vagina with his erect penis without a screen… ablution is void… The implication here is rubbing, not penetration… such acts are offensive and forbidden in the Islam, though the position of the jurist is to provide a legal answer for a scenario should it ever occur." (Marāqī al-Falāḥ 55–56)
#11 · Loud laughter in prayer
Breaks prayer AND wudu — but only in a prayer with rukūʿ/sujūd. Smiling doesn't affect either. In the funeral prayer / recital prostration (no bowing/sujūd), audible laughter voids the prayer but NOT the wudu.
#12 · Rubbing without a screen
The point is rubbing, not penetration (penetration → ghusl). State at the book's level; the book itself notes the act is forbidden and is addressed only for legal completeness. Don't dwell.
Roman Urdu
D: (11) namaz (rukūʿ-sujūd waali) mein buland qahqaha — namaz bhi wudu bhi toot jaata hai (muskurana nahin; janāza/sajda-e-tilāwat mein sirf namaz). (12) bila-aDh (screen) ragad.

⚠️ Two principles under the twelve

Strip the detail and the nullifiers reduce to two ideas: (1) something impure exits or flows (Families A & B) and (2) the person loses reliable control of the body (Family C). Family D's two cases sit slightly apart — teach them as named exceptions. If the cohort leaves with "flow / exit" and "loss of control," they can reason almost the whole chapter.

3 · The Ten Non-nullifiers — مَا لَا يَنْقُضُ الْوُضُوء

"There are ten things that do not nullify wudu." This is the mirror of §2 — teach it that way. Each item is the near-twin of a nullifier, sitting just on the other side of the line.

Where this fits: the third question — what looks like it should break wudu but doesn't? The right column of your mirror board, and the good-news half of the chapter.

Blood, body-matter & exits (#1–3)

From the book
"1/ The appearance of blood that does not flow from its injury onto another part of the skin does not break wudu. 2/ If a piece of flesh or scab is separated from the body without the flowing of blood… it does not break wudu… an example is a 'Madina blood vessel' that is termed 'rashta' in Persian… 3/ If a worm exits a wound, or exits from the ear or nose, it does not break wudu… However, if it exits through the anus, ablution is nullified."
The twins
#1 ↔ nullifier #3 (the line is flowing). #2 (rashta — a polyp / hanging flesh / pimple, if it comes off without flowing blood). #3 ↔ nullifier #1 (a worm exits — but only the two passages break wudu, never the ear/nose/wound).
Roman Urdu
(1) na-behta khoon nahin todta (behta todta hai). (2) bila-khoon gosht/khurand (rashta) — nahin todta. (3) kaan/naak/zakhm se keeda — nahin; magar peeche se nikle to todta hai.

Touching one's own body / a woman (#4–5)

From the book
"4/ Touching one's penis, anus or a woman's genitalia, does not nullify wudu… a man came to the Prophet ﷺ and said, 'What do you say about a man who touches his genitals in prayer?' Thereupon the Prophet ﷺ said to him, 'Is it not a body part from you?'… 5/ Touching a woman does not nullify wudu. A'ishah reported [The Prophet would fondle (and kiss) some of his wives, and then would pray without making wudu]." (at-Tirmidhī & an-Nasāʾī 165)
Key point
Neither touching one's own genitals nor touching a woman breaks wudu (the view of ʿUmar, ʿAlī, Ibn Masʿūd, Ibn ʿAbbās, Zayd ibn Thābit). The other hadith ("whoever touches his genitals is to do wudu") is read as wudu in its linguistic sense — i.e. wash the hands. Keep this far from nullifier #12 (rubbing without a screen) — they are unrelated.
Roman Urdu
Apne aap ko ya aurat ko chhoona wudu nahin todta (Ḥanafī). Yeh #12 wali "ragad" se bilkul alag baat hai. (Bachne ke liye wudu kar lena behtar — §1 tokri E.)

Vomit (sub-mouthful / phlegm) & sleep (#6–10)

From the book
"6/ Vomiting less than a mouthful is not an annulling factor. 7/ Vomiting phlegm (saliva) does not nullify wudu even if it is more than a mouthful because it has not mixed with any filth, and saliva is pure. 8/ To sway while sleeping where there is a possibility of falling does not break wudu… 9/ Sleeping in a firm manner is not an annulling factor… 10/ Sleeping in prayer does not break ablution even if one sleeps during bowing and prostration provided the person maintains his posture according to the sunna." (Abū Dāwūd 200)
The twins
#6 ↔ nullifier #4 (under the threshold). #7 (phlegm — wrong substance, pure — never breaks, even if copious). #8–10 ↔ nullifier #7: swaying while firmly seated, firm leaning sleep, and sunna-posture sleep in prayer all keep the wudu (if the posture collapses, void).
The Companions' image
They waited for ʿishāʾ until their heads drooped (sank), then prayed without renewing wudu — the proof that seated dozing is intact.
Roman Urdu
(6) kam qai nahin todti. (7) balgham — chahe zyada — paak hai, nahin todta. (8-10) jhoolna/jami/sunnat-posture waali neend — qaaim (posture bigde to toot-ta).

💡 Teaching tip — the five principles that write the list

Frame §3 as the good-news half. A learner who holds these five lines can place any new case: blood breaks only when it flows; vomit only at a mouthful (and never phlegm); sleep only when the posture relaxes the joints; touching one's own body or a woman never breaks it; exits break only from the two passages.

⚠️ This section is the cure for waswasa

Over-scrupulous learners re-do wudu at every doubt. Hammer: certainty is not lifted by doubt — a wudu you know you had is not broken by a maybe. The self-diagnosis: did the blood actually flow? was it a full mouthful? were my joints actually relaxed? If "no / not sure," the earlier wudu stands.

⚠️ Common Student Mistakes — Watch For These

1. "Any blood breaks wudu"

The Ḥanafī text says only flowing blood breaks wudu (nullifier #3). A drop that beads on the skin but does not run does NOT break it (non-nullifier #1). The hadith itself says "from all types of flowing blood." The deciding word is behna (to flow past the spot).

2. "Touching a woman breaks wudu" — the Shāfiʿī confusion

In the Ḥanafī school, touching a woman does not break wudu at all (non-nullifier #5 — the Prophet ﷺ would fondle his wives then pray without renewing wudu). The Shāfiʿīs hold otherwise — which is why the book recommends making wudu anyway to exit the disagreement (§1, Basket E). Keep it separate from nullifier #12 (rubbing without a screen), which is a different, extreme case.

3. "I dozed off, so my wudu is gone"

The rule is about posture, not sleep itself. Sleep isn't impurity — the worry is that relaxed joints silently release wind. Wudu breaks only when lying on the side, back, or hip, or slumping so the buttocks aren't firm. Firmly-seated sleep, and a light doze where you still hear voices, keep the wudu (non-nullifiers #8–10).

4. The vomit threshold

Vomit breaks wudu only at a mouthful ("the mouth cannot shut except with difficulty"). Less does not (non-nullifier #6). And phlegm/saliva never breaks it, even if copious (non-nullifier #7) — it isn't impure.

5. Over-scrupulous re-doing (waswasa)

Re-making wudu at the faintest doubt is not piety — it is waswasa. Certainty is not lifted by doubt. Teach §3 as reassurance and walk the self-diagnosis questions; a wudu you know you had stands until you are sure a nullifier occurred.

6. Confusing "farḍ" and "wājib" wudu

Wudu is farḍ for prayer and touching the Qur'an, but only wājib for ṭawāf. Don't flatten the two — and note that wudu for ṭawāf, though wājib, is still required (ṭawāf "is like prayer").

Every text below is quoted by Nur al-Idah itself in this chapter. Use 1–2 in class to anchor the rulings in the sources.

al-Wāqiʿa 56:79 — touching the Qur'an

"None shall touch except those who are clean."

The book's proof that wudu is farḍ to touch the muṣḥaf — even a single verse written on paper, a coin, or a wall.

"Ṭawāf is like prayer" (at-Tirmidhī 960)

The Prophet ﷺ said: "The tawaf around the ka'bah is like prayer except that you may speak, and whoever speaks should not speak unless it is good."

The basis for wudu being wājib for ṭawāf — it is like prayer, so the rank is wājib rather than farḍ.

Āl ʿImrān 3:135 — wudu after a fault

"And those who, having done an act of indecency or wronged their own souls, remember Allah and ask for forgiveness for their sins."

Cited with the hadith that whoever sins, then makes wudu and prays for forgiveness, is forgiven — the basis for recommending wudu after a mistake (§1, Basket B).

Wudu after anger (Abū Dāwūd 4766)

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Anger is from Satan, and the Shayatin are created from fire, and verily you extinguish fire with water; so if any of you becomes angry, he should perform wudu."

The basis for recommending wudu in a state of anger (§1, Basket B).

"Ablution is from all types of flowing blood" (al-ʿAynī 1/262)

The Ḥanafī proof that flowing blood/pus from outside the two passages breaks wudu — and, by its very wording, that only flowing blood does (nullifier #3 ↔ non-nullifier #1).

"Is it not a body part from you?" (at-Tirmidhī)

A man asked the Prophet ﷺ about touching his genitals in prayer; he replied: "Is it not a body part from you?" Imam Tirmidhī called it the best and soundest hadith on the subject.

The basis for non-nullifier #4 — touching one's own genitals does not break wudu.

A'ishah on touching one's wives (at-Tirmidhī & an-Nasāʾī 165)

A'ishah reported: "The Prophet ﷺ would fondle (and kiss) some of his wives, and then would pray without making wudu."

The basis for non-nullifier #5 — touching a woman does not break wudu in the Ḥanafī school.
