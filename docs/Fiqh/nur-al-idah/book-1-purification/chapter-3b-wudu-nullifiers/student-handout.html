Chapter 3b — Wudu (Part 2): The Status of the Ablution · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · The 3 types, the 12 nullifiers & the 10 non-nullifiers

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

Last lesson (Chapter 3a) you learned to make wudu. Today is about the wudu after you have it — its legal life. We answer three questions:

  1. How much does a wudu weigh? — the 3 types (farḍ / wājib / mandūb).
  2. What breaks it? — the 12 nullifiers.
  3. What looks like it breaks it but doesn't? — the 10 non-nullifiers.

The big idea: questions 2 and 3 are a mirror. Almost every "breaker" has a near-twin that does not break wudu — so the trick is to learn the principle behind the line, not a flat list.

Urdu mein: Pichhle sabaq mein wudu karna seekha; aaj uska darja aur zindagi: (1) kab farḍ/wājib/mandūb, (2) kis se toot-ta, (3) kis se nahin. §2 aur §3 ek aaina hain — usool yaad rakho, list khud yaad ho jaayegi.

Source: everything here comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text), the Chapter on Wudu (book pp. 56–64). No outside sources have been added. (How to perform wudu — the pillars and sunnas — was the previous handout, Chapter 3a.)

Rule 1 · The three types of wudu — by legal weight

The same washing carries three different weights, depending on what you are about to do with it.

TypeWhenFor what
Farḍ
(obligatory)		You cannot do the act without itPrayer (any prayer — even voluntary or funeral) · the prostration of recital · touching the Qur'an (even one verse on paper, a coin, or a wall — "None shall touch except those who are clean", al-Wāqiʿa 56:79)
Wājib
(necessary)		One notch below farḍṬawāf of the Kaʿba (the Prophet ﷺ: "ṭawāf is like prayer…", at-Tirmidhī 960)
Mandūb
(recommended)		Beautiful extra — rewarded, not requiredMany occasions — see the 5 baskets below

The recommended (mandūb) occasions — in 5 baskets

BasketExamples from the book
A · Stay constantly pureRefresh an existing wudu (in a different place) · before sleep · on waking
B · After a faultAfter backbiting / lying / slander · after any sin ("wudu expiates small sins") · after laughing loudly outside prayer · when angry (Abū Dāwūd 4766)
C · Worship & knowledgeFor every prayer · reciting Qur'an from memory · reading/narrating hadith · studying · the adhān, iqāma & sermon
D · Sacred contact & placesTouching books of fiqh · before ghusl · when junub wants to eat/sleep · after washing/carrying a corpse · visiting the Prophet ﷺ · on ʿArafah & Ṣafā–Marwah
E · After a disputed actAfter camel meat · after touching a woman or one's private part (to be free of the scholars' disagreement)

Urdu mein: Teen darje: farḍ (namaz, sajda-tilāwat, Qur'an chhoona), wājib (sirf ṭawāf), mandūb (lambi list — 5 tokriyon mein). Farḍ chhoote to amal hi nahin; mandūb chhoote to bas sawaab gaya.

Rule 2 · The 12 things that break wudu — in four families

Don't memorise twelve loose items. They fall into four families, and two master-keys explain almost all of them: flowing and loss of control.

FamilyThe nullifiers
A · Exits / flow
(#1–3)		1 Anything from the front/back passage (only exception: wind from a woman's front passage) · 2 Giving birth without blood · 3 Flowing blood or pus from anywhere else
B · Vomit
(#4–6)		4 Vomiting a mouthful (mouth can't shut except with difficulty) · 5 Small repeats from one cause add up to a mouthful · 6 Saliva overshadowed by blood
C · Loss of control
(#7–10)		7 Sleeping with the buttocks not firm (lying on side/back/hip) · 8 Buttocks lifting off the floor while asleep · 9/10 Fainting, insanity, drunkenness
D · Prayer cases
(#11–12)		11 Loud laughter in a prayer with bowing & prostration (breaks prayer and wudu) · 12 Rubbing without a screen

⚠️ The master-key word: "flowing"

Blood/pus breaks wudu only when it flows (runs past where it came out) — not when it merely appears. And sleep breaks wudu only when the body relaxes (lying down), because the worry is that relaxed joints silently release wind — sleep itself is not impurity.

Urdu mein: 4 families: A nikalna/behna, B qai, C control ka jaana, D namaz ke do khaas masle. Do chaabiyaan: behna aur be-ikhtiyaari. Buland qahqaha namaz mein — namaz bhi wudu bhi toot-ta hai (muskurana nahin).

Rule 3 · The 10 things that do NOT break wudu — the mirror

This is the good-news half. Each one is the twin of a "breaker", sitting just on the safe side of the line.

#Does NOT break wuduIts twin "breaker"
1Blood that appears but does not flow↔ flowing blood (#3)
2A scab / piece of flesh coming off without blood (a rashta)↔ flowing blood (#3)
3A worm from a wound, ear, or nose (but from the anus → breaks it)↔ only the two passages (#1)
4Touching one's own genitals(unrelated to #12)
5Touching a woman (the Prophet ﷺ kissed his wives, then prayed)(Ḥanafī school)
6Vomiting less than a mouthful↔ a mouthful (#4)
7Vomiting phlegm — even a lot — it is pure↔ a mouthful of vomit (#4)
8Swaying while seated upright (buttocks firm)↔ relaxed sleep (#7)
9Sleeping firmly, even leaning on a support↔ relaxed sleep (#7)
10Sleeping in prayer in the sunna posture (collapse → breaks it)↔ relaxed sleep (#7)

💡 The five principles that write the whole list

Blood breaks wudu only when it flows · vomit only at a mouthful (and never phlegm) · sleep only when the posture relaxes the joints · touching one's own body or a woman never breaks it · exits break wudu only from the two passages.

⚠️ Don't re-do wudu over a "maybe"

Certainty is not lifted by doubt. A wudu you know you had is not broken by a maybe. Ask: did the blood actually flow? was it a full mouthful? were my joints actually relaxed? If "no / not sure", your earlier wudu still stands.

Urdu mein: Yeh §2 ka aaina hai. Aurat ko chhoona aur apne aap ko chhoona wudu nahin todta. Na-behta khoon, kam qai, balgham, jami/seedhi neend — kuch nahin todta. Yaqeen shak se nahin jaata — baar baar wudu mat dohraao.
