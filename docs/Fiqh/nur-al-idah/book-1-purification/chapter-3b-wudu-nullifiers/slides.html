Rule 1 · The three types of wudu

Same washing — three weights, depending on what it is for.

TypeFor what
FARḌPrayer (even nafl & funeral) · prostration of recital · touching the Qur'an

"None shall touch except those who are clean."

Rule 2 · What breaks wudu — 4 families

A · Exits / flow (1–3)
the two passages · birth without blood · flowing blood/pus
B · Vomit (4–6)
a mouthful · small repeats add up · saliva overshadowed by blood
C · Loss of control (7–10)
relaxed/lying sleep · buttocks lift · faint / insanity / drunkenness
D · Prayer cases (11–12)
loud laughter in prayer · rubbing without a screen

12 ko 4 families mein yaad karo — list mein nahin.

The one word that decides: "flowing"

Blood/pus breaks wudu only when it flows — runs past where it came out.

A drop that just appears and stays put does not break it.

"Ablution is from all types of flowing blood."

— al-ʿAynī 1/262 (the Hanafi proof)

Behta khoon todta hai — sirf upar aaya khoon nahin.

Sleep: it's about posture, not sleep

Sleep itself is not impurity. The worry is that relaxed joints silently release wind.

BREAKS: lying on side / back / hip · buttocks not firm
SAFE: seated upright · light doze (you hear voices) · sunna prayer-posture

Karwat/peeth par neend = toot-ta; seedhe baithe, chuthad jamey = qaaim.

The two prayer cases

① Loud laughter in a prayer with bowing & prostration breaks both the prayer and the wudu.

Smiling does NOT affect either. In a funeral prayer / recital prostration, loud laughter voids only the prayer, not the wudu.

② Rubbing without a screen — the book notes the act is forbidden; it is addressed only for legal completeness.

Buland qahqaha namaz mein — namaz bhi wudu bhi gaya (muskurana nahin).

Rule 3 · What does NOT break wudu

The good-news half — each is the safe twin of a breaker.

Does NOT break itTwin
Blood that does not flow↔ flowing blood
Phlegm (pure) — even a lot↔ a mouthful of vomit
Touching one's own body / a woman↔ (Hanafi school)
Firm / seated / sunna-posture sleep↔ relaxed sleep
Worm from ear/nose/wound↔ only the two passages

Aurat ko aur apne aap ko chhoona wudu nahin todta.

Five principles that write the list

  • Blood breaks it only when it flows
  • Vomit only at a mouthful — never phlegm
  • Sleep only when the posture relaxes the joints
  • Touching one's own body or a woman never breaks it
  • Exits break it only from the two passages

A'ishah ؓ: "The Prophet ﷺ would fondle and kiss some of his wives, then pray without making wudu."

— at-Tirmidhī & an-Nasāʾī 165

⚠️ Don't re-do wudu over a "maybe"

Certainty is not lifted by doubt.

A wudu you know you had is not broken by a maybe.

Ask: did the blood actually flow? was it a full mouthful? were my joints actually relaxed? If "no / not sure" → your wudu stands.

Yaqeen shak se nahin jaata — baar baar wudu mat dohraao (yeh waswasa hai).
