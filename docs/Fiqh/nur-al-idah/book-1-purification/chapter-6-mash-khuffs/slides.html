Book I — Chapter 6: Mash on the Khuffs

Wiping over leather socks · Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (Hanafi)

Paaon dhone ka badal — mozon par mash: kab, kitni der, kya, kaise, aur kab khatam

Press → to begin · Press F for fullscreen

In wudū, washing the feet was a pillar

But peeling off wet socks five times a day is a real hardship.

Today: a concession (rukhṣa) — wipe over your leather socks instead.

So firmly established (by mass transmission) that Abū Ḥanīfa feared denying it is near-disbelief — "the heart of the sunna."

Yeh deen ki aasaani hai — magar shart aur waqt ke saath.

Three keys to the whole chapter

1 · Rukhṣa
a concession — replaces washing the feet; fenced with conditions + a clock
2 · The clock
24h resident / 72h traveller — starts at the first ḥadath after wearing
3 · Two wipings
khuff = timed; jabīra (bandage) = no clock (till the wound heals)

Learn these first — almost every ruling turns on one of them.

Teen chaabiyaan — inse poora chapter khud khul jaata hai.

Rule 1 · The basic ruling

Who
both men & women
Which state
MINOR impurity only (wudū) — NOT janāba (then: ghusl)
Material
leather or thick non-leather — must hold without tying & block water

The concession is for wudū only — never for major impurity.

Mard o aurat; sirf chhoti na-paaki; janāba mein nahin (ghusl karo).

Rule 2 · The 7 conditions

How you wear them
1 · put on in a complete wudū (feet washed)
What the sock must be
2 · cover the ankles · 3 · walkable · 4 · no tear ≥ 3 small toes · 5 · holds without tying · 6 · blocks water · 7 · front of foot present

Kaise pehno (wudū mein) + kaisa moza (takhna-dhaak, chalne-laayak, surākh-se-khaali, bina-baandhe, paani-rok, agla-hissa).

About tears

A tear ruins it only if it is ≥ three of the smallest toes. Smaller tears are fine.

Tears on one sock add up · tears on two socks are not combined.

Chhote surākh maaf. Ek moze ke judte hain, do mozon ke nahin.

Rule 3 · The clock

Resident
24 hours (one day & night)
Traveller
72 hours (three days & nights)

The clock starts at the FIRST ḥadath AFTER wearing — not at wearing.

Wudū 8 am → socks on → wudū breaks 12 pm → clock starts 12 pm.

Ghadi pehan-ne se nahin — pehli baar wudū tootne se.

Changing your status

Resident → traveller
complete the traveller's term (72h)
Traveller → resident
already wiped 24h → over (remove); not yet → complete 24h

Muqeem→musafir = 72 poore; musafir→muqeem = 24 mein khatam.

Rule 4 · What & how to wipe

The farḍ
area of 3 smallest fingers, on the top of each foot, once — not beneath/back/side, no repeat
The sunna
from the toes up to the shin, fingers spread (use the hand)

The Prophet ﷺ: "Verily, my Lord ordered me to wipe like this" — from the front of the khuf toward the leg, once, fingers spread.

— Tabarani 2/30

Farḍ: upar, teen ungli, ek baar. Sunnat: ungliyon se pinḍli tak, khuli ungliyaan.

Rule 5 · What ends it — the 4 nullifiers

#NullifierWhat you do
1anything that breaks wudūredo wudū + wipe
2removing the sockjust wash the feet
3majority of a foot gets wetjust wash the feet
4the period expires (24h/72h)just wash the feet

2, 3, 4 → only wash the feet (wudū is intact). Only #1 needs a fresh wudū.

Aakhri teen → sirf paaon dho lo. Sirf #1 mein naya wudū.

Rule 6 · What you may NOT wipe on

✗ Turban
water may not reach the head → the required quarter is missed
✗ Cap · veil · gloves
no hardship in removing them → no concession

No hardship → no concession. The head/hands are done directly.

Pagdi/topi/dupatta/dastaane par nahin — inka utaarna mushkil nahin.

The other wiping — the bandage (jabīra)

 Khuff (sock)Jabīra (bandage)
Time limit24h / 72hNone — till the wound heals
Applied in wudū?YesNo
Wipe one, wash other?NoYes
Falls off?wiping endswiping stays

Cannot wash/wipe an injury → wipe the majority of the bandage (wājib).

Patti: koi waqt nahin, gir jaaye to nahin tootta — khuff iske ulat.

Recap

The khuff:
worn in wudū · 7 conditions · clock 24h/72h (from the first ḥadath) · wipe 3 fingers on top, once · 4 nullifiers (last 3 → wash feet)
The limits & the mirror:
minor impurity only · not on turban/cap/veil/gloves · the jabīra = no clock, till the wound heals

Rukhṣa with a fence: ease, but conditions + a clock.

Khuff = waqt-waali aasaani; jabīra = zaroorat-waali (koi waqt nahin).
