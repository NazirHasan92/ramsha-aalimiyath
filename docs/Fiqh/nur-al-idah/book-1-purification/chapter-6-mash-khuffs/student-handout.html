Chapter 6 — Mash on the Khuffs (wiping over leather socks) · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · When you may wipe your socks, for how long, what exactly to wipe, and what ends it

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

In wudū, washing the feet to the ankles is a pillar. But peeling off wet socks five times a day is a real hardship — so the Sharīʿah gives an ease (rukhṣa): under conditions, you may wipe over your leather socks (khuffs) instead of washing the feet, for a fixed time. The book then teaches a second wiping — over a splint or bandage (jabīra) — which works on the opposite rules. Today we answer:

  1. The basic ruling — who, which impurity, what material.
  2. When am I allowed? — the 7 conditions the socks must meet.
  3. For how long? — the clock (24h / 72h) and when it starts.
  4. What & how do I wipe? — the farḍ and the sunna method.
  5. What ends it? — the 4 nullifiers.
  6. The other wiping — the bandage (jabīra), with no time limit.

Urdu mein: Wudū mein paaon dhona farḍ tha. Mozay baar baar utaarna mushkil → rukhṣa: charmay mozon par mash, ek muddat tak. Phir doosra mash — patti/jabīra par — jis ke usool ulte hain.

Source: everything here comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text), the Chapter on Wiping the Footgear (book pp. 86–94). No outside sources have been added.

The three keys to the whole chapter

Before the lists, learn these three. Almost every ruling turns on one of them:

KeyWhat it means
1 · Rukhṣa (a concession)Wiping the socks replaces washing the feet — an ease for a real hardship. It is firmly established (the sunna, by mass transmission), but it comes with conditions and a time limit.
2 · The clock24 hours for a resident, 72 hours for a traveller — and the clock starts at the first time wudū breaks after you put the socks on (not when you wear them).
3 · Two wipings, opposite timersThe khuff is timed (wear it in wudū, 24/72h). The jabīra (bandage) has no clock — it lasts until the wound heals.

Urdu mein: Teen chaabiyaan: (1) rukhṣa — paaon dhone ka badal, shart+waqt ke saath; (2) ghadi — 24/72 ghante, pehli ḥadath se shuru; (3) do mash — khuff waqt-waala, jabīra koi waqt nahin.

Rule 1 · The basic ruling — who, which impurity, what material

QuestionThe answer
Who?Both men and women.
Which impurity?Minor impurity only (wudū). Not major impurity — in janāba you must remove the socks and make ghusl.
What material?Leather and thick non-leather socks (e.g. thick cloth/cotton) — provided they hold to the feet without being tied and do not let water reach the skin.

Urdu mein: Mard o aurat dono; sirf chhoti na-paaki (wudū) ke liye — janāba mein nahin (ghusl karo). Charmay + mota kapra bhi, agar bina baandhe tikay aur paani khaal tak na pahunche.

Rule 2 · When you are allowed — the 7 conditions

Before wiping is even an option, the socks must meet seven conditions. Think of them in two groups:

GroupThe conditions
How you wear them1. Put them on after washing the feet, in a complete wudū. (If wudū breaks before you finish it, the wiping is ruined — redo it.)
What the sock must be2. Cover the feet up to and including the ankles. 3. Be walkable (not glass/wood/steel). 4. Free from a tear the size of three of the smallest toes or more (smaller tears are fine). 5. Hold to the feet without being tied. 6. Block water from reaching the skin. 7. The front of the foot must be present (≥ three smallest fingers' width).

⚠️ About tears

Several small tears on one sock are added together. But tears on two different socks are not combined — each sock is judged on its own.

Urdu mein: Kaise pehno: wudū mein (paaon dho kar). Kaisa moza: takhne dhaake, chalne-laayak, teen chhoti ungliyon jitne bade surākh se khaali, bina baandhe tikay, paani-rok, agla hissa maujood.

Rule 3 · For how long — the clock

TopicThe rule
How longResident: 24 hours (one day & night). Traveller: 72 hours (three days & nights).
When it startsAt the first time wudū breaks after wearing — not when you put the socks on. (Wudū at 8 am, socks on, wudū breaks at 12 pm → the clock starts at 12 pm.)
Resident → travellerComplete the traveller's term (72 hours).
Traveller → residentIf you have already wiped 24 hours → the wiping is over (remove the socks). If not → complete the 24-hour term.

💡 The clock does NOT start at wearing

The hours you spend in wudū after putting the socks on are free. The clock only begins ticking when your wudū first breaks.

Urdu mein: Muqeem 24, musafir 72 ghante. Ghadi pehan-ne se nahin — pehli baar wudū tootne se. Muqeem→musafir = 72 poore; musafir→muqeem = 24 mein khatam.

Rule 4 · What & how to wipe

PartThe rule
The farḍ (must)Wipe an area the size of three smallest fingers on the top of each foot — once. Not beneath, back, or side; and you do not repeat it.
The sunna (method)Begin from the toes and move up toward the shin, with the fingers spread. Using the hand is the sunna (but if the area gets wet with a cloth or poured water, it counts).

The Prophet ﷺ said, taking a man's hand, "Verily, my Lord ordered me to wipe like this" — from the front of the sock toward the leg, once, fingers spread. (Tabarani 2/30)

Urdu mein: Farḍ: har paaon ke upar teen chhoti ungliyon jitna — ek baar, sirf upar (neeche/peeche nahin). Sunnat: ungliyon ke siron se pinḍli ki taraf, ungliyaan khuli.

Rule 5 · What ends the wiping — the 4 nullifiers

#The nullifierWhat you do
1Anything that breaks wudū (wind, etc.)Make wudū again and wipe (no need to remove the socks)
2Removing the sock (even most of the foot bare)Just wash the feet (remove both, wash both)
3The majority of a foot becomes wet (over half)Just wash the feet
4The period expires (24h / 72h)Just wash the feet

⚠️ The key point

Nullifiers 2, 3, and 4 need only the feet washed — your wudū is still intact. Only nullifier 1 (a real ḥadath) needs a fresh wudū. (And if the period expires while you are praying, the prayer is void.)

Urdu mein: 4 naaqis: (1) jo wudū tore → dobara wudū; (2) moza utaarna; (3) aadhe se zyada paaon geela; (4) muddat khatam. Aakhri teen → sirf paaon dho lo (naya wudū nahin).

Rule 6 · What you may NOT wipe on — and the other wiping (the bandage)

You may not wipe on: the turban, the cap, the veil/headscarf, or gloves — because there is no hardship in taking these off (so no concession). The head and hands are wiped/washed directly.

But there is a second wiping the Sharīʿah allows — over a splint or bandage (jabīra) on an injury — and it works on the opposite rules to the sock:

 Khuff (sock)Jabīra (bandage)
Time limitYes — 24h / 72hNone — until the wound heals
Applied in wudū?YesNo — not required
Wipe one foot, wash the other?Not allowedAllowed
If it falls off (before healing)?Wiping endsWiping stays valid

When you cannot wash or wipe an injured limb, it is necessary to wipe the majority of the bandage over it. (Same logic for a medicine on an infected eye or a broken nail the doctor says not to wash — wipe over it; if even wiping harms, omit it.)

Urdu mein: Pagdi, topi, dupatta, dastaane par mash nahin (inka utaarna mushkil nahin). Patti (jabīra) par mash: koi waqt nahin, wudū mein baandhna shart nahin, gir jaaye to nahin tootta — khuff iske ulat.
