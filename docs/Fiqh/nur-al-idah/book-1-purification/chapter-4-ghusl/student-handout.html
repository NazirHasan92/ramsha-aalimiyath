Chapter 4 — Ghusl (the major bath) · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · When ghusl is obligatory, how to do it, and when it is sunna

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

You already know wudū — the minor purification, for four limbs. Today is its bigger sibling: ghusl, the major bath, which washes the whole body. Ghusl lifts major impurity — the state of janāba (after sexual discharge or intercourse) and, for women, the state after ḥayḍ (menstruation) and nifās (postnatal bleeding). We answer four questions:

  1. When must I make ghusl? — the causes that make it obligatory.
  2. What looks like it obligates ghusl but doesn't? — the 10 that do not (the mirror).
  3. How do I make a valid ghusl? — the 3 farḍ to wash.
  4. How is the complete ghusl done, and when is ghusl beautiful anyway? — the 12 sunnas + the sunna/recommended occasions.

Urdu mein: Wudū chhoti paaki thi — aaj badi paaki, ghusl: poora badan. Char sawaal: (1) kab farḍ, (2) kis se nahin hota, (3) kaise theek hota (3 farḍ), (4) kaamil tareeqa aur kab sunnat/mustahab.

Source: everything here comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text), the Chapter on Ghusl (book pp. 64–73). No outside sources have been added.

The three keys to the whole chapter

Before the lists, learn these three. Almost every ruling turns on one of them:

KeyWhat it means
1 · With desireSperm makes ghusl obligatory only when it leaves the body with sexual arousal. (Struck on the back so sperm dislodges without desire → no ghusl.)
2 · The head disappearsWhen the head of the penis disappears into a passage (penetration), ghusl is obligatory on both — even without ejaculation.
3 · Sperm, not the look-alikesOnly manī (sperm) obligates ghusl. Madhī and wadī are minor impurity → wash + wudū, no ghusl.

Rule 1 · When ghusl is obligatory — the causes

The book lists the causes 1–7 (6 & 7 are the women's causes). They fall into three groups:

GroupThe causes
A · Sperm with desire1 Sperm with arousal (wet dream / fantasy — both sexes) · 3 through intercourse with a corpse · 4 waking to a thin liquid with no remembered dream (only if not erect before sleeping) · 5 wetness after fainting/intoxication believed to be sperm
B · Penetration2 The head of the penis disappears in a passage → ghusl on both, even without ejaculation
C · Bleeding ends6 End of menstruation (ḥayḍ) · 7 End of postnatal bleeding (nifās)

Two more rules the book adds here: a person in major impurity before Islam must do ghusl after embracing it; and washing a dead Muslim is a communal obligation (farḍ kifāya).

⚠️ Two independent triggers — you don't need both

Ghusl becomes obligatory on either (a) sperm with desire OR (b) penetration to the head. So intimacy with penetration but no climax → ghusl is still obligatory on both.

Urdu mein: Teen group: A manī shahwat ke saath, B supaari chhup jaaye (dukhool — bina inzaal bhi dono par), C ḥayḍ/nifās khatam. Do sabab alag hain — ek bhi ho to ghusl farḍ.

Rule 2 · What does NOT obligate ghusl — the mirror (10)

This is the reassurance half. Each one fails a key — no sperm / no desire / no real penetration.

2Wadī (thick fluid after urinating)not sperm

Rule 3 · How to make a valid ghusl — the 3 farḍ

The book lists three things compulsory to wash in the major bath:

#FarḍNote
1Rinse the mouth (maḍmaḍa)farḍ in ghusl (only sunna in wudū)
2Rinse the nostrils (istinshāq)farḍ in ghusl (only sunna in wudū)
3Wash the entire body onceno dry patch anywhere

The proof is al-Māʾidah 6: "If you are in a state of ceremonial impurity, bathe your whole body." Because the verse names the whole body, the mouth and nose are farḍ here — unlike wudū.

⚠️ "Whole body" = reach every spot

Water must reach the inner navel, an open piercing/hole, the skin under the beard/moustache/eyebrows, and the inner foreskin (if it opens without difficulty). The braids rule: a man must undo his braids (water reaching the root while tied is not enough); a woman need not, provided water reaches the roots (Umm Salama: "throw three handfuls over it" — Abū Dāwūd 251).

Urdu mein: 3 farḍ: kulli, naak mein paani, poora badan ek baar (al-Māʾidah 6). Koi jagah sookhi na rahe. Mard choti khole; aurat ko zaroori nahin agar jad tak paani pahunche.

Rule 4 · The complete ghusl & when it is beautiful

A valid ghusl needs only the 3 farḍ. The complete, Prophetic ghusl has 12 sunnas — the method of the Maimūna hadith (Muslim 316 & 317), done in this order:

  1. Bismillah + the intention (niyyah)
  2. Wash the hands to the wrists
  3. Wash off any impurity on the body
  4. Wash the private parts
  5. Perform a wudū like the wudū for prayer (delay the feet if water gathers)
  6. Pour water over the whole body three times — head first, then right shoulder, then left
  7. Rub the body (dalk) and wash successively without pausing (muwālāt)

💡 The etiquettes — like wudū, with 3 differences

(1) Do not face the qibla (one is unclothed). (2) Bathe concealed, where no one sees you. (3) It is disliked to speak — even du'ā — because the body is exposed (the opposite of wudū).

When ghusl is SUNNA — 4 occasions

The Friday prayer · the two Eids · entering iḥrām (ḥajj/ʿumrah) · the pilgrim bathing at ʿArafah after the meridian.

When ghusl is RECOMMENDED — 16, in 4 baskets

BasketExamples from the book
A · Life-state changeAccepting Islam · reaching maturity (max 15) · recovering from insanity/fainting
B · After blood / the deadAfter ḥijāma (cupping) · after washing a corpse
C · Sacred nights & placesNight of Barā'ah · night of Qadr · entering the Madina · Muzdalifa at dawn · entering Makkah for ṭawāf
D · Special prayers & statesEclipse prayers · seeking rain · prayers of fear / darkness / strong wind · repentance · return from travel

Urdu mein: Kaamil ghusl = 12 sunan (Maimūna tareeqa). Ādāb wudū jaise, magar 3 farq (qibla nahin · chhup kar · baat-cheet makrūh). Sunnat 4 (Jumu'ah, Eid, iḥrām, ʿArafah); mustahab 16 (4 tokriyon mein).
