Rule 1 · When ghusl is obligatory

A · Sperm with desire
wet dream · waking wet (no dream) · after fainting
B · Penetration
the head disappears → both parties, even w/o ejaculation
C · Bleeding ends

Teen group: manī shahwat se · dukhool · ḥayḍ/nifās khatam.

Two independent triggers

Ghusl is farḍ on EITHER:

① sperm with desire   OR   ② penetration to the head

You do not need both — intimacy with penetration but no climax → still farḍ.

"When the private parts [of the male and female] cohabit with each other and the penis disappears in the vagina, bathing becomes obligatory [for both the male and female]."

— Ibn Mājah 611

Rule 3 · A valid ghusl — the 3 farḍ

1 · Mouth
maḍmaḍa (rinse the mouth)
2 · Nose
istinshāq (rinse the nostrils)
3 · Whole body
once — no dry patch

"If you are in a state of ceremonial impurity, bath your whole body."

— al-Māʾidah 6

Mouth & nose are farḍ in ghusl (only sunna in wudū) — the verse names the whole body.

3 farḍ: kulli, naak, poora badan. Muh-naak ghusl mein farḍ.

"Whole body" = reach every spot

No dry patch: navel · piercings · under beard/brows · inner foreskin · under rings.

MAN
must undo his braids (water to the root while tied is NOT enough)
WOMAN
need not undo braids if water reaches the roots

Umm Salama: "Should I undo my plaits to wash?" — "It is enough to throw three handfuls over it, then pour water all over your body."

— Abū Dāwūd 251

Mard choti khole; aurat ko zaroori nahin agar jad tak paani pahunche.

Rule 4 · The complete (sunna) ghusl

The 12 sunnas — the Maimūna method, in order:

  1. Bismillah + intention
  2. Wash the hands → wash off impurity → wash the private parts
  3. A wudū like the prayer wudū (delay the feet if water pools)
  4. Pour water over the whole body ×3 — head → right → left
  5. Rub the body (dalk) + wash without pausing (muwālāt)

Tarteeb: bismillah → haath → najaasat → sharmgaah → wudū → 3 baar paani → dalk.

Etiquettes — like wudū, with 3 differences

① No qibla
one is unclothed
② Conceal
bathe where no one sees you
③ No speaking
even du'ā is disliked (ʿawra exposed)

"God is Ever Concealed and loves modesty and concealment; so when one of you bathes, let him observe secrecy."

— Abū Dāwūd

Ādāb wudū jaise, magar 3 farq: qibla nahin · chhup kar · baat-cheet makrūh.

When ghusl is SUNNA — 4 occasions

Friday prayer — "the greatest of days"
The two Eids (the Prophet ﷺ bathed on the day of Fiṭr, Aḍḥā & ʿArafah)
Iḥrām — for ḥajj & ʿumrah
ʿArafah — the pilgrim, after the meridian

Even when perfectly pure — purity is a state you step into.

Sunnat 4: Jumu'ah, do Eid, iḥrām, ʿArafah.

When ghusl is RECOMMENDED — 16, in 4 baskets

A · Life-state
Islam · maturity · recovery from insanity/fainting
B · Blood / the dead
after ḥijāma · after washing a corpse
C · Sacred nights/places
Barā'ah · Qadr · Madina · Muzdalifa · Makkah ṭawāf
D · Special prayers/states
eclipse · rain · fear · darkness · wind · repentance · travel

The Prophet ﷺ would make ghusl from four: janāba, the Friday prayer, ḥijāma, and washing the deceased.

— Abū Dāwūd 348

Mustahab 16 ko 4 tokriyon mein yaad karo.

Recap

NOT when:
