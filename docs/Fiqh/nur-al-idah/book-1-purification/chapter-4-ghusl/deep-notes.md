# Chapter 4 — Deep Notes · Ghusl (the major bath)

**Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · Hasan Shurunbulali (Hanafi)**
*This file is Ramsha's personal learning material. Read **Part A** first to understand the chapter yourself, then **Part B** when you're ready to teach it to your classmates.*

*Reading guide: technical explanations are in English; key takeaways and counter-strategies are flagged with **Yaad rakho:** boxes in Roman Urdu. Skim the Urdu boxes for a fast revision pass.*

*Source constraint: every ruling, hadith, and verse below is drawn **only** from Nur al-Idah (book pp. 64–73). Nothing has been imported from outside fiqh sources or general knowledge.*

> **Where this sits in Book I.** Chapters 3a–3b covered **wudū** — the *minor* purification, which lifts *minor* impurity (ḥadath aṣghar). This chapter is its bigger sibling: **ghusl**, the *major* bath, which lifts *major* impurity — **janāba** (the state after sexual discharge or intercourse) and, for women, the state after **ḥayḍ** (menstruation) and **nifās** (postnatal bleeding). Wudū washes four limbs; ghusl washes the **whole body**. Teach this only once the cohort is solid on wudū — ghusl re-uses wudū inside it (one of its sunnas is "do a wudū like the wudū for prayer").

---

# Part A · Understanding the chapter

In the wudū chapters you learned the *minor* purification. This chapter is about the *major* one. The whole chapter answers **four plain questions**, in order:

| § | The question | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **When do I *have* to make ghusl?** | The causes that **obligate** the major bath (the book lists them 1–7) |
| 2 | **What looks like it should obligate ghusl but doesn't?** | The **10 things** that do **not** obligate it — the *mirror* of §1 |
| 3 | **How do I make a *valid* ghusl?** | The **3 farḍ** to wash (mouth · nose · whole body) + reaching every spot |
| 4 | **How do I make the *complete, Prophetic* ghusl, and when is ghusl beautiful even without being obligatory?** | The **12 sunnas** + the etiquettes + the **4 sunna occasions** + the **16 recommended occasions** |

Before the lists, grab **three master-keys**. Almost every ruling in §1 and §2 turns on one of them:

1. **"With desire" (bi-shahwa).** Sperm obligates ghusl **only when it leaves the body with sexual arousal** — a wet dream, fantasy, intimacy. If sperm comes out *without* desire (e.g. a man is struck on the back and it dislodges), there is **no ghusl** in the Ḥanafī school. *(This is the Ḥanafī–Shāfiʿī split: Shāfiʿī says any seminal emergence obligates ghusl — "water is from water"; the Ḥanafīs read that hadith as "ejaculation by way of desire.")*
2. **"The two parts meet" (the disappearance rule).** When the head of the penis **disappears** into a passage (vagina or anus), ghusl becomes obligatory on **both** parties — **even if no one ejaculates**. Penetration-to-the-head is its own cause, independent of discharge.
3. **Sperm (manī) vs the look-alikes (madhī / wadī).** Only **sperm** obligates ghusl. The two fluids people confuse it with — **madhī** (thin sticky pre-fluid from arousal) and **wadī** (thick cloudy fluid after urinating) — are *minor* impurity: they need washing + wudū, **not** ghusl. Telling these three apart is half of §2.

**Yaad rakho:** Ghusl = *badi* paaki (wudū *chhoti* paaki thi). Teen chaabiyaan: (1) **shahwat ke saath** — manī ghusl tab farḍ karti hai jab khwahish ke saath nikle (bina khwahish, jaise peeth par chot se, to ghusl nahin); (2) **do sharmgaahon ka milna / supaari ka chhup jaana** — dukhool ho jaaye to bina inzaal ke bhi ghusl farḍ; (3) **manī vs madhī/wadī** — sirf manī ghusl maangti hai, baaqi do sirf wudū/dhona maangti hain. In teen chaabiyon se aadha chapter khul jaata hai.

## 1 · When ghusl becomes obligatory (al-mūjibāt — the causes)

The book opens: *"The major bath is compulsory if one of the following occurs."* It then lists the causes **1 through 7** (where 6 and 7 are the two women's causes). Don't teach them as seven loose items — they fall into **three groups**.

### Group A — Sperm leaving the body *with desire* (#1, #3, #4, #5)

- **#1 · Discharge of sperm to the surface with sexual arousal.** It must *"separate from its location due to sexual arousal."* This can be from a **wet dream** or fantasising, and applies to **both men and women**. The book's sharp test-case: *"If one is struck in the back and consequently discharges sperm, he is not required to perform the major bath because it was not due to sexual arousal."* → **no desire, no ghusl** (master-key 1).
  - *A subtle Ḥanafī point worth understanding (don't teach it):* the desire must be present **when the sperm separates from its place** — not necessarily when it exits the body. So if a man feels desire, holds/squeezes until the desire fades, and the sperm comes out *later*, Abū Ḥanīfa and Muḥammad still say **ghusl is due** (the separation already happened with desire). Abū Yūsuf disagreed — and *"the legal ruling (fatwā) is in accordance with Abū Yūsuf's view"* (i.e. it must exit with desire). This is a fine madhhab detail; you understand it, the cohort does not need it.
- **#3 · Sperm discharged through intercourse with a corpse** obligates ghusl (the desire was present). *The book itself notes: such acts are unlawful and offensive and rarely occur — "it is the duty of a jurist to provide a solution" should the scenario arise.* State it at that level; do not dwell.
- **#4 · Waking to find a thin liquid, unable to recall a dream** → ghusl is obligatory — **but only if the penis was *not* erect before sleeping.** If it *was* erect beforehand, the fluid is in all likelihood **madhī**, and madhī does **not** require ghusl. *(This is master-key 3 hiding inside a cause: distinguish sperm from madhī.)*
- **#5 · Finding wetness after regaining consciousness** (after fainting or recovering from intoxication) **and believing it to be sperm** → ghusl obligatory.

### Group B — Penetration: the head disappears (#2)

- **#2 · The head of the penis disappearing in a passage** — vagina or anus — *"makes the major bath compulsory on both persons"* (the one penetrating and the one penetrated), **even without ejaculation.** The measure is *"the size of the head from the section removed in circumcision"* (i.e. the head of a living man's penis). The proof is the hadith: *"When the private parts [of male and female] cohabit with each other and the penis disappears in the vagina, bathing becomes obligatory on both the male and female."* (Ibn Mājah 611). This is master-key 2 — **penetration is its own trigger.**

### Group C — The end of a woman's bleeding (#6–7)

- **#6 · When a woman's menstrual period (ḥayḍ) ends.**
- **#7 · When her postnatal bleeding (nifās) ends.**
- For both, the major bath becomes **compulsory** before she can pray, fast, etc. *(The detailed rules of ḥayḍ and nifās themselves are **Chapter 7** — here, only the fact that their ending obligates ghusl.)*

### Two appended rules the book adds here

- **Pre-Islam janāba carries over.** If someone was in a state of major impurity (janāba, menses, etc.) **before** accepting Islam, they must still perform ghusl **after** embracing Islam — *"this is the correct view"* — because the trait of major impurity remains, and one cannot meet the conditions of prayer / touching the Qur'an without it. *But* if a person becomes Muslim while **not** in major impurity, ghusl is merely **recommended** on conversion (that reappears in §4's recommended list).
- **Washing a dead Muslim is farḍ kifāya.** *"It is a communal obligation (farḍ kifāya) to bathe a deceased Muslim. If no one performs this task, the sin falls upon the entire community."* (Note: this is ghusl performed *on* the deceased, not a cause of janāba — the book places it here as a related obligatory bath.)

**Yaad rakho:** Causes (book mein 1–7) ko **teen group** mein baant lo. **A — manī shahwat ke saath nikle** (#1 khwaab/khwahish; #3 murda ke saath — kitaab khud kehti hai aisa kaam haraam o naadir hai; #4 jaag kar geela paaye magar sone se pehle inteshaar na ho [warna madhī]; #5 be-hoshi/nashe ke baad geela paaye aur manī samjhe). **B — dukhool: supaari chhup jaaye** (#2) — bina inzaal ke bhi dono par ghusl (Ibn Mājah 611). **C — aurat ki bleeding khatam** (#6 ḥayḍ, #7 nifās). Do extra: Islam laane se pehle ki janāba bhi ghusl maangti hai; aur murde ko ghusl dena **farḍ kifāya** hai.

## 2 · What does NOT obligate ghusl (the ten) — the mirror

The book's heading: *"There are ten things a person is not required to perform the major bath from."* This is the **mirror** of §1 — and you should teach it exactly the way you taught the wudū non-nullifiers in 3b: as **twins**, each sitting just on the other side of a master-key line. Strip them down and *every one* fails one of the three keys: **no sperm / no desire / no real penetration-to-the-head.**

**The two look-alike fluids (key 3 — "it isn't sperm"):**

1. **Madhī** — *"a white, thin, sticky fluid released from a man's penis when he is aroused… long before orgasm,"* due to romantic play or kissing. (The female equivalent from amorous play is also termed madhī.) It is *minor* impurity → wash it + wudū, **no ghusl.**
2. **Wadī** — *"a white cloudy fluid that is thick and warm and has no smell, released generally after urinating."* Again minor impurity → **no ghusl.**

**The "no actual emission / no trace" cases (key 1 — "no sperm with desire"):**

3. **A sexual dream with no wet trace on waking** → **no ghusl** (nothing came out). *And women are the same:* the Prophet ﷺ was asked *"Does a woman need to perform ghusl [if she has a wet dream]?"* and replied *"Yes, if she dreams and sees the fluid."* (Bukhārī 282) — i.e. ghusl follows the **fluid**, not the dream.
4. **Giving birth without visible blood** does not obligate ghusl, since it is not classed as nifās (which requires actual blood). *Abū Ḥanīfa held that, as a precaution, she should perform ghusl, on the basis that a birth is rarely free of a little blood.*

**The "no real penetration / barrier / no ejaculation" cases (keys 1 & 2 — neither trigger is met):**

5. **Penetration through a cloth** that prevents the feeling of pleasure → **no ghusl** (but *recommended* as a precaution).
6. **Administering medicine into the anus** (e.g. a suppository) → no ghusl.
7. **Inserting a finger** (or the like) into the vagina or anus → no ghusl — *"given the deficiency of sexual desire."*
8–9. **Intercourse with an animal, or with a corpse, *without* ejaculation** → no ghusl (no emission; and the disappearance-trigger of §1 #2 is treated as not met here without discharge).
10. **A man penetrating a virgin without breaking her hymen and without ejaculating** → no ghusl. The book reasons: the intact virginity *"confirms that the circumcised parts did not meet"* in the way that would trigger ghusl — the hymen prevented the meeting of the private parts. *(This is the precise flip-side of master-key 2: if the head genuinely "disappeared / the parts met," ghusl is due — §1 #2; if the hymen shows they did **not** truly meet, it is not.)*

**Yaad rakho:** Yeh §1 ka **aaina** hai. Har "cheez" teen chaabiyon mein se ek par girti hai — **manī nahin / shahwat nahin / asl dukhool nahin.** Do mashhoor look-alike: **madhī** (geela, patla, chipchipa — josh ke waqt) aur **wadī** (gaadha, garam, bila-boo — peshaab ke baad) — dono *chhoti* napaaki, sirf dho lo + wudū, ghusl nahin. Khwaab dekha magar geela na paaya → ghusl nahin (mard aur aurat dono — "haan, jab woh paani dekhe", Bukhārī 282). Bila-khoon wiladat (Abū Ḥanīfa: ihtiyaatan ghusl). Aur bina inzaal: kapde ki aad, suppository, ungli, jaanwar/murda, kunwaari jiska parda saalim — sab mein ghusl nahin.

## 3 · How to make a *valid* ghusl — the 3 farḍ to wash

This is the heart of the practical chapter. The book heads it *"That Which Is Compulsory To Wash"* and states: *"It is compulsory to wash **three** things during the major bath."* That is the famous **three farḍ of ghusl**:

| # | Farḍ | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Rinse the mouth** (maḍmaḍa) | water reaches the whole mouth |
| 2 | **Rinse the nostrils** (istinshāq) | water reaches the soft part of the nose |
| 3 | **Wash the entire body once** | every washable part of the skin, one time |

The Qur'anic proof the book gives is **al-Māʾidah 6**: *"If you are in a state of ceremonial impurity, bathe your whole body."* The book notes this is *"a clear command requiring the washing of the entire body, which includes the nose and mouth"* — which is **why the mouth and nose are farḍ in ghusl** even though they are only *sunna* in wudū. (Imām Shāfiʿī disagreed, holding mouth/nose to be sunna even in ghusl; the Ḥanafī answer: the wudū verse orders washing the *face*, which excludes the inside of the mouth/nose — but the ghusl verse orders washing the whole body, which includes them.)

### "Wash the entire body" means *reach every spot* — the obligatory thoroughness

Farḍ #3 is not vague — the book spells out the awkward spots water **must** reach (no skin left dry):

- **The inner part of the foreskin** (the area usually removed in circumcision) — *provided it can be opened without difficulty*; if there is difficulty, the requirement is lifted.
- **The inner part of the navel.**
- **An open piercing or hole** in the body that has not sealed (be gentle — don't cause yourself hardship).
- **The skin under the beard, moustache, and eyebrows**, and *"the immediate inner part of the outer lips of the vagina — not the internal area."*

### The braids rule — the one place men and women differ

- **A man must undo his braids** and wash inside them *every* time he makes ghusl — and even if water reaches the root **while the hair is tied, it is not valid**; he must undo them. *"A man does not receive a dispensation based on beautification, although the woman does."*
- **A woman need NOT undo her braids**, *provided the water reaches the roots* — by agreement. The proof is the Umm Salama hadith: she asked, *"I am a woman who keeps my hair closely plaited. Should I undo it when I wash after sexual relations?"* The Prophet ﷺ replied, *"It is enough for you to throw three handfuls over it, then pour water all over your body and you will be purified."* (Abū Dāwūd 251) — the dispensation is because undoing and redoing the hair is a real hardship for her.

**Yaad rakho:** Ghusl ke **3 farḍ**: (1) **kulli** (muh dhona), (2) **naak mein paani** (narm jagah tak), (3) **poora jism ek baar**. Daleel: al-Māʾidah 6 — "agar janābat mein ho to poora badan dho lo" — isi liye muh aur naak ghusl mein **farḍ** hain (wudū mein sirf sunnat the). "Poora jism" ka matlab: **har jagah paani pahunche** — khatne ki andaruni jagah, naaf ka andar, khula chhed/piercing, daadhi/bhanwon ke neeche ki khaal. **Choti ka mas'ala:** mard ko choti **kholna farḍ** hai (bandhi choti ki jad tak paani pahunch bhi jaaye to kaafi nahin); aurat ko **kholna zaroori nahin** agar jad tak paani pahunch jaaye (Umm Salama — "teen chullu daal lo", Abū Dāwūd 251).

## 4 · The complete ghusl, and when it is beautiful

Once the 3 farḍ are met, the ghusl is **valid**. But the **complete, Prophetic** ghusl has 12 sunnas; and beyond the *obligatory* ghusl there is a whole gradient of occasions where ghusl is **sunna** or **recommended** even when you are *not* in a state of impurity. This is the same "ladder beyond farḍ" you taught in wudū 3a.

### The 12 sunnas — the Prophetic method (sunan al-ghusl)

The book says *"Twelve things are sunna in the major bath."* They describe, in order, the **masnūn ghusl** — essentially the method of the Maimūna hadith (Muslim 311 & 316). Teach it as a **sequence**, not a numbered list:

1. **Bismillah (tasmiyya)** — *"In the name of God, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate."*
2. **The intention (niyyah)** — on the tongue (tasmiyya) and in the heart (niyyah) together. *"Just as a person is rewarded for an intention in wudū, the same applies to bathing."*
3. **Wash the hands** to the wrists first (Aʿishah: the Prophet ﷺ *"began with washing his hands"*).
4. **Wash off any impurity** isolated on the body — *before it dries*, so one is content it is gone.
5. **Wash the private parts** — even if there is no visible impurity.
6. **Perform a wudū** like the wudū for prayer — washing the wudū-limbs (with wiping of the head), based on the Maimūna hadith.
7. **Delay washing the feet** if bathing in a spot where water gathers (wash them last, stepping aside — as the Prophet ﷺ did).
8. **Pour water over the whole body three times** — or submerge in running / a large quantity of water (a short immersion fulfils the sunna).
9. **Begin** by pouring over the **head, then the right shoulder, then the left.**
10. **Rub the body** with the water — *dalk.*
11. **Wash successively** (muwālāt) — without pausing between the actions.

*(The book counts these as twelve sunnas; the prose names them as the connected sequence above — the takeaway for the cohort is the **method**, not a memorised numbering.)*

### The etiquettes (ādāb) and disliked acts

*"The etiquettes of the major bath are the same as that of wudū"* — **with two differences**:

1. **Do not face the qibla** during ghusl, *"because one is naked almost always."*
2. Bathe **where no one can see you.** The proof: *"God is Ever Living and Ever Concealed… and He likes those who acquire modesty and concealment; so when anyone of you wants to bathe, let him observe secrecy."* (Abū Dāwūd) — and if you must bathe among others (men with men, women with women), choose the least-exposed spot. *(The sin of exposure is on an onlooker who looks at another's uncovered ʿawra.)*
3. And whereas in wudū supplication is encouraged, **in ghusl it is disliked to speak — even du'ā** — because the private parts are exposed.

### When ghusl is *sunna* — 4 occasions

> *"It is sunna to perform the major bath for four occasions."*

1. **The Friday prayer** — *"the greatest of days is Friday."*
2. **The two Eid prayers** (the Prophet ﷺ would perform ghusl on the day of Fiṭr, Aḍḥā, and ʿArafah).
3. **For iḥrām** — the state of sanctification entered for ḥajj and ʿumrah.
4. **For the one performing ḥajj, to bathe at ʿArafah** after the meridian (when ẓuhr time enters) — specific to the pilgrim.

### When ghusl is *recommended* — 16 occasions

> *"It is recommended to perform the major bath on sixteen occasions."*

Don't read sixteen loose items in class — **group them**:

- **Life-state changes:** (1) **accepting Islam** while free of janāba; (2) reaching **maturity by age** (max 15 in the Ḥanafī madhhab — see the wudū conditions chapter); (3) **recovering from insanity or fainting.**
- **After contact with blood / the dead:** (4) after **ḥijāma** (cupping); (5) after **washing a dead body**. *(The Prophet ﷺ would make ghusl from four things: janāba, the Friday prayer, ḥijāma, and washing the deceased — Abū Dāwūd 348.)*
- **Sacred nights & places:** (6) the **night of Barā'ah** (mid-Shaʿbān); (7) the **night of Qadr** (if witnessed); (8) **entering the Madina** of the Prophet ﷺ; (9) standing at **Muzdalifa** at dawn on the day of Sacrifice (10th Dhū'l-Ḥijja); (10) **entering Makkah for ṭawāf az-Ziyārah** (and for ṭawāf in general).
- **Special prayers & states:** (11–12) the **solar and lunar eclipse** prayers; (13) **seeking rain** (istisqā'); (14–16) the prayer of **fear**, of **overwhelming darkness**, and at a **strong wind** (Allah destroyed nations such as ʿĀd with wind) — also when one has **repented** from sins, when **returning from travel**, and when struck with **hidden filth** (wash the whole body and clothes as a precaution).

**Yaad rakho:** Valid ghusl = 3 farḍ. *Kaamil* (sunnat) ghusl = **12 sunan** — Maimūna wali tarteeb: bismillah+niyyah → haath dhona → najaasat dhona → sharmgaah dhona → namaz jaisa wudū → (paani jamta ho to paaon baad mein) → poore badan par 3 baar paani (pehle sar, phir daayaan kandha, phir baayaan) → **dalk** (malna) + **muwālāt** (bila-rukey). **Ādāb wudū jaise**, magar do farq: (1) qibla ki taraf muh nahin (nange ho), (2) chhup kar nahalo, aur (3) baat-cheet — du'ā tak — makrūh. **Sunnat 4 mauqe:** Jumu'ah, do Eid, iḥrām, ʿArafah (haaji). **Mustahab 16 mauqe** — group karo: zindagi ki tabdeeli (Islam, buloogh, hosh), khoon/murda ke baad (ḥijāma, mayyit dhona), muqaddas raatein/jaghein (Barā'ah, Qadr, Madina, Muzdalifa, Makkah-ṭawāf), aur khaas namazein/haalaat (kusoof/khusoof, istisqā', khauf, andhera, tez hawa, tauba, safar se waapsi, chhupi najaasat).

## The big picture — what Chapter 4 is silently teaching

Step back and the whole chapter rests on a few ideas worth handing the cohort:

1. **Major vs minor impurity is a two-tier system.** Wudū answers minor ḥadath; ghusl answers *major* — janāba, ḥayḍ, nifās. Knowing *which tier* a situation falls into is the whole game: a drop of madhī is minor (wudū); sperm-with-desire is major (ghusl). *(Do napaaki ke darje hain — pehchaano kaunsa.)*
2. **The Sharīʿah triggers ghusl on a *cause*, precisely defined — not on a vague feeling.** "With desire," "the head disappears," "actual sperm." Precision is mercy: it tells you exactly when you must, and protects you from doing it needlessly. *(Yeh chapter bhi, wudū ki tarah, **usool** par chalti hai — waswase ka ilaaj.)*
3. **The look-alike fluids (madhī, wadī) are the great confusion — and the book pre-empts it.** Most real-life ghusl anxiety comes from not knowing *what fluid that was*. Teaching the three apart is the single most useful service you do the cohort.
4. **Even the obligatory bath has a beautiful form, and ghusl reaches beyond obligation** — into Friday, Eid, iḥrām, sacred nights. Purity, again, is shown as a *state a believer steps into*, not just a fix for impurity. *(Paaki sirf napaaki ka ilaaj nahin — ek haalat hai jisme momin dakhil hota hai.)*

**Yaad rakho:** Pura chapter ek baat par khada hai: **napaaki ke do darje** hain, aur Sharīʿat **usool** se batati hai kaunsa darja kab lagta hai. Madhī/wadī ko manī se alag karna sab se kaam ki cheez hai. Aur ghusl sirf farḍ nahin — Jumu'ah, Eid, iḥrām tak ek **khoobsurat aadat** hai.

# Part B · Teaching the chapter

You now understand Chapter 4. This section is how to **deliver** it to the same adult, mostly Urdu-speaking cohort — who already know wudū from 3a/3b. Ghusl is a more **sensitive** chapter than wudū (it deals with sexual discharge, intimacy, women's bleeding), so a large part of your job here is teaching **precise, unembarrassed fiqh in dignified language** — and clearing up folk-confusion about the fluids.

---

## A note on the cohort's starting point

Expect two things. First, this is an **adult, mixed, modest** audience: the subject matter is intimate. Your tone should be **clinical and calm** — the book's own tone. It states even the uncomfortable cases plainly *"because it is the duty of a jurist to provide a solution."* Borrow that posture: matter-of-fact, eyes-front, no giggling, no euphemism-overload. Second, almost everyone carries **fluid confusion** — they call any discharge "napaaki" and either over-do ghusl or skip it. The single most valuable thing you teach is **manī vs madhī vs wadī**.

**Yaad rakho:** Audience baaligh aur mukhtalit hai — mauzoo nazuk hai. Lehja **kitaab jaisa**: saaf, sanjeeda, bila-sharmindagi. Aur sab se badi uljhan: **manī / madhī / wadī** ka farq — yahi sabse zyada faida deta hai.

---

## The five landmines for Urdu-speaking adult learners

### Landmine 1 · Confusing the three fluids (manī / madhī / wadī)

This is *the* landmine. Many believe any wetness obligates ghusl; others can't tell which fluid needs what. The book is precise: only **manī** (sperm, leaving *with desire*) obligates ghusl. **Madhī** (thin, sticky, on arousal) and **wadī** (thick, cloudy, after urinating) are *minor* impurity → wash + wudū, **no ghusl.**

**Counter-strategy:** Put a **three-column table** on the board — manī / madhī / wadī — with *appearance · when it comes · what it needs.* Hammer: ghusl is **only** for manī-with-desire. Give the book's tell-tale for #4: if the penis was **erect before sleeping** and you wake wet with no remembered dream, it's likely **madhī** → no ghusl.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Teen paani alag hain. **Manī** — gaadhi, josh/shahwat ke saath nikalti hai → **ghusl farḍ**. **Madhī** — patli, chipchipi, sirf uksaane/bose se → sirf **dho lo aur wudū**, ghusl nahin. **Wadī** — gaadhi, dhundli, peshaab ke baad → wahi, **wudū**, ghusl nahin. Yaad rakho: ghusl sirf **manī** se, woh bhi jab **khwahish** ke saath nikle. Agar sone se pehle inteshaar tha aur jaag kar geela paaya bina khwaab yaad ke — to woh aksar **madhī** hai, ghusl nahin."*

### Landmine 2 · "Ghusl needs ejaculation" — the penetration rule

Many think ghusl is only triggered by ejaculation. The book is explicit: **the head of the penis disappearing into a passage obligates ghusl on both parties — even without ejaculation** (§1 #2; Ibn Mājah 611). Penetration is its *own* trigger.

**Counter-strategy:** State the two **independent** triggers clearly: (a) **sperm with desire**, OR (b) **penetration to the head** — *either one alone* makes ghusl farḍ. So a married couple who have intimacy with penetration but no climax — **ghusl is still farḍ on both.** Keep the language dignified and brief.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Ghusl ke do **alag** sabab hain — ek bhi ho jaaye to ghusl farḍ. (1) **Manī shahwat ke saath** nikle. (2) **Supaari (sharmgaah ka sira) chhup jaaye** — yaani dukhool ho jaaye — chaahe inzaal **na** bhi ho, dono (mard aur aurat) par ghusl farḍ ho jaata hai. Hadith: 'jab sharmgaahein mil jaayein aur supaari chhup jaaye, dono par ghusl farḍ' (Ibn Mājah 611). To sirf inzaal ka intezaar mat karo — dukhool hi kaafi hai."*

### Landmine 3 · The 3 farḍ — "I just poured water, that's enough"

Some pour water over the head and consider ghusl done, missing that **rinsing the mouth and nose are farḍ in ghusl** (unlike wudū, where they're sunna), and that **every spot of skin** must get wet — no dry patch.

**Counter-strategy:** Drill the **three farḍ** (mouth · nose · whole body) and the *"reach every spot"* principle. Name the easy-to-miss spots from the book: navel, under rings/jewellery, piercings, **and the braids rule** (men must undo; women needn't if water reaches the roots — Umm Salama, Abū Dāwūd 251). One dry patch = ghusl incomplete.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Ghusl ke teen farḍ: **kulli, naak mein paani, aur poora badan**. Dhyaan do — wudū mein kulli aur naak sunnat the, magar **ghusl mein farḍ** hain (al-Māʾidah 6: 'poora badan dho lo'). Aur 'poora badan' ka matlab: **ek baal/khaal bhi sookhi na rahe** — naaf, chhed, anguthi ke neeche, daadhi ke neeche. **Mardon ko choti kholni hai**; auraton ko nahin, agar jad tak paani pahunch jaaye (Umm Salama: 'teen chullu daal lo')."*

### Landmine 4 · Women's questions — hayd/nifas ending, and the braids

Women in the cohort will have real, practical questions: *when* exactly does ghusl become due after a period, and the hair-undoing burden. The book gives you the two anchors you need **for this chapter** — and tells you where the rest lives.

**Counter-strategy:** Answer only what this chapter answers: **the *ending* of ḥayḍ and nifās obligates ghusl** (§1 #6–7), and **a woman need not undo her braids** if water reaches the roots (§3, Umm Salama). For the detailed mechanics of menstruation — durations, the colours of blood, istiḥāḍa — say honestly: *"Yeh poori tafseel **Chapter 7** (Ḥayḍ o Nifās) mein aayegi."* Don't improvise it here.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Do baatein is chapter ki hain: (1) **ḥayḍ ya nifās khatam** hone par ghusl **farḍ** ho jaata hai — phir hi namaz/roza durust. (2) Aurat ko **choti kholna zaroori nahin** agar jad tak paani pahunch jaaye — Nabi ﷺ ne Umm Salama ko farmaaya 'teen chullu sar par daal lo, kaafi hai' (Abū Dāwūd 251). Baaqi tafseel — kitne din, kis rang ka khoon, istiḥāḍa — woh **Chapter 7** mein."*

### Landmine 5 · "Ghusl is only when you're impure"

Learners think ghusl is purely a fix for janāba. They miss the whole **sunna/recommended gradient** — that ghusl for **Friday, Eid, iḥrām** is a beloved act even when perfectly pure.

**Counter-strategy:** After the obligatory material, lift the mood: present the **4 sunna occasions** and a **grouped** version of the 16 recommended ones (life-changes · blood/dead · sacred nights & places · special prayers). Frame ghusl the way you framed wudū's mandūb in 3b — purity as a **state a believer steps into**, not just damage-control. Anchor with the hadith that the Prophet ﷺ made ghusl from four things: janāba, Friday, ḥijāma, washing the dead (Abū Dāwūd 348).

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Ghusl sirf napaaki ka ilaaj nahin. **Jumu'ah, dono Eid, iḥrām, aur ʿArafah** ke din ghusl **sunnat** hai — chaahe aap bilkul paak hon. Aur **16 mauqe** aise hain jahan ghusl **mustahab** hai: Islam laana/baaligh hona, ḥijāma/mayyit dhona ke baad, Shab-e-Barā'ah o Qadr, Madina/Makkah mein daakhil hona, kusoof/istisqā'/khauf ki namaz. Nabi ﷺ chaar cheezon se ghusl farmaate: janāba, Jumu'ah, ḥijāma, mayyit dhona (Abū Dāwūd 348). Yaani paaki ek **khoobsurat aadat** hai."*

## Suggested 75-minute class plan

*Class shuru karne se pehle:* board par do cheezein taiyaar rakho — (a) ek **teen-column fluid table** (manī / madhī / wadī), aur (b) ek **do-column aaina** (GHUSL FARḌ ↔ GHUSL NAHIN), bilkul 3b wale mirror ki tarah. Yeh dono poore chapter ka naqsha hain.

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–6 min | Bridge from wudū: *"wudū **chhoti** paaki thi — aaj **badi** paaki, ghusl. Wudū chaar a'za, ghusl **poora badan**."* Name janāba / ḥayḍ / nifās as the three states ghusl lifts |
| 6–12 min | Teach the **three master-keys** up front (with desire · the head disappears · sperm-not-madhī/wadī) — everything hangs on these |
| 12–18 min | **The fluid table** (Landmine 1) — manī vs madhī vs wadī. This is the highest-value 6 minutes of the lesson |
| 18–32 min | **§1 · When ghusl is obligatory** — 3 groups (sperm-with-desire · penetration #2 · ḥayḍ/nifās end) + the two appended rules. Land Landmine 2 (penetration ≠ needs ejaculation) |
| 32–42 min | **§2 · The mirror — what does NOT obligate ghusl** (10) — place each opposite its trigger; the look-alike fluids reappear here. *Good-news / reassurance half* |
| 42–55 min | **§3 · How to make a valid ghusl** — the **3 farḍ** (mouth · nose · whole body, al-Māʾidah 6) + reach-every-spot + the braids rule. Land Landmines 3 & 4 |
| 55–64 min | **§4 · The 12 sunnas** (the Maimūna method, as a sequence) + the **3 etiquette differences** (no qibla · conceal · no speaking) |
| 64–72 min | **The gradient** — 4 sunna occasions + 16 recommended (grouped). Landmine 5 — purity as a state |
| 72–75 min | Q&A · assign handout exercises · preview Chapter 5 (Tayammum — the dry purification when there's no water) |

**Yaad rakho:** Is chapter ke do board-tools: **fluid table** (manī/madhī/wadī) aur **aaina** (FARḌ ↔ NAHIN). Pehle **teen chaabiyaan** parhao, phir lists khud aasaan. Lehja sanjeeda rakho. Sabse zyada qeemti waqt fluid table ko do.

## What you should NOT teach in Chapter 4

- ❌ **The detailed fiqh of ḥayḍ & nifās** (durations, colours of blood, istiḥāḍa, the "deemed excused" category) — that is **Chapter 7**. Here, say only: *the **ending** of ḥayḍ/nifās obligates ghusl.* If asked for more: *"Chapter 7 mein tafseel se aayega."*
- ❌ **Tayammum** (dry purification) — it begins right after this in the book, but it is **Chapter 5**. Don't pre-teach it; just preview it as "next."
- ❌ **Graphic elaboration on the corpse/animal cases** (§1 #3, §2 #8–9). State them at the book's own level — *"the book notes these acts are forbidden and rare, and a jurist's duty is only to give the legal answer."* Do not dwell; move on quickly.
- ❌ **The Abū Ḥanīfa / Abū Yūsuf fine point** on *when* the desire must be present (separation vs exit) — you understand it (Part A), but it will confuse the cohort. Teach the clean rule: **sperm with desire → ghusl.**
- ❌ **Re-teaching wudū** — sunna #6 ("do a wudū like the prayer wudū") only *references* wudū; a one-line reminder is enough, don't re-run 3a.
- ❌ **Cross-madhhab uṣūl** beyond what's useful — you may mention *that* the Shāfiʿīs differ on mouth/nose in ghusl and on "water from water," but don't turn it into a comparative-fiqh lecture.

**Yaad rakho:** 4 sirf **ghusl** hai — kab farḍ, kya farḍ dhona, sunan, aur kab mustahab. **Ḥayḍ/nifās ki tafseel = Ch 7; Tayammum = Ch 5.** Nazuk cases ko kitaab ke level par bayaan kar ke aage baD jao.

## Self-check before teaching

Before class, make sure you can:

- [ ] State the **three master-keys** (with desire · the head disappears · sperm vs madhī/wadī) and explain how each decides cases.
- [ ] Tell the **three fluids apart** — appearance, when each comes, and what each needs (only manī-with-desire → ghusl).
- [ ] List the **causes that obligate ghusl** in their 3 groups, and quote **Ibn Mājah 611** for the penetration rule.
- [ ] State the **3 farḍ of ghusl** (mouth · nose · whole body) and quote **al-Māʾidah 6**; explain why mouth/nose are farḍ here but sunna in wudū.
- [ ] Give the **braids rule** correctly (man undoes; woman needn't if water reaches the roots — Umm Salama, Abū Dāwūd 251).
- [ ] Walk the **12 sunnas as the Maimūna sequence**, and name the **3 etiquette differences** from wudū (no qibla · conceal · no speaking).
- [ ] Recall the **4 sunna occasions** and a **grouped** version of the **16 recommended** ones.

If yes to all seven — *tum tayyar ho.*

## After class — diagnostic checklist

For each classmate, watch for:

1. **Can they tell the three fluids apart?** *(Manī / madhī / wadī — kaunsi ghusl maangti hai? Sirf manī-with-desire?)* This is the single test of whether the chapter landed.
2. **Do they know penetration alone (no ejaculation) obligates ghusl?** *(Dukhool ho jaaye to bina inzaal ke bhi ghusl farḍ — yeh samjhe?)*
3. **Can they name the 3 farḍ and the reach-every-spot rule?** *(Kulli, naak, poora badan — aur koi jagah sookhi na rahe?)*
4. **Do the women know the braids dispensation?** *(Aurat ko choti kholna zaroori nahin agar jad tak paani pahunche?)*
5. **Do they see ghusl as also a sunna/recommended act, not only a fix for impurity?** *(Jumu'ah/Eid/iḥrām ka ghusl — paaki ek aadat hai?)*

**Yaad rakho:** Agar 2+ classmates ab bhi teen fluids ko gad-mad karte hain, ya "ghusl sirf inzaal se" par atke hain — to Chapter 5 (Tayammum) se pehle 5-minute ka **fluid-table + aaina refresher** lo. Yeh chapter tab kaamyaab hai jab cohort **bila-sharmindagi, theek se** jaane ke kab ghusl farḍ hai aur kab nahin.
