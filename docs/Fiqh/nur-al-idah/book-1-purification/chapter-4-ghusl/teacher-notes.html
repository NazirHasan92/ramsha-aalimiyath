🤲 A sensitive chapter — keep the tone of the book

Ghusl deals with sexual discharge, intimacy, and women's bleeding before an adult, mixed audience. Teach it the way the book writes it: clinical, calm, dignified. The book itself addresses even uncomfortable cases "because it is the duty of a jurist to provide a solution." Borrow that posture — matter-of-fact, no euphemism-overload, no embarrassment.

Every ruling, every verse, every hadith below is drawn only from Nur al-Idah (book pp. 64–73). Nothing has been added from outside sources. ★ Master Overview — نَقْشَةُ الْبَاب

One bird's-eye map. Wudū (Ch 3) was the minor purification; ghusl is the major bath — it lifts janāba, ḥayḍ, and nifās and washes the whole body. The chapter answers four questions: when ghusl is obligatory (and what does not obligate it — a mirror), how to make it valid, and when it is beautiful even without being obligatory.

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the student will be able to:

  1. Distinguish minor impurity (lifted by wudū) from major impurity / janāba (lifted by ghusl), and name the states ghusl lifts (janāba, ḥayḍ, nifās).
  2. Apply the three master-keys — sperm with desire, the head disappearing, and sperm vs madhī/wadī — to decide whether ghusl is due.
  3. Tell the three fluids apart (manī · madhī · wadī) and state what each requires.
  4. List the causes that obligate ghusl (the book's 1–7, in 3 groups) and the 10 that do not as its mirror.
  5. State the 3 farḍ of ghusl (mouth · nose · whole body) with al-Māʾidah 6, the reach-every-spot rule, and the braids rule.
  6. Walk the 12 sunnas (the Maimūna method) and the 3 etiquette differences from wudū.
  7. Recall the 4 sunna occasions and a grouped version of the 16 recommended ones.

0 · From Minor (Wudū) to Major (Ghusl) — the 3 master-keys

Open by linking back, then hand the cohort the three keys that decide almost every ruling in §1 and §2 before any list.

Where this fits: the bridge. Wudū lifted minor impurity from four limbs; ghusl lifts major impurity from the whole body.

💡 The 60-second bridge

"Wudū chhoti paaki thi — chaar a'za. Aaj badi paaki: ghusl — poora badan. Ghusl teen haalaton ko uthaata hai: janāba (mubāsharat/inzaal ke baad), ḥayḍ, aur nifās." Don't re-teach wudū — one of ghusl's own sunnas re-uses it.

⚠️ Teach these THREE master-keys first — everything hangs on them

1 · "With desire" (bi-shahwa): sperm obligates ghusl only when it leaves with sexual arousal. Struck on the back → sperm dislodges with no desire → no ghusl.
2 · "The head disappears": when the head of the penis disappears into a passage (penetration), ghusl is farḍ on both parties — even without ejaculation.
3 · Sperm vs the look-alikes: only manī (sperm) obligates ghusl. Madhī (thin, on arousal) and wadī (thick, after urinating) are minor impurity → wash + wudū, no ghusl.

The fluid table — manī · madhī · wadī

Manī (sperm)
Thick; leaves with desire (wet dream, intimacy). → Ghusl farḍ. The only fluid that obligates the major bath.
Madhī (pre-fluid)
"A white, thin, sticky fluid… released when he is aroused, long before orgasm," from romantic play or kissing (the female equivalent is also madhī). → Minor impurity: wash + wudū, no ghusl.
Wadī (post-urine)
"A white cloudy fluid, thick and warm, with no smell, released generally after urinating." → Minor impurity: wudū, no ghusl.
Roman Urdu
Teen paani: manī (shahwat ke saath → ghusl), madhī (uksaane par, patli → sirf wudū), wadī (peshaab ke baad, gaadhi → sirf wudū). Ghusl sirf manī se.

1 · When Ghusl Is Obligatory — الْمُوجِبَات

The book: "The major bath is compulsory if one of the following occurs" — then lists causes 1–7 (6 & 7 are the women's causes). Teach them in three groups, not as seven loose items.

Where this fits: the first question — when must I make ghusl? The left column of your mirror board.

Group A · Sperm leaving with desire (#1, #3, #4, #5)

From the book
"1- The discharge of sperm to the surface of the body obligates the major bath provided it separates from its location due to sexual arousal, not sexual intercourse… If one is struck in the back and consequently discharges sperm, he is not required to perform the major bath because it was not due to sexual arousal."
The four cases
#1 sperm with arousal — a wet dream or fantasy, both sexes. #3 sperm through intercourse with a corpse (the book notes such acts are unlawful and rare). #4 waking to a thin liquid with no remembered dream — ghusl only if the penis was not erect before sleeping (else it is likely madhī, no ghusl). #5 wetness found after fainting/intoxication, believed to be sperm.
★ Master-key 1
No desire → no ghusl. The Ḥanafī proof against Shāfiʿī's "water is from water": the hadith means "ejaculation by way of sexual desire."
Roman Urdu
A: manī shahwat ke saath nikle (#1 khwaab/khwahish — mard o aurat; #3 murda — haraam o naadir; #4 jaag kar geela, magar sone se pehle inteshaar na ho warna madhī; #5 be-hoshi/nashe ke baad). Bina shahwat → ghusl nahin.

Group B · Penetration — the head disappears (#2)

From the book
"2- If the head of the penis disappears in one of the passages of a living being (vagina or anus), the major bath becomes compulsory on both persons… The Prophet ﷺ said [When the private parts [of the male and female] cohabit with each other and the penis disappears in the vagina, bathing becomes obligatory [for both the male and female]]." (Ibn Mājah 611)
The independent trigger
Penetration to the head (the size "removed in circumcision") makes ghusl farḍ on both parties — even if no one ejaculates. This is a separate trigger from discharge: either one alone obligates ghusl.
Roman Urdu
B: supaari chhup jaaye (dukhool) → dono par ghusl farḍ, bina inzaal ke bhi (Ibn Mājah 611). Yeh manī-wala sabab se alag, mustaqil sabab hai.

Group C · A woman's bleeding ends (#6–7) + two appended rules

From the book
"6-7- When a female's menstrual periods or postnatal bleeding ends, it is compulsory for her to perform the major bath."
The two causes
The ending of ḥayḍ (menstruation) and of nifās (postnatal bleeding) obligates ghusl before she may pray/fast. (The full mechanics of ḥayḍ/nifās are Chapter 7 — teach only the ending here.)
Two appended rules
Pre-Islam janāba carries over — one in major impurity before Islam must do ghusl after embracing it ("the correct view"); but a convert not in major impurity → ghusl merely recommended. Washing a dead Muslim is farḍ kifāya — "if no one performs it, the sin falls on the entire community."
Roman Urdu
C: ḥayḍ (#6) aur nifās (#7) khatam hone par ghusl farḍ. Extra: Islam se pehle ki janāba bhi ghusl maangti hai; murde ko ghusl dena farḍ kifāya.

💡 Teaching tip — two independent triggers

Make it crisp: ghusl becomes farḍ on either (a) sperm with desire or (b) penetration to the head — you do not need both. A couple with intimacy but no climax → ghusl is still farḍ. (This pre-empts Landmine 2.)

2 · What Does NOT Obligate Ghusl — the Mirror (عَشَرَة)

The book: "There are ten things a person is not required to perform the major bath from." Teach it as the mirror of §1 (just like the wudū non-nullifiers in 3b). Every one fails a master-key: no sperm / no desire / no real penetration.

Where this fits: the second question — what looks like it should obligate ghusl but doesn't? The right column of your mirror board, and the reassurance half.

The two look-alike fluids (#1–2)

From the book
"1- Madhi… is a white, thin, sticky fluid that is released from a man's penis when he is aroused… long before orgasm. 2- Wadi… is a white cloudy fluid that is thick and warm and has no smell. It is released generally after urinating."
The point
Neither is sperm → no ghusl (key 3). Both are minor impurity: wash the spot + wudū. This is the #1 source of real-life ghusl confusion — teach it hard.
Roman Urdu
(1) madhī — patli, chipchipi, josh par. (2) wadī — gaadhi, peshaab ke baad. Dono manī nahin → sirf wudū, ghusl nahin.

No trace / no blood (#3–4)

From the book
"3- The major bath is not compulsory if one has a sexual dream but finds no wetness upon awakening. And the females are like them due to the words of the Prophet who was asked 'Does a woman need to perform ghusl?' Thereupon the Prophet said 'Yes, if she dreams and sees the fluid.' 4- Giving birth without visible blood does not obligate the major bath, since it is not regarded as nifās. Abu Hanifa maintained that as a precaution she should perform ghusl." (Bukhārī 282)
The point
#3 ghusl follows the fluid, not the dream — and women are the same. #4 a bloodless birth isn't nifās; Abū Ḥanīfa adds a precautionary ghusl since births are rarely bloodless.
Roman Urdu
(3) khwaab dekha magar geela na paaya → ghusl nahin (aurat bhi yahi — "haan, jab paani dekhe", Bukhārī 282). (4) bila-khoon wiladat → nahin (Abū Ḥanīfa: ihtiyaatan ghusl).

No real penetration / no ejaculation (#5–10)

From the book
"5- If a man inserts his penis but there is a cloth that prevents the feeling of pleasure, the major bath is not compulsory (recommended as a precaution). 6- Administering medicine in the anus (a suppository) does not require the bath. 7- Inserting a finger or something like it into the vagina or anus does not require the major bath, given the deficiency of sexual desire. 8/9- Intercourse with an animal or a deceased woman without discharging semen does not obligate it. 10- If a male penetrates a virgin, though he does not break her hymen and does not discharge semen, the major bath is not compulsory."
The point
Each fails a trigger — a barrier blocks pleasure (#5), no real sexual act (#6–7), or no emission and no true "meeting of the parts" (#8–10). The intact hymen in #10 "confirms that the circumcised parts did not meet."
Roman Urdu
(5) kapde ki aad, (6) suppository, (7) ungli, (8/9) jaanwar/murda bina inzaal, (10) kunwaari jiska parda saalim — sab mein ghusl nahin (na asl dukhool, na inzaal).

⚠️ This section is the cure for ghusl-anxiety

Frame §2 as the reassurance half. The cohort over-applies ghusl out of not knowing the fluids or thinking any contact triggers it. The mirror fixes both: no sperm-with-desire and no penetration-to-the-head → no ghusl. Walk each item opposite its §1 twin.

3 · How to Make a Valid Ghusl — the 3 Farḍ (فَرَائِض الْغُسْل)

The book heads this section "That Which Is Compulsory To Wash" and enumerates three things to wash in the major bath. These are the famous three farḍ — and the verse al-Māʾidah 6 is the proof.

Where this fits: the third question — how do I make a valid ghusl? Get these three right and the ghusl counts.

The three farḍ + why mouth & nose

From the book
"Washing the mouth, nostrils and the entire body once is obligatory in the major bath because Allah said [If you are in a state of ceremonial impurity, bath your whole body]. This is a clear command requiring the washing of the entire body, which includes the nose and mouth."
The three
(1) Rinse the mouth (maḍmaḍa) · (2) rinse the nostrils (istinshāq) · (3) wash the entire body once. Mouth & nose are farḍ in ghusl though only sunna in wudū — because the wudū verse orders washing the face (excludes inside the mouth/nose), but the ghusl verse orders the whole body (includes them). (Shāfiʿī holds them sunna even in ghusl.)
Qur'anic proof
"If you are in a state of ceremonial impurity, bath your whole body." (al-Māʾidah 6)
Roman Urdu
3 farḍ: kulli, naak mein paani, poora badan ek baar. Muh aur naak ghusl mein farḍ (wudū mein sirf sunnat the) — kyunki ghusl ki aayat "poora badan" kehti hai. Daleel: al-Māʾidah 6.

Reach every spot — the obligatory thoroughness

From the book
"It is obligatory to wash the inner area of the foreskin… provided it can be opened without difficulty. To wash the inner part of the navel is obligatory. Likewise, an open hole or slot in the body that has not sealed… It is obligatory to wash the skin underneath the beard, moustache, eyebrows, as well as the immediate inner part of the outer lips of the vagina, not the internal area."
No dry patch
"Whole body" = water must reach every washable spot: the inner navel, an unsealed piercing/hole, the skin under beard/moustache/eyebrows, the inner foreskin (if openable without difficulty — else lifted). Be gentle; don't cause yourself hardship.
Roman Urdu
"Poora badan" = koi jagah sookhi na rahe: naaf ka andar, khula chhed, daadhi/much/bhanwon ke neeche ki khaal, khatne ki andaruni jagah (agar bila-mashaqqat khul sake).

★ The braids rule — men vs women

From the book
"It is obligatory for a man to undo his braids and wash the inside… even if the water reaches the root of his hair while it is tied, it is not valid… a man does not receive a dispensation based on beautification, although the woman does. However, a female is not required to open her braids provided the water reaches the roots… [Umm Salama: 'Should I undo it when I wash after sexual relations?' He replied, 'It is enough for you to throw three handfuls over it, then pour water all over your body and you will be purified']." (Abū Dāwūd 251)
The split
Man: must undo his braids every ghusl — water reaching the root while tied is not enough. Woman: need not undo them, provided water reaches the roots — because undoing/redoing is a real hardship (the Umm Salama dispensation).
Roman Urdu
Mard: choti kholna farḍ (bandhi ki jad tak paani pahunch bhi jaaye to kaafi nahin). Aurat: kholna zaroori nahin agar jad tak paani pahunche (Umm Salama — "teen chullu", Abū Dāwūd 251).

4 · The 12 Sunnas + the Etiquettes (سُنَنُ الْغُسْل)

The book: "Twelve things are sunna in the major bath." They describe the Prophetic method — essentially the Maimūna hadith (Muslim 316 & 317). Teach it as a sequence, not a numbered list.

Where this fits: the fourth question — the complete ghusl beyond the bare 3 farḍ. The masnūn method + the ādāb.

The 12 sunnas — the Maimūna method (in order)

From the book
"It is sunna to start by uttering Bismillah… and to make one's intention… commence with washing the hands to the wrists… wash away any impurity isolated on the body… wash the sexual organs… perform ablution such as for prayer, washing the parts three times including the wiping of the head… delay the washing of the feet if the water gathers… pour water on the entire body three times… begin with the head, then the right shoulder, then the left… rub the body (dalk) and wash successively without pausing." (Maimūna — Muslim 316 & 317)
The sequence
1 Bismillah · 2 intention (niyyah) · 3 wash hands to the wrists · 4 wash off any impurity on the body · 5 wash the private parts · 6 a wudū like the prayer wudū · 7 delay the feet if water pools · 8 pour water over the whole body ×3 · 9 head, then right shoulder, then left · 10 rub the body (dalk) · 11 wash successively (muwālāt). The book counts these as twelve sunnas; the cohort needs the method, not a numbering.
Roman Urdu
Tarteeb: bismillah + niyyah → haath dhona → najaasat dhona → sharmgaah dhona → namaz jaisa wudū → (paani jamta ho to paaon baad mein) → poore badan par 3 baar paani (sar, daayaan kandha, baayaan) → dalk (malna) + muwālāt (bila-rukey).

The etiquettes (ādāb) — same as wudū, with 3 differences

From the book
"The etiquettes of the major bath are the same as that of wudu. The exception is that in the major bath, one is not to face the qibla because one is naked almost always… undertake the bath in a place where no one can see him… [God is Ever Living and Ever Concealed… so when anyone of you wants to bathe, let him observe secrecy]. And that which is disliked in ablution is disliked in the major bath, with the exception that it is disliked to speak, even if it is supplication, because the private parts are exposed." (Abū Dāwūd)
The three differences
1 Do not face the qibla (one is unclothed). 2 Bathe concealed, where no one sees you (if among others, the least-exposed spot). 3 It is disliked to speak — even du'ā — because the ʿawra is exposed (the opposite of wudū, where du'ā is encouraged).
Roman Urdu
Ādāb wudū jaise, magar 3 farq: (1) qibla ki taraf muh nahin, (2) chhup kar nahalo, (3) baat-cheet — du'ā tak makrūh (sharmgaah khuli hai).

💡 Teaching tip

The 12 sunnas are the way most people already bathe for ghusl — name the Prophetic order so it becomes worship, not just washing. Don't drill a 1-to-12 numbering; drill the flow: hands → clean off → private parts → wudū → pour ×3 (head, right, left) → rub.

5 · When Ghusl Is Sunna (4) & Recommended (16)

Beyond the obligatory bath, ghusl is beloved on many occasions even when one is perfectly pure — the same "ladder beyond farḍ" you taught in wudū. Don't read sixteen loose items: group them.

Where this fits: the top of the gradient — purity as a state a believer steps into, not only a fix for impurity.

When ghusl is SUNNA — 4 occasions

From the book
"It is sunna to perform the major bath for four occasions: 1- Friday prayer because it is the greatest of days… 2- The Eid prayers (the Prophet ﷺ would perform the major bath on the day of Fitr, Adha and Arafah). 3- It is sunna for the ihram… for hajj and umrah. 4- It is sunna for the one performing hajj to bathe at Arafah after the meridian."
The four
1 the Friday prayer · 2 the two Eids · 3 entering iḥrām (ḥajj/ʿumrah) · 4 the pilgrim bathing at ʿArafah after the meridian (ẓuhr).
Roman Urdu
Sunnat 4: Jumu'ah, do Eid, iḥrām, aur haaji ka ʿArafah mein zawaal ke baad ghusl.

When ghusl is RECOMMENDED — 16, in 4 baskets

From the book
"It is recommended to perform the major bath on sixteen occasions" — accepting Islam (free of janāba), maturity by age, recovery from insanity/fainting, after ḥijāma, after washing a dead body, the night of Barā'ah, the night of Qadr, entering the Madina, standing at Muzdalifa, entering Makkah for ṭawāf, the solar/lunar eclipse prayers, seeking rain, and the prayers of fear, darkness & strong wind (also repentance, return from travel, hidden filth).
A · Life-state changes
Accepting Islam (while free of janāba) · reaching maturity by age (max 15) · recovering from insanity / fainting.
B · After blood / the dead
After ḥijāma (cupping) · after washing a corpse. (The Prophet ﷺ made ghusl from four: janāba, Friday, ḥijāma, washing the dead — Abū Dāwūd 348.)
C · Sacred nights & places
Night of Barā'ah (mid-Shaʿbān) · night of Qadr · entering the Madina · Muzdalifa at dawn (10th Dhū'l-Ḥijja) · entering Makkah for ṭawāf az-Ziyārah.
D · Special prayers & states
Solar & lunar eclipse prayers · seeking rain (istisqā') · the prayers of fear, overwhelming darkness, strong wind · after repentance · returning from travel · when struck with hidden filth.

💡 Teaching tip — lift the mood here

After the heavy obligatory material, the gradient is a relief: ghusl for Friday, Eid, iḥrām is a beloved act even when perfectly pure. Frame it as purity being a state a believer steps into. Anchor with Abū Dāwūd 348 — the Prophet ﷺ's four ghusls.

⚠️ Common Student Mistakes — Watch For These

1. Confusing the three fluids (manī / madhī / wadī)

Only manī (sperm, leaving with desire) obligates ghusl. Madhī (thin, on arousal) and wadī (thick, after urinating) are minor impurity → wash + wudū, no ghusl. The tell-tale for the wake-up case: penis erect before sleeping → likely madhī → no ghusl.

2. "Ghusl needs ejaculation"

Two independent triggers: sperm with desire OR penetration to the head. The head disappearing into a passage obligates ghusl on both parties even without ejaculation (Ibn Mājah 611). Intimacy with penetration but no climax → ghusl is still farḍ.

3. "I just poured water over myself" — missing the 3 farḍ

The mouth and nose are farḍ in ghusl (unlike wudū, where they're sunna) — al-Māʾidah 6 orders washing the whole body. And "whole body" means no dry patch: navel, piercings, under the beard, under rings. One missed spot = ghusl incomplete.

4. The braids — men and women are different

A man must undo his braids (water reaching the root while tied is not enough). A woman need not, provided water reaches the roots — the Umm Salama dispensation (Abū Dāwūd 251). Don't flatten the two.

5. "Ghusl is only for impurity"

Ghusl is also sunna (Friday, the two Eids, iḥrām, ʿArafah) and recommended (16 occasions) even when perfectly pure. Purity is a state a believer steps into, not only damage-control.

6. Teaching ḥayḍ/nifās detail here

This chapter only says: the ending of ḥayḍ/nifās obligates ghusl. The durations, the colours of blood, istiḥāḍa, the "excused" category — all are Chapter 7. Don't improvise them.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an & Hadith

Every text below is quoted by Nur al-Idah itself in this chapter. Use 1–2 in class to anchor the rulings in the sources.

al-Māʾidah 6 — bath your whole body

"If you are in a state of ceremonial impurity, bath your whole body."

The book's proof that the 3 farḍ are mouth, nose, and the whole body — and why the mouth and nose are farḍ in ghusl though only sunna in wudū (the command names the whole body, which includes them).

"The penis disappears in the vagina" (Ibn Mājah 611)

The Prophet ﷺ said: "When the private parts [of the male and female] cohabit with each other and the penis disappears in the vagina, bathing becomes obligatory [for both the male and female]."

The basis for cause #2 — penetration to the head obligates ghusl on both parties, even without ejaculation.

The woman's wet dream (Bukhārī 282)

The Prophet ﷺ was asked, "Does a woman need to perform ghusl [if she has a wet dream]?" He replied, "Yes, if she dreams and sees the fluid."

The basis for non-obligation #3 — ghusl follows the fluid, not the dream; and a woman is the same as a man.

Umm Salama's braids (Abū Dāwūd 251)

Umm Salama asked, "I am a woman who keeps my hair closely plaited. Should I undo it when I wash after sexual relations?" He replied, "It is enough for you to throw three handfuls over it, then pour water all over your body and you will be purified."

The basis for the women's braids dispensation — she need not undo her plaits if water reaches the roots.

The method of the major bath (Muslim 317)

Maimūna described the Prophet ﷺ's ghusl: he washed his hands, poured water over his private parts and washed them with his left hand, then performed wudū, then washed his whole body, then moved aside and washed his feet.

The basis for the 12 sunnas — the masnūn order of the major bath.

The Prophet's ﷺ four ghusls (Abū Dāwūd 348)

The Prophet ﷺ would perform the major bath from four things: janāba (sexual impurity), the Friday prayer, ḥijāma (cupping), and washing a deceased.

Cited for the recommended occasions (after cupping, after washing the dead) and the Friday sunna.
