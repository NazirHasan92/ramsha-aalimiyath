Chapter 2 — Istinja & Toilet Adab · Student Handout

Nur al-Idah · Book I: Purification · The step before wudu

👋 Welcome — What you'll learn today

Chapter 1 taught us about water — the cause of purification. Chapter 2 teaches us istinja — the cleaning that comes before wudu. Without proper istinja, the wudu that follows has a weak foundation.

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  1. State when istinja is sunna and when it is obligatory (the dirham threshold).
  2. Choose the right method — stones, water, or both — and know which is best.
  3. Understand the procedure for men and women, and the role of hand-washing.
  4. Tell apart the haram (exposing the awra) from the makruh (bones, right hand, baked items) in istinja.
  5. Know the major lavatory aadab — entering, sitting, exiting — and the two du'as.

Source: everything in this handout comes from one book — Nur al-Idah by Imam Hasan Shurunbulali (the standard introductory Hanafi text), Chapter on Istinja (book pp. 39–44). No outside sources have been added.

Rule 1 · When is istinja sunna, when is it obligatory?

Step 0: Before istinja begins, make sure the leakage of urine has actually stopped. For men, the book suggests walking, coughing, or leaning on one side. For women, wait a short time, then clean. You may not begin wudu until you are satisfied the leakage has stopped.

Urdu mein: Istinja se pehle ek qadam hai — itminaan. Mard ke liye: thoda chalna, khansna, ek taraf jhuk jaana. Aurat ke liye: thodi der intezaar. Bina itminaan ke wudu jaiz nahin.

The threshold — by how far the filth has spread

The size of a dirham is the threshold unit:

  • For solids: ≈ 20 qirat ≈ 3.5 grams
  • For fluids: the area equivalent to a large coin

Easy way to remember: about the size of a small coin (think 5-rupee coin).

Situation Ruling What to use
Filth has NOT crossed the boundary of the outlet ✅ Sunna Stone OR water — either
Filth crosses boundary, ≤ dirham (≈ 3.5 g / small-coin size) ⚠️ Obligatory Water OR a liquid cleanser (e.g. rose water)
Filth crosses boundary, > dirham ⚠️ Obligatory WATER ONLY (liquid cleansers no longer suffice)
Major impurity state (post-intercourse, hayd, nifas) — at time of ghusl ⚠️ Obligatory regardless of quantity Water (but no exerting hardship)

Urdu mein: Dirham ka pemana yaad rakho — 3.5 gram (thos) ya bade sikke jitna area (mayye). Sirhad cross na hui — sunna. Cross hui par dirham tak — wajib (paani ya gulaab-jal). Dirham se zyada — sirf paani lazmi. Ghusl waale haalat (jinabat, hayd, nifas) — har quantity par wajib.

💡 The big insight

Istinja is not a single yes/no rule — it is a gradient. The weight of the ruling and the medium you must use both depend on how far the filth has spread and what state of impurity you are in. Once you see the gradient, almost every classroom question becomes easy to answer.

Rule 2 · The three method levels — stones, water, or both

The book gives three valid routes, ranked by preference:

Level Method Ranking
1 Stones alone (clean, not rough, not valuable) ✅ Sunna · valid by itself
2 Water alone ✅✅ Superior to stones alone
3 BOTH — wipe with stone FIRST, then wash with water ⭐ BEST — the Prophet's optimum

Urdu mein: Teen levels: (1) sirf patthar — sunna, jaiz. (2) sirf paani — patthar se behtar. (3) dono milaa kar (pehle wipe, phir wash) — sab se afzal. Aaj kal ke flush toilet (paani + tissue) bilkul Level 3 par hain — yahi Nabi ﷺ ka optimum tareeqa hai.

⚠️ The "three stones" rule — it's not as strict as people think

You may have heard "always use three stones". Nur al-Idah is more precise:

  • The sunna is to purify the area.
  • Using three stones is recommended (mandub), NOT an emphasized sunna.
  • Odd numbers (1, 3, 5, 7…) are preferred.
  • If two stones do the job — fine. If five are needed — fine. The point is clean, not count.

🕊️ The Hanafi proof — "no blame" hadith (Abu Dawud 35)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Whomever cleaned, then he should clean in odd numbers, and whoever observed this, has done well and whoever did not, there is no blame."

The decisive phrase: "there is no blame" — proof that the count is preferred, not strict.

Rule 3 · The procedure — by sex and anatomy

The book treats this section clinically. We will do the same.

For men — depends on scrotum length

Anatomy Sequence Why
Long / hanging scrotum 3 movements with 3 stones:
1. Front → Rear
2. Rear → Front
3. Front → Rear		 End at the rear so filth is not brought forward and spread by the long scrotum
Short / not-hanging scrotum Rear → Front (single direction) Optimal cleaning in this anatomy

For women

Front → Rear, single direction. The book gives the reason: "for fear of defiling her vagina." Modern hygiene science agrees fully.

Urdu mein: Auraton ke liye sirf aage se peeche — taa ke peeche ki gandgi aage (vagina) ki taraf na aaye.

The hand-washing rule (a counter-intuitive but important detail)

Wet the washing hand FIRST before touching the soiled area. The book explains why: "if he first touches the soiled area while his hand is dry, it may soak the faeces into the skin."

Then use the inside surface of the fingers — one, two, or three as needed. Not the back of the hand, not the palm centre.

Urdu mein: Pehle haath geela karo, phir lagao. Sookha haath gandgi ko khaal mein soak kar deta hai — yeh book ki seedhi tafseel hai. Ungliyon ke andar wali side use karo — ek, do, ya teen.

Finger order — different for men and women

  • Men: raise the middle finger first (over the other fingers), then advance the ring finger. One finger alone is not enough.
  • Women: raise the ring finger and middle finger simultaneously at the start. The book gives the reason: "for fear of being sexually aroused."

Urdu mein: Mard: pehle bicchli ungli (middle), phir anaamika (ring). Aurat: anaamika + bicchli ek saath. Reason kitaab mein diya hua hai — usi ko bayan karna hai.

After cleaning

  1. Clean until the foul smell is gone.
  2. Wash the hand a second time.
  3. Relax the muscles for thorough cleaning — BUT NOT if fasting.

⚠️ The fasting precaution

If you are fasting, do NOT fully relax the buttocks — water or wet finger entering the body cavity (jawf) breaks the fast. Also dry the buttocks before standing, because wetness can be drawn inward when standing.

Yeh Hanafi madhhab ka jawf wala asool hai — andar paani jaana roza torta hai. Isi liye yeh ehtiyaat.

Rule 4 · What is forbidden vs disliked in istinja

Two different categories of "don't" — and the difference matters.

HARAM — forbidden, sinful

WhatConsequence
Exposing the awra (private area) in the presence of others The person is regarded as a fasiq (an immoral person — legal term)

If onlookers are present, perform istinja under the clothes. Even if filth exceeds the dirham size, do your best to clean WITHOUT exposing yourself.

Urdu mein: Doosron ke saamne satar khol kar istinja karna haraam hai. Aisa karne wala fasiq kehlata hai — yeh fiqhi taur par bohot bara dhabba hai. Logon ke saamne ho — kapdon ke neeche karo. Privacy pehle, mukammal safaai doosra.

MAKRUH — disliked, but istinja is still valid

  1. Bones — "food for your brothers of the jinn" (the Prophet ﷺ).
  2. Food of humans or animals — "do not waste, do not waste."
  3. Baked items — bricks, tiles, earthenware, charcoal, glass, gypsum.
  4. Items of value — silk, cotton (israaf — wasting wealth).
  5. The right hand — unless there is a valid reason (e.g. injured left hand).

🕊️ The Salman hadith — three rules in one (Bukhari and Muslim 267)

Salman (RA) said: "The Prophet forbade us from cleaning the private parts with the right hand, and from cleansing with less than three stones, or cleansing with dung or bone."

Three rulings in a single hadith. The textual backbone of Rule 4.

🕊️ The right-hand hadith (Abu Dawud 31)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When anyone of you urinates, he must not touch his penis with his right hand, and when he goes to relieve himself he must not wipe with his right hand, and when he drinks, he must not drink in one breath."

The principle: right hand = honour functions (eating, drinking, greeting, holding the Mushaf). Left hand = body cleaning. Functional differentiation — not hierarchy of worth.

Urdu mein: Daahna haath izzat ke kaamon ke liye (khaana, salaam, Mushaf), baayan haath safaai ke kaamon ke liye. Yeh "ooncha-neecha" nahin — kaam ki taqseem hai. Bilkul jaise surgeon operation aur cleanup ke alag gloves rakhta hai.

Rule 5 · Lavatory etiquette — entering, sitting, exiting

A complete sunna-aligned bathroom visit has a mirror pattern: left in + du'a, right out + du'a. Below is the full adab sequence.

Entering

  1. Enter with the LEFT foot.
  2. Recite: Bismillah · Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith (In the name of Allah · O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the male and female unclean spirits).

🕊️ The "cover" hadith (At-Tirmidhi 606)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The cover between the eyes of the jinn and the private parts of the children of Adam is when the person enters the lavatory, he says, 'In the name of Allah'."

Bismillah is the cover; the isteʿaadhah is the protection.

Inside

  1. Sit leaning on the LEFT side — physically aids the body in releasing waste.
  2. Do not talk unless there is a need (the Abu Dawud 15 hadith on Allah's wrath at two persons relieving themselves while talking).
  3. ★ Do NOT face the qibla or turn your back to it — even within a building. This is makruh tahriman (prohibitively disliked).
  4. Do not face the sun or moon (great signs of Allah).
  5. Do not face a blowing wind (filth blown back).
  6. Do not relieve yourself in water (even flowing).
  7. Do not relieve yourself in a place of shade (rest area for others).
  8. Do not relieve yourself in a hole, on a road, or in a cemetery.
  9. Do not relieve yourself under a fruit tree.
  10. Do not urinate standing unless there is a reason (e.g. injured back).
  11. Do not urinate in the place of wudu (satanic whispers).

🕊️ The qibla hadith (Bukhari 144)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "If the need to defecate arose, then do not face the qibla, and do not turn around giving it your back, however turn east or west."

🕊️ The "two cursed" hadith (Muslim 269)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Fear the two that are cursed." They asked: "Who are those that are cursed, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "The one that relieves himself in the road or walkway of the people or in their shade."

The disliked locations are not arbitrary — defiling shared space is cursed.

🕊️ The Anas hadith on standing (At-Tirmidhi, The Book of Purification 14)

Anas (RA) said: "If the Prophet wanted to attend to a need, he would not lift his clothes until he was close to the ground."

Exiting

  1. Exit with the RIGHT leg.
  2. Recite: Ghufranak · Alhamdulillah alladhi adhhaba 'anni al-adha wa 'afani (I ask Your forgiveness · All praise be to Allah, who has repelled harm from me and protected me).

Urdu mein: Daakhle aur nikalne ka mirror pattern: andar baayaan paaon + Bismillah + A'udhu billahi…. Bahar daahna paaon + Ghufranak + Alhamdulillah…. Sab se aham: qibla ki taraf na rukh, na peeth (makruh tahriman). Khade ho ke peshab, paani mein, gaddhe mein, raaste par, daraakht ke neeche, chhaan mein, wudu ki jagah — sab makruh.

💡 The deepest lesson of the chapter

Even the bathroom — the humblest place in the house — is wrapped in Bismillah and Alhamdulillah. Islam does not compartmentalise the sacred from the mundane. If you can remember Allah here, you can remember Him everywhere.
