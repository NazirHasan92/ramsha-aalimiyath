# Part A · Understanding the chapter

This chapter looks, on its surface, like a series of small rules about the bathroom — left foot, right foot, three stones, don't face the qibla. But underneath, Imam Hasan Shurunbulali is teaching you **one big principle**: *the body has private dignity*, and the way a Muslim handles even the most humble bodily functions is governed by law, sunna, and adab. Cleanliness is half of faith — and Chapter 2 is where that half begins, before wudu, before prayer.

**Yaad rakho:** Chapter 1 mein paani ki taʿreef thi — *cause of purity*. Chapter 2 mein woh paani ka **pehla istemaal** seekh rahe ho — *istinja*. Yeh wudu se pehle ka qadam hai. Bina istinja ke wudu ki bunyaad hi kachhi reh jaati hai. Aur sirf "saaf hone" ki baat nahin — yahaan *aadaab*, *haya*, aur *fiqhi hukm* — teenon ek saath aate hain.

Work through the five concepts below in order. For each, ask yourself: *can I explain this to an adult who has never read fiqh before, without making them uncomfortable?* (*Kya main yeh kisi sharminda kiye baghair samjha sakti hoon?*) The subject is delicate; the teaching must be calm and clinical.

## 1 · The threshold — when istinja is sunna, when obligatory

Before any istinja begins, there is a smaller rule that students often miss: **make sure the leakage of urine has actually stopped**. Nur al-Idah opens this section by stating:

> *"On passing urine, a person must ensure that the leakage of urine has stopped and his mind is satisfied concerning the issue. This is achieved through one's regular habits, such as walking, coughing, or leaning on one's side. The woman is not required to do this. She is to wait a small time and then clean herself. It is not permissible to begin wudu until one is satisfied that the leakage of urine has stopped."*

So the very first move is **not** wiping or washing — it is **mental satisfaction** that the body has finished. For men, the book lists physical aids — *walking, coughing, leaning to one side*. These are pragmatic ways of confirming the bladder has emptied. For women, the book says simply: *wait a small time*, then clean.

**Yaad rakho:** Istinja se bhi pehle ek qadam hai — **itminaan**. Mard ke liye: thoda chalna, khansna, ek taraf jhuk jaana. Aurat ke liye: thodi der intezaar. *Bina itminaan ke wudu shuru karna jaiz nahin* — yeh book ka saaf hukm hai.

Once leakage has stopped, the next question is: **is istinja sunna or obligatory in this case?** Nur al-Idah lays out a precise threshold using the **size of a dirham** (≈ 3.5 grams for solids, or the area of a large coin for fluid).

### The threshold grid

| Situation | Ruling | What to use |
|---|---|---|
| Filth has **NOT crossed** the boundary of the outlet | **Sunna** | Stone OR water (either is fine) |
| Filth crosses the boundary, **up to** size of a dirham | **Obligatory** | Water OR a liquid cleanser (e.g. rose water) |
| Filth crosses the boundary, **more than** dirham size | **Obligatory with water specifically** | Water |
| Post-intercourse / hayd / nifas — in the major-impurity state | **Obligatory** to wash filth in exit passage, even if minimal | Water |

So the law is gradient — it scales with *how far the filth has spread* and *what state of impurity the person is in*. A small everyday case (filth stays within the outlet) → istinja is sunna, do it because the Prophet ﷺ did it. A spread larger than a coin → mandatory cleaning, and **only water will do**. A major-impurity state (after intercourse, during ghusl) → mandatory regardless of quantity.

The book is careful to add: *"One is not to exert difficulty upon himself when undertaking this cleaning"* in the major-impurity case. Hardship is not the goal — diligence is.

**Yaad rakho:** Dirham ka pemana yaad rakho — **3.5 gram (thos cheez)** ya **ek bade sikke ke barabar (mayye cheez)**. Iss se kam aur sirhad ke andar — sunna. Sirhad cross kare lekin dirham se kam — wajib (paani ya gulaab-jal). Dirham se zyada — paani lazmi. Ghusl waale haalat mein — har soorat mein wajib, chaahe na ke barabar ho.

### The big insight you must take from §1

People assume istinja is a single binary act — "you do it or you don't". The book is teaching you it is **a graded response system**, calibrated to the actual state of the body. Once you see the gradient, you can answer almost every classroom question:

- *"Sirf urine ki ek boond — kya wudu se pehle dhona zaroori hai?"* → boundary cross hui? Nahin → sunna. Haan → wajib.
- *"Bohot thoda lagaa — kya stones se kaafi hai?"* → dirham se kam → haan, stones bhi theek (lekin paani superior).
- *"Hayd ke time bhi yeh threshold lagega?"* → nahin — wahaan har quantity wajib hai.

The threshold rule is the **skeleton** of this chapter. Everything else hangs on it.

## 2 · The method — stones, water, or both

Once you've established that istinja must happen (sunna OR obligatory), the next question is **with what**. Nur al-Idah gives you a clear hierarchy:

### The hierarchy

1. **Stones alone** — *sunna* method, valid by itself. Must be a clean stone, not rough (like burnt clay), not something valuable.
2. **Water alone** — **superior** to stones alone. *"Washing with water is superior than simply wiping the filthy area."*
3. **Both combined** — the **best** method. *"Whereas the best method is to combine between washing with water and wiping. Hence, the individual wipes, and then washes."*

Notice the sequence in option 3: **wipe first, then wash**. Wiping with the stone removes the bulk of the filth; washing with water finishes the cleansing. This is the prophetic optimum.

**Yaad rakho:** Teen levels yaad karo — **(1) sirf patthar** = sunna, jaiz. **(2) sirf paani** = behtar. **(3) dono milaa kar** (pehle patthar, phir paani) = **sab se afzal** — yeh Nabi ﷺ ka tareeqa hai. Aaj kal log doosre + teesre level par chalein hain (tissue + water), pehla level jaante hi nahin — par yeh bhi sahih hai.

### The "three stones" rule — and what it actually says

Many students hear "three stones" and assume it's a hard rule. Nur al-Idah is more precise:

> *"The sunna is to purify the area, and to use a prescribed number of stones is **recommended, and not an emphasized sunna**, and the recommended number of stones to use is three, even if the area is cleaned in less than that."*

So the three-stone count is **recommended** (mandub), not an emphasized sunna. The emphasized sunna is *purification of the area*. If two stones do the job — fine. If you need four — fine. The point is *clean*, not *count*.

Imam Shafi'i held that three is **necessary**, citing the hadith *"Perform istinja with three stones"*. The Hanafis rely on a different prophetic hadith:

> *"Whoever cleaned, then he should clean in odd numbers, and whoever observed this, has done well and whoever did not, there is no blame."* (Abu Dawud 35, as quoted in Nur al-Idah)

The phrase **"there is no blame"** (*la haraj*) is the Hanafi proof — it shows the count is not strict. *Odd numbers preferred* is the additional refinement (1, 3, 5, 7…).

**Yaad rakho:** "Teen patthar" ka rule **wajib nahin** — *mustahab* hai. Asal hukm: *paak ho jaao*. Agar do mein ho gaya, do bhi theek. Hadith: *"jis ne pak kiya woh achchha kiya, aur jis ne nahin (teen mein) — koi haraj nahin"*. Yeh Hanafi madhhab ki bunyaadi dalil hai is masle par.

### The big insight you must take from §2

The chapter is teaching you that **purification is the goal — the method is the route, not the destination**. The Prophet ﷺ gave you three valid routes (stones / water / both) and a preferred shape (odd numbers, three recommended). When a classmate asks *"sahi tareeqa kya hai?"* — your answer is: *"Koi bhi tareeqa jis se area saaf ho jaaye sahi hai. Sab se behtar — pehle patthar, phir paani. Sab se khaalis sunna — odd number stones, behtar teen."*

## 3 · Procedure by anatomy — male, female, and the finger rule

This is the section students dread most — but the book treats it with clinical calm, and you must too. The procedure differs by sex and by anatomy because **the goal is optimal cleaning without spreading filth or causing other harm**.

### Male — by scrotum length

Nur al-Idah distinguishes two male anatomies:

**Long / hanging scrotum:** the procedure is **three movements with three stones**:
1. First stone: **front → rear** (from near the testicles backward)
2. Second stone: **rear → front**
3. Third stone: **front → rear**

Why end at the rear? *"It is recommended to conclude the wiping at the rear so that the filth is not brought to the front and spread all over due to the long scrotum."* The geometry of the body decides the order.

**Short scrotum (not hanging loosely):** the procedure is simpler — **rear → front**, because in this anatomy "*cleaning can be optimally achieved*" by that single direction.

### Female

The female procedure is **front → rear** — *"for fear of defiling her vagina"*. This is the only direction; the reason is hygienic (preventing contamination of the vagina from the rear).

### The hand and finger rule

Three more details that the book is explicit about:

1. **Wash the hand FIRST before touching the soiled area.** *"If he first touches the soiled area while his hand is dry, it may soak the faeces into the skin."* So wet your washing hand first; dry hand → faeces soaks in → harder to clean. Counter-intuitive but practical.

2. **Use the inside of the fingers** — *"one, two or three fingers if needed"*. Not the back of the hand, not the palm centre — the **inside surface** of the fingers, which can apply pressure precisely.

3. **Finger order — different by sex:**

   - **Male:** raise the **middle finger** over the other fingers at the beginning, then advance the **ring finger** forward. *"It insures optimum cleaning. The male is not to restrict the cleaning to only one finger because cleaning will not be optimally achieved."*
   - **Female:** raise the **ring finger together with the middle finger simultaneously** at the start — *"for fear of being sexually aroused"*. The reasoning is preserved in the book and is not for you to omit.

**Yaad rakho:** Mardon ke liye — **bicchla ungli pehle, phir chhungliya wali (anaamika) aage**. Auraton ke liye — **anaamika + bicchla ek saath**. Mard ki shape: ek-ek karke. Aurat ki shape: ek saath. Reason book mein diya hua hai (auraton ke liye jinsi tahreek ka khof) — ratta nahin maarne wala, samajh ke aage badhna hai.

### Cleaning thoroughly — and the fasting precaution

The book then adds:

> *"One is to perform the utmost in cleaning the area until the foul smell is gone, and is to thoroughly relax the buttocks or hips in order to clean what is in the passage to the best of one's ability, **though this relaxation of the buttocks and hips is performed provided one is not fasting**, and the basis for this is that there is water present around the anus which will nullify one's fast if it enters."*

So during Ramadan or any other fast: **don't relax** the buttocks completely while cleaning, because water that enters the body breaks the fast. Hanafi madhhab is strict about this — any fluid entering the *jawf* (internal cavity) nullifies the fast.

### After cleaning

Two final mechanical rules:
- **Wash the hand a second time** after finishing (the hand has touched filth).
- **If fasting, dry the buttocks before standing** — because wetness on the body, when standing, can be drawn inward and break the fast.

**Yaad rakho:** Roza rakhne walon ke liye **do extra ehtiyaat**: (a) buttocks zyada na khulwao — paani andar gaya to roza toot jayega, (b) khade hone se pehle khushk karo — kheechne se andar ja sakta hai. Yeh Hanafi madhhab ki sakht ihtiyaat hai *jawf* (jismani guhha) ke baare mein.

### The big insight you must take from §3

The chapter is not being prudish — it is being **anatomically precise** because Allah's law accommodates *real bodies*. The "long scrotum / short scrotum" distinction is not embarrassment; it is *fiqh that takes the body seriously*. Teach this section calmly. The cohort will respect you for it.

## 4 · What is forbidden vs disliked in istinja

Now the book switches from **how** to **with what** (and what you absolutely cannot do).

### Forbidden — and the consequence

> *"It is not permitted to expose the private area [`awra] for istinja during the presence of others. If there are onlookers, then istinja is performed under the clothes. If one uncovers the private area in the presence of others, he is regarded as an immoral person [fasiq] due to its unlawfulness."*

This is the chapter's strongest line. **Exposing one's private parts in front of others — even to perform a religious cleaning — makes the person a *fasiq*.** The fiqh weight of this word matters: a fasiq's testimony is not accepted in court, his religious standing is compromised. Privacy of the body is not optional manners — it is *unlawful to breach*.

There's a related ruling: if filth has crossed the boundary and exceeds dirham size *but* you are in a place where you cannot conceal yourself, you must "do your best in removing the impurity without exposing the private area to onlookers." The principle is: **privacy first, completeness second.**

**Yaad rakho:** Yaad rakhne wali sab se aham baat: **doosron ke saamne satar khol kar istinja karna haraam hai**, aur aisa karne wala *fasiq* kehlata hai. Agar logon ke saamne ho — kapdon ke neeche karo. Privacy pehle, mukammal safaai doosra.

### Disliked — the list

The book lists **six categories** of disliked materials/practices:

1. **Bones** — Hadith: *"Do not perform istinja with faeces or bones, for it is food for your brothers of the jinn"* (the Prophet's reasoning).
2. **Food of humans or animals** — wasteful, and: *"Do not waste, do not waste."*
3. **Baked bricks and anything made with fire** — tiles, earthenware, charcoal, glass, gypsum.
4. **Items of value or respectable** — silk, cotton. (Wasting valuable cloth on filth is disrespectful to wealth.)
5. **Right hand** — unless there is a valid reason.

The right-hand rule has its own foundation — the **Salman hadith**:

> *"The Prophet forbade us from cleaning the private parts with the right hand, and from cleansing with less than three stones, or cleansing with dung or bone."* (Abu Dawud 31, as quoted in Nur al-Idah)

And another prophetic hadith — *"When anyone of you urinates, he must not touch his penis with his right hand, and when he goes to relieve himself he must not wipe with his right hand, and when he drinks, he must not drink in one breath."* (Bukhari/Muslim 267)

The principle is: **the right hand is for honour — eating, drinking, greeting, holding the Mushaf. The left hand is for cleaning the body. Don't reverse them.**

**Yaad rakho:** *Daahna haath izzat ke kaamon ke liye, baayan haath safaai ke kaamon ke liye*. Yeh sirf riwaayati adab nahin — Hadith mein saaf hukm hai. Bones, khaana, banayi gayi cheezein (tile/charcoal/glass), keemati cheez (silk/cotton) — sab makruh. *Wajh*: yaa to israaf, yaa to jinnon ka khaana, yaa to qeemat ki be-adabi.

### The big insight you must take from §4

There are **two different categories of "don't"** in this section:
- **Haram** (one item only): exposing the awra in front of others.
- **Makruh** (many items): bones, food, baked materials, valuable cloth, right hand.

When a classmate asks *"yeh allowed hai ya nahin?"* — your first job is to tell them *which category*. "*Makruh* hai" means *"avoid, but if you did it, the istinja is still valid."* "*Haram* hai" means *"invalid as a way to do it; sinful."*

## 5 · Lavatory etiquette — entering, sitting, exiting

The final section of the chapter zooms out from the act of cleaning to the **whole visit to the bathroom**. There are roughly a dozen sunnas/aadab — let me group them for you.

### Entering

1. **Enter with the LEFT foot.**
2. **Seek refuge in Allah from Satan** before entering. The book quotes two hadiths:
   - *"The cover between the eyes of the jinn and the private parts of the children of Adam is when the person enters the lavatory, he says, 'In the name of Allah'."* (At-Tirmidhi 606)
   - *"The evil ones of the jinns are present, so if anyone enters the lavatory, he should say, 'I seek refuge with Allah from the unclean spirits, male and female'."* (Abu Dawud 6)

The standard du'a — *Bismillah, Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith* — combines both. **Bismillah is the cover; the seeking refuge is the protection.**

### While inside

3. **Sit leaning on the LEFT side.** This is a physical lean — weight on the left, pressure released on the right. Anatomically this assists the body in releasing waste.
4. **Don't talk** unless there is a need. The book quotes: *"When two persons go together for relieving themselves uncovering their private parts and talking together, Allah the Great and Majestic, becomes wrathful at this action."* (Abu Dawud 15)
5. **Prohibitively disliked** (*makruh tahriman*): facing the qibla, or turning the back to the qibla — *even within a building*. The Prophet's words: *"If the need to defecate arose, then do not face the qibla, and do not turn around giving it your back, however turn east or west."* (Bukhari 144)
6. **Disliked to face the sun or moon** directly — *"because they are great signs of Allah's creation."*
7. **Disliked to face a blowing wind** — *"the filth is likely to be blown back onto one's body or clothes."* (Practical hygiene reason.)
8. **Disliked to urinate/defecate in water** — even flowing water.
9. **Disliked to relieve oneself in a place of shade** — *"because it may be a rest area for other people."*
10. **Disliked to relieve oneself in a hole, on a road, or in a cemetery.** The "two cursed" hadith:
    > *"Fear the two that are cursed."* They asked: *"Who are those that are cursed, O Messenger of Allah?"* He said: *"The one that relieves himself in the road or walkway of the people or in their shade."* (Muslim 269)
11. **Disliked to relieve oneself under a fruit tree.**
12. **Disliked to urinate standing** unless there is a reason (e.g. an injured back). Anas's hadith: *"If the Prophet wanted to attend to a need, he would not lift his clothes until he was close to the ground."* (At-Tirmidhi, The Book of Purification 14)
13. **Disliked to urinate in the place of wudu** — *"due to the onset of satanic whispers which may be brought on by such a practice."*

### Exiting

14. **Exit with the RIGHT leg** — *"because it has more right to be advanced when exiting such a place."*
15. **Say a du'a on exit:**
    > *"All praise be to Allah, the One who has repelled harm from me and protected me."* And the Prophet ﷺ said at the time of exiting: *"I ask Your forgiveness."* (*Ghufranak* — Abu Dawud 30)

So a complete bathroom visit, sunna-aligned, looks like:
- Left foot in + *Bismillah, A'udhu billahi min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith*
- Sit leaning left, silent, not facing/backing qibla, not standing, not under tree / in road / in water
- Right foot out + *Ghufranak* + *"Alhamdulillah… alladhi adhhaba 'anni al-adha wa 'afani"*

**Yaad rakho:** Daakhle aur nikalne ka **mirror pattern**: andar **baayaan paaon** + *Bismillah + isteʿaadhah*. Bahar **daahna paaon** + *Ghufranak*. Andar **baayaan jhuk** ke baitho, **qibla ko na rukh karo, na peeth**, **khade ho ke peshab nahin**, **paani mein nahin**, **chhaan mein nahin**, **daraakht ke neeche nahin**, **raaste par nahin**. Yeh pooray taur par **adab ka silsila** hai — sirf "rules" nahin.

### The big insight you must take from §5

The bathroom — the most private, humble place in the home — is treated by Sacred Law with the same dignity as the masjid. Every entry has a du'a, every exit has a du'a, every direction has a rule. **Islam doesn't compartmentalise the sacred and the mundane**; even the dirtiest function of the body is wrapped in *Bismillah* and *Alhamdulillah*. This is the deepest spiritual lesson of the chapter — and the one most likely to move your cohort emotionally.

## The big picture — what this chapter is silently teaching

Step back. Chapter 2 of Nur al-Idah teaches the rulings of istinja and toilet adab — but underneath, it has handed you **four enduring principles** that will recur throughout Book I:

1. **The body has dignity.** *(Jism ki izzat hai.)* The awra is protected even during cleaning. The right hand is for honour. The body is not a thing to be managed; it is an *amanah* (trust).
2. **Law is graded, not binary.** *(Hukm darjat mein hota hai, sirf "haan/nahin" mein nahin.)* The dirham threshold, the sunna/wajib/haram scale, the makruh list — every situation has a specific calibration.
3. **Adab is the wrapper around ahkam.** *(Adab ahkam ka libas hai.)* You can do the rulings without the etiquette and still be valid — but Islam wants both. The Prophet ﷺ entered with Bismillah, exited with Ghufranak — even though both are sunna, not obligatory.
4. **Even the lavatory is a place of dhikr.** *(Baathroom mein bhi dhikr hai.)* This is the most profound teaching. If you remember Allah here, you will remember Allah everywhere.

**Yaad rakho:** Yeh chapter sirf "kaise dhona hai" nahin — yeh poori **shariʿat ki tarbiyat ka chhota laboratory** hai. *Hukm + adab + dhikr + haya* — chaaron ek saath, baathroom jaise jagah par. Imam Shurunbulali ne is liye yeh chapter wudu se pehle rakha — kyunki bina is bunyaad ke wudu khali act ban jata hai.

# Part B · Teaching the chapter

Now you understand the chapter. This section is how to *deliver* it to your classmates — adult, mostly Urdu-speaking Muslims, in a mixed-gender class (or you may need to teach women separately for certain sections — see below).

---

## A note on delicacy

This chapter touches anatomy and bodily functions. Adult learners — even adult Muslim learners — can become uncomfortable if the teacher's tone wavers. **Three rules for delivery:**

1. **Use the book's own language.** When Nur al-Idah says "private area" or "outlet" or "scrotum", use those words. Not slang, not euphemism — the clinical fiqh vocabulary. If the *Imam* could write it, you can teach it.
2. **Stay calm and matter-of-fact.** No giggling, no apologetic body language, no "this is awkward but…". The cohort takes its cue from you. If you treat the chapter as ordinary fiqh, they will too.
3. **Pause for questions only at concept boundaries** — not during the anatomical descriptions themselves. Finish the section, then say *"sawaal?"*.

**Yaad rakho:** Aurat aur mard ka procedure alag hai. Agar class mixed hai aur tum mahsoos karo ke specific anatomy ki tafseel embarrassment paida karti hai — keh do: *"yeh tafseel handout par hai, ghar par padh kar sawaal bhejna"*. Lekin general rules (threshold, method, hand, lavatory adab) sab ke saamne batao.

## The five landmines for Urdu-speaking adult learners

### Landmine 1 · The qibla rule and modern flush toilets

Most homes today have **fixed western-style toilets**. The classmate may say: *"Hamare ghar ka toilet to qibla ki taraf hi hai — kya karein?"*

**Counter-strategy:** Be honest about the Hanafi position. Nur al-Idah says facing/backing the qibla is *makruh tahriman* — **prohibitively disliked**, not haram. If the toilet is fixed and re-orientation is impossible, the rule is to **avoid where avoidable** and **not stress where unavoidable**. The Prophet's instruction was given in an outdoor desert context; the principle (don't disrespect the qibla direction) carries forward, but the modern reality is recognised.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Hanafi madhhab mein qibla ke taraf rukh ya peeth karna makruh tahriman hai — haram nahin. Agar tumhare ghar ka toilet pehle se qibla ki taraf hai aur badalna mumkin nahin — to bus jitna mumkin ho saans kar ke karwaT lo, aur Allah se mafi maango. Yeh haalat-i-majboori hai, gunaah-i-kabira nahin. Lekin agar naye ghar mein toilet lagwa rahe ho — koshish karo ke seedha qibla ki taraf na ho."*

### Landmine 2 · "Sirf paani + tissue se nahin hota?"

Many modern Muslims use **water + tissue** only and have never heard that **stones alone** were the original sunna. They may even doubt stones are valid.

**Counter-strategy:** Don't shame anyone. Affirm what they do (water + tissue effectively = both stones and water → highest level, mubah aur afzal). But teach the truth: **stones alone is a valid sunna method**. If a Muslim is traveling, in nature, has no water — they may use stones (or anything analogous: clean toilet paper, anything non-prohibited) and be completely fine.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Aaj kal ke flush toilets mein paani aur tissue dono hain — yeh actually pooray fiqh ka **sab se aala level** hai. Imam-i-akram ne yahi sab se behtar bataya — wipe phir wash. Lekin yeh bhi yaad rakhna ke agar kabhi safar mein ho, paani na milay — sirf tissue (ya saaf patthar) bhi kaam ka hai. Wudu ho jaayegi. Yeh Allah ki taraf se aasani hai, na ke shart-i-naqs."*

### Landmine 3 · The right-hand rule feels arbitrary

Adult learners with a modern, equality-conscious mindset may push back: *"Daahna baayan ka kya jhagda hai? Donon haath to hain."*

**Counter-strategy:** Don't get defensive. The principle behind the rule is **functional differentiation** — different tools for different jobs, so that the high-value functions (eating, greeting, holding the Mushaf) are never mentally associated with the body's filth. It's the same logic by which a surgeon doesn't use the same gloves for the operation and the cleanup. The right/left distribution is not a hierarchy of worth — it's a hierarchy of *function*.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Daahne haath ko 'oonchaa' aur baayan ko 'neechaa' samajhna ghalat fahmi hai. Asal asool **kaam ki taqseem** hai — izzat ke kaam (khaana, salaam, Mushaf) daahne se; safaai ke kaam baayen se. Jaise surgeon operation ke aur safaai ke gloves alag rakhta hai. Yeh dignity ka asool hai, na ke daahne haath ki 'badi' aur baayan haath ki 'choti' hone ka."*

### Landmine 4 · The "size of a dirham" feels strange

The dirham threshold is technical and the unit unfamiliar. Classmates may struggle to internalise *"how much is 3.5 grams of filth?"*

**Counter-strategy:** Convert it to a **visual analogy** they can carry home. A 3.5g coin is about the **size of a 5-rupee or 5-rupee equivalent coin** — circular, smaller than a 10-rupee coin, larger than a small button. Tell them: *"agar phaila hua hissa is sikke ke barabar ya zyada ho — paani lazmi. Is se kam aur sirhad cross hui ho — koi bhi mayye cheez (paani, gulaab-jal) chalegi."* The visual sticks better than the gram count.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Dirham ka size yaad rakhne ka aasan tareeqa: **chhote sikke jitna**. Aaj kal ke 5-rupee ke sikke ke around. Iss se kam phailaa ho aur sirhad cross hui ho — paani ya gulaab-jal — koi bhi. Iss se zyada — sirf paani. Iss se kam aur sirhad cross hi nahin hui — sirf sunna."*

### Landmine 5 · The fasting-precaution feels paranoid

The "don't relax buttocks during fasting" rule will sound excessive to modern listeners. *"Itna ehtiyaat kyun?"*

**Counter-strategy:** Connect it to the **Hanafi position on the *jawf*** — anything entering the internal body cavity (the *jawf*) breaks the fast, even if not eaten. This includes water, smoke, anything. The book is consistent with this position: during normal istinja, relax fully; during fasting, restrain — because water that *enters* the body is not "external cleaning", it's "internal entry", which voids the fast.

**Urdu mein samjhao:** *"Hanafi madhhab mein **jismani guhha** (jawf) mein koi bhi cheez ka jaana roza tortha hai — chahe paani, dhuaan, dawaa. Toilet mein paani lagaate waqt, agar buttocks bilkul khol diye to paani andar ja sakta hai. Isliye roze ki haalat mein **thoda ehtiyaat** — naa khulwao, aur khade hone se pehle khushk karo. Yeh paranoia nahin — *jawf* ke asool ki seedhi tatbeeq hai."*

## Suggested 75-minute class plan

*Class shuru karne se pehle:* board par **chapter title** (*Kitab al-Tahara — al-Istinja*) likho, aur ek **left-right diagram** — baayaan haath, baayan paaon, baayan side ke saamne; daahna haath/paaon/side ke saamne — kyunki yeh chapter ka mirror pattern hai.

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–5 min | Greetings · why this chapter comes BEFORE wudu (3 sentences — bunyaad hai) |
| 5–15 min | **Threshold rule** — leakage stopped, dirham boundary, sunna/wajib gradient. Use a board diagram with three circles (less than dirham + within boundary, > dirham, ghusl case) |
| 15–25 min | **Method** — 3 levels (stones / water / both), 3-stones-recommended-not-emphasized, Hanafi vs Shafi'i evidence |
| 25–40 min | **Procedure** — male long/short scrotum, female front-to-rear, hand-wash-first, finger order. **Stay calm**. Walk through clinically. Don't take questions mid-explanation. |

## Self-check before teaching

Before the class, make sure:

- [ ] You can state the **dirham threshold** in three forms — gram weight, coin size, and the boundary-of-outlet language. *(Dirham ka teen tareeqon se: 3.5g, sikke ke barabar, sirhad cross.)*
- [ ] You can list the **three method levels** in order — stones / water / both — without notes. *(Stones, paani, dono — teenon ka darja zubaani.)*
- [ ] You can explain why the **three-stones rule is mandub, not emphasized sunna** — including the *"there is no blame"* hadith. *(Hadith zubaani.)*
- [ ] You can describe the **male long-scrotum procedure** in three movements without flinching. *(Front-rear-front, bina jhijhak ke.)*
- [ ] You can recite the **two qibla hadiths** — At-Tirmidhi 606 (the "cover" hadith) and Bukhari 144 (the "do not face/back" hadith). *(Donon zubaani.)*
- [ ] You can recite the **Salman hadith** word-for-word from the book. *(Salman hadith.)*
- [ ] You can give the **entering and exiting du'as** in Arabic + Roman Urdu meaning. *(*Bismillah + isteʿaadhah* andar; *Ghufranak* bahar.)*
- [ ] You can explain the **fasting precaution** — why it's there, what the *jawf* principle is.

If yes to all eight — *tum tayar ho.*

## After class — diagnostic checklist

For each classmate, watch for:

1. **Did they conflate "haram" and "makruh"?** *(Kya woh sab "don't"s ko bara samajh rahe hain?)* — Exposing awra is haram; bones is makruh. The categories matter.
2. **Did the "three stones" rule stick as flexible, not rigid?** *(Kya woh samajh gaye ke teen patthar lazmi nahin?)*
3. **Did they grasp the threshold gradient?** *(Sunna → wajib bil-mayyi → wajib bil-ma' — yeh teen darje yaad hain?)*
4. **Did the right-hand principle land — or did it feel arbitrary?** *(Daahne-baayen ki taqseem ko *function* ke aetebaar se samajh rahe hain ya *worth* ke aetebaar se?)*
5. **Can they recite the entering and exiting du'as from memory next class?** *(Du'a check karna agle hafte.)*

**Yaad rakho:** Agar 2+ classmates qibla-rule-and-modern-toilet par phans rahe hain — woh ek alag short session ka mauzu ban sakta hai (5 minute, Chapter 3 ke shuru mein). Yeh chapter is liye foundational hai ke iske baad wudu (Ch 3) directly aata hai — bunyaad mazboot honi chahiye. Agar cohort istinja ke 5 concepts par mazboot nahin, wudu ka pillar bhi hila hua rahega.
