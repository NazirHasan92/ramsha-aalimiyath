Why istinja comes BEFORE wudu

Chapter 1 taught us about water — the cause of purity.

Chapter 2 teaches us the first use of that water — istinja.

Without proper istinja, the wudu that follows has a weak foundation.

Sirf "safaai" ki baat nahin — yahaan hukm + adab + dhikr + haya ek saath aate hain.

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. State when istinja is sunna and when obligatory (the dirham threshold)
  2. Choose the right method — stones, water, or both
  3. Understand the procedure for men, women, and the finger rule
  4. Distinguish haram from makruh in istinja
  5. Know the major lavatory aadab + the two du'as

Rule 1 · The Threshold Rule

Istinja is not a single yes/no rule — it is a gradient.

Its weight (sunna or obligatory) and its required medium (any liquid or only water) depend on:

  • How far the filth has spread
  • What state of impurity you are in

Istinja ek darja-bandi hai — "haan/nahin" nahin.

Step 0 — Confirm leakage has stopped

Before istinja even begins, the body must be done.

Men

Walking · coughing · leaning to one side — until satisfied

Thoda chalna, khansna, ek taraf jhuk jaana

Women

Wait a small time, then clean

Thodi der intezaar, phir safaai

Wudu may not begin until leakage has stopped.

The دِرْهَم · Dirham — the threshold unit

دِرْهَم

A classical Islamic coin used as a fiqh measure:

  • For solids: ≈ 20 qirat ≈ 3.5 grams
  • For fluids: area of a large coin

📌 Easy memory trick: roughly the size of a small coin (5-rupee).

Dirham ≈ chhote sikke jitna area.

The threshold table

SituationRulingUse
NOT past boundary of outletSunnaStone OR water
Past boundary, ≤ dirhamWajibAny liquid (water, rose water)
Past boundary, > dirhamWajibWater only
Major impurity (janaba, hayd, nifas)WajibWater — any quantity

Sirhad cross na hui — sunna. Cross hui par dirham tak — wajib (any liquid). Dirham se zyada — sirf paani. Ghusl waale haalat — har quantity wajib.

The big insight 💎

The threshold IS the skeleton of this chapter.

Most classroom questions reduce to:

"Is yeh sunna case hai ya wajib case?"

Once you see the gradient — sunna → wajib (any liquid) → wajib (water) → wajib (ghusl, any quantity) — you can answer almost any case.

Rule 2 · The three method levels

Three valid routes, ranked by preference:

  1. Stones alone — sunna, valid
  2. Water alone — superior
  3. Both combined — ⭐ BEST (Prophet's optimum)

Level 1 · Stones alone

The original sunna method. The stone must be:

  • ✅ Clean
  • ✅ Not rough (no burnt clay)
  • ✅ Not valuable

Modern equivalent: clean toilet tissue.

Aaj kal: saaf tissue isi level par hai. Safar mein, paani na ho — yeh tareeqa kaafi hai.

Level 2 · Water alone

"Washing with water is superior than simply wiping the filthy area."

— Nur al-Idah

Sirf paani — patthar se behtar.

Level 3 · BOTH combined ⭐ BEST

"Whereas the best method is to combine between washing with water and wiping. Hence, the individual wipes, and then washes."

Wipe FIRST, then WASH.

📌 Modern flush toilet + tissue + water = already Level 3

Tum log pehle se hi Nabi ﷺ ka best tareeqa karte ho!

The "three stones" rule — what it actually says

You may have heard: "always use three stones".

Nur al-Idah is more precise:

  • Three stones is recommended (mandub)
  • NOT an emphasized sunna
  • Odd numbers preferred (1, 3, 5, 7…)
  • If 2 cleaned the area — 2 is fine

The goal is "clean", not "count".

🕊️ The Hanafi proof (Abu Dawud 35)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said:

"Whomever cleaned, then he should clean in odd numbers, and whoever observed this, has done well and whoever did not, there is no blame."

The decisive phrase: "there is no blame" (la haraj).

"Koi haraj nahin" — Hanafi madhhab ki dalil ke teen patthar wajib nahin, mustahab hain.

Wet the washing hand FIRST.

"If he first touches the soiled area while his hand is dry, it may soak the faeces into the skin."

Then use the inside of the fingers — one, two, or three as needed.

Pehle haath geela karo, phir lagao. Ungliyon ki andar wali side.

Rule 4 · Forbidden vs Disliked

Two different categories of "don't":

HARAM

Unlawful · sinful

One item only

MAKRUH

Disliked · avoid

istinja still valid

HARAM · Exposing the awra

"It is not permitted to expose the private area for istinja during the presence of others… he is regarded as an immoral person [fasiq]."

In Hanafi fiqh, a fasiq's testimony is not accepted in court. The word is heavy.

📌 If onlookers present → do istinja under the clothes.

Privacy pehle, mukammal safaai doosra.

MAKRUH · 5 items

  1. 🦴 Bones — food for jinn
  2. 🍞 Food — waste (israaf)
  3. 🧱 Baked items — bricks, tiles, charcoal, glass, gypsum
  4. 🪡 Items of value — silk, cotton
  5. ✋ Right hand (unless valid reason)

Sab makruh — istinja valid, par avoid karna chahiye.

🕊️ The Salman hadith (Bukhari and Muslim 267)

Salman (RA) said:

"The Prophet forbade us from cleaning the private parts with the right hand, and from cleansing with less than three stones, or cleansing with dung or bone."

Three rulings in a single hadith — the textual backbone of Rule 4.

The right hand — why?

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When anyone of you urinates, he must not touch his penis with his right hand, and when he goes to relieve himself he must not wipe with his right hand, and when he drinks, he must not drink in one breath." (Abu Dawud 31)

👉 Right hand

For HONOUR functions:

Eating · Drinking · Greeting · Holding the Mushaf

👈 Left hand

For BODY-CLEANING functions:

Istinja · cleaning filth · etc.

Yeh "ooncha-neecha" nahin — kaam ki taqseem hai. Surgeon's separate gloves wali misaal.

Rule 5 · آدَابُ قَضَاءِ الْحَاجَة

Lavatory etiquette — from the act to the whole visit.

A complete sunna-aligned visit has a mirror pattern:

🚪 In: LEFT foot + du'a

🚪 Out: RIGHT foot + du'a

Entering — LEFT foot + du'a

The combined entry du'a:

بِسْمِ اللهِ، اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْخُبُثِ وَالْخَبَائِثِ

Bismillah · Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith

"In the name of Allah · O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the male and female unclean spirits."

Nur al-Idah gives these in English (the hadiths of At-Tirmidhi 606 + Abu Dawud 6); the Arabic above is the well-known supplication wording, not printed in the book.

🕊️ Why Bismillah? (At-Tirmidhi 606)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said:

"The cover between the eyes of the jinn and the private parts of the children of Adam is when the person enters the lavatory, he says, 'In the name of Allah'."

Bismillah is the cover. The isteʿaadhah is the protection.

Inside the lavatory — adab

  1. Sit leaning on the LEFT side (helps the body release waste)
  2. Do NOT talk unless need
  3. ★ Do NOT face or back the QIBLA — even within a building (makruh tahriman)
  4. Do not face the sun or moon (signs of Allah)
  5. Do not face the wind (filth blown back)

★ The qibla rule — makruh tahriman

Prohibitively disliked — one level below haram.

The Prophet (ﷺ) said (Bukhari 144):

"If the need to defecate arose, then do not face the qibla, and do not turn around giving it your back, however turn east or west."

📌 Applies even inside a building. The walls don't remove the rule.

Haram nahin, lekin haram ke bohot kareeb. Naya toilet lagwa rahe ho — qibla se duur rakho.

🕊️ The "two cursed" hadith (Muslim 269)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Fear the two that are cursed."

They asked: "Who are those that are cursed, O Messenger of Allah?"

He said: "The one that relieves himself in the road or walkway of the people or in their shade."

Disliked locations are not arbitrary — defiling shared space is cursed.

Exiting — RIGHT leg + du'a

The combined exit du'a:

غُفْرَانَكَ، الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي أَذْهَبَ عَنِّي الْأَذَى وَعَافَانِي

Ghufranak · Alhamdulillah alladhi adhhaba 'anni al-adha wa 'afani

"I ask Your forgiveness · All praise be to Allah, who has repelled harm from me and protected me."

The book prints the Alhamdulillāh… clause in Arabic; Ghufrānak it gives in English ("I ask Your forgiveness", Abu Dawud 30). The Arabic above is the well-known supplication wording.

💎 The big insight

Even the lavatory is a place of dhikr.

Every entry has a du'a. Every exit has a du'a. Every direction has a rule.

The humblest place in the house is wrapped in Bismillah and Alhamdulillah.

Islam doesn't compartmentalise the sacred from the mundane.

Agar tum Allah ko yahaan yaad rakh sakte ho — har jagah yaad rakh paaoge.
