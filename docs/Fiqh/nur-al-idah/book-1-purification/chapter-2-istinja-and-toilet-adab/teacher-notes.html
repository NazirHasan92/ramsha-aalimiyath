🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, the student will be able to:

  1. State the threshold rule — when istinja is sunna, when obligatory with any liquid, and when obligatory with water specifically.
  2. Define the dirham measure (≈ 3.5 g solids; large-coin-size for fluid) and explain its role.
  3. Distinguish the three method levels — stones alone, water alone, both combined — and rank them by preference.
  4. Explain why three stones is recommended but not an emphasized sunna, citing the Hanafi evidence.
  5. Describe the procedure by anatomy — male long/short scrotum, female front-to-rear, and the finger usage order for each.
  6. Distinguish haram (exposing the awra) from makruh (bones, food, baked items, right hand…) in istinja.
  7. List the major lavatory etiquette (aadab) — entering, sitting, exiting, qibla rule, and the disliked spots/positions.
  8. Recite the entering du'a and the exiting du'a from memory.

1 · The Threshold Rule — حُكْمُ الِاسْتِنْجَاء

Istinja is not a single uniform rule — it is a gradient. Its weight (sunna or obligatory) and its required medium (any liquid or only water) depend on how far the filth has spread and what state of impurity the person is in.

Step 0 · Confirming leakage has stopped

From the book
"On passing urine, a person must ensure that the leakage of urine has stopped and his mind is satisfied concerning the issue. This is achieved through one's regular habits, such as walking, coughing, or leaning on one's side. The woman is not required to do this. She is to wait a small time and then clean herself. It is not permissible to begin wudu until one is satisfied that the leakage of urine has stopped."
Roman Urdu
Istinja se pehle itminaan — mard ke liye: thoda chalna, khansna, ek taraf jhuk jaana. Aurat ke liye: thodi der intezaar. Bina itminaan ke wudu jaiz nahin.
Why it matters
If wudu is begun before the body has finished, the wudu is invalid — because new filth will appear during/after.

دِرْهَم · Dirham — the threshold unit

Meaning
A classical Islamic coin. As a fiqh measure: ≈ 20 qirat ≈ 3.5 g for solids; for fluid, the area equivalent to a large coin.
Roman Urdu
Dirham = ≈ 3.5 gram (thos cheez) ya bade sikke ke barabar area (mayye). Yaad rakhne ka aasaan tareeqa: chhote sikke jitna.
Source
"A dirham = 20 qirat with respect to solids (or approx 3.5 grams) and with respect to fluid, the size is equivalent to its area (or the expanse of a large coin)." (Maraky al-Falah 28, cited in Nur al-Idah footnote)

Case A · Filth NOT past the boundary → Sunna

From the book
"Istinja is sunna when the filth that exits from the private part has not spread past the boundary of the outlet. For example: if a person defecates and the faecal matter does not smear beyond the perimeter of the anus, then istinja is sunna. This applies to male and female."
Ruling
Sunna — performed because the Prophet (ﷺ) did it. Stone OR water — either is fine.
Roman Urdu
Sirhad cross na hui — sunna. Patthar ya paani, koi bhi chalega. Mard aur aurat dono ke liye yahi hukm.

Case B · Past boundary, ≤ dirham → Obligatory (any liquid)

From the book
"If the filth crosses the boundary of its outlet and is equal to the size of a dirham, it is necessary to remove it with water or with a liquid cleanser such as rose water."
Ruling
Obligatory (wajib). Any liquid cleanser is acceptable.
Roman Urdu
Sirhad cross hui, par dirham ke barabar tak — wajib. Paani, gulaab-jal, koi bhi mayye cheez chalegi.

Case C · Past boundary, > dirham → Obligatory with water

From the book
"And if the filth passes the boundary of the outlet and is more than the size of a dirham, then it is obligatory to clean with water."
Ruling
Obligatory — and water specifically. Liquid cleansers no longer suffice when the spread exceeds the dirham measure.
Roman Urdu
Dirham se zyada phailaa — sirf paani. Gulaab-jal etc. ab kaafi nahin.

Case D · Major-impurity state → Always obligatory

From the book
"When a person is observing the major bath from post intercourse discharge, menstrual bleeding and postnatal bleeding, it is obligatory to wash the filth in the exit passage, even if it is a minimal quantity. One is not to exert difficulty upon himself when undertaking this cleaning."
Ruling
In any major-impurity (janaba, hayd, nifas) state — washing the filth in the exit passage is obligatory regardless of quantity. But: no exerting hardship.
Roman Urdu
Ghusl wali haalat (jinabat, hayd, nifas) mein — quantity ka koi farq nahin, har soorat mein wajib. Lekin: zyada takleef nahin uthaani.

⚠️ The threshold IS the skeleton of the chapter

Most classroom questions in Chapter 2 reduce to: "is yeh sunna case hai ya wajib case?" Drill this gradient until they can answer instantly. Use real scenarios on the board — "thodi urine ki boond → ?", "thoda zyada phailaa (sikke jitna) → ?", "hayd ke time → ?".

💡 Teaching tip

Draw the threshold as a horizontal arrow on the board with three stops: (1) within boundary = sunna, (2) ≤ dirham = wajib (any liquid), (3) > dirham = wajib (water only). Then add a vertical override: "if in major-impurity state, always wajib regardless of horizontal position". This visual is the cleanest way to teach the gradient.

Level 1 · Stones alone — the sunna method

From the book
"It is sunna to perform istinja with a clean stone or the like provided it is not rough such as burnt clay and not valuable…"
Conditions on the stone
(1) Clean · (2) Not rough or harsh (so it doesn't damage the skin) · (3) Not valuable (no silk, cotton, or items of worth — see Concept 4).
Roman Urdu
Saaf, na khurdura, na keemati patthar — yeh sunna ka tareeqa hai. Aaj kal tissue paper bhi isi asool par chalta hai (clean, not harsh, not valuable).

Level 2 · Water alone — superior to stones alone

From the book
"…and washing with water is superior than simply wiping the filthy area."
Roman Urdu
Sirf paani — patthar se behtar. Asar bhi behtar, safaai bhi behtar.

Level 3 · BOTH combined — ★ the Prophet's optimum

From the book
"Whereas the best method is to combine between washing with water and wiping. Hence, the individual wipes, and then washes. And it is permitted if a person uses only one method."
Sequence
Wipe FIRST (stone removes bulk) — then WASH (water finishes the cleansing).
Roman Urdu
Pehle patthar (yaa tissue) se wipe, phir paani se wash. Yeh sab se behtar tareeqa — aaj kal ke flush toilet (water + tissue) bilkul isi level par hain.
Modern relevance
Today's "water + tissue" practice = Level 3. The Prophetic optimum is already what most modern Muslims do, without realising it has this fiqh ranking.

The "three stones" rule — what it really says

From the book
"The sunna is to purify the area, and to use a prescribed number of stones is recommended, and not an emphasized sunna, and the recommended number of stones to use is three, even if the area is cleaned in less than that."
Hanafi vs Shafi'i
Shafi'i: three is necessary (citing "Perform istinja with three stones"). Hanafi: three is mandub (recommended), not emphasized sunna — relying on the "no blame" hadith below.
Odd numbers
Odd counts preferred (1, 3, 5, 7…). The emphasis is on clean, not count.
Roman Urdu
"Teen patthar" wajib nahin — mustahab hai. Asal hukm: paak ho jaao. Agar do mein ho gaya — do bhi theek. Tak (odd) numbers behtar. Hanafi madhhab Imam Shafi'i se yahaan farq karta hai.

🕊️ The Hanafi proof — "no blame" hadith (Abu Dawud 35)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Whomever cleaned, then he should clean in odd numbers, and whoever observed this, has done well and whoever did not, there is no blame."

The decisive phrase: "la haraj" — "there is no blame". This shows the count is preferred but not strict. The emphasized sunna is the cleaning itself, not the number.

💡 Teaching tip

When introducing the "three levels", ask the class: "Aap log abhi konsa level use karte ho?" Most will realise they're already at Level 3 (water + tissue). Validate this — then explain Level 1 (stones alone) as the original sunna that allows travelers and outdoor cases. Don't make modern toilet habits feel inferior — they aren't.

Male · long / hanging scrotum

From the book
"If the male's scrotum is hanging loosely or stretched, then the procedure of istinja is as follows: one is to wipe with the first stone from the front part near the testicles through to the rear, and with the second from the rear to the front, and with the third from the front to the rear, and this is the etiquette."
Sequence
Stone 1: front → rear · Stone 2: rear → front · Stone 3: front → rear.
Why end at rear
"It is recommended to conclude the wiping at the rear so that the filth is not brought to the front and spread all over due to the long scrotum." Anatomy decides the order.
Roman Urdu
Long scrotum waale mardon ke liye — pehla patthar aage se peeche, doosra peeche se aage, teesra aage se peeche. Aakhri harkat peeche par khatam — taa ke gandgi aage na phaile.

Male · short / not-hanging scrotum

From the book
"If however, the scrotum is not hanging loosely, then one is to begin from the rear to front because in this situation, cleaning can be optimally achieved."
Sequence
Single direction: rear → front.
Roman Urdu
Short scrotum — seedha peeche se aage. Is anatomy mein yahi optimal safaai deti hai.

Female · procedure

From the book
"The female is to begin from the front to the rear, for fear of defiling her vagina."
Sequence
Front → rear, single direction.
Why
To prevent contamination of the vagina from the rear. Modern hygiene science fully agrees with this.
Roman Urdu
Auraton ke liye sirf aage se peeche — taa ke peeche ki gandgi aage (vagina) ki taraf na aaye. Modern medical hygiene bhi yahi kehti hai.

The hand-washing rule

From the book
"After wiping, the male is to first wash his hand because if he first touches the soiled area while his hand is dry, it may soak the faeces into the skin; thereafter, he is to rub the soiled area with water using the inside of his finger, two fingers or three if needed."
The rule
Wet the washing hand first, then approach the soiled area. Dry hand → faeces soaks into skin → harder to remove.
Which surface
Use the inside surface of the fingers — one, two, or three as needed. Not the back of the hand, not the palm centre.
Roman Urdu
Pehle haath geela karo, phir lagao. Ungliyon ke andar wali side use karo — ek, do, ya teen jitni zaroorat ho.

Finger order — male

From the book
"The male is to raise his middle finger over the other fingers at the beginning of the istinja, and thereafter is to raise his ring finger forward, as it insures optimum cleaning. The male is not to restrict the cleaning to only one finger because cleaning will not be optimally achieved."
Order
Middle finger first (raised over the others) — then ring finger advances. One finger alone is not enough.
Roman Urdu
Mard: pehle bicchli ungli (middle) — phir anaamika (ring) aage. Sirf ek ungli kaafi nahin — optimal safaai nahin hoti.

Finger order — female

From the book
"The female is to raise her ring finger along with her middle finger simultaneously at the start of istinja for fear of being sexually aroused. This means that the woman uses the majority of her fingers when she begins."
Order
Ring + middle finger simultaneously at the start (not one then the other).
Why different
The reason given in the book: "for fear of being sexually aroused". The book preserves this reasoning — preserve it when you teach.
Roman Urdu
Aurat: anaamika + bicchli ek saath shuru se. Wajh kitaab mein di hui hai (jinsi tahreek ka khof) — usi ko bayan karo, ratta nahin maarne wala.

Cleaning thoroughly & the fasting precaution

From the book
"One is to perform the utmost in cleaning the area until the foul smell is gone, and is to thoroughly relax the buttocks or hips in order to clean what is in the passage to the best of one's ability, though this relaxation of the buttocks and hips is performed provided one is not fasting, and the basis for this is that there is water present around the anus which will nullify one's fast if it enters and one must be cautious not to allow their wet finger from entering the private area, otherwise the fast breaks."
Normal case
Clean until the foul smell is gone. Relax the muscles to allow thorough cleaning.
Fasting case
Do NOT fully relax — water or wet finger entering the body cavity (jawf) breaks the fast.
After finishing
(1) Wash the hand a second time. (2) If fasting, dry the buttocks before standing.
Roman Urdu
Aam haalat mein — utmost safaai, foul smell tak khatam. Roze ki haalat mein — buttocks zyada na khulwao, khade hone se pehle khushk karo. Kyunki jawf mein paani jaana roza torta hai.

HARAM · Exposing the awra (private area)

From the book
"It is not permitted to expose the private area [awra] for istinja during the presence of others. If there are onlookers, then istinja is performed under the clothes. If one uncovers the private area in the presence of others, he is regarded as an immoral person [fasiq] due to its unlawfulness."
The weight of "fasiq"
In Hanafi fiqh, a fasiq's testimony is not accepted in court; his religious standing is compromised. This is not light language — privacy of the body is sacred.
When you cannot conceal yourself
"If the filth crosses the boundary of one's exit passage and is greater than the size of a dirham, prayer is not valid if there is something available to remove the impurity, such as water. One is to do his best in removing the impurity without exposing the private area to onlookers."
Roman Urdu
Doosron ke saamne satar khol kar istinja karna haraam hai. Aisa karne wala fasiq. Logon ke saamne ho — kapdon ke neeche karo. Privacy pehle, mukammal safaai doosra.

MAKRUH · Bones & food

From the book
"It is disliked to use bones and the food of humans or animals because it is deemed as waste and because the Prophet (ﷺ) prevented us from doing so as he said [Do not perform istinja with faeces or bones, for it is food for your brothers of the jinn] and he said [Do not waste, do not waste]."
Reason 1
Bones — food of the jinn (per the Prophet's hadith).
Reason 2
Food — waste. "Do not waste, do not waste."
Roman Urdu
Haddi (jinnon ka khaana) aur khaana (israaf) dono makruh. Reference: an-Nasai I/72, Ibn Majah 424.

MAKRUH · Baked items, items of value, right hand

From the book
"It is disliked to use baked bricks and anything made with fire, such as tiles, as well as earthenware, charcoal, glass, gypsum and items of value or respectable such as silk or cotton. It is disliked to use the right hand unless there is a valid reason."
Baked items
Bricks, tiles, earthenware, charcoal, glass, gypsum — anything fired/manufactured.
Items of value
Silk, cotton — disrespect to wealth (israaf).
Right hand
Disliked unless valid reason (injury to left hand, etc.). The right hand is for honour: eating, drinking, greeting, holding the Mushaf.
Roman Urdu
Aag se banayi cheezein (eent, tile, koyla, sheesha), keemati cheezein (silk, cotton), aur daahna haath — sab makruh. Daahna haath izzat ke kaamon ke liye; baayan haath safaai ke liye.

🕊️ The Salman hadith — three rules in one (Bukhari and Muslim 267)

Salman (RA) said: "The Prophet forbade us from cleaning the private parts with the right hand, and from cleansing with less than three stones, or cleansing with dung or bone."

Three rulings in a single hadith: (1) no right hand · (2) no cleansing with less than three stones · (3) no dung or bone. This hadith is the textual backbone of Concept 4.

🕊️ The right-hand hadith (Abu Dawud 31)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When anyone of you urinates, he must not touch his penis with his right hand, and when he goes to relieve himself he must not wipe with his right hand, and when he drinks, he must not drink in one breath."

The principle: right hand = honour functions (drinking, greeting, eating). Left hand = body cleaning. The differentiation is functional, not hierarchical.

⚠️ Don't conflate haram and makruh

This is the single most important distinction in Concept 4. A makruh act of istinja (using the right hand, using a baked tile) is disliked but the istinja is still valid — wudu can proceed. The haram act (exposing awra in front of others) is sinful — the person is a fasiq for doing it, even if the istinja "technically worked".

💡 Teaching tip

Recite the Salman hadith aloud in class — slowly. Three rules in one hadith is rare and elegant; the cohort will remember it. Then quiz: "Daahna haath se istinja — kya hai?" Makruh. "Doosron ke saamne satar khol kar istinja — kya hai?" Haram. Drill the haram/makruh distinction until it's reflexive.

5 · Lavatory Etiquette — آدَابُ قَضَاءِ الْحَاجَة

From the act of cleaning to the whole bathroom visit. A complete sunna-aligned visit has a mirror pattern: left foot in + du'a, right foot out + du'a.

Where this fits: the last concept, and most emotionally connective. Even the lavatory has dhikr. End the class with this section if you can.

Entering — LEFT foot, du'a

From the book
"One is to enter the lavatory with the left foot and is to seek protection in Allah from Satan the rejected before entering."
Du'a (combined from the two hadiths)
Bismillah · Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith. (In the name of Allah · O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the male and female unclean spirits.)
Roman Urdu
Baayaan paaon andar + Bismillah (jinn ki nazar se parda) + A'udhu billahi min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith (gande ruhon se panaah).

Inside — sit leaning LEFT, don't talk

From the book
"One is to sit leaning upon his left side and is not to talk unless there is a need."
Why lean left
Physically aids the body in releasing waste. Practical sunna with anatomical reasoning.
Hadith on talking
"When two persons go together for relieving themselves uncovering their private parts and talking together, Allah the Great and Majestic, becomes wrathful at this action." (Abu Dawud 15)
Roman Urdu
Andar baayan jhuk ke baitho, baat-cheet mat karo (zaroorat na ho to). Yeh sunna jismani aetebaar se bhi sahi hai, aadab ke aetebaar se bhi.

★ The qibla rule — makruh tahriman

From the book
"It is prohibitively disliked to face the qibla at the time of relief and to turn around giving it one's back even if the toilet is within a building. The Prophet (ﷺ) said [If the need to defecate arose, then do not face the qibla, and do not turn around giving it your back, however turn east or west]." (Bukhari 144)
The weight
Makruh tahriman — prohibitively disliked. One level below haram. Sinful to do deliberately, but does not invalidate istinja.
Inside a building
The book explicitly includes the indoor case — "even if the toilet is within a building". Walls do not remove the rule.
Modern context
If a fixed toilet at home faces the qibla and cannot be moved, the rule is: avoid where avoidable, don't stress where unavoidable. The Hanafi position recognises hardship.
Roman Urdu
Qibla ki taraf rukh ya peeth karna makruh tahriman (haram nahin, lekin haram ke bohot kareeb). Ghar ke andar bhi yahi hukm. Naya toilet lagwa rahe ho — qibla se duur rakho. Pehle se lagaa hai aur badal nahin sakte — Allah se mafi maango aur naa-ilaaji ki haalat mein karo.

Disliked positions, places, and times

Facing sun/moon
"To face the sun or moon directly is disliked because they are great signs of Allah's creation."
Facing wind
"To face a blowing wind is disliked at the time of relief because the filth is likely to be blown back onto one's body or clothes." (Practical hygiene.)
In water
"It is disliked to urinate or defecate in water even if it is flowing…"
In shade
"…and the same applies in a place of shade because it may be a rest area for other people."
In hole / road / cemetery
"It is disapproved to urinate or defecate in a hole, on a road or a cemetery." (Muslim 269 — the "two cursed" hadith below.)
Under fruit tree
"To relieve oneself under a fruit tree is disapproved."
Standing
"To urinate standing is disliked unless there is a reason, such as an injured back." Anas: "If the Prophet wanted to attend to a need, he would not lift his clothes until he was close to the ground." (At-Tirmidhi, The Book of Purification 14)
In place of wudu
"It is also disliked to urinate in the place of wudu due to the onset of satanic whispers which may be brought on by such a practice…"
Roman Urdu
Disliked places yaad rakho: paani, gaddha, raasta, qabristan, phaldar daraakht, chhaan, wudu ki jagah. Disliked position: khade ho ke peshab (bina wajh). Sab se aham: qibla ki taraf rukh/peeth — makruh tahriman.

Exiting — RIGHT leg, du'a

From the book
"One is to exit the lavatory with the right leg because it has more right to be advanced when exiting such a place."
Du'a from the book
"One is then to say, 'All praise be to Allah, the One who has repelled harm from me and protected me.' And the Prophet (ﷺ) said at the time of exiting [I ask Your forgiveness]" (i.e. Ghufranak; Abu Dawud 30).
Combined du'a
Ghufranak. Alhamdulillah alladhi adhhaba 'anni al-adha wa 'afani. (I ask Your forgiveness. All praise be to Allah, who repelled harm from me and protected me.)
Roman Urdu
Daahna paaon bahar + Ghufranak (mafi maangna) + Alhamdulillah (taklif door hone ka shukr). Yeh entry ka mirror hai — daakhle ke ulta.

🕊️ The "two cursed" hadith (Muslim 269)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Fear the two that are cursed." They inquired: "Who are those that are cursed, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "The one that relieves himself in the road or walkway of the people or in their shade."

Lesson: the disliked locations are not arbitrary — they harm other Muslims (their walkway, their shade, their rest areas). Defiling shared space is not just unhygienic; it is cursed.

🕊️ The "cover" hadith (At-Tirmidhi 606)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The cover between the eyes of the jinn and the private parts of the children of Adam is when the person enters the lavatory, he says, 'In the name of Allah'."

The textual basis for saying Bismillah on entering the bathroom. Bismillah is the cover; the isteʿaadhah is the protection.

💡 Teaching tip — the mirror pattern

End the class by drawing the mirror pattern on the board:

  • Entering: LEFT foot + Bismillah + isteʿaadhah
  • Exiting: RIGHT leg + Ghufranak + Alhamdulillah

Tell them: "Even the bathroom — the humblest place in the house — is wrapped in Bismillah and Alhamdulillah. Islam doesn't compartmentalise the sacred from the mundane. If you can remember Allah here, you will remember Him everywhere." This is the chapter's deepest spiritual lesson.

⚠️ Common Student Mistakes — Watch For These

1. Treating "three stones" as strict

Many classmates have heard "use three stones" as a rule and assume that's the obligation. Nur al-Idah is clear: three stones is recommended (mandub), not an emphasized sunna. The emphasized requirement is purification. The Hanafi proof is the "no blame" hadith — "la haraj".

2. Confusing haram with makruh

Exposing the awra in front of others = haram (one becomes fasiq). Using bones, baked items, the right hand = makruh. A makruh act of istinja is still valid; the haram act is sinful regardless. Drill this distinction.

3. Skipping the "leakage stopped" step

Classmates often miss the pre-istinja step: ensure the leakage of urine has stopped. Without this confirmation, wudu cannot validly begin — even if istinja was technically performed.

4. Thinking "qibla rule" is haram

Some classmates may say "qibla ki taraf rukh karna haram hai". Nur al-Idah's specific ruling is makruh tahriman — prohibitively disliked, one level below haram. Sinful to do deliberately, but istinja itself is not invalidated.

5. Misapplying the dirham threshold

The dirham measures the area of filth that has spread past the outlet's boundary, not the total quantity of waste. A classmate may confuse this. Use a visual: a circular area roughly the size of a 5-rupee (or analogous local) coin.

6. Forgetting the fasting precaution

When fasting, do NOT fully relax the buttocks (water entering the jawf breaks the fast), and dry the area before standing. This is the Hanafi position consistent with the broader jawf rule for sawm.

📿 Connect to the Qur'an & Hadith

Every hadith below is quoted by Nur al-Idah itself in Chapter 2. Use 1–2 in class to anchor the rulings in the texts.

The Salman hadith — three rules in one (Bukhari and Muslim 267)

Salman (RA) said: "The Prophet forbade us from cleaning the private parts with the right hand, and from cleansing with less than three stones, or cleansing with dung or bone."

This single hadith is the textual backbone of Concept 4 — no right hand, no fewer than three stones (Shafi'i reading), no bones.

The "no blame" hadith — Hanafi proof on the count (Abu Dawud 35)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Whomever cleaned, then he should clean in odd numbers, and whoever observed this, has done well and whoever did not, there is no blame."

The decisive phrase: "there is no blame" — Hanafi evidence that three is recommended, not obligatory.

The right-hand hadith (Abu Dawud 31)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When anyone of you urinates, he must not touch his penis with his right hand, and when he goes to relieve himself he must not wipe with his right hand, and when he drinks, he must not drink in one breath."

The right hand is for honour functions. The principle is functional, not hierarchical.

The "cover" hadith (At-Tirmidhi 606)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The cover between the eyes of the jinn and the private parts of the children of Adam is when the person enters the lavatory, he says, 'In the name of Allah'."

Textual basis for saying Bismillah on entering the lavatory — Bismillah is the cover.

The "evil ones of the jinn" hadith (Abu Dawud 6)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The evil ones of the jinns are present, so if anyone enters the lavatory, he should say, 'I seek refuge with Allah from the unclean spirits, male and female'."

Textual basis for the isteʿaadhah on entering — Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min al-khubuthi wal-khaba'ith.

The "two-persons" hadith (Abu Dawud 15)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When two persons go together for relieving themselves uncovering their private parts and talking together, Allah the Great and Majestic, becomes wrathful at this action."

Textual basis for the "no talking" rule and the gravity of bathroom privacy.

The qibla hadith (Bukhari 144)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "If the need to defecate arose, then do not face the qibla, and do not turn around giving it your back, however turn east or west."

Textual basis for the makruh tahriman of facing/backing the qibla.

The "two cursed" hadith (Muslim 269)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Fear the two that are cursed." They asked: "Who are those that are cursed, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "The one that relieves himself in the road or walkway of the people or in their shade."

The disliked locations are not arbitrary — defiling shared space is cursed.

The Anas hadith on standing (At-Tirmidhi, The Book of Purification 14)

Anas (RA) said: "If the Prophet wanted to attend to a need, he would not lift his clothes until he was close to the ground."

Textual basis for the dislike of urinating standing — preserves modesty and prevents splashing.
